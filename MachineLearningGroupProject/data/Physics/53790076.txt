From a biological standpoint, the goal-directed molecular motions inside living cells are carried out by [[biopolymer]]s acting like [[molecular machine]]s (e.g. [[myosin]], RNA/DNA polymerase, [[Ion pump (biology)|ion pumps]], etc.). These molecular machines are driven by '''conformons''', that is sequence-specific mechanical strains generated by free energy released in chemical reactions or stress induced destabilisations in supercoiled biopolymer chains. Therefore, conformons can be defined as packets of conformational energy generated from substrate binding or chemical reactions and confined within biopolymers.<ref>{{Cite book|title=Molecular Theory of the Living Cell - Springer|journal = Molecular Theory of the Living Cell: Concepts|last=Ji|first=Sungchul|language=en|doi=10.1007/978-1-4614-2152-8|bibcode = 2012mtlc.book.....J|year = 2012|isbn = 978-1-4614-2151-1|url = http://cds.cern.ch/record/1453271}}</ref>

On the other hand, from a physics standpoint, the conformon is a localization of elastic and electronic energy which may propagate in space with or without dissipation.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Atanasov|first=Victor|last2=Dandoloff|first2=Rossen|title=Quantum-elastic bump on a surface|url=http://stacks.iop.org/0143-0807/38/i=1/a=015405?key=crossref.1c8a808e8c6ca8672e53732ec813d2f7|journal=European Journal of Physics|volume=38|issue=1|pages=015405|doi=10.1088/0143-0807/38/1/015405|arxiv=1402.2078|bibcode=2017EJPh...38a5405A|year=2017}}{{Dead link|date=July 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> The mechanism which involves dissipationless propagation is a form of molecular superconductivity.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Balakrishnan|first=Radha|last2=Dandoloff|first2=Rossen|date=2008-01-01|title=Effect of conformations on charge transport in a thin elastic tube|url=http://stacks.iop.org/0951-7715/21/i=1/a=001|journal=Nonlinearity|language=en|volume=21|issue=1|pages=1–11|doi=10.1088/0951-7715/21/1/001|issn=0951-7715|arxiv=0803.3407|bibcode=2008Nonli..21....1B}}</ref> On quantum mechanical level both elastic/vibrational and electronic energy can be quantised, therefore the conformon carries a fixed portion of energy. This has led to the definition of quantum of conformation (shape).<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Atanasov|first=Victor|last2=Omar|first2=Yasser|date=2010-01-01|title=Quanta of local conformational change: conformons in α-helical proteins|url=http://stacks.iop.org/1367-2630/12/i=5/a=055003|journal=New Journal of Physics|language=en|volume=12|issue=5|pages=055003|doi=10.1088/1367-2630/12/5/055003|issn=1367-2630|arxiv=0906.0456|bibcode=2010NJPh...12e5003A}}</ref>
