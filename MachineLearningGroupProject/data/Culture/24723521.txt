The following [[Outline (list)|outline]] is provided as an overview of and topical guide to culture:

'''[[Culture]]''' &ndash; set of patterns of human activity within a [[community]] or social group and the symbolic structures that give significance to such activity. Customs, laws, dress, architectural style, social standards, religious beliefs, and traditions are all examples of cultural elements.  Since 2010, Culture is considered the Fourth Pillar of Sustainable Development by UNESCO.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.agenda21culture.net/sites/default/files/files/documents/en/zz_culture4pillarsd_eng.pdf |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2017-12-28 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20171228112948/http://www.agenda21culture.net/sites/default/files/files/documents/en/zz_culture4pillarsd_eng.pdf |archivedate=2017-12-28 }}</ref> More: [[Agenda 21]] for Culture or in short [[Culture 21]].

== Cultural groups ==

* [[Community]] &ndash; a social unit of any size that shares common values. Communities range in size and scope from neighbourhoods to national communities to international communities. They can be physical (face-to-face) or virtual (online). 
** [[People]] &ndash; a plurality of persons considered as a whole, as is the case with an ethnic group or nation. Collectively, for example, the contemporary Frisians and Danes are two related Germanic peoples, while various Middle Eastern ethnic groups are often linguistically categorized as the Semitic people. See the list of contemporary ethnic groups for more examples. 
*** [[Ethnic group]] &ndash; socially defined category of people who identify with each other based on common ancestral, social, cultural or national experience. Membership of an ethnic group tends to be defined by a shared cultural heritage, ancestry, origin myth, history, homeland, language and/or dialect, symbolic systems such as religion, mythology and ritual, cuisine, dressing style, physical appearance, etc.
* [[Society]] &ndash; a group of people involved in persistent interpersonal relationships, or a large social grouping sharing the same geographical or social territory, typically subject to the same political authority and dominant cultural expectations. Human societies are characterized by patterns of relationships (social relations) between individuals who share a distinctive culture and institutions; a given society may be described as the total of such relationships among its constituent members. 
** [[Civilization]] &ndash; any complex society characterized by urban development, social stratification, symbolic communication forms (typically, writing systems), and a perceived separation from and domination over the natural environment.

== Elements of culture ==
* '''[[The arts]]''' &ndash; vast subdivision of culture, composed of many creative endeavors and disciplines. The arts encompasses visual arts, literary arts and the performing arts.
** '''[[Clothing]]''' &ndash; [[Fashion]], [[Jewelry]]
** '''[[Gastronomy]]''' &ndash; the art and science of good eating,<ref>Merriam-Webster [http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/gastronomy "Gastronomy - Definition"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120127172514/http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/gastronomy |date=2012-01-27 }}</ref> including the study of food and culture.
*** [[Outline of food preparation|Food preparation]] &ndash; act of preparing foodstuffs for eating. It encompasses a vast range of methods, tools, and combinations of ingredients to improve the flavour and digestibility of food.
*** '''Food and drink''' <!-- See [[:Category:Food and drink]] -->
**** [[Outline of cuisines|Cuisines]] &ndash; a cuisine is a specific set of cooking traditions and practices, often associated with a specific culture.
**** [[Outline of chocolate|Chocolate]] &ndash; raw or processed food produced from the seed of the tropical ''Theobroma cacao'' tree.
**** [[Outline of wine|Wine]] &ndash; alcoholic beverage, made of fermented fruit juice, usually from grapes.<ref name=britannica>{{cite encyclopedia |encyclopedia=Encyclopædia Britannica |title=wine |url=http://www.britannica.com/eb/article-9106005/wine |accessdate=25 June 2008 |publisher=Encyclopædia Britannica Online |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080617101909/http://www.britannica.com/eb/article-9106005/wine |archivedate=17 June 2008 }}</ref>
** '''[[Outline of literature|Literature]]''' &ndash; the art of written works.
*** [[Children's literature]] &ndash; stories, books, and poems for children.
*** [[Outline of fiction|Fiction]] &ndash; any form of narrative which deals, in part or whole, with events that are not factual, but rather, imaginary and invented by its author(s). ''[[#Fiction|See below]].''
*** [[Non-fiction]] &ndash; a form of any narrative, account, or other communicative work whose assertions and descriptions are understood to be factual.
*** [[Outline of poetry|Poetry]] &ndash; literary art in which language is used for its aesthetic and evocative qualities in addition to, or instead of, its apparent meaning.
*** [[Outline of critical theory|Critical theory]]<!-- critical theory based on literary criticism --> &ndash; examination and critique of society and culture, drawing from knowledge across the social sciences and humanities.
** {{anchor|Performing arts}}'''[[Outline of performing arts|Performing arts]]''' &ndash; those forms of art that use the artist's own body, face, and presence as a medium.
*** [[Circus]] &ndash; performance of a company of clowns, acrobats, trained animals, trapeze artists, musicians, hoopers, tightrope walkers, jugglers, unicyclists and other object-manipulating and stunt-oriented artists, and a ringmaster.
*** [[Comedy]] &ndash; any discourse or work generally intended to be humorous or to amuse by inducing laughter, especially in theatre, television, film and stand-up comedy.
**** [[Stand-up comedy]] &ndash; performance by a comedian in front of a live audience, usually speaking directly to them.
*** [[Outline of dance|Dance]] &ndash; art form of movement of the body, usually rhythmic and to music,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.britannica.com/eb/article-9110116/dance|title=Dance - performing arts|website=britannica.com|accessdate=24 April 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080727013318/http://www.britannica.com/eb/article-9110116/dance|archivedate=27 July 2008}}</ref> used as a form of expression, social interaction, or presented in a spiritual or performance setting.
*** [[Outline of film|Film]] &ndash; moving pictures, the art form that records performances visually.
*** [[Outline of theatre|Theatre]] &ndash; a collaborative form of fine art that uses live performers to present the experience of a real or imagined event before a live audience in a specific place.
*** [[Outline of music|Music]] &ndash; an art form the medium of which is sound and silence.
**** Music genres
***** [[Outline of jazz|Jazz]] &ndash; a musical style that originated at the beginning of the 20th century in African American communities in the Southern United States, mixing African and European music traditions.
***** [[List of opera topics|Opera]] &ndash; an art form in which singers and musicians perform a dramatic work combining text (called a libretto) and musical score.<ref>Some definitions of opera: "dramatic performance or composition of which music is an essential part, a branch of art concerned with this" (''[[Concise Oxford English Dictionary]]''); "any dramatic work that can be sung (or at times declaimed or spoken) in a place for performance, set to original music for singers (usually in costume) and instrumentalists" (Amanda Holden, ''Viking Opera Guide''); "musical work for the stage with singing characters, originated in early years of 17th century" (''[[Pears Cyclopaedia]]'', 1983 ed.).</ref>
**** Musical instruments &ndash; devices created or adapted to make musical sounds.
***** [[Outline of guitars|Guitars]] &ndash; the guitar is a plucked string instrument, usually played with fingers or a pick. The guitar consists of a body with a rigid neck to which the strings, generally six in number, are attached. Guitars are traditionally constructed of various woods and strung with animal gut or, more recently, with either nylon or steel strings.
*** [[Outline of stagecraft|Stagecraft]] &ndash; technical aspects of theatrical, film, and video production. It includes, but is not limited to, constructing and rigging scenery, hanging and focusing of lighting, design and procurement of costumes, makeup, procurement of props, stage management, and recording and mixing of sound.
** '''[[Outline of the visual arts|Visual arts]]''' &ndash; art forms that create primarily visual works.
*** [[Outline of architecture|Architecture]] &ndash; The art and science of designing and erecting buildings and other physical structures.
**** [[Outline of classical architecture|Classical architecture]] &ndash; the architecture of classical antiquity and later architectural styles influenced by it.
*** [[Outline of crafts|Crafts]] &ndash; recreational activities and hobbies that involve making things with one's hands and skill.
*** [[Outline of design|Design]] &ndash; the process for planning the overall look of an object
*** [[Outline of drawing and drawings|Drawing]] &ndash; visual art that makes use of any number of drawing instruments to mark a two-dimensional medium.
*** [[Outline of film|Film]] &ndash; moving pictures.
*** [[Outline of painting|Painting]] &ndash; the practice of applying paint, pigment, colour or another medium to a surface with a brush or other object.
**** [[Outline of painting history|History of painting]]
*** [[Outline of photography|Photography]] &ndash; art, science, and practice of creating pictures by recording radiation on a radiation-sensitive medium, such as a photographic film, or electronic image sensors.
*** [[Outline of sculpture|Sculpture]] &ndash; three-dimensional artwork created by shaping or combining hard materials - typically stone such as marble - or metal, glass, or wood.
* '''[[Outline of entertainment|Entertainment]]''' &ndash; any activity which provides a diversion or permits people to amuse themselves in their leisure time. Entertainment is generally passive, such as watching opera or a movie.
** {{anchor|Fiction}}[[Outline of fiction|Fiction]] &ndash;  any form of narrative which deals, in part or whole, with events that are not factual, but rather, imaginary and invented by its author(s).
*** [[Outline of James Bond|James Bond]] &ndash; fictional character created in 1953 by writer Ian Fleming. Since then, the character has grown to icon status, featured in many novels, movies, video games and other media.
*** [[Fantasy]] &ndash; genre of fiction using magic and the supernatural as primary elements of plot, theme or setting, often in imaginary worlds, generally avoiding the technical/scientific content typical of Science fiction, but overlapping with it
**** [[Outline of Middle-earth|Middle-earth]] &ndash; fantasy setting by writer J.R.R. Tolkien, home to hobbits, orcs, and many other mystical races and creatures.
*** [[Outline of science fiction|Science fiction]] &ndash; a genre of fiction dealing with imaginary but more or less plausible (or at least nonsupernatural) content such as future settings, futuristic science and technology, space travel, aliens, and paranormal abilities.  Exploring the consequences of scientific innovations is one purpose of science fiction, making it a "literature of ideas."<ref>{{cite web |author1=Marg Gilks |author2=Paula Fleming |author3=Moira Allen |name-list-style=amp |title=Science Fiction: The Literature of Ideas |publisher=WritingWorld.com |year=2003 |url=http://www.writing-world.com/sf/sf.shtml |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080526013038/http://www.writing-world.com/sf/sf.shtml |archivedate=2008-05-26 }}</ref>
** '''[[Outline of games|Games]]''' &ndash;  structured playing, usually undertaken for enjoyment, involving goals, rules, challenge, and interaction.
*** Board games
**** [[Outline of chess|Chess]] &ndash; two-player board game played on a chessboard, a square-checkered board with 64 squares arranged in an eight-by-eight grid. Each player begins the game with sixteen pieces: One king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns.
*** Card games
**** [[Outline of poker|Poker]] &ndash; family of card games that share betting rules and usually (but not always) hand rankings.
*** [[Outline of video games|Video games]] &ndash; electronic games that involve interaction with a user interface to generate visual feedback on a video device.
** '''[[Outline of performing arts|Performing arts]]''' &ndash; those forms of art that use the artist's own body, face, and presence as a medium. ''[[#Performing arts|See above]]''.
** '''[[Outline of sports|Sports]]''' &ndash;  organized, competitive, entertaining, and skilful activity requiring commitment, strategy, and fair play, in which a winner can be defined by objective means. Generally speaking, a sport is a game based in physical athleticism.
*** '''Ball games'''
**** [[Outline of baseball|Baseball]] &ndash; bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of nine players each. The aim is to score runs by hitting a thrown ball with a bat and touching a series of four bases arranged at the corners of a ninety-foot diamond.
**** [[Outline of basketball|Basketball]] &ndash; team sport in which two teams of five players try to score points by throwing or "shooting" a ball through the top of a basketball hoop while following a set of rules.
**** [[Outline of tennis|Tennis]] &ndash; sport usually played between two players (singles) or between two teams of two players each (doubles), using specialized racquets to strike a felt-covered hollow rubber ball over a net into the opponent's court.
*** [[Outline of canoeing and kayaking|Canoeing and kayaking]] &ndash; two closely related forms of watercraft paddling, involving manually propelling and navigating specialized boats called canoes and kayaks using a blade that is joined to a shaft, known as a paddle, in the water.
*** '''[[Combat sport]]s'''
**** [[Outline of fencing|Fencing]] &ndash; family of combat sports using bladed weapons.
**** [[Outline of martial arts|Martial arts]] &ndash; extensive systems of codified practices and traditions of combat, practised for a variety of reasons, including self-defence, competition, physical health and fitness, as well as mental and spiritual development.
*** [[Outline of cycling#Cycling sport|Cycling sport]] &ndash; bicycle racing and track cycling.
*** [[Outline of motorcycles and motorcycling|Motorcycling]] &ndash; riding a motorcycle. A variety of subcultures and lifestyles have been built up around motorcycling and motorcycle racing.
*** [[Outline of running|Running]] &ndash; moving rapidly on foot, during which both feet are off the ground at regular intervals.
* [[Outline of the humanities|Humanities]] &ndash; academic disciplines that study the human condition, using methods that are primarily analytical, critical, or speculative, as distinguished from the mainly empirical approaches of the natural sciences.
** '''Area studies''' <!-- See [[:Category: Area studies]] --> &ndash; comprehensive interdisciplinary research and the academic study of the people and communities of particular regions. Disciplines applied to include history, political science, sociology, cultural studies, languages, geography, literature, and related disciplines.
*** [[Outline of sinology|Sinology]] &ndash; study of China and things related to China, such as its classical language and literature.
** [[Outline of classical studies|Classical studies]] &ndash; a branch of the Humanities comprising the languages, literature, philosophy, history, art, archaeology and all other cultural elements of the ancient Mediterranean world (Bronze Age ca. BC 3000 – Late Antiquity ca. AD 300–600); especially Ancient Greece and Ancient Rome.
* [[Mass media]] &ndash; diversified media technologies and their content that are intended to reach a large audience by mass communication. Includes radio and television programming; mass publishing of books, magazines, and newspapers; web content; and films and audio recordings.
* [[Tradition]] &ndash; belief or behaviour passed down within a group or society with symbolic meaning or special significance with origins in the past. Common examples include holidays or impractical but socially meaningful clothes (like lawyer wigs or military officer spurs), but the idea has also been applied to social norms such as greetings.
** '''Celebration''' &ndash;
*** [[Outline of festivals|Festivals]] &ndash; entertainment events centring on and celebrating a unique aspect of a community, usually staged by that community.
* [[Tourism]] &ndash; travel for recreational, leisure, or business purposes. The World Tourism Organization defines tourists as people "travelling to and staying in places outside their usual environment for not more than one consecutive year for leisure, business and other purposes." Tourism is important, and in some cases, vital for many countries. It was recognized in the ''Manila Declaration on World Tourism of 1980'' as "an activity essential to the life of nations because of its direct effects on the social, cultural, educational, and economic sectors of national societies and their international relations."<ref name="unwto1034">{{cite web|year=1995|url=http://pub.unwto.org/WebRoot/Store/Shops/Infoshop/Products/1034/1034-1.pdf|title=UNWTO technical manual: Collection of Tourism Expenditure Statistics|page=10|publisher=World Tourism Organization|accessdate=26 March 2009|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100922120940/http://pub.unwto.org/WebRoot/Store/Shops/Infoshop/Products/1034/1034-1.pdf|archivedate=22 September 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite conference|url= http://www.univeur.org/CMS/UserFiles/65.%20Manila.PDF|title= Manila Declaration on World Tourism|date= 10 October 1980|pages= 1–4|location= [[Manila]], [[Philippines]]|conference= World Tourism Conference|url-status= dead|archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20121120180003/http://www.univeur.org/CMS/UserFiles/65.%20Manila.PDF|archivedate= 20 November 2012|access-date= 14 May 2013}}</ref>
** [[Tourist attraction]] &ndash; place of interest where tourists visit, typically for its inherent or exhibited natural or cultural value, historical significance, natural or built beauty, offering leisure, adventure and amusement.

== Types of cultures ==
* [[Organizational culture]] &ndash; behaviour of humans within an organization and the meaning that people attach to those behaviours. An organization's culture includes its vision, values, norms, systems, countries, symbols, language, assumptions, beliefs, and habits.

=== Cultures by aspect ===

* [[Consumer culture]] &ndash; a society based on consumerism
* [[High context culture]] &ndash; a culture with the tendency use high context messages, resulting in catering towards in-groups
* [[Low context culture]] &ndash; culture with a tendency not to cater towards in-groups
* [[Remix culture]] &ndash; a society which allows and encourages [[derivative work]]s
* [[Participatory culture]] &ndash; a culture in which private persons (the public) do not act as consumers only, but also as contributors or producers ([[prosumers]])
* [[Permission culture]] &ndash; a society in which copyright restrictions are pervasive and enforced to the extent that any uses of copyrighted works need to be explicitly leased
* [[Traditional culture]] &ndash; a community that chooses to remain focused on subsistence as a major cornerstone of their economic behaviour, as well as, adheres to their ancestral belief-systems and mannerism.<ref>https://www.britannica.com/topic/primitive-culture/Introduction</ref>

** [[Cultural anthropology]] &ndash; branch of anthropology focused on the study of [[cultural variation]].
* [[Outline of archaeology|Archaeology]] – history studies in the physical aspects or [[Artifact (archaeology)|artefact]]s of cultures.
** [[culture-historical archaeology]]

** [[Sociobiology]]
** [[Social neuroscience]]
** [[Cultural neuroscience]]
* [[Cultural history]] &ndash; an academic discipline that combines the approaches of anthropology and history to look at popular cultural traditions and cultural interpretations of historical experience. It examines the records and narrative descriptions of past knowledge, customs, and arts of a group of people.
* [[Cultural studies]] &ndash; an academic discipline that studies the forces from which the whole of humankind construct their daily lives. It seeks to understand how meaning is generated and disseminated through practices, beliefs, and political, economic, or social structures within a given culture.
** [[Ethnic studies]]

* [[Culture theory]] &ndash; seeks to define the heuristic concept of culture in operational and/or scientific terms.
* [[Human geography]] &ndash; social science that studies the world, its people, communities, and cultures with an emphasis on relations of and across space and place.
