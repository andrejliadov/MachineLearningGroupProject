{{short description|Outdoor kitchen in the Philippines, Kuwait, Bahrain}}
A '''dirty kitchen''' is an outdoor kitchen in the [[Philippines]], [[Kuwait]], [[Bahrain]] and many other [[West Asian]] countries either separate from or adjoining the main [[house]], the reasons for which isolation or separation includes fire safety, keeping the smoke and fuel smell out, and keeping charcoal dust and oil grime out.<ref>{{cite web|last1=McMurrain|first1=Gary|title=Paradise Kitchen Is A Dirty Kitchen|url=http://retiringtothephilippines.com/guide/about-the-philippines/paradise-kitchen-dirty-kitchen/|website=Retiring to the Philippines|accessdate=May 15, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=The Kano|title=Dirty Kitchens in the Philippines|url=http://www.philippinesplus.com/2013/12/21/dirty-kitchens-philippines/|website=Philippines Plus|accessdate=May 15, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Graywolf|title=The Dirty Kitchen|url=http://www.livinginthephilippines.com/forum/index.php?topic=45568.0|website=Living in the Philippines|accessdate=May 15, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=de la Vega|first1=Jenn|title=Dirty Kitchens|url=https://medium.com/@jdelavegs/dirty-kitchens-c85ea235e4cc#.mwlkc2qza|website=Medium.com|accessdate=May 15, 2016}}</ref>

[[Rural area|Rural]] versions of the dirty kitchen have [[firewood]]-run stoves on kitchen tables literally made of dirt.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Annika|title=Dirty Kitchen in the Philippines|url=http://wanderlusting.me/lifeinthephilippines/dirty-kitchen-in-the-philippines/|website=Wanderlusting.Me.|accessdate=May 15, 2016}}</ref>
