A '''factory tour''' is an organized visit to a [[factory]] to observe the products being [[Manufacturing|manufactured]] and the processes at work. Manufacturing companies offer factory tours to improve [[public relations]].

[[Breweries]] and [[distilleries]], together with manufacturers of clothes, pottery and glass, are amongst the most popular factory visits. The popularity of factory tours has declined,{{When|date=January 2020}} as factories no longer represent the cutting edge of technology.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Grabar|first1=Henry|title=Why Factories Arenâ€™t Tourist Attractions Anymore|url=https://slate.com/culture/2018/04/behemoth-by-joshua-freeman-reviewed.html|accessdate=3 April 2018|work=[[Slate (magazine)|Slate]]|date=3 April 2018}}</ref>

Some countries have set up industrial-related museums where tours to see their processing is part of the museum attractions, such as the [[BRAND'S Health Museum]], [[Taiwan Metal Creation Museum]], [[Taiwan Mochi Museum]] and [[Taiyen Tongxiao Tourism Factory]] in [[Taiwan]].
