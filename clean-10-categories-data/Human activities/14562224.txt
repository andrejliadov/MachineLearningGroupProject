'''Goofing off''' is a slang term for engaging in recreation or an idle pastime while obligations of work or society are neglected.  Common obligations neglected in the course of goofing off include schoolwork, paid employment, social courtesies and the expectations of relations. Goofing off at [[school]] is considered to be a regular [[behaviour]] in the Western world, which is engaged in by all students at one time or another.<ref>{{citation|title=The Nature of "Goofing Off" among Junior High School Adolescents|author=Robert Everhart|journal=Adolescence|volume=17|issue=65|pages=177–88|date=Spring 1982|url=http://eric.ed.gov/ERICWebPortal/custom/portlets/recordDetails/detailmini.jsp?_nfpb=true&_&ERICExtSearch_SearchValue_0=EJ262892&ERICExtSearch_SearchType_0=no&accno=EJ262892}}</ref> 

When goofing off occurs within the classroom, teachers can resolve the matter quickly by direct confrontation.<ref>{{citation|url=https://www-s.ed.uiuc.edu/ed-online/cter/courses/edpsy399ol/course_docs/lessn_suppl/sample-paper/Classroom_Management.pdf |format=PDF |title=Classroom Management and Conflict Resolution: From "Goofing Off" to Violence in the Classroom |author=John P. Fields }}{{dead link|date=March 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>  [[Employer]]s may use [[wage]] [[wikt:premium|premium]]s to discourage goofing off by their [[employee]]s, although it is suggested that the effects of such [[incentive]]s causes [[aging]] to have a negative effect upon [[earnings]] sooner than would be otherwise expected.<ref>{{citation|title=Goofing off, Aging and Earnings|author=Kenneth V. Greene|journal=Southern Economic Journal|issue=2|date=October 1979|pages=623–627|publisher=Southern Economic Association|volume=46|doi=10.2307/1057434|jstor=1057434}}</ref> Goofing off has been shown to improve work or study in the right environments, and can relieve stress.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/224678|title=3 Ways Goofing Off at Work Can Make Your Company More Innovative|first=Lisa|last=Evans|date=October 16, 2012|website=Entrepreneur}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2013/03/29/living/play-at-work-irpt/index.html|title=Goofing off on company time? Go for it|first= Rachel |last=Rodriguez|website=CNN}}</ref> It may be a form of creativity and experimentation, providing useful learning experiences and discoveries.<ref>{{citation|title=The Gift of ADHD|isbn=1-57224-389-9|author=Lara Honos-Webb|pages=105|year=2005|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xIVyVMZJEs0C|publisher=New Harbinger Publications|location=Oakland, CA}}</ref>

Some research has indicated that women tend to feel more [[guilt (emotion)|guilt]] than men about taking time for themselves and so use breaks to become more organized.<ref>{{citation|title="Take Five:" Simplify |author=Cynthia R. Shuster |url=http://www.extension.iastate.edu/NR/rdonlyres/9301EFAC-B1E2-4F53-9D75-40720E0124FB/93491/TakeFiveSimplify.pdf |format=PDF |publisher=Ohio State University |year=2001 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100510130926/https://www.extension.iastate.edu/NR/rdonlyres/9301EFAC-B1E2-4F53-9D75-40720E0124FB/93491/TakeFiveSimplify.pdf |archivedate=2010-05-10 }}</ref>

Perhaps the most famous example of goofing off is the tale reported by [[Suetonius]] of [[Nero]] fiddling while [[Rome]] burned.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Roman/Texts/Suetonius/12Caesars/Nero*.html#38|title=Suetonius • Life of Nero|website=penelope.uchicago.edu}}</ref><ref>{{citation|title=Nero Fiddled while Rome Burned|author=Mary Francis Gyles|date=Jan 1947|url=http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Journals/CJ/42/4/Nero_Fiddled*.html|journal=The Classical Journal|pages=211–217|volume=42|issue=4}}</ref>
