'''Brian Sydney Barritt''' (1934–2011) was an English author, artist, and [[counterculture]] figure. He served in the British army, and was a friend and collaborator to such notables as [[Timothy Leary]], [[William Burroughs]], and [[Alex Trocchi]]. He was particularly active in the [[Beat Generation|Beatnik]], [[psychedelia|psychedelic]], and [[Krautrock]] scenes.

== Books ==
Barritt was the author of several books, including ''Whisper'',<ref>Barritt, Brian (1971). ''Whisper''. Whisper Productions. {{asin|B0018HF31A}}.</ref> ''The Road of Excess: A Psychedelic Autobiography''<ref>{{cite book| last = Barritt | first = Brian| title =The Road of Excess: A Psychedelic Autobiography| publisher = PSI Publishing| date = 1998| isbn = 0953274101}}</ref> and ''The Nabob of Bombasta''.<ref>{{cite book| last = Barritt | first = Brian| title =The Nabob of Bombasta| publisher = The Big Hand Books| date = 2010| isbn = 0956416306}}</ref>
He also collaborated with Timothy Leary on his book, ''Confessions of a Hope Fiend''.<ref>{{cite book| last = Higgs | first = John| title =I Have America Surrounded| publisher = Friday Books| date = 2006|pages = 169–170| isbn = 1905548257}} '...in the opinion of the counterculture journalist John Bryon, "Barritt helped make ''Hope Fiend'' the best stylistic job Tim had yet produced."'</ref>

==Relationship to Timothy Leary==
Barritt first made contact with Leary when Leary was on the run after his escape from prison.<ref>{{cite book| last = Leary | first = Timothy| title =Confessions of a Hope Fiend| url = https://archive.org/details/confessionsofhop00learrich | publisher = Bantam|page = [https://archive.org/details/confessionsofhop00learrich/page/195 195]|date = 1973| isbn = 0552680702 }}</ref> Barritt wrote to Leary in Algeria, asking him to write a foreword to the then unpublished, ''Whisper''. Leary had seen parts of the book before which had been handed to him whilst he was a prisoner at San Luis Obispo, USA. By the time Leary received his letter, Barritt was already in Algiers waiting to see him.<ref>{{cite book| last = Higgs | first = John| title =I Have America Surrounded| publisher = Friday Books|page = 141|date = 2006| isbn = 1905548257}}</ref>
Their relationship deteriorated by the early 1980s with Leary describing Barritt as a 'lying junky' in his autobiography, Flashbacks.<ref>{{cite book| last = Leary | first = Timothy| title =Flashbacks| publisher = Heinemann|page = 322|date = 1983| isbn = 0434409758}}</ref>

==Collaborations with Musicians==
Barritt collaborated with [[Martin Glover]] from [[Killing Joke]] on his novel, ''The Nabob of Bombasta''. He was later introduced to the rave scene by [[The Orb]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/obituaries/brian-barritt-counter-culture-writer-who-collaborated-with-timothy-leary-2215925.html|title=Brian Barritt: Counter-culture writer who collaborated with Timothy Leary|author=John Higgs|work=The Independent}}</ref>
