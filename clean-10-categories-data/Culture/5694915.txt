The '''Institute For Figuring''' ('''IFF''') is an organization based in [[Los Angeles, California]] that promotes the public understanding of the [[poetry|poetic]] and [[aesthetics|aesthetic]] dimensions of [[science]], [[mathematics]] and the technical arts.<ref>{{citation|title=Many Hands Make Fractals Tactile|newspaper=[[New York Times]]|first=Margaret|last=Wertheim|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/01/22/science/usc-exhibit-shows-fractals-built-from-paper.html|date=January 21, 2013}}.</ref> Founded by [[Margaret Wertheim]] and Christine Wertheim, the Institute hosts public [[lecture]]s and [[Art exhibition|exhibition]]s, publishes books and maintains a [[website]].<ref>{{citation|title=Review: 'Physics on the Fringe' bridges science, art worlds|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=June 7, 2012|first=Holly|last=Myers|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2012/jun/07/entertainment/la-et-cm-art-review-physics-fringe-institute-for-figuring-20120605}}.</ref><ref>{{citation|url=http://www.laweekly.com/arts/institute-for-figurings-new-space-an-art-gallery-for-math-and-science-nerds-2373115|newspaper=[[LA Weekly]]|first=Carol|last=Cheh|date=May 11, 2012|title=Institute for Figuring's new space: An art gallery for math and science nerds}}.</ref>
