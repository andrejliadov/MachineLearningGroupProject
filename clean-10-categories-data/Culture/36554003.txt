'''Black Astrum''' is an English [[business card]] manufacturer based in [[London]] and the Royal County of [[Berkshire]]. The company specialises in made to order diamond encrusted business and members cards, supplying them across Europe, the Middle East and Asia.<ref name="luxurylaunches"> kamakshi. [http://www.luxurylaunches.com/jewelry/worlds_most_expensive_business_card_costs_1500_a_pop.php "World's most expensive business card is diamond studded and costs $1500 a pop"], ''[[Luxury Launches]]'',  9 January 2012. Retrieved on 26 July 2012</ref> The company offers its signature cards on a strict invitation-only basis with each card custom designed according to customers' specifications.<ref name="news.yahoo">Mareilla Tecca [https://news.yahoo.com/blogs/technology-blog/serious-impression-exclusive-diamond-encrusted-1-500-business-023051573.html "Make a serious impression with these exclusive diamond-encrusted $1,500 business cards"], ''[[Yahoo]]'', 16 January 2012. Retrieved on 26 July 2012</ref> The product has been included in ''[[Tatler (1901)|Tatler]]'' magazine's 'Object of Desire' and Millionaire Asia's 'Must have item for men'.{{cn|date=November 2012}}

== The Signature card ==
Black Astrum's signature cards are made out of Swiss Hesa-Glas <ref name="cardonizer">[http://www.cardonizer.com/business_cards/black_astrum "Business Card for: Black Astrum"], ''[[Cardonizer]]''. Retrieved on 26 July 2012</ref> with scratch and chemical resilient coating.<ref name="news.yahoo" /> Each card is rumored to cost around $1,500,<ref name="huffingtonpost">[http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/01/11/black-astrium-worlds-most-exepensive-business-card_n_1199306.html "Black Astrum Premieres World's Most Expensive Business Cards Featuring Swiss Metal, Diamonds "], ''[[Huffpost Business]]'', 1 January 2012. Retrieved on 26 July 2012 </ref> with pricing dependent on the number of diamonds encrusted on each card.

== History ==
Black Astrum originally started as a result of a one-off request from a wealthy [[Middle Eastern]] family.<ref name="youtube">[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rr6oP0JrCr8 "CNN Weekend Shows : Diamond-studded business cards" 0.37 seconds], [[CNN]], 14 January 2012. Retrieved on 26 July 2012</ref> The founder of the company Sufian Khawaja, pleased with the final design, decided to launch Black Astrum officially in 2011 and sell the cards to a select, wealthy few.<ref name="foxbusiness">Lauren Covello [http://www.foxbusiness.com/personal-finance/2012/01/18/would-spend-1500-on-business-card/ "Would You Spend $1,500 on a Business Card?"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120122083358/http://www.foxbusiness.com/personal-finance/2012/01/18/would-spend-1500-on-business-card/ |date=22 January 2012 }}, ''[[foxbusiness]]'', 19 January 2012. Retrieved on 29 July 2012</ref> Khawaja himself has a background in design and engineering and resides in the county of Berkshire, England.<ref name="foxbusiness" />
