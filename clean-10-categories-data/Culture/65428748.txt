A '''baati''' or sheed is a cotton [[house dress]] from [[Somalia]] with a loose fit and short sleeves.<ref>{{cite web |title=Culture of Somalia |url=https://fortuneofafrica.com/somalia/culture-of-somalia/ |website=Fortune of Africa}}</ref> The garment is noted for its versatility; it can be used as swimwear or as a sling to carry an infant.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Farah |first1=Safy-Hallan |title=The Baati Dress Proves That Modest Fashion Can Be Liberating |url=https://www.vogue.com/article/halima-aden-baati-modest-fashion |website=Vogue}}</ref>

In 2018, [[Miss Universe]] contestant Muna Juma accused [[fast fashion]] brand [[Zara (retailer)|Zara]] of [[cultural appropriation]] when they advertised a dress closely resembling a baati.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Matera |first1=Avery |title=Zara Accused of Culturally Appropriating a Somali Baati |url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/zara-cultural-appropriation-somali-baati |website=Teen Vogue |accessdate=26 September 2020}}</ref>
