'''Normative religion''' describes the social boundaries of [[religious identity]] at a macro-level, particularly for the named [[world religions]]. It has a broader definition than theological [[orthodoxy]], including all those commonly accepted within the religion as a [[social group]] of mutual identification, for example with connected [[Religious liberalism|liberal tendencies]]. Normative religion is often contrasted with that of [[new religious movements]], such that the [[Nation of Islam]] is considered outside of "normative Islam"<ref>{{Cite book|last=Djupe|first=Paul A.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=frt7RDOT1PUC&pg=PA219|title=Encyclopedia of American Religion and Politics|last2=Olson|first2=Laura R.|date=2014|publisher=Infobase Publishing|year=|isbn=978-1-4381-3020-0|location=|pages=|language=en}}</ref> and [[Messianic Judaism]] outside of "normative Judaism".<ref>{{Cite book|last=Greenspoon|first=Leonard Jay|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=DBBZBQAAQBAJ&pg=PA222|title=Who is a Jew?: Reflections on History, Religion, and Culture|date=2014|publisher=Purdue University Press|isbn=978-1-55753-692-1|language=en}}</ref>
