{{short description|Boar, sacrificed as a part of Yule celebrations in Germanic paganism}}
{{italic title}}
The '''{{lang|non|sonargǫltr}}''' or '''{{lang|non|sónargǫltr}}''' was the [[boar]] sacrificed as part of the celebration of [[Yule]] in [[Germanic paganism]], on whose bristles solemn vows were made, a tradition known as {{lang|non|[[heitstrenging]]}}.

{{lang|non|[[Hervarar saga ok Heiðreks]]}} refers to the tradition of swearing oaths on Yule Eve by laying hands on the bristles of the boar, who was then sacrificed in the {{lang|non|sonar-[[blót]]}}:
{{Verse translation |lang=non
|Ok skyldi þeim gelti blóta at sónarblóti. Jólaaptan skyldi leiða sónargöltinn í höll fyrir konúng; lögðu menn þá hendr yfir burst hans ok strengja heit.<ref>{{lang|non|Saga Heiðreks konungs ins vitra}}, H-text of {{lang|non|[[Hervarar saga ok Heiðreks]]}}, chapter 10, from [http://www.heimskringla.no/wiki/Saga_Hei%C3%B0reks_konungs_ins_vitra Heimskringla.no]. Note that this text uses the {{lang|non|sónar}} spelling.</ref><ref>For the alternate version, in which the procedure is the same but the word {{lang|non|sonargǫltr}} does not occur, see Richard North, ''Heathen Gods in Old English Literature'', Cambridge Studies in Anglo-Saxon England 22, Cambridge, 1997, {{ISBN|978-0-521-55183-0}}, [https://books.google.com/books?id=X_LKUIqNvPQC&pg=PP1&dq=Heathen+Gods+in+Old+English+Literature&lr=&ei=g2DTSqn6CIiWMPrOkIsO#v=onepage&q=sacred%20pig%2C%20gold%2C%20winter%20festival&f=false p. 74].</ref>
|And they would sacrifice a boar in the {{lang|non|sonarblót}}. On Yule Eve the sonar-boar was led into the hall before the king; then people laid their hands on its bristles and made vows.
}}

One of the prose segments in "{{lang|non|[[Helgakviða Hjörvarðssonar]]}}" adds that the oaths were sworn while drinking the {{lang|non|[[symbel#Scandinavian sumbel|bragarfull]]}} toast:
{{Verse translation |lang=non
|Um kveldit [jólaaftan] óru heitstrengingar. Var fram leiddr sónargöltr. Lögðu menn þar á hendr sínar ok strengðu menn þá heit at bragarfulli.
|That evening [of Yule Eve] the great vows were taken; the sacred boar was brought in, the men laid their hands thereon, and took their vows at the king's toast.<ref>"{{lang|non|[[Helgakviða Hjörvarðssonar]]}}", prose before verse 31, Old Norse and [[Henry Adams Bellows (businessman)|Henry Adams Bellows]]' translation from [http://www.voluspa.org/helgakvidahjorvardssonar31-35.htm voluspa.org]. Again the {{lang|non|sónar}} spelling is used.</ref>
}}

In {{lang|non|[[Ynglinga saga]]}} the {{lang|non|sonarblót}} is used for [[divination]] ({{lang|non|til frettar}}).<ref name="Simek">"{{lang|non|Sonargǫltr}}", [[Rudolf Simek]], ''Dictionary of Northern Mythology'', tr. Angela Hall, Cambridge: Brewer, 1993, repr. 2000, {{ISBN|978-0-85991-369-0}}, p. 298.</ref><ref name="deVries367">[[Jan de Vries (linguist)|Jan de Vries]], ''[[Altgermanische Religionsgeschichte]]'', Volume 1, Grundriß der germanischen Philologie begründet von Hermann Paul 12/I, 2nd ed. Berlin: [[De Gruyter]], 1956, repr. as 3rd ed. 1970, {{OCLC|747429}}, p. 367 {{in lang|de}}.</ref>

The association with the Yule {{lang|non|blót}} and with the ceremonial {{lang|non|bragarfull}} gives the vows great solemnity, so that they have the force of [[oath]]s. This becomes a recurring topos in later sagas,<ref>de Vries, p. 504.</ref> although we have only these two saga mentions attesting to the custom of making vows on the sacrificial animal.<ref>Peter Habbe, ''Att se och tänka med ritual: kontrakterande ritualer i de isländska släktsagorna'', Vägar till Midgård 7, Lund: Nordic Academic Press, 2005, {{ISBN|9789189116795}}, [https://books.google.com/books?id=DXgyEBtb51cC&pg=RA1-PA43&dq=offergalten&hl=en&ei=KziITtqhEYvDswbXwZzhAQ&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=3&ved=0CDcQ6AEwAg#v=onepage&q=offergalten&f=false p. 43] {{in lang|sv}}.</ref>

Geburtstag}}, ed. It was formerly usual to spell the word {{lang|non|sónargǫltr}} and to interpret it as "atonement-boar" (the rare element {{lang|non|sónar-}} can also mean "sacrifice").<ref name="Grimm51"/><ref>"{{lang|is|Són}}", ''An Icelandic–English Dictionary'', initiated by Richard Cleasby, subsequently revised, enlarged, and completed by {{lang|is|[[Guðbrandur Vigfússon]]}}, 2nd ed. with supplement by [[William Craigie|William A. Craigie]], Oxford: Oxford/Clarendon, 1957, repr. 1975, {{ISBN|978-0-19-863103-3}}, p. 580, [http://lexicon.ff.cuni.cz/png/oi_cleasbyvigfusson/b0580.png online at Germanic Lexicon Project].</ref> However, following [[Eduard Sievers]], it is usually now spelled with a short {{lang|non|o}} and taken as meaning "herd boar, leading boar", as [[Lombardic language|Lombardic]] {{lang|lng|sonarþair}} is defined in the {{lang|la|[[Edictum Rothari|Edictus Rothari]]}} as the boar "which fights and beats all other boars in the herd".<ref name="Simek"/><ref name="deVries367"/><ref name="Rosen214"/>
