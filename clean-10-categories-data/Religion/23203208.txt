'''Jeffrey William Driver''' (born 6 October 1951<ref>[[Who's Who]] 2008: London, [[A & C Black]], 2008 {{ISBN|978-0-7136-8555-8}}</ref>) is an Australian Anglican bishop. He is the former [[Anglican Diocese of Adelaide|Archbishop of Adelaide]] and [[Metropolitan (religion)|Metropolitan]] of [[South Australia]] in the [[Anglican Church of Australia]].<ref>[http://www.anglicancommunion.org/tour/diocese.cfm?Idind=17 Anglican Communion]</ref>

== Early life ==
Driver grew up in the New South Wales country town of [[Cowra, New South Wales|Cowra]]. His theological education was undertaken through the [[Australian College of Theology]]. After a short career as a newspaper journalist he was [[ordained]] in 1977 and began his career as an assistant [[curate]] in [[Bathurst, New South Wales|Bathurst]]. He held [[Vicar|incumbencies]] at Mid-Richmond and Jamison and was later [[Archdeacon]] of [[Young, New South Wales]] and also Rector of the parish of [[St Paul's Church, Manuka|St Paul's Manuka]] in Canberra. Driver was Executive Director of [[St Mark's National Theological Centre]] in Canberra for a time, and founding Head of [[Charles Sturt University|Charles Sturt University's]] School of Theology. In 2001, Driver was consecrated and appointed [[Bishop]] of [[Gippsland]] in eastern Victoria,<ref>[http://www.gippsanglican.org.au/history.html Diocesan history] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090913110406/http://www.gippsanglican.org.au/history.html |date=13 September 2009 }}</ref> a position he held until his [[Translation (ecclesiastical)|translation]] to [[Adelaide]] in 2005.<ref>[http://morgue.anglicansonline.org/050522/new_this_week.html Anglicanism on-line]</ref>

Driver holds a PhD from [[Charles Sturt University]], MTh. from the [[Sydney College of Divinity]], as well as Scholar and Licentiate in Theology from the [[Australian College of Theology]].<ref name="SACC2010" />

Driver continues to lecture and write on biblical studies and Anglican ecclesiology, and has been closely involved with the work of the Anglican Church's caring body, [[Anglicare]], in a number of dioceses.<ref name="adel_Arch" /> He attended the [[Global South (Anglican)|Global South]] Fourth Encounter that took place in [[Singapore]], on 19–23 April 2010.<ref>[http://www.virtueonline.org/singapore-global-south-observers-reflect-worldwide-anglicanism SINGAPORE: Global South Observers Reflect on Worldwide Anglicanism, Virtue Online, 25 April 2010]</ref>

On 10 April 2016, Driver announced that he would retire in August 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.adelaidenow.com.au/news/south-australia/anglican-archbishop-of-adelaide-jeffrey-driver-announces-his-retirement/news-story/f0179a17a3c62ecbf31f57e4a01b9aed|title=Anglican Archbishop of Adelaide Jeffrey Driver announces his retirement|date=10 April 2016|accessdate=10 April 2016|work=The Advertiser|first=Nigel|last=Hunt}}</ref> He did, and it was announced in December 2016 that his successor would be [[Geoffrey Smith (bishop)|Geoffrey Smith]], the Assistant Bishop, General Manager and Registrar of the [[Anglican Diocese of Brisbane|Diocese of Brisbane]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.news.com.au/national/breaking-news/adelaide-gets-new-anglican-archbishop/news-story/0706c426a323741d2be7a119ba493a6a |accessdate=12 March 2017 |title=Adelaide gets new Anglican archbishop |date=3 December 2016 |work=News.com.au}}</ref>
