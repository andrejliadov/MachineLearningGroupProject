The '''Commission for the Promotion and Protection of the Rights of Cultural, Religious and Linguistic Communities''' ('''CRL Rights Commission''') is an independent [[chapter nine institution]] in [[South Africa]]. It draws its mandate from the [[Constitution of South Africa|South African Constitution]] by way of the Commission for the Promotion and Protection of the Rights of Cultural, Religious and Linguistic Communities Act of 2002.<ref>{{cite web|title=CRL Act|url=http://www.crlcommission.org.za/crlact.php|publisher=crlcommission.org.za|access-date=10 November 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130131225550/http://www.crlcommission.org.za/crlact.php|archive-date=31 January 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Mandate==
The CRL Rights Commission is mandated "to promote respect for and further the protection of the rights of cultural, religious and linguistic communities; promote and develop peace, friendship, humanity, tolerance, national unity among and within cultural, religious and linguistic communities on the basis of equality, non-discrimination and free association; to promote the right of communities to develop their historically diminished heritage and to recognise community councils".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.info.gov.za/speech/DynamicAction?pageid=461&sid=30600&tid=83341 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20121225033105/http://www.info.gov.za/speech/DynamicAction?pageid=461&sid=30600&tid=83341 |url-status=dead |archive-date=25 December 2012 |title=Preparatory engagement on the status of Cultural, Religious and Linguistic Rights in South Africa will be held in KwaZulu-Natal province |publisher=info.gov.za |date=12 September 2012 |accessdate=10 November 2012 }}</ref>

==Vision and mission==
The vision of the CRL Rights Commission is "a united South African nation that protects and promotes the cultural, religious and linguistic rights of all its diverse communities". Its mission  is to "promote and protect the rights of cultural, religious and linguistic communities".<ref>{{cite web|title=Vision & Mission|url=http://www.crlcommission.org.za/visionmission.php|publisher=crlcommission.org.za|access-date=10 November 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130131225248/http://www.crlcommission.org.za/visionmission.php|archive-date=31 January 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Commissioners==
On 2 April 2014, 12 new commissioners of the CRL Commission were inaugurated at the Constitutional Court. This was subsequent to their appointment by President Jacob Zuma in terms of section 11(4) read with 13(1) of the CRL Rights Commission Act 19 of 2002 with effect from 1 March 2014, for a period of five years. The inauguration ceremony was presided over by Justice Edwin Cameron. The 12 new members of the CRL Rights Commission are:

Ms Thoko Nonhle Mkhwanazi-Xaluva (Chairperson)

Prof Luka David Mosoma (Deputy Chairperson)

Dr Anton Wynard Knoetze

Mr Renier Stephanus Schoeman

Mr Sicelo Emmanuel Dlamini

Mr Raymond Trollip

Mr Richard Daryll Botha

Ms Violet Nomalanga Tyamzashe

Ms Bernedette Muthien

Ms Pumla Primrose Madiba

Ms Helen Julia Mabale

Ms Sheila Fihliwe Khama.<ref>{{cite web |title=Commissioners |url=http://www.crlcommission.org.za/commissioners.php |publisher=crlcommission.org.za |access-date=5 May 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140418093117/http://www.crlcommission.org.za/commissioners.php |archive-date=18 April 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
