The '''King Faisal Prize''' ({{lang-ar|جائزة الملك فيصل}}, formerly '''King Faisal International Prize''') is an annual award sponsored by [[King Faisal Foundation]] presented to "dedicated men and women whose contributions make a positive difference".<ref name="Homepage KFP">{{cite web|title=Homepage KFP|url=http://www.kff.com/EN01/KFIP/KFIPIndex.html}}</ref><ref name="Selection Procedure">{{cite web|title=Selection Procedure|url=http://www.kff.com/EN01/KFIP/KFIPSelectionProc.html}}</ref>  The foundation awards prizes in five categories: Service to [[Islam]]; [[Islamic studies]]; the [[Arabic]] language and [[Arabic literature]]; [[science]]; and [[medicine]]. Three of the prizes are widely considered as the most prestigious awards in the Muslim world.

The first King Faisal Prize was awarded to the Pakistani scholar [[Abul A'la Maududi]] in the year 1979 for his service to Islam. In 1981, [[Khalid of Saudi Arabia]] received the same award.<ref name=kkhalid>{{cite web|title=King Khalid |url=http://www.kka.org.sa/Ar/Pages/KingKhaled.aspx |publisher=King Khalid Award |accessdate=8 August 2012 |language=Arabic |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120124152140/http://www.kka.org.sa/Ar/Pages/KingKhaled.aspx |archivedate=24 January 2012 }}</ref>  In 1984, [[Fahd of Saudi Arabia]] was the recipient of the award.<ref name="Eds.1986">{{cite book|title=Middle East Contemporary Survey, Vol. 8, 1983-84|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=t32OO3DkDikC&pg=PA166|publisher=The Moshe Dayan Center|isbn=978-965-224-006-4|page=166}}</ref> In 1986, this prize was co-awarded to [[Ahmed Deedat]] and French [[Roger Garaudy]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://kfip.org/service-to-islam/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2015-01-07 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150106191329/http://kfip.org/service-to-islam/ |archivedate=2015-01-06 }}

==Award process==

===Designation of subjects===

Each year, the selection committees designate subjects in Islamic Studies, Arabic Literature, and Medicine. Selected topics in Islamic Studies category are aimed at highlighting areas of importance in [[Muslim]] societies. Arabic Literature  topics relate to specialized areas within the [[List of academic disciplines|discipline]]. Topics in Medicine are supposed to reflect current areas of international concern. The Science category covers a broad range of subcategories e.g. [[physics]], [[mathematics]], [[chemistry]] and [[biology]].<ref name="Selection Procedure" />

===Nomination ===

[[List of Muslim educational institutions|Islamic institutions]], [[University|universities]] and previous winners of the King Faisal Prize can nominate a person for the award. Nominations from ordinary individuals or political parties are not accepted.<ref name="Nomination: Service To Islam">{{cite web|title=Nomination: Service To Islam|url=http://www.kff.com/EN01/KFIP/KFIPTopics1STI1435H2014G.html}}</ref> The nominee or nominated institution(s) must be known for their leading practical or intellectual role in the service of [[Islam]] and [[Muslim]]s.<ref name="Nominations: Medicine">{{cite web|title=Nominations: Medicine|url=http://www.kff.com/EN01/KFIP/KFIPTopics4MED1435H2014G.html}}</ref> Nominated works must be [[Publication|published]], benefit mankind and enrich [[Knowledge|human knowledge]].<ref name="Nominations: Medicine" />

===Selection===

Winners of the Prize for category "Service to Islam" are chosen directly by the respective selection committee. For other Prize categories, pre-selection by [[peer review]]ers is carried out, which is followed by scrutiny of the works of worthy nominees by selected referees of each discipline. [[Autonomy|Autonomous]], international, specialist selection committees are then convened at the [[headquarters]] of [[King Faisal Foundation]] in [[Riyadh]] each January to make their final decisions.<ref name="Selection Procedure" />

==Prizes==

The prize in each of the five categories consists of:

# A hand written [[Diwani]] [[Islamic calligraphy|calligraphy]] certificate, summarizing the [[Laureate|laureate's]] work.
# A commemorative 24 [[Carat (mass)|carat]], 200 [[gram]] [[gold medal]], uniquely cast for each prize.
# A cash [[Financial endowment|endowment]] of [[Saudi riyal|SR]] 750,000 ([[United States dollar|US$]] 200,000).

Co-winners in any category share the monetary [[Grant (money)|grant]]. The Prizes are awarded during a ceremony in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, under the auspices of the [[King of Saudi Arabia]].<ref name="Selection Procedure" />
