'''Irreligion in Peru''' refers to [[atheism]], [[deism]], [[religious skepticism]], [[secularism]], and [[humanism]] in [[Peru]]vian society. According to article 2 of the [[Constitution of Peru|Peruvian Constitution]]: ''"No person shall be persecuted on the basis of his ideas or beliefs''".<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Political Constitution of Peru|url=http://www.congreso.gob.pe/Docs/files/CONSTITUTION_27_11_2012_ENG.pdf|last=|first=|date=September 2009|website=Portal Institucional e Información sobre la Actividad Parlamentaria y Legislativa del Estado Peruano|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref> According to the [[2017 Peru Census|2017 Peruvian Census]] data, 1 180 361 Peruvians or 5.1% of the population older than 12 years old describes themselves as being irreligious,<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Perú: Perfil Sociodemográfico - Informe Nacional|url=https://www.inei.gob.pe/media/MenuRecursivo/publicaciones_digitales/Est/Lib1539/libro.pdf|last=|first=|date=August 2018|website=Instituto Nacional de Estadística e Informática|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> but some sources put this number higher at 8.2%.<ref>{{Cite web|title=La visita del Papa Francisco a nuestro país|url=http://cpi.pe/images/upload/paginaweb/archivo/23/opnac_visita_papa_francisco_201802.pdf?fbclid=IwAR2wVp3ZNK0fAtU6MxBT0T5Mjth_6do06mOdwgFCbQGBx9-W93Ecvqf54cA|last=|first=|date=February 2018|website=Compañía Peruana de Estudios de Mercados y Opinión Pública|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref>

The irreligious population is predominantly urban (85,5% live in cities) and males (61,4% are male), and most are young people within the ages between 18 and 29 (40,4%). Only 11,8% of irreligious people are 50 years old or older.<ref name=":1" />

Even though Peru is considered to be a Secular state, according to article 50 of the Peruvian Constitution<ref name=":0" /> the subject of [[Catholic Church|Roman Catholicism]] is mandatory in Peruvian public schools.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Perú: niegan retiro de clases de religión en los colegios públicos|url=https://peru.com/actualidad/mi-ciudad/peru-niegan-retiro-clases-religion-colegios-publicos-noticia-497608|last=|first=|date=February 2017|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref> Many [[Nonprofit organization|non-profit organizations]] promote the removal of the subject of Roman Catholicism in public schools, such as the Secular Humanist Society of Peru, or the Association of Peruvian Atheists.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Más de un millón de peruanos no profesan ninguna religión|url=https://elcomercio.pe/peru/millon-peruanos-sigue-religion-noticia-571715-noticia/|last=Perú|first=Redacción El Comercio|date=October 27, 2018|website=El Comercio Perú|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref>
