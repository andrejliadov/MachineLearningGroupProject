The [[COVID-19 pandemic]] has significantly impacted liturgical celebrations of the [[Catholic Church]] worldwide. The Pontifical Foundation [[Aid to the Church in Need]] (ACN) stated that the pandemic has not become "just a medical, social and economic problem, but also a pastoral problem", which leaded ACN to start encouraging a special program for the actions of priests and religious against the virus spread.<ref name="ACN">{{cite web| URL= https://www.acn.org.br/ajuda-da-acn-durante-pandemia/| title=Ajuda da ACN durante pandemia| date=2020-06-02| publisher=[[Aid to the Church in Need]]| accessdate=2020-08-12}}</ref>

In March 2020 all public [[Mass in the Catholic Church|Masses]] were suspended in Vatican City and Italy due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Italy|coronavirus pandemic]]. These suspensions began in late-February in the Archdioceses of [[Archdiocese of Milan|Milan]] and [[Patriarchate of Venice|Venice]] and were extended to the rest of the Italian peninsula on 8 March.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mares |first1=Courtney |title=Diocese of Rome cancels all public Masses, announces day of fasting and prayer |url=https://www.catholicnewsagency.com/news/diocese-of-rome-cancels-all-public-masses-announces-day-of-fasting-and-prayer-54167 |accessdate=12 March 2020 |work=Catholic News Agency |date=8 March 2020}}</ref> Setting an example for churches unable to celebrate public Masses due to the lockdown, Pope Francis began livestreaming daily Mass from his residence at [[Domus Sanctae Marthae]] on 9 March.<ref>{{cite news |title=Pope Francis' morning Mass broadcast live every day |url=https://www.vaticannews.va/en/pope/news/2020-03/pope-francis-daily-mass-casa-santa-marta-coronavirus.html |accessdate=12 March 2020 |work=Vatican News |date=8 March 2020}}</ref> Outside Italy, Mass in cities around the world were suspended in the days that followed.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Nerozzi |first1=Timothy |title=Japanese dioceses suspend Masses amid coronavirus epidemic |url=https://www.catholicnewsagency.com/news/japanese-dioceses-suspend-masses-amid-coronavirus-epidemic-16651 |accessdate=13 March 2020 |work=Catholic News Agency |date=4 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Do we have Mass? Coronavirus closures and dispensations in US dioceses |url=https://www.catholicnewsagency.com/news/do-we-have-mass-coronavirus-closures-and-dispensations-in-us-dioceses-96801 |accessdate=16 March 2020 All over the world, many churches have suspended the presence of the faithful in their [[Mass in the Catholic Church|Masses]], and resorted to virtual services for broadcasting the celebrations, such as [[live streaming]] or other ones, like television and radio.<ref name="Brasil">{{cite web| URL= https://noticias.cancaonova.com/brasil/arquidioceses-e-dioceses-suspendem-atividades-por-conta-coronavirus/| title=Arquidioceses e dioceses suspendem atividades por conta do coronavírus| date=2020-03-18| publisher=[[Canção Nova]] Notícias| accessdate=2020-08-12}}</ref><ref name="Parke">{{cite web| last=Parke| first=Caleb| title=Churches cancel Sunday service, move online amid coronavirus outbreak| url=https://www.foxnews.com/us/coronavirus-update-church-sunday-service-online| publisher=[[Fox News]]| accessdate=2020-03-16| language=en |date=2020-03-13}}</ref> The Vatican announced that the [[Holy Week]] celebrations in [[Rome]], which take place at the end of [[Lent]] — Christian penitential period —, would be canceled. Some dioceses ordered their churches to be closed to the public, while in other dioceses, such as the [[Archdiocese of New York]], although they canceled the Masses, their churches remained open for prayer.<ref name="Burke">{{cite newspaper| last=Burke| first=Daniel| title=What churches, mosques and temples are doing to fight the spread of coronavirus| url=https://www.cnn.com/2020/03/14/world/churches-mosques-temples-coronavirus-spread/index.html| publisher=CNN| accessdate=2020-03-16| language=en| date=2020-03-14}}</ref> In Spain, many cities canceled their [[Holy Week]] festivities. This event is usually celebrated with parades and significant collections with tourism; in [[Seville]], it was the first time that events were canceled since [[1933]].<ref>{{cite newspaper| last=Saiz| first=Eva| title=Sevilla suspende su Semana Santa por el coronavirus| url= https://elpais.com/sociedad/2020-03-14/sevilla-suspende-su-semana-santa-por-el-coronavirus.html| accessdate=2020-04-01| work=El País |date=2020-03-14| language=es}}</ref> Due to the interruption of several Catholic religious activities (if not their totality), [[Pope Francis]] greatly encouraged the prayer of the [[Rosary|Holy Rosary]].<ref>{{cite web| URL=https://www.wellingtontimes.com.au/story/6689774/catholic-masses-suspended-bishop-encourages-private-prayer-in-difficult-days/?cs=1546| title=Coronavirus, COVID-19: Catholic church masses suspended in diocese| date=2020-03-20| work=Wellington Times| accessdate=2020-08-12}}</ref>
