'''Elizabeth Laura Adams''' (1909–1982) was an African-American Catholic writer, best known for her [[autobiography]] [[Dark Symphony (book)|''Dark Symphony'']] (1942).<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=iTWu0aSofkkC|title=Encyclopedia of African American Women Writers|last=Page|first=Yolanda Williams|date=2007-01-30|publisher=Greenwood Publishing Group|year=|isbn=9780313334290|location=|pages=1–6|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hQ2JlxYANrwC&pg=PA1&lpg=PA1|title=African American Authors, 1745-1945: Bio-bibliographical Critical Sourcebook|last=Nelson|first=Emmanuel Sampath|date=2000|publisher=Greenwood Publishing Group|year=|isbn=9780313309106|location=|pages=1–5|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Born in [[Santa Barbara, California]] on February 9, 1909 to Lula Josphine and Daniel Adams, Adams grew up in the [[Great Depression|Depression era]].<ref name=":0" /> After her father's death, Adams converted to [[Catholic Church|Catholicism]].<ref name=":0" />

== Reception ==
Reception of ''Dark Symphony'' was very good, with it being a [[bestseller]] among Catholics.<ref name=":0" />

==Works==
* Consecrated (1936)
* The Country Doctor (1942)
* The Art of Living Joyfully (1942)
* Dark Symphony (1942)
* Children under Fire (1943)
