In April 2020, the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] reached the French aircraft carrier {{ship|French aircraft carrier|Charles de Gaulle||2}} and the Aeronaval Group of the [[Naval Action Force]], its [[carrier battle group]].

== Background ==
''Charles de Gaulle'' was constructed for the [[French Navy]] in [[Brest, France|Brest]] from 1980 to 2001.<ref name="InsideOver">{{Cite news |last=Bartoccini |first=Davide |url=https://www.insideover.com/indepths/war/the-charles-de-gaulle-aircraft-carrier-frances-naval-power.html |title=The Charles de Gaulle aircraft carrier: France's naval power |date=7 February 2020 |work=InsideOver |access-date=24 April 2020 |url-status=live |others=Translated by Ruth Lebens}}</ref> The ship's completion was severely delayed by budget cuts and mechanical problems, which included a snapped propeller.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Whitney|first=Craig R. |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1997/08/08/world/brest-french-home-port-sees-future-of-mothballs.html |title=Brest, French Home Port, Sees Future of Mothballs|date=8 August 1997|work=The New York Times|access-date=24 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Fleming|first=Charles|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB1002834194102673920 |title=Troubled Aircraft Carrier Illustrates France's Poor Military Preparedness|date=12 October 2001 |work=The Wall Street Journal|access-date=24 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> A mid-sized carrier, ''Charles de Gaulle'' is smaller than American [[supercarrier]]s but larger than similar ships operated or retired by Russia, India, and Brazil.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Lyons|first=Patrick J.|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/10/world/what-in-the-world/whos-got-a-carrier-to-rival-the-us-navy-hint-not-russia.html |title=Who's Got a Carrier to Rival the U.S. Navy's? (Hint: Not Russia.) |date=9 August 2016|work=The New York Times|access-date=24 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

After ''Charles de Gaulle'' was commissioned, the French government quickly deployed it to support the [[War in Afghanistan (2001–present)|War in Afghanistan]]. The ship also saw active service in supporting NATO's [[2011 military intervention in Libya|military intervention in Libya]] (2011) and the [[International military intervention against ISIL]] (2015). In 2017, the aircraft carrier underwent a nearly two-year refit which modernized it for an intended additional twenty years of service.<ref name="InsideOver"/> In January 2020, the French government ordered ''Charles de Gaulle'' and its associated [[carrier battle group#France|carrier battle group]] to deploy to the Eastern Mediterranean for further operations against ISIL, after which it would transit through the [[Strait of Gibraltar]] and engage in multinational training exercises in the Atlantic Ocean and [[North Sea]].<ref name="Invisible Foe">{{Cite news|last=Breeden|first=Aurelien |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/04/19/world/europe/france-navy-ship-coronavirus.html |title=How an Invisible Foe Slipped Aboard a French Navy Ship|date=19 April 2020|work=The New York Times|access-date=24 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="leopold.naval">{{Cite news|last=Vavasseur|first=Xavier|url=https://www.navalnews.com/naval-news/2020/01/french-carrier-strike-group-begins-foch-deployment/ |title=French Carrier Strike Group Begins 'Foch' Deployment|date=22 January 2020|work=Naval News|access-date=24 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Causes and event ==
After several weeks on station in the eastern Mediterranean, ''Charles de Gaulle'' proceeded to the Atlantic and North Sea for naval exercises with a carrier battle group composed of the air-defense [[frigate]] {{ship|French frigate|Chevalier Paul||2}}, the anti-submarine frigate {{ship|French frigate|La Motte-Picquet||2}}, the command and replenishment ship {{ship|French tanker|Somme}}, and a nuclear-powered submarine.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.meretmarine.com/fr/content/le-charles-de-gaulle-son-escorte-et-le-groupe-aerien-sont-rentres-toulon-et-en-bretagne |title=Le Charles de Gaulle, son escorte et le groupe aérien sont rentrés à Toulon et en Bretagne |work=Mer et Marine |last=Groizeleau|first=Vincent|date=13 April 2020 |language=fr |accessdate=27 April 2020}}</ref> From 13 to 15 March, ''Charles de Gaulle'' was docked in Brest for a brief port visit. The enactment of a strict lockdown to combat the ongoing coronavirus pandemic came two days after the ship left Brest, and the sailors were allowed [[shore leave]] that included seeing family members and visiting local commercial establishments.<ref name="Invisible Foe"/><ref>{{Cite news|last=Willsher|first=Kim|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/apr/16/inquiry-french-aircraft-carrier-crew-catch-coronavirus-charles-de-gaulle |title=Inquiry after 668 of French aircraft carrier's crew catch coronavirus|date=16 April 2020|work=The Guardian|access-date=24 April 2020|url-status=live|last2=Sabbagh|first2=Dan}}</ref> ''Charles de Gaulle''{{'}}s crew did not have contact with the outside world for the next three weeks.<ref name="franceinfo 2020-04-11">{{Cite news |url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/sante/maladie/coronavirus/coronavirus-le-porte-avions-francais-charles-de-gaulle-et-ses-50-cas-de-covid-19-a-bord-est-attendu-a-toulon-dimanche-apres-midi_3910907.html |title=Coronavirus : le porte-avions français "Charles-de-Gaulle", et ses 50 cas de Covid-19 à bord, est attendu à Toulon dimanche après-midi |work=France Télévisions/France Info |language=fr|date=11 April 2020|access-date=24 April 2020}}</ref> Belgium's [[frigate]] {{ship|Belgian frigate|Leopold I|F930|2}}, part of the carrier battle group, was ordered to prepare to leave the force on 24 March after [[COVID-19 pandemic on naval ships#Leopold I|detecting a single coronavirus case]].<ref name="leopold.naval"/><ref name="leopold.bruxelles2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.bruxelles2.eu/2020/04/covid-19-did-belgium-make-the-right-choice-by-interrupting-the-foch-mission/?lang=en |title=Covid-19. Did Belgium make the right choice by interrupting the Foch mission|first=Nicolas|last=Gros-Verheyde |work=Bruxelles2 |date=10 April 2020}}</ref> The nature of warships, which includes working with others in small enclosed areas and a lack of private quarters for the vast majority of crew, lent themselves to the rapid spread of the disease to a degree even greater than that seen on [[COVID-19 pandemic on cruise ships|cruise ships]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Faturechi|first=Robert|url=https://www.propublica.org/article/after-discovering-a-sailor-with-coronavirus-the-us-navy-crowded-dozens-into-one-room |title=After Discovering a Sailor With Coronavirus, the U.S. Navy Crowded Dozens Into One Room|date=16 March 2020|work=ProPublica|access-date=24 April 2020|url-status=live|last2=Rose|first2=Megan|last3=Miller|first3=T. Christian}}</ref> Indeed, on ''Charles de Gaulle'' there were places where 20 people would sleep together on three or four floors of [[Berth (sleeping)|berths]].<ref name="franceinfo-04-18">{{Cite news|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/sante/maladie/coronavirus/marins-contamines-au-covid-19-sur-le-charles-de-gaulle-a-des-endroits-il-y-a-une-vingtaine-de-matelots-qui-dorment-sur-3-ou-4-etages-de-couchettes_3922411.html |title=Marins contaminés au Covid-19 sur le "Charles-de-Gaulle" : "À des endroits, il y a une vingtaine de matelots qui dorment sur 3 ou 4 étages de couchettes" |language=fr |access-date=18 April 2020 |website=Franceinfo}}</ref>

During this time, several [[common cold]]s were registered on board, but the level of sick sailors was not above the ordinary for the frigid −5&nbsp;°C temperature and strong winds of the region ''Charles de Gaulle'' was operating in.<ref name="Invisible Foe"/><ref name="figaro">{{Cite news|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/flash-actu/france-un-tiers-des-marins-du-porte-avions-charles-de-gaulle-positifs-au-coronavirus-20200415|title=Coronavirus: un tiers des marins du porte-avions Charles-de-Gaulle sont infectés|language=fr|access-date=18 April 2020|newspaper=[[Le Figaro]]}}</ref> Starting on 5 April, however, the number of crew members reporting to the infirmary began growing exponentially to alarming levels. Two sailors were medically scanned and found to be infected by the coronavirus, after which the French defense minister [[Florence Parly]] ordered the carrier to return to its home port of Toulon.<ref name="Invisible Foe"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.france24.com/fr/20200408-coronavirus-plusieurs-cas-suspects-à-bord-du-porte-avions-français-charles-de-gaulle |title=Coronavirus : plusieurs cas home early due to possible COVID-19 cases |work=Reuters |date=8 April 2020 |access-date=8 April 2020}}</ref> On 10 April, French news outlets reported that out of 66 tests conducted on board the York Times |agency=Reuters |date=10 April 2020 |access-date=11 April 2020}}</ref> There were 1,760 personnel on board.<ref name="reuters-heading"/>

The carrier arrived in Toulon on the afternoon of 12 April, where the personnel began a 14-day quarantine.<ref name="franceinfo 2020-04-11"/><ref name="europe1_between">{{Cite news |url=https://www.europe1.fr/societe/covid-19-a-bord-du-charles-de-gaulle-les-marins-qui-nont-pas-respecte-les-regles-doivent-etre-sanctionnes-3961696 |title=Les marins du Charles-de-Gaulle "qui n'ont pas respecté les règles doivent être sanctionnés" |access-date=18 April 2020 |work=Europe1.fr |language=fr}}</ref> The ship was to be decontaminated over the course of several weeks.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Hernandez|first=Nathalie|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/sante/maladie/coronavirus/vapeur-d-eau-a-haute-temperature-et-produit-antivirus-comment-va-se-passer-la-decontamination-du-porte-avions-charles-de-gaulle_3914589.html |title=Vapeur d'eau à haute température et produit antivirus : comment va se passer la décontamination du porte-avions Charles-de-Gaulle|date=14 April 2020|work=Radio France/France Info|language=fr|access-date=27 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

At first, the origin of the outbreak was unknown; there had been no contact with the outside world after the ship's stopover in Brest.<ref name="franceinfo 2020-04-11"/><ref name="europe1_between"/>{{efn|Between 13 and 16 March.}} The captain had given the crew orders to avoid an infection and a family day had been cancelled. However, the girlfriend of a sailor said in an interview on [[Easter Sunday]] that some sailors had eaten in the same restaurants as their wives or families. She also said that sailors of other nationalities had been there, too.<ref name="europe1_between"/> As of 18 April, Brest is considered the point of infection.<ref name="franceinfo-04-18"/>

On 15 April 2020, the Ministry of Armed Forces reported that, out of the 1,767 tests conducted on the members of the carrier battle group, 668 returned positive, with the vast majority of cases being aboard ''Charles de Gaulle''.<ref name="groupe.aeronaval.mda.twitter">{{cite tweet |author=Ministère des Armées |author-link=Ministry of Armed Forces (France) |user=Armees_Gouv |number=1250513114161909763 |date=15 April 2020 |title=Le 13 avril au soir, tous les éléments du groupe aéronaval ont rejoint leurs bases... |language=fr}}</ref>{{efn|30% of the tests were still inconclusive.<ref name="groupe.aeronaval.mda.twitter" />}}  The 1,700 sailors of ''Charles de Gaulle'' were subsequently quarantined for two weeks.<ref name="groupe.aeronaval.mda.twitter" /><ref name="groupe.aeronaval.bleu">{{Cite web |url=https://www.francebleu.fr/infos/societe/coronavirus-au-moins-500-marins-du-charles-de-gaulle-positifs-au-covid-19-1586960222 |title=Coronavirus : Près de 700 marins positifs au Covid-19, la majorité sur le porte-avions Charles de Gaulle |date=15 April 2020 |work=[[France Bleu]] |language=fr}}</ref>

On 17 April, the numbers reported went up to more than 1,000 infected, from 2,000 tested, with 500 showing symptoms, 24 admitted to hospital and one of them to an [[intensive care unit]] (ICU).<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.tagesschau.de/ausland/corona-frankreich-flugzeugtraeger-101.html |title = 1000 Seeleute auf "Charles de Gaulle" infiziert |date=17 April 2020 |work=[[Tagesschau (German TV series)|Tagesschau]] |language=de}}</ref>

[[File:US_Navy_110321-N-0000X-027_Adm._Samuel_J._Locklear,_III_speaks_with_an_aircrew_team_from_the_French_Navy_aircraft_carrier_Charles_de_Gaulle_(R91).jpg|thumb|Admiral [[Samuel J. Locklear]] inside ''Charles de Gaulle'' (2011)]]
On 18 April, it was reported that 1,081 out of 2,300 people on both ''Charles de Gaulle'' and its escort, [[frigate]] {{ship|French frigate|Chevalier Paul||2}}, tested positive. Two investigations have been launched. One on the situation in Brest and one lead by admiral [[Christophe Prazuck]] on the epidemic on board of the carrier strike group.<ref name="figaro"/>

Reports for 23 April state that about 15 people were still in hospital with three in intensive care.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://der-farang.com/de/pages/wie-ein-virus-zwei-kriegsschiffe-lahmlegte |title=Wie ein Virus zwei Kriegsschiffe lahmlegte, Frankreich |first1=Julia |last1=Naue |first2=Lena |last2=Klimkeit |date=24 April 2020 |website=Der Farang |language=de}}</ref> It was said that only about half of the positively tested persons were presenting symptoms. This correlates with a similar case on a US aircraft carrier. During the previous voyage coughing went an everywhere item on board. It's further said that the average age of this military ship is below the average age in the overall population (of France).{{citation needed|date=May 2020}}{{clarify|date=May 2020}}

By 11 May the number of hospitalized cases dropped to two, with one of them in intensive care. In total 20 sailors were still ill.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.navalnews.com/naval-news/2020/05/covid-19-aboard-french-aircraft-carrier-98-of-the-crew-now-cured/|title=COVID-19 Aboard French Aircraft Carrier: 98% of the Crew Now Cured|date=11 May 2020}}</ref>

Did Belgium make the right choice by interrupting the Foch mission|first=Nicolas|last=Gros-Verheyde |work=Bruxelles2 |date=10 April 2020}}</ref> The nature of warships, which includes working with others in small enclosed areas and a lack of private quarters for the vast majority of crew, lent themselves to the rapid spread of the disease to a degree even greater than that seen on [[COVID-19 pandemic on cruise ships|cruise ships]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Faturechi|first=Robert|url=https://www.propublica.org/article/after-discovering-a-sailor-with-coronavirus-the-us-navy-crowded-dozens-into-one-room |title=After Discovering a Sailor With Coronavirus, the U.S. Navy Crowded Dozens Into One Room|date=16 March 2020|work=ProPublica|access-date=24 April 2020|url-status=live|last2=Rose|first2=Megan|last3=Miller|first3=T. Christian}}</ref> Indeed, on ''Charles de Gaulle'' there were places where 20 people would sleep together on three or four floors of [[Berth (sleeping)|berths]].<ref name="franceinfo-04-18">{{Cite news|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/sante/maladie/coronavirus/marins-contamines-au-covid-19-sur-le-charles-de-gaulle-a-des-endroits-il-y-a-une-vingtaine-de-matelots-qui-dorment-sur-3-ou-4-etages-de-couchettes_3922411.html |title=Marins contaminés au Covid-19 sur le "Charles-de-Gaulle" : "À des endroits, il y a une vingtaine de matelots qui dorment sur 3 ou 4 étages de couchettes" |language=fr |access-date=18 April 2020 |website=Franceinfo}}</ref>

During this time, several [[common cold]]s were registered on board, but the level of sick sailors was not above the ordinary for the frigid −5&nbsp;°C temperature and strong winds of the region ''Charles de Gaulle'' was operating in.<ref name="Invisible Foe"/><ref name="figaro">{{Cite news|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/flash-actu/france-un-tiers-des-marins-du-porte-avions-charles-de-gaulle-positifs-au-coronavirus-20200415|title=Coronavirus: un tiers des marins du porte-avions Charles-de-Gaulle sont infectés|language=fr|access-date=18 April 2020|newspaper=[[Le Figaro]]}}</ref> Starting on 5 April, however, the number of crew members reporting to the infirmary began growing exponentially to alarming levels. Two sailors were medically scanned and found to be infected by the coronavirus, after which the French defense minister [[Florence Parly]] ordered the carrier to return to its home port of Toulon.<ref name="Invisible Foe"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.france24.com/fr/20200408-coronavirus-plusieurs-cas-suspects-à-bord-du-porte-avions-français-charles-de-gaulle |title=Coronavirus : plusieurs cas suspects à bord du porte-avions français Charles-de-Gaulle |language=fr |work=France 24 |trans-title=Coronavirus: several suspected cases on board the French aircraft carrier Charles de Gaulle |date=8 April 2020 |access-date=8 April 2020}}</ref><ref name="reuters-heading">{{cite web |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-france-military/french-military-ship-heading-back-to-port-due-to-possible-covid-19-cases-idUSKBN21Q0SY |title=French aircraft carrier heads home early due to possible COVID-19 cases |work=Reuters |date=8 April 2020 |access-date=8 April 2020}}</ref> On 10 April, French news outlets reported that out of 66 tests conducted on board the |url=http://www.leparisien.fr/politique/coronavirus-50-cas-de-contamination-a-bord-du-charles-de-gaulle-trois-marins-evacues-10-04-2020-8297511.php |title=Coronavirus : 50 cas de contamination à bord Hospital|fr|Hôpital d'instruction des armées Sainte-Anne}}.<ref name="gaulle.parisien" /><ref name="gaulle.times">{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/reuters/2020/04/10/world/europe/10reuters-health-coronavirus-france-aircraftcarrier.html |title=France Reports 50 COVID-19 Cases Aboard Aircraft Carrier |newspaper=The New York Times |agency=Reuters |date=10 April 2020 |access-date=11 April 2020}}</ref> There were 1,760 personnel on board.<ref name="reuters-heading"/>

The carrier arrived in Toulon on the afternoon of 12 April, where the personnel began a 14-day quarantine.<ref name="franceinfo 2020-04-11"/><ref name="europe1_between">{{Cite news |url=https://www.europe1.fr/societe/covid-19-a-bord-du-charles-de-gaulle-les-marins-qui-nont-pas-respecte-les-regles-doivent-etre-sanctionnes-3961696 |title=Les marins du Charles-de-Gaulle "qui n'ont pas respecté les règles doivent être sanctionnés" |access-date=18 April 2020 |work=Europe1.fr |language=fr}}</ref> The ship was to be decontaminated over the course of several weeks.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Hernandez|first=Nathalie|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/sante/maladie/coronavirus/vapeur-d-eau-a-haute-temperature-et-produit-antivirus-comment-va-se-passer-la-decontamination-du-porte-avions-charles-de-gaulle_3914589.html |title=Vapeur d'eau à haute température et produit antivirus : comment va se passer la décontamination du porte-avions Charles-de-Gaulle|date=14 April 2020|work=Radio France/France Info|language=fr|access-date=27 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

At first, the origin of the outbreak was unknown; there had been no contact with the outside world after the ship's stopover in Brest.<ref name="franceinfo 2020-04-11"/><ref name="europe1_between"/>{{efn|Between 13 and 16 March.}} The captain had given the crew orders to avoid an infection and a family day had been cancelled. However, the girlfriend of a sailor said in an interview on [[Easter Sunday]] that some sailors had eaten in the same restaurants as their wives or families. She also said that sailors of other nationalities had been there, too.<ref name="europe1_between"/> As of 18 April, Brest is considered the point of infection.<ref name="franceinfo-04-18"/>

On 15 April 2020, the Ministry of Armed Forces reported that, out of the 1,767 tests conducted on the members of the carrier battle group, 668 returned positive, with the vast majority of cases being aboard ''Charles de Gaulle''.<ref name="groupe.aeronaval.mda.twitter">{{cite tweet |author=Ministère des Armées |author-link=Ministry of Armed Forces (France) |user=Armees_Gouv |number=1250513114161909763 |date=15 April 2020 |title=Le 13 avril au soir, tous les éléments du groupe aéronaval ont rejoint leurs bases... |language=fr}}</ref>{{efn|30% of the tests were still inconclusive.<ref name="groupe.aeronaval.mda.twitter" />}}  The 1,700 sailors of ''Charles de Gaulle'' were subsequently quarantined for two weeks.<ref name="groupe.aeronaval.mda.twitter" /><ref name="groupe.aeronaval.bleu">{{Cite web |url=https://www.francebleu.fr/infos/societe/coronavirus-au-moins-500-marins-du-charles-de-gaulle-positifs-au-covid-19-1586960222 |title=Coronavirus : Près de 700 marins positifs au Covid-19, la majorité sur le porte-avions Charles de Gaulle |date=15 April 2020 |work=[[France Bleu]] |language=fr}}</ref>

On 17 April, the numbers reported went up to more than 1,000 infected, from 2,000 tested, with 500 showing symptoms, 24 admitted to hospital and one of them to an [[intensive care unit]] (ICU).<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.tagesschau.de/ausland/corona-frankreich-flugzeugtraeger-101.html |title = 1000 Seeleute auf "Charles de Gaulle" infiziert |date=17 April 2020 |work=[[Tagesschau (German TV series)|Tagesschau]] |language=de}}</ref>

[[File:US_Navy_110321-N-0000X-027_Adm._Samuel_J._Locklear,_III_speaks_with_an_aircrew_team_from_the_French_Navy_aircraft_carrier_Charles_de_Gaulle_(R91).jpg|thumb|Admiral [[Samuel J. Locklear]] inside ''Charles de Gaulle'' (2011)]]
On 18 April, it was reported that 1,081 out of 2,300 people on both ''Charles de Gaulle'' and its escort, [[frigate]] {{ship|French frigate|Chevalier Paul||2}}, tested positive. Two investigations have been launched. One on the situation in Brest and one lead by admiral [[Christophe Prazuck]] on the epidemic on board of the carrier strike group.<ref name="figaro"/>

Reports for 23 April state that about 15 people were still in hospital with three in intensive care.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://der-farang.com/de/pages/wie-ein-virus-zwei-kriegsschiffe-lahmlegte |title=Wie ein Virus zwei Kriegsschiffe lahmlegte, Frankreich |first1=Julia |last1=Naue |first2=Lena |last2=Klimkeit |date=24 April 2020 |website=Der Farang |language=de}}</ref> It was said that only about half of the positively tested persons were presenting symptoms. This correlates with a similar case on a US aircraft carrier. During the previous voyage coughing went an everywhere item on board. It's further said that the average age of this military ship is below the average age in the overall population (of France).{{citation needed|date=May 2020}}{{clarify|date=May 2020}}

By 11 May the number of hospitalized cases dropped to two, with one of them in intensive care. In total 20 sailors were still ill.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.navalnews.com/naval-news/2020/05/covid-19-aboard-french-aircraft-carrier-98-of-the-crew-now-cured/|title=COVID-19 Aboard French Aircraft Carrier: 98% of the Crew Now Cured|date=11 May 2020}}</ref>
