'''Ava''' is a medical technology company that developed the Ava bracelet, a wearable [[fertility]] tracker.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mashable.com/2016/11/19/ava-fertility-wearable-series-a/|title=The next frontier in wearables is helping couples conceive|last=Hinchliffe|first=Emma|website=Mashable|language=en|access-date=2019-05-03}}</ref><ref name=":4">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/08/27/business/women-fertility-apps-pregnancy.html|title=Women Struggling to Get Pregnant Turn to Fertility Apps|last=Morrissey|first=Janet|date=2018-08-27|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-05-03|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> The Ava bracelet was the first fertility-tracking [[Wearable technology|wearable device]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/viviennedecker/2016/09/01/26-year-old-ceo-lea-von-bidder-launches-ava-the-first-fertility-tracking-sensor-bracelet/|title=26-Year-Old CEO Lea von Bidder Launches Ava, The First Fertility Tracking Sensor Bracelet|last=Decker|first=Vivienne|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2019-05-03}}</ref>

The device tracks women’s cycles by measuring the wearer’s temperature, heart rate, heat loss and other conditions. Data collected from the bracelet is displayed on an app, and intended to help women manage their reproductive health including preventing pregnancy, conceiving, and tracking health during pregnancy or menopause.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.fastcompany.com/40577535/fertility-tracker-ava-just-announced-a-significant-round-of-funding|title=Fertility tracker Ava just announced a significant round of funding|last=Raphael|first=Rina|date=2018-05-30|website=Fast Company|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://social.techcrunch.com/2015/09/22/ava-is-a-bracelet-that-aims-to-predict-the-most-likely-days-for-women-to-get-pregnant/|title=Ava Is A Bracelet That Aims To Predict The Most Likely Days For Women To Get Pregnant|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref>

The company was founded in [[Zürich|Zurich]], Switzerland by Lea Von Bidder, Pascal Koenig and Peter Stein.<ref name=":1" /> In September 2015, the company took part in [[TechCrunch]]’s Startup Battlefield.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://social.techcrunch.com/2016/11/15/ava-the-fertility-wearable-raises-9-7m-to-help-families-conceive/|title=Ava, the fertility wearable, raises $9.7M to help families conceive|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-10}}</ref> In November 2015, Ava raised a $2.6 million funding round led by Swisscom and [[Zurich Cantonal Bank|ZKB]].<ref name=":3" /> In 2016, the Ava bracelet was approved as a Class One medical device by the [[Food and Drug Administration|FDA]].<ref name=":3" /> The company began shipping the Ava bracelet to customers in July 2016.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://social.techcrunch.com/2016/07/26/ava-launches-a-wearable-to-help-couples-conceive/|title=Ava launches a wearable to help couples conceive|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnet.com/news/meet-ava-the-wearable-that-helps-couples-get-pregnant/|title=Meet Ava, the wearable that helps couples get pregnant|last=Rhodes|first=Samantha|website=CNET|language=en|access-date=2019-05-03}}</ref> 

In November 2016, the company raised a $9.7 million Series A funding round.<ref name=":2" /> In May 2018, the company announced it had raised $30 million in a series B funding round.<ref name=":0" />

In 2018, co-founder Lea von Bidder stated that at least 1,000 women had given birth after using the Ava bracelet since 2016.<ref name=":4" />

In 2019, actress [[Claire Holt]] became a brand ambassador for the company.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wearable-technologies.com/2018/12/ava-fertility-tracker-to-partners-with-claire-holt-to-promote-fertility-pregnancy-awareness/|title=Ava Fertility Tracker to Partners with Claire Holt to Promote Fertility, Pregnancy Awareness|last=Draper|first=Sam|date=2018-12-19|website=Wearable Technologies|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-23}}</ref>
