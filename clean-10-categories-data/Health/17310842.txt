'''Aquajogging''' is a cross training and rehabilitation method using low impact resistance training. It is a way to train without impacting joints.<ref name="jake">{{cite web |last1=Shelley |first1=Jake |title=Aquajogging: how, when and why to try it |url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/the-running-blog/2016/may/11/aquajogging-how-when-and-why-to-try-it |accessdate=30 April 2019}}</ref> Participants wear a flotation device and move in a running motion in the deep end of a pool. Equipment, aside from a pool, can include a flotation belt and weights.<ref name="allie">{{cite web |title=Aqua Jogging - Lessons Learned|first1=Allie|last1=Burdick |url=https://www.runnersworld.com/women/a20802898/aqua-jogging-lessons-learned/ |website=Runner's World}}</ref> It is useful in training for running because the muscles used are similar.<ref name="john">{{cite web |last1=Davis |first1=John |title=3 Scientifically Supported Tips to Help You Get the Most out of Pool Running: The Latest Research |url=https://runnersconnect.net/deep-water-running-for-cross-training/ |website=Runners Connect |accessdate=30 April 2019}}</ref>
