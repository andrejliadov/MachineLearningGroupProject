==Formulation==

==Criticism==
Acronyms have been described as [[jargon]].<ref name="Guardian2011">{{cite web|last1=Healthcare|first1=Guardian|title=Glossary of healthcare jargon and acronyms|url=https://www.theguardian.com/healthcare-network/2011/aug/22/glossary-nhs-healthcare-jargon-acronyms|website=The Guardian|access-date=27 September 2017|date=22 August 2011}}</ref>{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} Studies have been conducted investigating the effect of acronyms on communication and in some studies even healthcare professionals are unclear as to the meaning of many acronyms.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Sinha|first1=S.|last2=McDermott|first2=F.|last3=Srinivas|first3=G.|last4=Houghton|first4=P. W. J.|title=Use of abbreviations by healthcare professionals: what is the way forward?|journal=Postgraduate Medical Journal|date=1 April 2011|volume=87|issue=1029|pages=450–452|doi=10.1136/pgmj.2010.097394}}</ref> The use of acronyms to describe [[medical trial]]s has been criticised as potentially leading to incorrect assumptions based on similar acronyms, difficulty accessing trial results when common words are used, and causing a cognitive bias when positive acronyms are used to portray trials (e.g. "HOPE" or "SMART").<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Fred|first1=Herbert L.|last2=Cheng|first2=Tsung O.|title=Acronymesis|journal=Texas Heart Institute Journal|date=2003|volume=30|issue=4|pages=255–257|issn=0730-2347|pmc=307708|pmid=14677733}}</ref>

Use of abbreviations, such as those relating to the [[route of administration]] or [[Dose (biochemistry)|dose]] of a [[medication]] can be confusing and is the most common source of [[medication error]]s.<ref name="ACSQH">{{cite web|last1=Australian Commission on Safety and Quality of Healthcare|title=Recommendations for Terminology, Abbreviations and Symbols used in the PRescribing and Administration of Medicines|url=https://www.safetyandquality.gov.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/32060v2.pdf|access-date=27 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160326214455/http://www.safetyandquality.gov.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/32060v2.pdf|archive-date=26 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Use of some acronyms has been shown to impact upon the safety of patients in hospitals, and "do not use lists" have been published at national level in the US.<ref name="Glassman">{{cite book|last1=Glassman|first1=Peter|title=The Joint Commission's "Do Not Use" List: Brief Review (NEW)|publisher=Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (US)|url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK133373/|language=en}}</ref>

==Examples==
A number of sources provide lists of initialisms and acronyms commonly used in health care. The terms listed are used in the English language within the [[health care]] systems and by [[healthcare professional]]s of various countries.<ref name="MedicineNet">{{cite web|title=List of Common Medical Abbreviations, Acronyms & Definitions|url=http://www.medicinenet.com/common_medical_abbreviations_and_terms/article.htm|website=MedicineNet|access-date=27 September 2017|language=en}}</ref> Examples of terms include [[Blood pressure|BP]], [[COPD]], [[TIMI]] score, and [[SOAP note|SOAP]]. There is no standardised list.<ref name="MedicineNet" />
