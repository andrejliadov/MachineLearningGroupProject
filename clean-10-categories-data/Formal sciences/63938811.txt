The '''AI Song Contest''' ({{Lang-nl|AI Songfestival}}) is an international competition based on the [[Eurovision Song Contest]], which features songs that have been composed using [[artificial intelligence]] (AI). The inaugural edition took place on 12 May 2020 and was organised by the Dutch broadcaster [[VPRO]], in collaboration with [[NPO 3FM]] and [[Nederlandse Publieke Omroep (organization)|NPO Innovation]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=FAQ - The AI Song Contest|url=https://www.vprobroadcast.com/titles/ai-songcontest/faq.html|last=|first=|date=|website=VPRO International|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>

== Format ==
Each competing team has to compose a "Eurovision-like" song of maximum three minutes using artificial intelligence.<ref name=":0" /> Human input is allowed, but the more AI is used, the more points the entry will get from the expert jury.<ref name=":0" /> The entries are also evaluated by the public through online ratings. The entry that receives most points wins the competition.

== 2020 edition ==
[[File:Wikimania_2014_MMB_42_Lieven_Scheire.jpg|thumb|[[Lieven Scheire]], presenter|210px]]

A total of thirteen teams, representing seven countries in Europe as well as Australia, competed in the inaugural edition of the AI Song Contest in 2020.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Teams - The AI Song Contest|url=https://www.vprobroadcast.com/titles/ai-songcontest/teams.html|last=|first=|date=|website=VPRO International|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref> As there were no pre-qualifying rounds, multiple teams from each country could enter the competition.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Karen van Dijk (VPRO) over Het AI Songfestival|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-iYHlWePceE|last=|first=|date=8 May 2020|website=De Nationale AI-cursus|publisher=[[YouTube]]|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=12 May 2020|language=nl}}</ref> The contest was won by [[Uncanny Valley (musical collective)|Uncanny Valley]], who represented Australia with the song "Beautiful the World".<ref>{{Cite news|last=Wakefield|first=Jane|date=12 May 2020|title=Australia wins AI 'Eurovision Song Contest'|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-52632916|url-status=live|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>

=== Presenter ===
The show was hosted by the Belgian comedian [[Lieven Scheire]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Introducing the AI Song Contest!|url=https://eurovision.tv/story/introducing-the-ai-song-contest|last=|first=|date=11 May 2020|website=Eurovision.tv|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref> The points from the online voting were announced by the Dutch television presenter [[Emma Wortelboer]], who had been the Netherlands' spokesperson for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2019]], while composer and AI researcher Vincent Koops revealed the points awarded by the jury. <ref name=":2" />

=== Expert panel ===
The jury consisted of three AI experts, who assessed each entry based on the usage of artificial intelligence in their compositions:<ref>{{Cite web|title=AI Panel - The AI Song Contest|url=https://www.vprobroadcast.com/titles/ai-songcontest/ai-panel.html|last=|first=|date=|website=VPRO International|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>

* {{Flag|Netherlands}} – Vincent Koops ([[RTL Nederland]]) <small>(spokesperson)</small>
* {{Flag|United States}} – Anna Huang ([[Google Brain]])
* {{Flag|United Kingdom}} – Ed Newton-Rex ([[ByteDance]])
