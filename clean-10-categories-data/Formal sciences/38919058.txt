The '''Oxford Model''' or the Oxford Econometric Model was created by [[Lawrence Klein]] and  [[James Ball (economist)|Sir James Ball]].<ref name=Wood>{{cite book|last=Wood|first=John Cunningham|author-link=John Cunningham Wood|title=Critical Assessments of Contemporary Economists|year=2005|publisher=[[Routledge]]|location=New York|isbn=978-0415310628|page=279|url=https://books.google.com/?id=jWxcKtahWEcC&lpg=PA279&dq=%22Lawrence%20Klein%22%20%22James%20Ball%22&pg=PA279#v=onepage&q&f=false|display-authors=etal}}</ref>  It included a [[Phillips curve|Phillips-type relation]] and led to an "explosion" of [[Econometrics|macroeconometric forecasting]].<ref name=Wood/>
