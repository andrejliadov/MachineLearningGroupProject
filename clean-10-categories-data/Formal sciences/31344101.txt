'''Statistical regions of Montenegro''' are defined, as of 2011, by the [[Montenegro|Montenegrin]] ''Regional Development Law'' (''Zakon o regionalnom razvoju'').<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.minekon.gov.me/ResourceManager/FileDownload.aspx?rid=66699&rType=2&file=Predlog%20zakona%20o%20regionalnom%20razvoju.pdf&alphabet=cyr |title=Draft of Regional Development Law }} - Montenegrin only</ref> The regions, as defined by law, roughly correspond to the informal and colloquial division of Montenegro, often used by the Montenegrin media and citizens.

Regions are not administrative divisions per se; they are used for statistical and analytical purposes, to help create the outline for more uniform economic development of Montenegro. This official definition of the regions of Montenegro is one of many definitions that are in everyday use in the country. However, this division into three regions is most widespread:

==Central Region==
This region consists of four municipalities. It is the most populous of the regions, and contains the capital of Podgorica, historical capital of Cetinje, and the industrial center of Nikšić. Most of Montenegrin economic, cultural, educational and administration base is located within the region.

Prominent tribal, historical and geographical subregions in the central region are: [[Nikšići (tribe)|Nikšići]] ([[Nikšić Municipality|Župa, Rudine), Golija]], [[Banjani]], [[Grahovo, Montenegro|Grahovo]], [[Krivošije]], [[Brda (Montenegro)|Brda]] ([[Piperi (tribe)|Piperi]], [[Rovca]], [[Bratonožići]], [[Kuči]], [[Bjelopavlići]], [[Vasojevići|Lijeva rijeka]]), [[Zeta valley|Zeta]] (Upper and Lower), [[Skadar lake|Skenderija]], [[Malesija, Montenegro|Malesija]], [[Old Montenegro]] ([[Pješivci]], [[Čevo]], [[Cuce]], [[Bjelice]], [[Njeguši]], [[Cetinje|Cetinje field]], [[Ceklin]], [[Cetinje Municipality|Komani, Zagarač]], [[Lješanska nahija|Lješani]] and [[Rijeka Crnojevića|Rijeka]]).

==Northern Region==
Northern region comprises eleven municipalities and is the largest by area. It encompasses the sparsely populated mountainous part of Montenegro. With the decline of the heavy industries in the 1990s, the region has seen perpetual economic hardship and migration of the population to the two southern regions.

Prominent tribal, historical and geographical subregions of the northern statistical region are: [[Drobnjaci|Drobnjak]] (Uskoci, [[Žabljak Municipality|Jezera, Šaranci]]), [[Tara (river)|Zatarje]], [[Pljevlja]], [[Piva (tribe)|Piva]], [[Brda (Montenegro)|Brda]] ([[Morača (tribe)|Morača]] (Upper and Lower), [[Vasojevići]]), Polja, [[Kolašin]] (Upper and Lower), [[Sandžak|Southern Sanjak]], [[Plav, Montenegro|Plav and Gusinje]], [[Bihor (region)|Bihor]] (Upper and Lower), [[Lim river|Polimlje]] and [[Tara (river)|Potarje]].
