'''Mahmoud Khatami''' (Persian: محمود خاتمی; born 1962 or 1963)<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.worldcat.org/identities/lccn-no2006035314/ |title=Khātamī, Maḥmūd 1962 or 1963- |work=WorldCat Identities |access-date=25 December 2018}}</ref> is an Iranian [[philosopher]].

== Life==
Mahmoud Khatami grew up in [[Tehran]]. Showing an early interest in humanities, he attended the Seminary of Islamic Studies, which gained him the traditional degree of ''[[Ijtihad]]'',{{Citation needed|date=March 2017}} the highest level in Islamic religious and theological learning. Concurrently, he attended the [[University of Tehran]] to pursue his secular education for BA, MA and MS, and finally a PhD in philosophy. Afterwards,  he continued his further education in [[England]], where he was awarded his second PhD and post- doc in the field of Philosophical Psychology.{{Citation needed|date=March 2017}}

Returning to Iran, he was appointed to the  Department of Philosophy at [[University of Tehran]] in 1997 where he is now a Professor of Contemporary Philosophy. In 2002, he was appointed as Fellow of Iran's Academy of the Arts. In the meantime, Khatami has been invited, and  visiting professor at Iranian as well as Western universities,{{Citation needed|date=March 2017}} and has been rewarded and awarded for his academic excellence both inside and outside Iran.
