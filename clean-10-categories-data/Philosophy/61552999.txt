The '''Encyclopedia of Concise Concepts by Women Philosophers''', ECC for short, is the first [[online encyclopedia]] that exclusively contains entries on concepts from the work of [[Women in philosophy|female philosophers]]. The ECC is an open access database developed by the [https://historyofwomenphilosophers.org/ Center for the History of Women Philosophers and Scientists] that went online on June 15, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://historyofwomenphilosophers.org/ecc/|title=Encyclopedia of Concise Concepts by Women Philosophers - History Of Women Philosophers|website=historyofwomenphilosophers.org|access-date=2019-08-21}}</ref> Each entry contains a brief explanation of a philosophical concept developed or significantly advanced by a woman philosopher as well as a list of primary and secondary sources for further research. The articles deal with female philosophers from the history of philosophy, from the antiquity to present. The articles are written by internationally recognized researchers and [[Peer review|peer-reviewed]] before publication. The encyclopedia is part of the digital collection of [[Paderborn University]] and articles include a DOI handle to make them available as official academic sources.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://digital.ub.uni-paderborn.de/ecc/nav/classification/2980790|title=Digitale Sammlungen / The Encyclopedia of Concise Concepts by Women Philosophers|website=digital.ub.uni-paderborn.de|access-date=2019-08-21}}</ref>

Editors-in-chief are [[Ruth Hagengruber]] and Mary Ellen Waithe.
