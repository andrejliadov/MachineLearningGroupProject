'''María Elósegui Itxaso''' (born, 7 December 1957, [[San Sebastián]]) is a [[Spain|Spanish]] jurist, philosopher and [[Professor]] of [[Philosophy of law|Philosophy of Law]] at the Faculty of Law at the [[University of Zaragoza]]. She was appointed in January 2018 a judge at the [[European Court of Human Rights]] (ECHR). 

== Education ==
Maria Elósegui graduated with a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D]] from the [[University of Navarra]] in 1987 and followed up on her studies with a second master in philosophy at the [[University of Glasgow]] in 1989.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Composition of the European Court of Human Rights|url=https://www.echr.coe.int/pages/home.aspx?p=court/judges|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=31 October 2020|website=[[European Court of Human Rights]]}}</ref> Following she studied law at the [[Saint-Louis University, Brussels|University of Sain-Louis, Brussels]], [[Belgium]] from where she graduated with a [[Master of Science|MSc]] in 1994.<ref name=":0" /> She obtained a [[Doctor of Juridical Science]] from the University of Navarra in 2002.<ref name=":0" />

== Professional career ==
Between1982 and 1988 she was teaching philosophy in [[Bilbao]], and from 1988 to 1989 she researched at the Glasgow University.<ref name=":0" /> She became a  Professor of Philosophy of Law at the University of Zaragossa in 1994 and lectured until 2018.<ref name=":0" /> She was also assigned as a member of the [[European Commission against Racism and Intolerance]] (ECRI) between 2013 and 2017.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Mujika|first=Mikel|date=|title=Una donostiarra hace historia como primera jueza española en Estrasburgo|url=https://www.noticiasdegipuzkoa.eus/actualidad/deusto/2018/01/24/donostiarra-historia-primera-jueza-espanola/537035.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-10-31|website=www.noticiasdegipuzkoa.eus}}</ref> Since 2018, she is the representative of Spain in the European Court of Human Rights,<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=January 2018|title=PACE elects María Elósegui Ichaso judge to the European Court of Human Rights in respect of Spain|url=https://pace.coe.int/en/news/6931/pace-elects-maria-elosegui-ichaso-judge-to-the-european-court-of-human-rights-in-respect-of-spain|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=31 October 2020|website=Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe}}</ref> succeeding [[Luis López Guerra]]<ref>{{Cite web|last=AGENCIAS|first=RTVE es /|date=2018-01-23|title=Elósegui, primera juez española del Tribunal Europeo de DD.HH {{!}} RTVE|url=https://www.rtve.es/noticias/20180123/maria-elosegui-nueva-jueza-espanola-del-tribunal-europeo-derechos-humanos/1665790.shtml|access-date=2020-10-31|website=RTVE.es|language=es}}</ref> 

=== Controversies ===
In 2018, she dissented with the rest of the ruling judges at the ECHR and mentioned in her opinion that what the Pussy Riots have done, is not covered by liberty of expression and would have deserved a punishment.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Periódico|first=El|date=2018-08-30|title=Polémico voto particular de la jueza Elósegui en el Tribunal de Estrasburgo|url=https://www.elperiodico.com/es/politica/20180830/jueza-elosgui-voto-particular-pussy-riot-7009250|access-date=2020-10-31|website=elperiodico|language=es}}</ref> The Pussy Riots have performed a protest performance at the [[Cathedral of Christ the Saviour]] in [[Moscow]] in 2012 against the return of [[Vladimir Putin]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Press|first=Associated|date=2012-04-22|title=Russians rally support for Orthodox church over Pussy Riot controversy|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2012/apr/22/russians-support-orthodox-church-pussy-riot|access-date=2020-10-31|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> 

== Publications ==
During her career, she published numerous books concerning to rule of law, except the one about the Peine el Viento.<ref name=":1" />
