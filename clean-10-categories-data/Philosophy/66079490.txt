'''Arif Ahmed''' is a philosopher at the [[University of Cambridge]], where he became a fellow of [[Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge|Gonville and Caius College]] in 2015,<ref name=cai>{{cite web|url=https://www.cai.cam.ac.uk/people/arif-ahmed|title=Arif Ahmed|publisher=Gonville and Caius College|accessdate=2020-12-11}}</ref> [[Reader (academic rank)|university reader]] in philosophy in 2016,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.phil.cam.ac.uk/people/teaching-research-pages/ahmed/ahmed-page|title=Arif Ahmed|work=Cambridge Faculty of Philosophy|accessdate=2020-12-11}}</ref> and and Nicholas Sallnow-Smith College Lecturer in 2019.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://issuu.com/gonvillecaiuscollege/docs/once_a_caian..._issue_19_single_pages/s/154823|title=Investing in teaching|magazine=Once a Caian|issue=19|year=2019}}</ref> His interests in philosophy include [[decision theory]] and religion, from an atheist and [[libertarian]] point of view.<ref name=cai/>

At Cambridge, he has been an advocate for tolerance of diverse political views, in reaction to the university administration's cancellation of an invitation to right-wing academic [[Jordan Peterson]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/education/2020/dec/10/cambridge-university-urged-to-re-invite-rightwing-academic-jordan-peterson|newspaper=The Guardian|title=Cambridge University urged to re-invite rightwing academic Jordan Peterson|first=Ben|last=Quinn|date=10 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|newspaper=The Times|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/the-cambridge-professor-fighting-academic-mccarthyism-jhzctnnpp|title=The Cambridge professor fighting 'academic McCarthyism': Freedom of speech is fragile, Arif Ahmed tells Damian Whitworth|first=Damian|last=Whitworth|date=5 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2020/12/09/cambridge-university-dons-win-free-speech-row-defeat-new-authoritarian/|title=Cambridge University dons win free speech row, defeat new 'authoritarian' rules|first=Camilla|last=Turner|date=9 December 2020}}</ref>
