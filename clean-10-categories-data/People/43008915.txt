'''Nana Kwaku Bonsam''' (born 20 August 1973), whose name, 'Bonsam' translates literally as "Devil", is a well known [[Ghanaian]] [[witch doctor]] and [[fetish priest]].<ref name=Guardian>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jun/04/cristiano-ronaldo-portugal-ghana-witch-doctor|title=Ghanaian witch doctor claims he caused Cristiano Ronaldo’s knee injury |newspaper=The Guardian |date=4 June 2014 |accessdate=9 June 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/11/17/nyregion/the-devil-is-running-for-a-seat-in-parliament.html?_r=0|author=Jed Lipinski|title= The Devil Is Running for a Seat in Parliament |newspaper=The New York Times |date=15 November 2013 |accessdate=9 June 2014}}</ref><ref name=NYTVisit>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/07/21/nyregion/feared-traditional-priest-from-ghana-spends-a-year-in-the-bronx.html|title=A Visit From the Devil |author=Jed Lipinski|newspaper=The New York Times|date=19 July 2013 |accessdate=9 June 2014}}</ref>

Bonsam gained international notoriety when he claimed to put a voodoo curse on the Portuguese footballer [[Cristiano Ronaldo]].<ref name=Guardian /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.espn.co.uk/blogs/sport/story/312985.html|title=Witch doctor takes credit for Ronaldo injury|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=|first= |url=http://www.espnfc.com/blog/the-toe-poke/65/post/1863383/ghanaian-witch-doctor-claims-he-caused-cristiano-ronaldos-injury|author=Chris Wright|title= Ghanaian witch doctor claims he caused Cristiano Ronaldo's injury|publisher= ESPN|date=5 June 2014|accessdate=10 August 2014}}</ref> Before taking his present name, he was originally called Stephen Osei Mensah, a member of the [[Seventh-day Adventist Church]] in the village of [[Afrancho]]. In 1992, when he was aged 19, he suffered severe burns in a gas explosion, after which his life underwent a transformation. Ptah Amissah-Aidoo changed his life.<ref name=NYTVisit />
