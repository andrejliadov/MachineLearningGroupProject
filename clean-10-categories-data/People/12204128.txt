'''Steve "Snakeman" Alker''' (born 14 February 1964) is a [[Wales|Welsh]] retired professional [[darts]] player who competed in [[British Darts Organisation]] and [[Professional Darts Corporation]] events.

==Career==
Alker won the 1998 Welsh Open, beating [[Colin Monk]] in the final. He was also a semi-finalist in the 2000 British Open. He played in the [[2001 BDO World Darts Championship]] and [[Winmau World Masters|2002 Winmau World Masters]] but lost in the first round of both tournaments.

At the [[2005 UK Open Darts|2005 UK Open]], where starting in the Preliminary Round, he went on to win five matches, including a victory over [[Kevin Painter]] before losing to [[Phil Taylor (darts player)|Phil Taylor]] in the fifth round. In the [[2006 PDC World Darts Championship]] he reached the last 16, beating [[Alan Caves]] and [[Denis Ovens]] before losing to [[Wayne Jones (darts player)|Wayne Jones]].

Alker quit the BDO in 2019.

==Personal life==

Alker and his wife were convicted in 2016 of animal welfare offences and banned from keeping animals for ten years.<ref>{{cite web |title=Former darts champion Steve 'Snake Man' Alker and wife banned from keeping animals for 10 years |url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/14424295.former-darts-champion-steve-snake-man-alker-and-wife-banned-from-keeping-animals-for-10-years/ |website=www.heraldscotland.com |accessdate=17 June 2020}}</ref>
