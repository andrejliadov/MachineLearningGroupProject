'''Kehinde Bankole''' (born 27 March 1985) is a Nigerian actress, model and television host. She made her entertainment debut in the 2003 Miss Commonwealth Nigeria beauty contest, then proceeded to the 2004 [[Most Beautiful Girl in Nigeria]] pageant. She won the revelation of the year award at the [[2009 Best of Nollywood Awards]], two years after her first screen feature in [[Wale Adenuga]]'s ''[[Super story]]''.

== Personal life ==
She was born in Ogun State as the fourth child of six of architect Babatunde Bankole and Titilayo Bankole, an administrative secretary. She has a twin sister, Taiwo, who also acts occasionally. Bankole studied at Tunwase Nursery and Primary School, [[Ikeja]]. She studied Mass Communication at [[Olabisi Onabanjo University]] but took a break to concentrate more on her modelling career in 2004.<ref name="afri">{{cite web|url=http://m.afrinolly.com/movieActors.php?movieCelebDetails=221 |title=Kehinde Bankole |publisher=afrinolly.com |accessdate=18 November 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140819170744/http://m.afrinolly.com/movieActors.php?movieCelebDetails=221 |archivedate=19 August 2014 }}</ref>

== Career ==

=== Modelling career ===
Bankole first entertainment experience was when she auditioned as a contestant in Miss Commonwealth Nigeria pageant in 2003, although she did not win, she claimed the "exposure" was all she really wanted as she made the top 10.<ref name="afri" /> She also had a shot at [[Most Beautiful Girl in Nigeria]] but could not make the top five list. After the contract of [[Genevieve Nnaji]] expired as a Lux ambassador, Bankole was chosen along with [[Sylvia Udeogu]] and [[Olaide Olaogun]] to become to new face of Lux in 2007.<ref name="punch">{{cite web|url=http://www.punchng.com/spice/style/fashion-should-be-simple-kehinde-bankole/ |title=Fashion should be simple — Kehinde Bankole |publisher=punchng.com |accessdate=18 November 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141129045008/http://www.punchng.com/spice/style/fashion-should-be-simple-kehinde-bankole/ |archivedate=29 November 2014 }}</ref>

=== Acting ===
Bankole made her acting debut during her reign as Lux ambassador in the family drama ''[[Super story]]: Everything it Takes'', playing the character Caro. She has also starred in other [[Wale Adenuga]]'s productions like ''[[Papa Ajasco]]'' and ''This Life''.<ref name="punch" /><ref name="tribune">{{cite web|url=http://www.tribune.com.ng/glitz/item/17155-my-beauty-opens-doors-for-me-kehinde-bankole/17155-my-beauty-opens-doors-for-me-kehinde-bankole |title=My beauty opens doors for me... —Kehinde Bankole |publisher=tribune.com.ng |accessdate=18 November 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141108014348/http://tribune.com.ng/glitz/item/17155-my-beauty-opens-doors-for-me-kehinde-bankole/17155-my-beauty-opens-doors-for-me-kehinde-bankole |archivedate= 8 November 2014 }}</ref> On television, her most notable role is as housewife Kiki Obi in ''Desperate Housewives Africa'', the Nigerian remake of the American hit series.

=== Talk show host ===
Bankole hosts daytime talkshows including "Soul Sisters" and "African Kitchen".<ref name="afri" />
