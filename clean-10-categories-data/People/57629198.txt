'''[[Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz|Gottfried Wilhelm (von) Leibniz]]''' (1 July 1646 [O.S. 21 June] – 14 November 1716); German polymath, philosopher logician, mathematician.<ref>Rescher, N. (2003). ''On Leibniz'', Pittsburgh, Pittsburgh University Press.</ref> Developed differential and integral calculus at about the same time and independently of Isaac Newton. Leibniz earned his keep as a lawyer, diplomat, librarian, and genealogist for the House of Hanover, and contributed to diverse areas. His impact continues to reverberate, especially his original contributions in logic and binary representations.<ref>Davis, M. (2011). ''The universal computer: The road from Leibniz to Turing'', (AK Peters/CRC Press).</ref>
