[[Amy Adams]] is an American actress who made her film debut in the 1999 [[black comedy]] ''[[Drop Dead Gorgeous (film)|Drop Dead Gorgeous]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.etcanada.com/movies/photos/amy_adams_best_roles.aspx#!ac058025d41cff6b5c18732cea1bcb31 |title=Amy Adams' Best Roles |publisher=[[Entertainment Tonight Canada]] |accessdate=May 5, 2015 |last=Bettridge |first=Daniel |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150829004511/http://www.etcanada.com/movies/photos/amy_adams_best_roles.aspx#!ac058025d41cff6b5c18732cea1bcb31 |archivedate=August 29, 2015 }}</ref> She went on to guest star in a variety of television shows, including ''[[That '70s Show]]'', ''[[Charmed]]'', ''[[Buffy the Vampire Slayer (TV series)|Buffy the Vampire Slayer]]'', and ''[[The Office (U.S. TV series)|The Office]]'', and also appeared in minor film roles. In 2002, she had her first major role in [[Steven Spielberg]]'s biographical crime drama ''[[Catch Me If You Can]]''. However, the film did not launch her career, as Spielberg had hoped.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2008/11/amyadams200811 |title=Some Enchanted Amy |work=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]] |publisher=[[Condé Nast]] |date=November 2008 |accessdate=May 5, 2015 |last=Shnayerson |first=Michael |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518084135/http://www.vanityfair.com/news/2008/11/amyadams200811 |archivedate=May 18, 2015 }}</ref> Three years later, she had her breakthrough by playing a joyful pregnant woman in ''[[Junebug (film)|Junebug]]'' (2005),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2014/12/18/370938167/a-watery-goodbye-to-all-that |title=A Watery 'Goodbye To All That' |publisher=[[NPR]] |date=December 18, 2014 |accessdate=May 5, 2015 |last=Jenkins |first=Mark |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150501143849/http://www.npr.org/2014/12/18/370938167/a-watery-goodbye-to-all-that |archivedate=May 1, 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sundance.org/pdf/press-releases/2009-06-24-screenings.pdf |title=Sundance Institute Celebrates Summer with Junebug and Outdoor Screenings of Riding Giants and Songcatcher |publisher=[[Sundance Institute]] |date=June 24, 2009 |accessdate=May 5, 2015 |format=PDF |page=1 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208055732/http://www.sundance.org/pdf/press-releases/2009-06-24-screenings.pdf |archivedate=December 8, 2015 }}</ref> for which she received her first [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]] nomination.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2006 |title=The 78th Academy Awards |publisher=[[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] (AMPAS) |accessdate=March 24, 2015 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141109220946/http://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2006 |archivedate=November 9, 2014 }}</ref> Two years later, Adams starred in the [[Disney]] romantic comedy ''[[Enchanted (film)|Enchanted]]'', for which she was nominated for her first [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress&nbsp;– Motion Picture Comedy or Musical|Golden Globe Award for Best Actress (Comedy or Musical)]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/56983/65th-annual-golden-globe-nominations |title=65th Annual Golden Globe Nominations |publisher=[[E!]]. [[NBCUniversal]] |date=December 13, 2007 |accessdate=March 24, 2015 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402125319/http://www.eonline.com/news/56983/65th-annual-golden-globe-nominations |archivedate=April 2, 2015 }}</ref>

In 2008, Adams played a naive nun in the drama ''[[Doubt (2008 film)|Doubt]]'', opposite [[Philip Seymour Hoffman]] and [[Meryl Streep]], for which she received her second Oscar nomination for Best Supporting Actress.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2009 |title=The 81st Academy Awards |publisher=Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS) |accessdate=March 24, 2015 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141110111206/http://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2009 |archivedate=November 10, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.psfilmfest.org/news/detail.aspx?NID=162&year=2008 |title=Amy Adams to receive the Spotlight Award for Doubt |publisher=[[Palm Springs International Film Festival]] |date=December 4, 2008 |accessdate=March 24, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402173217/http://www.psfilmfest.org/news/detail.aspx?NID=162&year=2008 |archivedate=April 2, 2015 }}</ref> She then appeared in the comedy-drama ''[[Julie & Julia]]'', co-starring Streep, and played [[Amelia Earhart]] in the adventure comedy sequel ''[[Night at the Museum: Battle of the Smithsonian]]'' (both 2009). The following year, she expanded into dramatic roles by playing a tough barmaid in [[David O. Russell]]'s sports drama ''[[The Fighter]]'' (2010), which gained her a third Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actress.<ref>{{cite news|first=Lennon|last=Christine|title=Amy Adams on Muppets (film)|The Muppets]]'' (2011), Adams played the strong-willed wife of a cult leader in [[Paul Thomas Anderson]]'s drama ''[[The Master (2012 film)|The Master]]'', opposite Hoffman.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/nov/22/amy-adams-trouble-with-the-curve |title=Amy Adams: 'David O Russell said to me: 'You are so not the princess type' |first=John |last=Patterson earned her a fourth Best Supporting Actress nomination at the Oscars.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2013 |title=The 85th Academy Awards |publisher=Academy of Motion
