'''Maliq [[Bey]] Bushati'''<ref>{{cite book|title=A Biographical Dictionary of Albanian History|chapter=Bushati, Maliq Bey|date=2012|last=Elsie|first=Robert|authorlink=Robert Elsie|publisher=I.B.Tauris|page=60}}</ref> ([[Shkodër]], 8 July 1880 – 15 February 1946) was an [[Albanians|Albanian]] public official and politician. After the [[Italian invasion of Albania]] he became an Interior Minister in the [[Shefqet Vërlaci|Vërlaci]] government and afterwards [[Prime Minister of Albania]] during the [[Albania under Italy|Italian occupation]] from 13 February to 12 May 1943, thus an [[Axis powers|Axis]] [[Collaboration with the Axis Powers during World War II#Yugoslavia|collaborator]].<ref>{{cite book |title=Albania at war, 1939–1945 |last=Fischer |first=Bernd Jürgen|date=1999|authorlink=Bernd Jürgen Fischer|publisher=Purdue University Press |isbn=9781557531414 |page=141 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=vTZnAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA141}}</ref>

Along with two other Axis Collaborators [[Lef Nosi]] and father [[Anton Harapi]], he was sentenced to death by [[communist Albania]].

== Biography ==
Maliq was born in Shkodër on 8 July 1880<ref name=":Kaçi">{{Cite book|title=Kryeministrat dhe ministrat e shtetit shqiptar në 100 vjet|last=Dervishi|first=Kastriot|publisher=55|year=2012|isbn=9789994356225|location=Tiranë|pages=93–94|language=sq}}</ref> to Hyssen Beg in the ''Alibegaj'' branch of the [[Bushati family]]. His father followed military education at the local school and in [[Istanbul]] afterwards. He served with the rank of ''[[binbaşı]]'' in [[Salonica Vilayet|Vilayet of Salonica]] during the first years of the 20th century, only to die suddenly at a young age in 1903.<ref name=":Hamdi">{{Cite book|title=Bushatllinjtë|last=Bushati|first=Hamdi|publisher=Idromeno|year=2003|isbn=9789992784129|location=Shkodër|pages=369–378|language=sq}}</ref>
