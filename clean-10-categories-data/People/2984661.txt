'''Dipoenus''' and '''Scyllis''' were early [[Art in Ancient Greece|ancient Greek]] [[sculpture|sculptors]] from [[Crete]] who worked together and were said to have been pupils of [[Daedalus]]. [[Pliny the Elder|Pliny]] assigns to them the date 580 BC, and says that they worked at [[Sicyon]], which city from their time onwards became one of the great schools of sculpture. <ref>[[Pliny the Elder]], ''Natural History'', [http://attalus.org/translate/pliny_hn36a.html#9 xxxvi.9â€“10]. </ref> They also made statues for [[Cleonae (Argolis)|Cleonae]] and [[Argos, Peloponnese|Argos]]. They worked in [[wood]], [[ebony]] and [[ivory]], and apparently also, in [[marble]].<ref>{{EB1911|inline=1|wstitle=Dipoenus and Scyllis |volume=8 |page=306}}</ref>
