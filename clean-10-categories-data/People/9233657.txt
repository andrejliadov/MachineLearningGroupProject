'''Agrippinus''' was the one of the earliest known [[bishop of Carthage|bishops of Carthage]] around the 230s.<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|last=Handl|first=András|last2=Dupont|first2=Anthony|title=Who was Agrippinus? Identifying the First Known Bishop of Carthage|journal=Church History and Religious Culture|volume=98|pages=344-366|doi=10.1163/18712428-09803001}}</ref> During his episcopacy, he dealt with the issue of how to treat Christian converts from schism or heresy.  He called a synod of bishops of [[Numidia]] and [[Africa (Roman province)|Africa]], probably around 230-235,<ref name=":0" /> which decided that such converts should be fully baptized.<ref name="DGRBM">{{Citation  | last = Christie  | first = Albany James  | author-link = Albany James Christie | contribution = Agrippinus  | editor-last = Smith  | editor-first = William  | title = [[Dictionary of Greek and Roman Biography and Mythology]]  | volume = 1  | pages = 82  | publisher = [[Little, Brown and Company]]  | place = Boston  | year = 1867  | contribution-url = http://www.ancientlibrary.com/smith-bio/0091.html }}</ref>

Subsequently, [[St. Cyprian]] would mention the positive reputation of Agrippinus (''bonæ memoriæ vir'').<ref name="CE">{{cite Catholic Encyclopedia|wstitle=Agrippinus|volume=1|first=John Joseph|last=A'Becket}}</ref>

[[Augustine of Hippo|St. Augustine]], in his arguments against the [[Donatist]]s, would remark that Agrippinus and Cyprian maintained the unity of the church despite being doctrinally mistaken.<ref name=CE/>
