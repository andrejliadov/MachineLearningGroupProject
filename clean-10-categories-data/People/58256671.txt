'''Martin Chungong''' (born 17 February 1957) is the eighth Secretary General of the [[Inter-Parliamentary Union|Inter-Parliamentary Union (IPU)]]. Chungong, a Cameroonian, is the first non-European and first African to be elected IPU Secretary General. He assumed this post on 1 July 2014.

==Early life and education==
Martin Chungong attended the [[University of Yaoundé]], from where he has a post-graduate degree. He then went to the [[University of Ottawa]], where he obtained a master's degree in Applied Linguistics in 1982. He also holds a post-graduate diploma from the Polytechnic of Central London (now the [[University of Westminster]]). He is fluent in English, French and Spanish.

==Career==
Chungong worked at the [[National Assembly of Cameroon]] for 14 years, including as the Administrative Secretary for Cameroon's representation to the IPU.  He also taught linguistics, translation in the Universities of Buea and Yaoundé in Cameroon.

Joining the IPU in 1993, he worked on parliamentary capacity-building in countries in transition or emerging from conflict, and served as Director for the Promotion of Democracy (2005-2011) and Director of the Division of Programmes (2011-2014). He also served as Secretary of the IPU Standing Committee on Democracy and Human Rights for eight years.

In 2012, Chungong became Deputy Secretary General of the IPU.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ipu.org/about-us/structure/secretary-general|title=Secretary General|date=28 February 2016|publisher=}}</ref>

==Secretary General==
In 2014, Martin Chungong became the first non-European and first African to be elected Secretary General of the IPU in the organization's 125 years.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Crossette |first1=Barbara |title=An African Elected Head of the Inter-Parliamentary Union |url=https://www.passblue.com/2014/03/21/an-african-elected-head-of-the-inter-parliamentary-union/ |accessdate=24 August 2018 |agency=PassBlue |publisher=PassBlue |date=21 March 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Jeune Afrique |title=Cameroun : Martin Chungong, l’apôtre des Parlements du monde |url=http://www.jeuneafrique.com/38053/politique/cameroun-martin-chungong-l-ap-tre-des-parlements-du-monde/ |accessdate=24 August 2018 |agency=Jeune Afrique |date=12 December 2014}}</ref> He was re-elected for a second term at the IPU Assembly in Dhaka in 2017.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Xinhua |title=Martin Chungong re-elected as IPU Secretary General  |url=http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2017-04/05/c_136185035.htm |accessdate=24 August 2018 |agency=Xinhua |date=5 April 2017}}</ref>

As the head of the IPU, Chungong develops programmes to help parliaments become stronger, more gender-sensitive and more representative.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Setting standards|url=https://www.ipu.org/our-impact/strong-parliaments/setting-standards|access-date=2020-07-15|website=Inter-Parliamentary Union|language=en}}</ref> As chair of the Management Committee on Accountability of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) Governance Network, he has introduced governance guidelines on strengthening democracy.<ref>{{Cite web|title=About GOVNET - OECD|url=http://www.oecd.org/dac/accountable-effective-institutions/about-govnet.htm|access-date=2020-07-15|website=www.oecd.org}}</ref>

Chungong is the Parliamentary Representative on the Steering Committee of the Global Partnership for Effective Development Cooperation,<ref>http://effectivecooperation.org/2017-18-global-partnership-work-programme/</ref> a multi-stakeholder group that works to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals through cooperation between various sectors, such as governments, parliaments, private sector, multilateral organizations, civil society and trade unions.<ref>{{Cite web|title=2017-18 Global Partnership Work Programme {{!}} Global Partnership for Effective Development Co-operation|url=https://www.effectivecooperation.org/content/2017-18-global-partnership-work-programme|access-date=2020-07-15|website=www.effectivecooperation.org}}</ref>

As part of his work on gender equality, Chungong is the Chair of the Global Board of the International Gender Champions.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Martin Chungong {{!}} International Gender Champions|url=https://genderchampions.com/champions/martin-chungong|access-date=2020-07-15|website=genderchampions.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Martin Chungong succeeds Arancha González as Chair of the IGC Global Board {{!}} International Gender Champions|url=https://genderchampions.com/news/martin-chungong-succeeds-arancha-gonzalez-as-chair-of-the-igc-global-board|access-date=2020-07-15|website=genderchampions.com}}</ref> He is also a Champion of the UN Programme to end violence against women in armed conflict.

Chungong was appointed by then UN Secretary-General Ban Ki-moon to shape the efforts of the Scaling Up Nutrition (SUN) movement in eliminating all forms of malnutrition.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://scalingupnutrition.org/|title=Working together in the fight against malnutrition in all its forms|website=SUN}}</ref>

He sits on the Board of the Partnership for Maternal, Newborn and Child Health (PMNCH).<ref>{{Cite web|title=PMNCH {{!}} Inter-Parliamentary Union|url=https://www.who.int/pmnch/about/governance/board/interparliamentary-union/en/|access-date=2020-07-14|website=WHO}}</ref>

He is a member of the High-Level Commission on the Nairobi Summit on ICPD25<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-11-14|title=Nairobi Summit on ICPD25: we will work to make the next 10 years, years of action, results for women and girls|url=https://esaro.unfpa.org/en/news/nairobi-summit-icpd25-we-will-work-make-next-10-years-years-action-results-women-and-girls|access-date=2020-07-14|website=UNFPA ESARO|language=en}}</ref> (25 years after the International Conference on Population and Development) Follow-up.

==Personal life==
He is married to Stella Chungong, a medical doctor. They have two adult children, Cindy and Martin Jr.
