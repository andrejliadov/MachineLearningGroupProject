'''Mina Ahadi''' ({{lang-fa|مینا احدی|Minâ Ahadi}}, born 1956) is an Iranian-Austrian political [[activist]]. As a [[Communism|Communist]] political [[Activism|activist]], she is a member of the Central Committee and Politburo of the [[Worker-communist Party of Iran]]. 

==Advocacy==
Mina Ahadi is opposed to faith-based laws and promotes citizenship rights and one secular law. Ahadi is also the main figure of [[International Committee Against Executions]] and International Committee Against Stoning. She is also the main founder of the German [[Central Council of Ex-Muslims]].<ref>{{in lang|de}} [[Central Council of Ex-Muslims|Zentralrat der Ex-Muslime]]: [http://www.ex-muslime.de/de/archiv/ahadi_bio.htm Kurzbiographie Mina Ahadi] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070908191542/http://ex-muslime.de/de/archiv/ahadi_bio.htm |date=2007-09-08 }}, 2007</ref> The Central Council of Ex-Muslims aims to break the taboo that comes with renouncing Islam and to oppose apostasy laws and political Islam. 

== Life ==
Ahadi's husband, who was also a political activist, was executed in Iran on the date of the couple's anniversary. His execution became her motivation to fight against capital punishment.<ref>{{in lang|de}} [[Central Council of Ex-Muslims|Zentralrat der Ex-Muslime]]: [http://www.ex-muslime.de/de/archiv/ahadi_bio.htm Kurzbiographie Mina Ahadi] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070908191542/http://ex-muslime.de/de/archiv/ahadi_bio.htm |date=2007-09-08 }}, 2007</ref>

She lives and works in Germany and helped to gain the freedom of [[Nazanin Fatehi]] in Iran. Due to death-threats against her, she has been living under police protection from the moment of her public appearance as the chairwoman of the Central Council of Ex-Muslims.<ref name="EM-Bio">{{in lang|de}} [[Central Council of Ex-Muslims|Zentralrat der Ex-Muslime]]: [http://www.ex-muslime.de/de/archiv/ahadi_bio.htm Kurzbiographie Mina Ahadi] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070908191542/http://ex-muslime.de/de/archiv/ahadi_bio.htm |date=2007-09-08 }} (German), 2007</ref><ref>{{in lang|de}} Anna Reimann: [http://www.spiegel.de/politik/deutschland/0,1518,466082,00.html Vorkämpfer für Islam-Austritte mit dem Tod bedroht] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070516192016/http://www.spiegel.de/politik/deutschland/0,1518,466082,00.html |date=2007-05-16 }}, ''[[Der Spiegel]]'', February 21, 2007</ref> Since October 2018 she is official "ambassador" for the [[registered association]] [[intaktiv e.V.]] which is against circumcision of male children.<ref>https://intaktiv.de/intaktiv-botschafter/#mina-ahadi-vorsitzende-des-zentralrats-der-ex-muslime</ref><ref>https://hpd.de/artikel/neue-botschafter-fuer-genitale-selbstbestimmung-mina-ahadi-und-ann-marlene-henning-16033</ref>

On 20 October 2007, she was awarded the [[Secularist of the Year]] prize by the [[UK]]'s [[National Secular Society]].<ref>[[National Secular Society]]:[http://www.secularism.org.uk/iraniandissidentminaahadiwinssec.html Iranian dissident Mina Ahadi wins Secularist of the Year prize] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080714155114/http://www.secularism.org.uk/iraniandissidentminaahadiwinssec.html |date=2008-07-14 }}, accessed on June 6, 2009</ref> Ahadi has two daughters.
