'''Mary Sophia Alston''' ({{nee|O'Sullivan}}; 1856&ndash;1932) was an Australian philanthropist. She was patron of the central executive of Melbourne's [[St Vincent's Hospital, Melbourne|St Vincent's Hospital]] and president of the [[Royal Women's Hospital]]. Her husband, [[James Alston]], was an innovator and entrepreneur of [[windmill]]s. 

==Life==
Alston was born in 1856, in [[Warrnambool]],<ref name="obit-record"/><ref name="austdictbio"/> to J. J. O'Sullivan and his wife, Anne, as one of four children to the couple.<ref name="parents"/> After marrying James Alston she moved to "Majella" in [[St Kilda Road, Melbourne]]<ref name="obit-australasian"/> and gave birth to four sons and three daughters.<ref name="sons-daughters"/> She also had a property at which she would regularly spend time, "Montalto", in Danks St, [[Port Melbourne]].<ref name="montalto"/> She would regularly holiday at her summer residence at the seaside town of [[Mount Martha, Victoria|Mount Martha]].<ref name="table-talk-mt-martha"/>

Over the years she gave money generously to various charities, with one paper estimating at her death that she "distributed thousands of pounds in five and ten-[[Guinea (coin)|guinea]] subscriptions", which they reported she did in "stealth" as she preferred to remain in the background.<ref name="donations-in-stealth"/> She was an extremely active charity worker, involved in 20 welfare committees in Melbourne towards the end of her life.<ref name="welfare-committees"/> During the [[First World War]] she was vice-president of the Victorian Red Cross Society.<ref name="obit-advocate"/> She participated in a long list of organisations: she was president of the [[St Vincent's Hospital, Melbourne|St Vincent's Hospital]] Auxiliary, taking over from Mrs N. R. Napier in 1928, and later was to resign but remain on as the vice-president;<ref name="the-herald-auxilliary"/> patroness of the central executive of St Vincent's Hospital;<ref name="obit-theargus"/> president of the [[Royal Women's Hospital]] until the beginning of her illness in August 1932;<ref name="obit-theargus"/> a vice-president of the [[Victoria League]];<ref name="austdictbio"/> honorary member of the Ladies Aid Association of the Homeopathic Hospital;<ref name="obit-theargus"/> president of the Victorian Association of Creches until June 1929;<ref name="creche-council"/> and president of the Loreto Free Kindergarten.<ref name="loreto-president"/> Her other concerns included the [[Queen Victoria Hospital, Melbourne|Queen Victoria]] and [[Alfred Hospital|Alfred]] hospitals, the Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children, the City Newsboys' Society, The Women's Orchestral Association, the Victoria Bush Nurses Association, and she was a member of the [[Lyceum Club (Australia)|Lyceum Club]].<ref name="obit-theargus"/><ref name="austdictbio"/><ref name="theherald-obit"/><ref name="obit-australasian"/>

==Death and legacy==
After an illness lasting four months<ref name="weekly-times-death"/> she died on 13 December 1932 at "Majella"&mdash;the primary residence she lived in since she had arrived in Melbourne.<ref name="obit-australasian"/> A [[Requiem Mass]] was held for her at [[St Patrick's Cathedral, Melbourne|St Patrick's Cathedral]] and she was buried at [[Melbourne General Cemetery]].<ref name="obit-advocate"/> At time of her death she had left real estate valued at £18,425 and personal properly valued at £9,676.<ref name="estate"/> In her will she bequeathed property to her children, gifts to a brother, sisters, and £100 each to the [[St Vincent de Paul Society|St Vincent de Paul]]'s Orphanage for Boys, St. Vincent's and Women's Hospitals, and the St Vincent de Paul's Orphanage for Girls.<ref name="estate"/> After her death a memorial was erected at the Women's Hospital in her honour for services to the hospital.<ref name="womens-hospital-memorial"/> The Loreto Free Kindergarten dedicated "The Mary Alston Room", where the Loreto Kindergarten committee met and children attended for a "quiet hour", in her honour.<ref name="mary-alston-room"/> St Vincent's Hospital also endowed the Mary Alston bed in recognition of her service.<ref name="mary-alston-bed"/>
