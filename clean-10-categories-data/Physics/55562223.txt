'''''Los Alamos Science''''' was the [[Los Alamos National Laboratory]]'s flagship publication in the years 1980 to 2005. Its main purpose was to present the laboratory's research and its significance to national security to the scientific community, and US government policymakers.<ref name="about">{{cite web | url=https://la-science.lanl.gov/about.shtml  
| title=About Los Alamos Science | website=Los Alamos Science | publisher=Los Alamos National Laboratory | access-date=25 September 2019 }}</ref>

Special issues appeared on subjects such as particle physics,<ref>[https://la-science.lanl.gov/lascience11.shtml No.11, Summer/Fall 1984]</ref> [[Stanislaw Ulam]],<ref>[https://la-science.lanl.gov/lascience15.shtml No.15, 1987]</ref> and the [[Human Genome Project]].<ref>[https://la-science.lanl.gov/lascience20.shtml No.20, 1992]</ref>
"Pedagogical articles" were intended to explain difficult concepts in one field to scientists and students in other fields.<ref name="about"/>
