'''Carol Lynn Alpert''' is an American science communication and public engagement expert and the Director of Strategic Projects at the [[Museum of Science (Boston)|Museum of Science]] (MOS). She is also an Associate in Applied Physics in the [[John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences]] at [[Harvard University]] and Co-Director of the Center for Integrated Quantum Materials.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.seas.harvard.edu/about-us/directory?search=%22Carol%20Lynn%20Alpert%22|title=Directory {{!}} Harvard John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences|website=www.seas.harvard.edu|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200229205415/https://www.seas.harvard.edu/about-us/directory?search=%22Carol+Lynn+Alpert%22|archive-date=29 February 2020|access-date=2020-02-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2018/12/three-from-harvard-named-aaas-fellows/|title=Ten from Harvard named AAAS Fellows|date=2018-12-19|website=Harvard Gazette|language=en-US|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190910042219/https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2018/12/three-from-harvard-named-aaas-fellows/|archive-date=10 September 2019|access-date=2020-02-29}}</ref>

In October 2018 she was elected a Fellow of the [[American Association for the Advancement of Science]] in the section on General Interest in Science and Engineering.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Science|first=American Association for the Advancement of|date=2018-11-30|title=2018 AAAS Fellows approved by the AAAS Council|url=https://science.sciencemag.org/content/362/6418/1010|journal=Science|language=en|volume=362|issue=6418|pages=1010â€“1013|doi=10.1126/science.362.6418.1010|issn=0036-8075|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190413120838/https://science.sciencemag.org/content/362/6418/1010|archive-date=13 April 2019|doi-access=free}}</ref>

In 2019 she worked with [[IBM]] and exhibit developers to create the first museum exhibit on quantum computing in the U.S., "A Look Inside a Quantum Computer." It featured a model of the dilution refrigerator of an IBM System One. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.americaninno.com/boston/inno-news-boston/want-to-see-the-first-integrated-quantum-computing-chip-head-to-the-museum-of-science/|title=Want to See The First Integrated Quantum Computing System? Head to The Museum of Science|last=Patel|first=Shafaq|date=18 April 2019|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190503091435/https://www.americaninno.com/boston/inno-news-boston/want-to-see-the-first-integrated-quantum-computing-chip-head-to-the-museum-of-science/|archive-date=3 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/museum-of-science-boston-launches-national-quantum-science-communication-competition-300787869.html|title=Museum of Science, Boston Launches National Quantum Science Communication Competition|last=Science|first=The Museum of|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190410175834/https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/museum-of-science-boston-launches-national-quantum-science-communication-competition-300787869.html|archive-date=10 April 2019|access-date=2020-02-29}}</ref>

She graduated magna cum laude from [[Harvard College]] in 1980.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://e3s-center.berkeley.edu/news-events/events/stc-meeting/speakers/carol-lynn-alpert/|title=Carol Lynn Alpert|website=Center for Energy Efficient Electronics Science}}</ref>
