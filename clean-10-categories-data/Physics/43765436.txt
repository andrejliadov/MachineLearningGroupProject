The '''Sponsoring Consortium for Open Access Publishing in Particle Physics''' (or '''SCOAP<sup>3</sup>''') is an international collaboration in the [[high-energy physics]] community to convert traditional [[closed access]] physics journals to [[open access]],<ref name="CopePhillips2014">{{cite book|author1=Bill Cope|author2=Angus Phillips|title=The Future of the Academic Journal|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3p7_AwAAQBAJ&pg=PA242|date=1 July 2014|publisher=Elsevier Science|isbn=978-1-78063-464-7|pages=242–}}</ref> freely available for everyone to read and reuse, shifting away the burden of the publishing cost from readers (traditional model) and authors (in the case of [[hybrid open access]] journals). Under the terms of the agreement, authors retain copyrights and the articles published under SCOAP<sup>3</sup> will be in perpetuity under a [[CC-BY]] license.<ref name="MeleMorrison2013">{{cite journal|last1=Mele|first1=Salvatore|last2=Morrison|first2=Heather|last3=D'Agostino|first3=Dan|last4=Dyas-Correia|first4=Sharon|title=SCOAP3 and Open Access|journal=Serials Review|volume=35|issue=4|year=2013|pages=264–271|issn=0098-7913|doi=10.1080/00987913.2009.10765254}}</ref> The initiative was promoted by [[CERN]] in collaboration with international partners.<ref>{{cite report |author=Yeomans, Joanne|date=11 January 2006 |title=Colloquium on Open Access (OA) Publishing in Particle Physics, 7-8 December 2005, CERN : minutes |url=https://cds.cern.ch/record/920982 |publisher=CERN |docket=CERN-OPEN-2006-003}}</ref><ref>{{cite report |author=Rüdiger Voss|date=22 June 2006 |title=Report of the task force on open access publishing in particle physics |url=https://cds.cern.ch/record/966160 |publisher=CERN |isbn=9789290832744 }}</ref><ref>{{cite conference |url=https://cds.cern.ch/record/986997 |title=From preprints to Open Access journals |last1=Aymar |first1=Robert |author-link1=Robert Aymar|date=27 September 2006 |location=Oxford |conference=Moving Towards Open Access: A JISC Conference for Research Funders, Authors, Publishers and Librarians}}</ref><ref name="MeleDallman2006">{{cite journal|last1=Mele|first1=Salvatore|last2=Dallman|first2=David|last3=Vigen|first3=Jens|last4=Yeomans|first4=Joanne|title=Quantitative analysis of the publishing landscape in high-energy physics|journal=Journal of High Energy Physics|volume=2006|issue=12|year=2006|pages=S01|issn=1029-8479|doi=10.1088/1126-6708/2006/12/S01|bibcode=2006JHEP...12S.001M|arxiv=cs/0611130}}</ref><ref>{{cite report |authors=Dallman, David Peter; Mele, S; Vigen, Jens; Voss, Rüdiger; Yeomans, Joanne |date=21 October 2006 |title= Establishing a consortium for Open Access (OA) publishing in particle physics |url=https://cds.cern.ch/record/991112 |publisher=CERN |docket=CERN-OPEN-2006-063}}</ref><ref>{{cite report |author=Bianco, Stefano |display-authors=etal |date=19 April 2004 |title=Towards Open Access Publishing in High Energy Physics : Report of the SCOAP3 Working Party |url=https://cds.cern.ch/record/1034368 |publisher=CERN |docket=CERN-OPEN-2007-015}}</ref><ref name="Aymar2009">{{cite journal|last1=Aymar|first1=Robert|title=Scholarly Communication in High-Energy Physics: Past, Present and Future Innovations|journal=European Review|volume=17|issue=1|year=2009|pages=33–51|issn=1062-7987|doi=10.1017/S1062798709000556|doi-access=free}}</ref><ref name="HeuerHoltkamp2008">{{cite journal|last1=Heuer|first1=Rolf-Dieter|last2=Holtkamp|first2=Annette|last3=Mele|first3=Salvatore|last4=de Kemp|first4=Arnoud|title=Innovation in scholarly communication: Vision and projects from High-Energy Physics|journal=Information Services & Use|volume=28|issue=2|year=2008|pages=83–96|issn=1875-8789|doi=10.3233/ISU-2008-0570|bibcode=2008arXiv0805.2739H|arxiv=0805.2739}}</ref>

Participating countries in the agreement sponsor SCOAP<sup>3</sup> journals through the consortium, and contribute according to their scientific output. More productive countries pay more, while lower-output countries pay less.

==Participating journals==
SCOAP<sup>3</sup> supports journals mostly publishing High-Energy Physics content fully, and those articles in other journals that have been submitted by their authors to a High-Energy Physics category on arXiv.org.<ref name=SCOAP3Journals>{{cite web |url=http://scoap3.org/scoap3journals |title=SCOAP3 Journals |work=scoap3.org}}</ref> Each year more than 4,000 articles are published in open access as part of the initiative.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://repo.scoap3.org/|title=SCOAP3 Repository|website=repo.scoap3.org|language=en|access-date=2018-12-02}}</ref>

In 2012, SCOAP<sup>3</sup> reached agreements with 12 subscription journals to make their articles openly accessible. This agreement would cover 90% of all published particle physics articles from 2014 onwards.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Van Noorden|first=Richard|date=2012-09-24|title=Open-access deal for particle physics|journal=Nature|language=en|volume=489|issue=7417|pages=486|doi=10.1038/489486a|pmid=23018944|bibcode=2012Natur.489..486V}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last=Van Noorden|first=Richard|date=2014-01-07|title=Particle-physics papers set free|journal=Nature|language=en|volume=505|issue=7482|pages=141|doi=10.1038/505141a|pmid=24402260|bibcode=2014Natur.505..141V|doi-access=free}}</ref> Of the original 12 journals, two journals pulled out of the agreement: ''[[Physical Review C]]'' and ''[[Physical Review D]]''.
<ref>{{cite web|title=SCOAP<sup>3</sup> Journals: APCs and articles|url=http://scoap3.org/scoap3journals/journals-apc|publisher=SCOAP<sup>3</sup>|access-date=2014-09-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Summary of Executive Board discussion and actions on Open Access and SCOAP3 at June 18/19, 2013 meeting|url=http://www.aps.org/about/governance/executive/openaccess.cfm|publisher=[[American Physical Society]]|access-date=2014-09-11}}</ref>
On April 19, 2016, SCOAP3 announced the extension of the initiative until 2019 with 8 journals participating.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://scoap3.org/scoap3-initiative-extended-for-three-more-years/|title=SCOAP3 initiative extended for three more years – SCOAP3|website=scoap3.org|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-02}}</ref> From 2018 on also APS joined with three journals to a total of 11 supported journals at the moment.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://scoap3.org/aps_joins_scoap3/|title=APS to join SCOAP3 from 2018 – SCOAP3|website=scoap3.org|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aps.org/newsroom/pressreleases/scoap3.cfm|title=APS and CERN Sign an Open Access Agreement for SCOAP3|website=www.aps.org|language=en|access-date=2018-12-02}}</ref>

== Members ==
Countries are usually represented by one or few library consortia, funding agencies or research organizations that act as a coordinator for the country.<ref name="LoizidesSchmidt2016">{{cite book|author1=F. Loizides|author2=B. Schmidt|title=Positioning and Power in Academic Publishing: Players, Agents and Agendas: Proceedings of the 20th International Conference on Electronic Publishing|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Lgy3DAAAQBAJ&pg=PA14|date=26 May 2016|publisher=IOS Press|isbn=978-1-61499-649-1|pages=14–}}</ref><ref name="SöllnerKutz2017">{{cite book|last1=Söllner|first1=Konstanze|title=Praxishandbuch Open Access|last2=Kutz|first2=Angelika|last3=Mittermaier|first3=Bernhard|chapter=3d. SCOAP3 – Goldener Open Access in der Hochenergiephysik|year=2017|pages=181–189|doi=10.1515/9783110494068-021|editor1-last=Söllner|editor1-first=Konstanze|editor2-last=Mittermaier|editor2-first=Bernhard|isbn=9783110494068}}</ref><ref name="OberländerReimer2019">{{cite book|author1=Anja Oberländer|author2=Torsten Reimer|title=Open Access and the Library|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ZxKQDwAAQBAJ&pg=PA101|date=4 April 2019|publisher=MDPI|isbn=978-3-03897-740-7|pages=101–}}</ref> Currently, 43 countries as well as 3 intergovernmental organisations ([[CERN]], [[IAEA]], [[JINR]]) are members of the consortium.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://scoap3.org/participating-countries |title=Current partners |work=scoap3.org}}</ref>
