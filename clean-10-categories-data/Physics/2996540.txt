'''''Advances in Physics''''' is a bimonthly [[scientific journal]] published by [[Taylor & Francis]] that was established in 1952. The journal is also issued as a supplement to the ''[[Philosophical Magazine]]''.<ref name=lcc/> [[Peer review]] is determined on a case-by-case basis. The [[editor-in-chief|editors-in-chief]] are John Chalker and [[David Sherrington (physicist)|David Sherrington]] of [[Oxford University]].<ref name=author>

==Aims and scope==
The focus of the journal is critical [[article review|reviews]] that are relevant to [[condensed matter physics|condensed matter physicists]]. Each review is intended to present the author's perspective. Readers are expected to have a fundamental knowledge of the subject. These reviews are sometimes complemented by a "Perspectives" section which publishes shorter articles that may be controversial, with the intention of stimulating debate.<ref name=jnldetail/> The intended audience consists of physicists, [[materials scientists]], and [[physical chemists]] working in universities, industry, and research institutes.<ref name=jnldetail/><ref name=audience>Intended audience

Broad, [[interdisciplinarity|interdisciplinary]] coverage includes topics ranging from condensed matter physics, [[statistical mechanics]], [[quantum information]], [[cold atoms]], and [[soft matter physics]] to [[biophysics]].<ref name=jnldetail/>
