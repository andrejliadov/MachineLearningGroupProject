'''Edward Joseph McParland''' is an Irish architectural historian and author. He was elected as Pro-Chancellor of [[University of Dublin|University of Dublin, Trinity College]] in 2013, and continues to give lectures after his retirement in 2008.<ref name=":2" /> McParland is the co-founder of the [[Irish Architectural Archive]] which was established in 1976, and he has contributed extensively to architectural conservation in Ireland.

== Education ==
McParland attended [[Belvedere College]] before completing an MSc in mathematics at [[University College Dublin]]. He attended [[Christ's College, Cambridge]] to study Fine Arts starting in 1965, and was elected a fellow of [[Jesus College, Cambridge]] in 1971, before being awarded his PhD in 1975.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Prochancellor TCD|url=https://www.tcd.ie/chancellor/prochancellor/current/mcparland.php#:~:text=He%20was%20educated%20in%20Belvedere,completed%20his%20PhD%20in%201975.|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>

== Career ==
McParland's first post at Trinity College Dublin was as Lecturer in the History of Art Department in 1973. He was elected Fellow in 1984, and appointed to the role of [[List of chancellors of the University of Dublin|Pro-Chancellor]] in 2013, a post which he still holds in retirement.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|date=2015-10-19|title=Edward Joseph McParland|url=https://www.ria.ie/ga/edward-joseph-mcparland|access-date=2020-08-18|website=Royal Irish Academy|language=ga}}</ref><ref name=":5">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=5 March 2013|title=Inauguration of Dr Edward McParland as Pro-Chancellor|url=https://www.tcd.ie/provost/addresses/2013-03-05_ProChancellorInauguration.php|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>

McParland founded the [[Irish Architectural Archive|National Trust Archive]] (NTA) with [[Nicholas Robinson (historian)|Nicholas Robinson]] in 1976, and he holds the position of board member and Company Secretary.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Origins and Development – Irish Architectural Archive|url=https://iarc.ie/about-the-iaa/origins-and-development/|access-date=2020-08-17|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Irish Georgian Society Conservation Awards Judges|url=https://www.igs.ie/education/awards/about-the-awards|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref> He co-founded the [[Irish Landmark Trust]], and serves on the committee of the [[Alfred Beit|Alfred Beit Foundation]] and the [[Irish Georgian Society|Irish Georgian Foundation]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Turning the old and odd into self-sustaining assets|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/life-and-style/homes-and-property/turning-the-old-and-odd-into-self-sustaining-assets-1.393727|access-date=2020-08-18|website=The Irish Times|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":5" />

He served as editor of the ''Carlovian'', the journal of the [[Carlow Historical & Archaeological Society]].

In 2016, McParland gave a talk on [[Sir Thomas Robinson, 1st Baronet|Sir Thomas Robinson]] who founded the Armagh Robinson Library.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Conlin|first=Carol|date=2016-06-28|title=Talk by Edward McParland on Thomas Robinson|url=http://armaghrobinsonlibrary.co.uk/wp/talk-by-edward-mcparland-on-thomas-robinson/|access-date=2020-08-17|website=Armagh Robinson Library & No 5|language=en-US}}</ref>

McParland remains active in scholarly societies, including a role as vice-president of the [[Ulster Architectural Heritage Society]], and a member of the [[Royal Irish Academy]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Who We Are|url=https://www.ulsterarchitecturalheritage.org.uk/about/who-we-are/|access-date=2020-08-18|website=UAH|language=en}}</ref>

== Awards and recognition ==
In recognition of McParland's contributions to scholarship in Irish architecture, he was elected an Honorary Fellow of the [[Royal Institute of the Architects of Ireland]], and made an Honorary Member of the [[Royal Society of Ulster Architects]].  He is a retired Fellow of the [[Society of Antiquaries of London]].<ref name=":4" /><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Provost and President, TCD|url=https://www.tcd.ie/provost/addresses/2013-03-05_ProChancellorInauguration.php|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>
