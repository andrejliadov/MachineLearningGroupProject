'''Nikita Andreevich Lomagin''' ({{lang-ru|Никита Андреевич Ломагин}}) is a Russian historian, economist and international relations expert, known for his comprehensive studies of socio-economic aspect of the [[Siege of Leningrad]].

== Academic career ==
Nikita Lomagin graduated from the Faculty of [[History]] of the [[Leningrad university|Saint-Petersburg State University]] in 1986, briefly worked for the [[Russian Academy of Sciences]] and then returned to his alma mater to join the newly-founded Faculty of International Relations as professor of Global Policy. In 1997 he also graduated from the Faculty of Law of his University, since 1999 - professor of Global Economics in the Department of Economics of his alma mater.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ломагин Никита Андреевич {{!}} Экономический факультет СПбГУ|url=http://www.econ.spbu.ru/ru/people/lomagin-nikita-andreevich|access-date=2020-09-17|website=econ.spbu.ru}}</ref> In 2000 he joined [[European University at Saint Petersburg|European University at Saint-Petersburg]] where from 2014 onwards he has served as a professor in the Department of [[Political science|Political Science]] at EUSP, Academic Director of the ENERPO program, Director of the ENERPO Research Center.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lomagin, Nikita {{!}} EUSP.org|url=https://eusp.org/en/people/lomagin|access-date=2020-09-17|website=eusp.org}}</ref>

Main subject of his studies in the Russian history is the Blockade of Leningrad during the [[Great Patriotic War]]. Lomagin's first thesis considered the counter-measures of the [[Communist Party of the Soviet Union|Communist Party]] against the [[Nazi Germany|German Nazi]] [[Propaganda in Nazi Germany|propaganda]] in [[Leningrad]] under siege. The thesis accomplished in 1989 was [[Classified information|classified]] for official use only due to its content considered then politically-sensitive. His second thesis that earned him a doctoral degree was accomplished in 2005 and concerned the political control over the population of besieged Leningrad.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Рыбакова|first=Елена|title=Никита Ломагин: "Практически все священнослужители работали как осведомители" — OpenSpace.ru|url=http://os.colta.ru/society/russia/details/36844/|access-date=2020-09-17|website=os.colta.ru|language=ru}}</ref>

Lomagin extensively worked in the archives on the topic of the Nazi blockade of Leningrad published "''In the Vise of Hunger''" - a collection of documents of German military and paramilitary forces as well as of the [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] [[NKVD]] (People's Commissariat for Internal Affairs) that reflect the German aims, plans and actions that concern the besieged city, and the situation inside the starving Leningrad, on its supply routes and on the frontline. The book also features the letters that the city dwellers exchanged with their relatives and friends living on the non-occupied Soviet territory. Based on the revealed documents, Lomagin concludes that despite the tremendous death toll of the starving, but resisting Leningrad the perspective of surrender to the [[Wehrmacht|German Wehrmacht]] would have brought far overreaching human losses and strategic obstacles to the allied war effort against the Axis.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2019-01-27|title=Nicht erobern, sondern vernichten (Not to conquer, but to kill)|url=https://www.fr.de/kultur/leningrad-blockade-11487417.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-09-17|website=fr.de|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-03-17|title=Институт памяти нужен, чтобы мы сохранили ленинградскую идентичность|url=https://www.fontanka.ru/2019/03/17/033/|access-date=2020-09-17|website=fontanka.ru|language=ru}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Историк Никита Ломагин: спецраспределитель в Елисеевском работал всю блокаду|url=http://novayagazeta.spb.ru/articles/8410/|access-date=2020-09-17|website=Новая Газета}}</ref> 

In 2016 he was appointed the official opponent to the thesis of a revisionist historian [[Kirill Alexandrov]] that was dedicated to the phenomenon of Soviet collaborators to the occupying Nazi regime.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-03-02|title=Защита с генералом Власовым|url=https://www.fontanka.ru/2016/03/01/173/|access-date=2020-09-17|website=fontanka.ru|language=ru}}</ref>

Other areas of scientific interest for Lomagin are international economic and political relations of [[Russia]], international cooperation in the [[Arctic|Arctic region]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-09-29|title=A Nation of Wannabe Europeans|url=https://www.ozy.com/around-the-world/a-nation-of-wannabe-europeans/71533/|access-date=2020-09-17|website=OZY}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last1=Contact|first1=Press|last2=Frontiers|first2=Director of Arctic|last3=Oooye@edakonvazapltqanvl.nasivwna.mznoxy|last4=+ 47 99738185|last5=+47 77750349|title=Professor Nikita Lomagin, Vice-Rector for GR at the European University of St. Petersburg will speak at Arctic Frontiers|url=https://www.mynewsdesk.com/no/arctic-frontiers/news/professor-nikita-lomagin-vice-rector-for-gr-at-the-european-university-of-st-petersburg-will-speak-at-arctic-frontiers-146865|access-date=2020-09-17|website=Mynewsdesk|language=en}}</ref>
