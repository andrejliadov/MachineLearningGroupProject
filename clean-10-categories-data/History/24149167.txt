The World Monuments Watch is a flagship advocacy program of the New York-based private non-profit organization [[World Monuments Fund]] (WMF) that is dedicated to [[Historic preservation|preserving]] the [[cultural heritage|historic, artistic, and architectural heritage]] around the world.<ref name=2006_press_release>{{cite web | title = World Monuments Fund Announces 2006 World Monuments Watch List Of 100 Most Endangered Sites | publisher = World Monuments Fund | author = Holly Evarts | date = 21 June 2005 | url = http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_release.pdf|accessdate=3 September 2009|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080516051456/http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_release.pdf |archivedate = 16 May 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Selection process==
Every two years, it publishes a select list known as the Watch List of 100 Most Endangered Sites that is in urgent need of preservation funding and protection. It is a call to action on behalf of threatened cultural heritage monuments worldwide. The sites are nominated by governments, conservation professionals, site caretakers, [[non-government organization]]s (NGOs), concerned individuals, and others working in the field.<ref>{{cite web | title = World Monuments Fund Announces 2008 World Monuments Watch List Of 100 Most Endangered Sites | author = Holly Evarts | date = 6 June 2007 | publisher = World Monuments Fund | url = http://www.wmf.org/pdf/Watch_2008_release.pdf|accessdate=3 September 2009|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20090521092007/http://www.wmf.org/pdf/Watch_2008_release.pdf |archivedate = 21 May 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref> An independent panel of international experts then select 100 candidates from these entries to be part of the Watch List, based on the significance of the sites, the urgency of the threat, and the viability of both advocacy and conservation solutions.<ref name=2006_press_release /> A site’s inclusion on the Watch List attracts international attention, helping to raise funds needed for its rescue and spurring local governments and communities to take an active role in protecting the cultural landmark.

==2004 Watch List==
The 2004 World Monuments Watch List of 100 Most Endangered Sites was launched on 24 September 2003 by WMF President [[Bonnie Burnham]] and WMF partner [[American Express]].<ref name=2004_press_release>{{cite web | title = 2004 List of 100 Most Endangered Sites Announced | publisher = World Monuments Fund | date = 24 September 2003 | url = http://www.archaeology.org/online/news/wmf04/index.html |accessdate=3 September 2009}}</ref> For the first time, a site from [[Antarctica]] was included, ensuring that the Watch List geographically covers every continent.<ref name=2004_press_release />
{{cquote|By working to preserve these treasures, WMF and its partners are helping to save for future generations the structures and places that tell us who we are. Be it a palace, a cave painting, an archaeological site, or a town, the sites on the Watch list speak of human aspirations and achievements. To lose any one of them would diminish us all.|200px||Bonnie Burnham, WMF president|launch of 2004 Watch List<ref name="WMF 2004 Watch List press release"/>}}
