==The Allan Martin Award==
This biennial award has been named for [[A. W. Martin]]<ref name="bibliography">{{cite web |title= Bibliography of A. W. Martin's Writings |publisher= University of Melbourne |url= http://www.mup.unimelb.edu.au/ebooks/0-522-85388-9/WhigView_Biblio.pdf |accessdate= 2007-07-21 |archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20070621192316/http://www.mup.unimelb.edu.au/ebooks/0-522-85388-9/WhigView_Biblio.pdf |archivedate= 21 June 2007 |url-status= dead |df= dmy-all }}</ref> (1926–2002) and is administered jointly by the [[Australian National University]] and the [[Australian Historical Association]].  The award is to encourage "early career historians" for work relating to Australian History.<ref name="martin">{{cite web|title=The Allan Martin Award |publisher=AHA |url=http://www.theaha.org.au/Allan%20Martin%20Award%20-%20Info&Application%20form_2008.doc |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070804170123/http://www.theaha.org.au/Allan%20Martin%20Award%20-%20Info%26Application%20form_2008.doc |url-status=dead |archive-date=4 August 2007 |accessdate=2007-07-21 |df=dmy }}</ref>  Submissions for this award are to be work that is being prepared for publication and can be in any form, e.g. a monograph, a series of academic articles, an exhibition or documentary film, or some mix of these.<ref name = "martin" />

==Blackwell AHA Prize==

The publishers, Blackwell Publishing Asia, have sponsored a prize for the best postgraduate paper at a Regional Conference.

The AHA information states that the "prize will be judged on two criteria: 1) oral presentation of the paper 2) written version of the conference paper. The written version of the conference paper (not a longer version) is to be submitted at the start of the conference. The winner of the prize will be announced at the close of the conference."<ref name="blackwell">{{cite web|title=Blackwell AHA Prize for the Best Postgraduate Paper at the Regional Conference 2007 |publisher=AHA |url=http://www.theaha.org.au/prizesandawards.htm |accessdate=2007-07-21 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070709215021/http://www.theaha.org.au/prizesandawards.htm |archivedate=9 July 2007 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref>

* '''2007 Winners'''

:: Nell Musgrove ([[University of Melbourne]]) for ''Private Homes, Public Scrutiny: Surveillance of 'the family' in postwar Melbourne''

==WK Hancock Prize==

The WK Hancock Prize is run by [[Australian Historical Association]] (AHA)<ref name = "AHA">{{cite web |title= The Australian Historical Association |publisher= AHA |url= http://www.theaha.org.au/ |accessdate= 2007-07-17| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20070717183526/http://www.theaha.org.au/| archivedate= 17 July 2007 | url-status= live}}</ref> with the Department of Modern History, [[Macquarie University]].  It was instituted in 1987 in honour of [[Sir (William) Keith Hancock|Sir Keith Hancock]] and his life achievements.

The award is for the first book of history by an Australian scholar and for research using original sources.  It is awarded biennially for a first book published in the preceding two years with the award presented at the AHA's National Biennial Conference.

==The Serle Award==

The Serle Award was first presented in 2002.  The award was established through the generosity of Mrs Jessie Serle for the historian [[Geoffrey Serle]] (1922–1998).<ref name="Serle Award">{{cite web|title=The Serle Award |publisher=AHA |url=http://www.theaha.org.au/Serle%20Award%20Info&application%20form_2008.doc |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070804165921/http://www.theaha.org.au/Serle%20Award%20Info%26application%20form_2008.doc |url-status=dead |archive-date=4 August 2007 |accessdate=2007-07-21 |df=dmy }}</ref>

The Serle Award is for the best thesis by an "early career researcher" and will be payable on receipt of publisher’s proofs, which must be within twelve months of notification of the award.

The biennial award will be administered by [[The Australian Historical Association]].<ref name = "Serle Award" />
