The [[Book of Joshua]] lists almost 400 ancient [[Levant]]ine city names (including alternative names and derivatives in the form of words describing citizens of a town) which refer to over 300 distinct locations in [[Palestine (region)|Palestine]], [[Jordan]], [[Lebanon]] and [[Syria]]. Each of those cities, with minor exceptions (e.g. [[Hamath]], [[Gubla]]) is placed in one of the 12 regions, according to the [[tribes of Israel]] and in most cases additional details like neighbouring towns or geographical landmarks are provided. It has been serving as one of the primary sources for identifying and locating a number of [[Middle Bronze Age|Middle Bronze]] to [[Iron Age]] [[Levant]]ine cities mentioned in ancient [[Egypt]]ian and [[Canaan]]ite documents, most notably in the [[Amarna correspondence]].
