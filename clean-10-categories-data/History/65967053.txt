'''Zeidan A. Kafafi''' (Arabic: زيدان الكفافي) is a Jordanian [[Archaeology|archaeologist]] and academic who has directed and otherwise contributed to numerous excavations in and around [[Jordan]] and has assisted the institutional development of local academic institutions. He most recently served as President of [[Yarmouk University]]. 

Among his contributions to the field of [[Near Eastern archaeology|Near Eastern Archaeology]] are the significant discoveries at the site of '[['Ain Ghazal|Ain Ghazal]], Jordan. 

== Education and university appointments ==
Kafafi holds a B.A. and M.A. from the [[University of Jordan]] (1971, 1977) and a Ph.D. from the [[Free University of Berlin|Free University Berlin]] (1982).<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Dr. Zeidan Kafafi - Home|url=http://faculty.yu.edu.jo/kafafi/SitePages/Home.aspx|access-date=2020-11-29|website=faculty.yu.edu.jo}}</ref> 

He joined [[Yarmouk University]]'s faculty in the eighties, first in the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences and then as a founding member of the Institute of Archaeology and Anthropology, becoming full professor thereafter, in 1993.<ref name=":0" /> He later served as Dean of the Faculty of Archaeology and Anthropology (2009–2011).<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Royal Decree approves appointment of three university presidents|url=https://en.royanews.tv/news/13782/Royal-Decree-approves-appointment-of-three-university-presidents|access-date=2020-11-29|website=en.royanews.tv|language=en}}</ref> Kafafi developed the M.A. program in Cultural Resource Management at Yarmouk in the late 1990s with support from USAID, and he has advised a number of undergraduate and graduate dissertations.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Dr. Zeidan Kafafi - About Me|url=http://faculty.yu.edu.jo/kafafi/SitePages/About%20Me.aspx|access-date=2020-11-29|website=faculty.yu.edu.jo}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Ellis|first=Brittany|date=2018-10-05|title=Community Archaeology at Tall Hisban|url=https://publications.acorjordan.org/2018/10/05/community-archaeology-at-tall-hisban/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-11-29|website=ACOR Jordan|language=en-US}}</ref> 

[[Abdullah II of Jordan|King Abdullah]] appointed Kafafi as president of Yarmouk University in 2018.<ref name=":1" /> 

Kafafi has also held leadership positions at [[Hashemite University]], served as president of the Jordanian Club of [[von Humboldt Fellows]], and is a member of the board of the organization Friends of Archaeology and Heritage (FoAH).<ref name=":0" /> He has served as a leading voice in the development of numerous higher education institutions in Jordan ([[Petra|Petra's]] Scientific Committee, [[Al al-Bayt University|Al Al-Bayt University]] Museum, Jordan's Ministry of Higher Education, the [[Dead Sea Scrolls]] Committee) and even elsewhere in the world (such as [[King Saud University]] in Riyadh).<ref name=":0" />

Kafafi was a member of the Royal Committee that first led to the establishment of the [[The Jordan Museum|Jordan Museum]], and he served as Acting Director of the museum from 2012–2013. He has also been involved in a number of other curation and museum projects in addition to his research.<ref name=":2" /> 

== Research ==
Prof. Kafafi's research interests largely revolve around the [[Neolithic Period|Neolithic period]], but also cover the [[Chalcolithic]], [[Bronze Age|Bronze]], and [[Iron Age|Iron]] Ages.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Rawashdeh|first=Saeb|date=2020-08-09|title=Jordanian scholar explores trade routes linking civilisations during antiquity|url=http://www.jordantimes.com/news/local/jordanian-scholar-explores-trade-routes-linking-civilisations-during-antiquity|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-11-29|website=Jordan Times|language=en}}</ref>

=== Field exploration ===
'''[['Ain Ghazal]]'''

Alongside Gary Rollefson, Kafafi undertook advanced excavations at '[['Ain Ghazal|Ain Ghazal]] in the early 1990s, serving as co-director of the project.<ref>{{Cite web|title='Ain Ghazal|url=https://acorjordan.org/ain-ghazal/|access-date=2020-11-29|website=Acor Jordan|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Rollefson|first=Gary|date=2016-08-18|title=The Aftermath of 'Ain Ghazal: What Happened after 7000 BC?|url=https://publications.acorjordan.org/2016/08/18/the-aftermath-of-ain-ghazal-what-happened-after-7000-bc/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-11-29|website=ACOR Jordan|language=en-US}}</ref> This is one of the most significant sites in Jordan, having yielded some of the [['Ain Ghazal Statues|oldest known human figures]]. 

'''Abu Hamid'''

Initially discovered during a 1975 survey of the Jordan Valley, the site of Abu Hamid was thereafter excavated under the partial direction Kafafi during the 1980s, in collaboration with colleagues from the Department of Antiquities, French Center of Scientific Affairs, and [[French Ministry of Foreign Affairs]], with funding provided by the [[National Geographic Society]].<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Dollfus|first1=G.|last2=Kafafi|first2=Z.|date=1986|title=Preliminary Results of the First Season of the Joint Jordano-French Project at Abu Hamid|url=http://publication.doa.gov.jo/Publications/ViewChapterPublic/2572|journal=Annual of the Department of Antiquities of Jordan|volume=30|pages=353–369|via=DOA Publication Archive}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Dollfus|first1=G.|last2=Kafafi|first2=Z.|date=1987|title=Tell Abu Hamid|url=http://publication.doa.gov.jo/Publications/ViewChapterPublic/956|journal=Studies in the History and Archaeology of Jordan|volume=03|pages=231–235|via=DOA Publication Archive}}</ref> The first field season at Abu Hamid took place in 1986. 

'''[[Deir Alla]]'''

Following initial Dutch-run excavations at the site, a partnership was formed between the [[Leiden University|University of Leiden]] and the Jordanian Department of Antiquities in 1986. This was succeeded by formal involvement of Yarmouk University, as represented first by Prof. Moawiyah Ibrahim and then by Prof.  Kafafi.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=كفافي|first=زيدان|date=2010|title=قالب لصناعة الحلي والمجوهرات من تل دير علا|url=http://publication.doa.gov.jo/Publications/ViewChapterPublic/2354|journal=Annual of the Department of Antiquities of Jordan|language=Arabic|volume=54|pages=13–17|via=DOA Publication Archive}}</ref> He has presented and published on the site internationally throughout the past three decades and previously served as co-director of the project from 1996–2010.<ref name=":0" />

'''Mugheir'''

With sponsorship from [[Tubingen University]], Kafafi contributed to excavations at Mugheir, Jordan in the mid-1980s. 

'''Wadi Az-Zarqa/Dulayl'''

Kafafi served as co-director of the archaeological project known as [[Wadi Az-Zarqa/Dulayl]], located near the [[Zarqa River]] and Tall al-Birah. The project is a joint effort of Yarmouk (Kafafi) and [[Sapienza University of Rome|University of Rome La Sapienza]].<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Palumbo|first1=G.|last2=Kafafi|first2=Z.|display-authors=etal |date=1997|title=The Wadi az-Zarqa'/Wadi ad-Dulayl Archaeological Project: Report on the 1996 Fieldwork Season|url=http://publication.doa.gov.jo/Publications/ViewChapterPublic/1095|journal=Annuals of the Department of Antiquities of Jordan|volume=41|pages=9–26|via=DOA Publication Archive}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last=Multiple authors|date=2001|title=The Wadi az-Zarqa'/Wadi ad Dulayl Archaeological Project: Report on the 1997 and 1999 Fieldwork Seasons|url=http://publication.doa.gov.jo/Publications/ViewChapterPublic/1152|journal=Annual of the Department of Antiquities of Jordan|volume=45|pages=83–118|via=DOA Publication Archive}}</ref>

==== [[Tall Damiyah]] ====
Kafafi has been serving as co-director of an excavation at [[Tall Damiyah|Tell Damiyah]] in collaboration with Dutch archaeologists from the [[National Museum of Antiquities, Netherlands|National Museum of Antiquities]] in Leiden since 2014. Their focus is on the Iron Age period at the site.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kafafi|first=Lucas P. In addition, he has served on a number of editorial boards, including ''Asian Culture and History'', ''Mediterranean Archaeology and Archaeometry'', ''Jordan Journal for History and Archaeology'', and ''[[Palestine Exploration Quarterly]]''.
