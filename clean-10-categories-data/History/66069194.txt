'''Nile Green''' is Professor of History and the current holder of the Ibn Khaldun Endowed Chair in World History at the [[University of California, Los Angeles]] (UCLA).<ref name="History.ucla.edu">{{cite web |title=Nile Green |url=https://www.history.ucla.edu/faculty/nile-green |website=History.ucla.edu |access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref><ref name="Nile Green">{{cite web |title=Nile Green |url=https://www.gf.org/fellows/all-fellows/nile-green/ |website=John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation |access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref> He specializes in Islamic history of Asia, the Middle East, Africa and Europe, including that of the wider [[Persianate society|Persianate]] world.<ref name="History.ucla.edu"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Nile Green |url=https://www.international.ucla.edu/cisa/person/751 |website=UCLA |access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref> He has authored seven monographs and over seventy articles and has edited seven books.<ref name="Nile Green"/> He was a founding director of UCLA's Program on [[Central Asia]], in addition to various boards, including the ''[[International Journal of Middle East Studies]]''.<ref name="Nile Green"/> According to his profile page, Green currently functions as a member of the South Asia Council of the Association of Asian Studies, the Executive Committee of the American Institute of Afghanistan Studies, in addition to being a member of the editorial boards of ''[[Iranian Studies (journal)|Iranian Studies]]'', ''Iran-Nameh'', ''Afghanistan'', the ''Journal of South Asian Intellectual History'' and the ''South Asia Across the Disciplines'' book series.<ref name="History.ucla.edu"/>
