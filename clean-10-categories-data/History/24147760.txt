The World Monuments Watch is a flagship advocacy program of the [[New York City|New York]]-based private [[non-profit organization]] [[World Monuments Fund]] (WMF) that is dedicated to [[Historic preservation|preserving]] the [[cultural heritage|historic, artistic, and architectural heritage]] around the world.<ref name=press_release>{{cite web | title = World Monuments Fund Announces 2006 World Monuments Watch List Of 100 Most Endangered Sites | publisher = World Monuments Fund | author = Holly Evarts | date = 21 June 2005 | url = http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_release.pdf|accessdate=3 September 2009|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080516051456/http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_release.pdf |archivedate = May 16, 2008|url-status=dead}} </ref>

==Selection process==
Every two years, it publishes a select list known as the Watch List of 100 Most Endangered Sites that is in urgent need of preservation funding and protection. The sites are nominated by governments, conservation professionals, site caretakers, [[non-government organization]]s (NGOs), concerned individuals, and others working in the field.<ref>{{cite web | title = World Monuments Fund Announces 2008 World Monuments Watch List Of 100 Most Endangered Sites | author = Holly Evarts | date = 6 June 2007 | publisher = World Monuments Fund | url = http://www.wmf.org/pdf/Watch_2008_release.pdf|accessdate=3 September 2009|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20090521092007/http://www.wmf.org/pdf/Watch_2008_release.pdf |archivedate = May 21, 2009|url-status=dead}} </ref> An independent panel of international experts then select 100 candidates from these entries to be part of the Watch List, based on the significance of the sites, the urgency of the threat, and the viability of both advocacy and conservation solutions.<ref name=press_release /><ref>{{cite web | title = 2006 Watch List Fact Sheet | publisher = World Monuments Fund | author = Holly Evarts | date = June 2005 | url = http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_%20facts.pdf|accessdate=4 September 2009|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080516052056/http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_%20facts.pdf |archivedate = May 16, 2008|url-status=dead}} </ref>  For the succeeding two-year period until a new Watch List is published, these 100 sites can avail grants and funds from the WMF, as well as from other foundations, private donors, and corporations by capitalizing on the publicity and attention gained from the inclusion on the Watch List.<ref name=press_release />

==2006 Watch List==
The 2006 World Monuments Watch List of 100 Most Endangered Sites was launched on June 21, 2005 by WMF President Bonnie Burnham.<ref name=press_release /><ref>{{cite web | title = World Monuments Watch list of 100 Most Endangered Sites | publisher = World Monuments Fund | url = http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_100.pdf |accessdate=3 September 2009|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080516051359/http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_100.pdf |archivedate = May 16, 2008|url-status=dead}} </ref><ref name=highlights>{{cite web | title = 2006 Watch List Highlights | author = Holly Evarts | date = June 2005 | publisher = World Monuments Fund | url = http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_highlights.pdf |accessdate=4 September 2009|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080516051330/http://www.wmf.org/pdf/WMW_2006_highlights.pdf |archivedate = May 16, 2008|url-status=dead}} </ref> It marked the first time that an entire country was placed on the Watch List. [[Iraq]], long considered as the "[[Fertile Crescent|cradle of human civilization]]" and within whose borders lie an estimated 10,000 [[archaeological site]]s, has been left vulnerable to widespread [[looting]], [[vandalism]], and other acts of violence in the wake of the [[2003 invasion of Iraq|2003 military invasion]].<ref name=press_release />
{{cquote|The World Monuments Watch provides a valuable barometer of the state of heritage preservation worldwide… The biennial Watch list tells us not only which sites are in peril, but also what kinds of threats—natural disaster, war, pollution, neglect, or other issues—are endangering the world's heritage.|200px||Bonnie Burnham, WMF president|launch of 2006 Watch List<ref name=press_release />}}
On October 6, 2005, nearly four months after the publication of the 2006 Watch List and more than a month after the significant devastation brought about by [[Hurricane Katrina]] on America's [[Gulf Coast]], the WMF, together with partners [[American Express]] Foundation and [[National Trust for Historic Preservation]], decided to place the ''Gulf Coast and [[New Orleans]] '' as the 101st endangered site on the 2006 Watch List.<ref>{{cite web | title = In Katrina's Wake: Restoring a Sense of Place, WMF Gallery | author = Holly Evarts | date = 2 August 2006 | publisher = World Monuments Fund | url = http://www.wmf.org/pdf/usa_GulfCoast_factsheet_2006.pdf |accessdate=3 September 2009|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080516051833/http://www.wmf.org/pdf/usa_GulfCoast_factsheet_2006.pdf |archivedate = May 16, 2008|url-status=dead}} </ref><ref>{{cite web | title = World Monuments Fund and National Trust for Historic Preservation with Support from American Express Team Up to Help Preserve Gulf Coast's Historic Landmarks Devastated by Hurricane Katrina | date = 12 November 2005 | publisher = World Monuments Fund | url = http://www.stoneindustrynews.com/pdf/2005-11.pdf |accessdate=4 September 2009}}</ref>
