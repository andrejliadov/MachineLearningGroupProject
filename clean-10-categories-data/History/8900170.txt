The year '''1955''' saw a number of significant events in '''[[radio broadcasting]]''' history.

==Events==
*2 May – Opening at [[Wrotham transmitting station|Wrotham]], [[Kent]], of the [[United Kingdom]]'s first [[Very high frequency|VHF]]/[[Frequency modulation|FM]] transmitters.
*September ([[Labor Day]] weekend) – First [[Sig Alert]] broadcast in California.<ref>{{cite news|last=Purdum|first=Todd S.|date=1997-05-18|title=How Los Angeles Traffic Snarls Spawned a Word|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1997/05/18/us/how-los-angeles-traffic-snarls-spawned-a-word.html|work=The New York Times|access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref>
*22 September – The character [[Grace Archer]] dies in the [[BBC]] radio serial ''[[The Archers]]''; a spoiler for the launch of [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]] in the UK on the same day.<ref>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/archers/timeline/timeline_50s.shtml BBC: The Archers, timeline.]</ref>
*20 October – [[Disc jockey]] [[Bill Randle]] of [[WERE]] ([[Cleveland]]) is the key presenter of a concert at [[Brooklyn High School (Ohio)]], featuring [[Pat Boone]] and [[Bill Haley & His Comets]] and opening with [[Elvis Presley]], not only Elvis's first performance north of the [[Mason–Dixon line]], but also his first filmed performance, for a documentary on Randle titled ''[[The Pied Piper of Cleveland]]''.
*17 November – Creation of the ''Communauté des radios publiques de langue française'' (CRPLF) - an association of the French-language public broadcasters of [[France]], [[Switzerland]], [[Belgium]], and [[Canada]], later to become, in 2002, the ''[[Public Francophone Radios|Radios francophones publiques]]'' (RFP).
*26 November – In [[Sweden]], the national public broadcaster Radiotjänst (modern-day [[Sveriges Radio]]) begins transmission, via [[FM broadcasting|FM]], of its [[Sveriges Radio P2|second radio channel]] in [[Stockholm]], [[Gothenburg]], [[Malmö]], and [[Örebro]].
