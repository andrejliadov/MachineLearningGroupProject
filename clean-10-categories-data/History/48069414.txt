The '''''Journal of Intelligence History''''' is a biannual [[peer-reviewed]] [[academic journal]] covering the [[history]] of [[espionage]]. It was established in 2001  and is the official journal of the International Intelligence History Association.<ref>Peter Gill Peter, and Mark Phythian. "What is intelligence studies?." ''The International Journal of Intelligence, Security, and Public Affairs'' 18.1 (2016): 5-19.</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://intelligence-history.org/?page_id=16 |title=Journal of Intelligence History|publisher=International Intelligence History Association|website= |accessdate=2015-10-05|date=2013-04-04}}</ref> The journal is published by [[Taylor & Francis]] and the [[editors-in-chief]] are Chris Moran ([[University of Warwick]]) and Shlomo Shpiro ([[Bar-Ilan University]]). Scholars have acknowledged its role.<ref>Damien Van Puyvelde,  and Sean Curtis, "‘Standing on the shoulders of giants’: diversity and scholarship in Intelligence Studies." ''Intelligence and National Security'' 31.7 (2016): 1040-1054.</ref><ref>Martin Rudner,  "Intelligence studies in higher education: Capacity-building to meet societal demand." ''International Journal of Intelligence and CounterIntelligence'' 22.1 (2009): 110-130.</ref>
