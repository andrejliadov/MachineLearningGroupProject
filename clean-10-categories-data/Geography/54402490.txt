The two '''Henkin brothers,''' [https://bessmertnybarak.ru/Khenkin_Evgeniy_Aleksandrovich/ Evgeny] (December 14, 1900 – January 3, 1938) and [https://www.moypolk.ru/saint-petersburg/soldiers/henkin-yakov-aleksandrovich Yakov](December 2, 1903 – December 13, 1941), were significant early 20th century amateur street and documentary photographers who primarily recorded the daily life of, respectively, [[Berlin, Germany]] and [[Leningrad]] (now [[St. Petersburg]]), Russia, in the late 1920s and 1930s, during the interwar years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.politico.eu/interactive/in-pictures-berlin-leningrad-1920s-henkin-brothers-photography/|title=In pictures: Slices of a life long lost|first=Denis A.|last=Maslov|date=Jan 14, 2020|website=POLITICO|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref> Their photographs, mostly never printed in their lifetimes and kept as negatives, were re-discovered by the descendants of [[Yakov Henkin]] in the late 2000s.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://hermitage-magazine.ru/articles/bratya-henkinyi-otkryitie-lyudi-leningrada-i-berlina-1920-1930-h-godov/|title=БРАТЬЯ ХЕНКИНЫ: Открытие. Люди Ленинграда и Берлина 1920-1930-х годов – Журнал Государственный Эрмитаж|website=hermitage-magazine.ru|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref> This collection, known as the Henkin Brothers Archive, consists of around 7000 frames of photography.

The two brothers lived in two major European cities - Evgeny in Berlin and Yakov in Leningrad - during a crucial period in history and shared many themes and compositions in their photography.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://blog.courtauld.ac.uk/documentingfashion/tag/berlin/|title=Berlin Archives|website=Documenting Fashion|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref>  This aspect of their heritage shows links between the two cities and countries right before the confrontation and destruction of [[World War II]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cinnamoncircle.com/blog/2017/07/14/a-unique-exhibition-at-hermitage-st-petersburg-shows-ties-between-leningrad-and-berlin/|title=A unique exhibition at Hermitage, St. Petersburg shows ties between Leningrad and Berlin – Cinnamon Circle|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref><ref>http://m.germania-online.diplo.de/Vertretung/russland-dz/ru/03-kultur/kunst/ausstellungen/henkin-brueder-in-ermitage.html</ref> The Archive is valued as a historical and cultural artifact, an important collection of vernacular photography, as well as an affecting testament to life in Europe in the interwar period.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.shopcecilia.com/blogs/news/hidden-treasures-the-photography-of-the-henkin-brothers|title=Hidden Treasures: The Photography of the Henkin Brothers|website=Cecilia|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dekoder.org/ru/article/bratya-henkiny-berlin-leningrad|title=Братья Хенкины|date=Nov 26, 2019|website=дekoder &#124; DEKODER &#124; Европейская журналистика и наука по-русски |accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://blog.tagesanzeiger.ch/zoom/index.php/120639/henkin-brothers/|title=Als sich die Weltgeschichte ins Bild schlich|date=Nov 22, 2019|website=Zoom Fotoblog|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sept.info/henkin-photographie|title=Dans le quotidien des années 30|date=Nov 25, 2019|website=Sept|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref>

==Biography==

Evgeny and Yakov Henkin were born in [[Rostov-on-Don]], in the European South of the [[Russian Empire]], in the family of Alexander Henkin and his wife Anna, née Erberg. Several years after the [[1917 October Revolution]], Evgeny moved to Germany and lived in Berlin from 1925 to 1936. In 1930, Yakov moved with his wife Frida and their only daughter Galina to Leningrad. The brothers' younger sister Sofia also settled in Leningrad in the 1920s. The brothers were part of the large and creative [[Henkin]] family, which included their uncles Vladimir<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%A5%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%BD,_%D0%92%D0%BB%D0%B0%D0%B4%D0%B8%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%80_%D0%AF%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%87|title=Хенкин, Владимир Яковлевич — Википедия|last=Харьков|first=Российская империя[1|last2=Москва|first2=СССР[1|website=ru.wikipedia.org|language=ru|access-date=2019-04-07|last3=империя|first3=Российская|last4=Ссср}}</ref> and Victor<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%A5%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%BD,_%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BA%D1%82%D0%BE%D1%80_%D0%AF%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%87|title=Хенкин, Виктор Яковлевич — Википедия|last=империя|first=Российская|last2=Ссср|website=ru.wikipedia.org|language=ru|access-date=2019-04-07}}</ref>{{Circular reference|date=November 2020}} Henkin, both prominent actors in the 1920s and 30s, and their cousin Kirill Henkin, in post-World War II years a writer and subsequently a journalist with [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://artrz.ru/menu/1805200013/1805352786.html|title=Искусство и архитектура русского зарубежья - ХЕНКИН Евгений Александрович|website=artrz.ru|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://artrz.ru/menu/1805200013/alpha_%D0%A5.html|title=Искусство и архитектура русского зарубежья - Фотографы|website=artrz.ru|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref>

Photography wasn't the profession of either brother. Yakov was an economist, while Evgeny was a musician, playing the [[theremin]], an early electronic instrument.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://paperpaper.ru/dw/henkiny|title=7000 кадров Ленинграда и Берлина 30-х годов|website=«Бумага»|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref> [http://henkinbrothers.com/material/]

In 1936, [[Evgeny Henkin]] returned to the Soviet Union. He was arrested by the Soviet secret police [[NKVD]] in late 1937, at the height of Stalinist purges. Accused of being a German spy, he was shot in early January 1938. He is buried at the [[Levashovo Memorial Cemetery]], the site of mass execution and burial of victims of political persecution of the Stalinist era.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sciencespo.fr/mass-violence-war-massacre-resistance/en/document/levashovo-cemetery-and-great-terror-leningrad-region|title=The Levashovo cemetery and the Great Terror in the Leningrad region {{!}} Sciences Po Mass Violence and Resistance - Research Network|date=2016-01-19|website=www.sciencespo.fr|language=en|access-date=2019-04-07}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9B%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B0%D1%88%D0%BE%D0%B2%D1%81%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%B5_%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BC%D0%BE%D1%80%D0%B8%D0%B0%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B5_%D0%BA%D0%BB%D0%B0%D0%B4%D0%B1%D0%B8%D1%89%D0%B5|title=Левашовское мемориальное кладбище — Википедия|website=ru.wikipedia.org|language=ru|access-date=2019-04-07}}</ref>{{Circular reference|date=November 2020}}

In 1941, Yakov enlisted as a volunteer in the weeks after Germany invaded the Soviet Union. He was mortally wounded on the Leningrad Front shortly thereafter. He is buried in a [[World War II]] memorial site at the Pundolovskoe cemetery of the Suoranda village on the outskirts of St. Petersburg.

==Henkin Brothers Archive Association (HBAA)==

In late 2016, the not-for-profit “[http://henkinbrothers.com Henkin Brothers Archive Association]” (HBAA) was created under Swiss law in [[Lausanne, Switzerland]]. The main objective of the HBAA is to bring the Henkin brothers' creative heritage to public and specialist attention and to disseminate information about the two photographers, their lives and times, and the indelible images they left behind. The HBAA seeks to preserve and conserve the negatives contained in the Archive, as well as to organize and to collaborate on exhibitions, publications, research projects, presentations, and other activities. The HBAA owns the Archive and holds the copyright to all its images (© Henkin Brothers Archive Association (HBAA)).  The Association includes direct descendants of the Henkin brothers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://henkinbrothers.com/|title=Henkin Brothers Archive|accessdate=Jun 26, 2020}}</ref>
