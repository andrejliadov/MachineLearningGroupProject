'''Ronald Francis (Ron) Abler''' (born May 30, 1939) is an American geographer at [[Pennsylvania State University]] and an Elected Fellow of the [[American Association for the Advancement of Science]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.aaas.org/fellow/abler-ronald |title=Abler, Ronald |publisher=aaas.org |accessdate=April 21, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.geog.psu.edu/people/abler-ronald |title=Abler, Ronald |publisher=psu.edu |accessdate=April 21, 2017 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170723183129/http://www.geog.psu.edu/people/abler-ronald |archivedate=July 23, 2017 }}</ref> 

Abler obtained his PhD in 1968 from the [[University of Minnesota]], where he had started his academic career as faculty member in 1967.<ref name="BW 2010">Barney Warf, ''Encyclopedia of Geography,'' 2010. p. 1</ref>

Abler served as president of the [[American Association of Geographers]] in 1985-86, and as president of the [[International Geographical Union]] from 2008 to 2012.<ref name="BW 2010"/> He is a recipient of the [[Samuel Finley Breese Morse Medal]] and the [[Victoria Medal (geography)|Victoria Medal]]. 

Abler is a key early figure in the development of [[geography of media and communication]].
