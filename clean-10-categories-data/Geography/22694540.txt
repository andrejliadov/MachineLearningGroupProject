'''''The Exploration of the Colorado River and Its Canyons''''' by [[John Wesley Powell]] is a classic<ref>According to [[Wallace Stegner]] "Nearly everyone who runs any part of the canyons now .. either carries this story of Powell's in his duffelbag or has it read or recited to him around the fire while the tamed Colorado slips past." Source: Penguin edition, "Introduction", 2003, {{ISBN|0-14-243752-2}}</ref><ref>National Geographic ranked it 4th on its list. {{cite web |url=http://www.nationalgeographic.com/adventure/0404/adventure_books.html |title=Extreme Classics: The 100 Greatest Adventure Books of All Time |work=[[National Geographic Adventure (magazine)|National Geographic Adventure]] |date=May 2004 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040902081030/http://www.nationalgeographic.com/adventure/0404/adventure_books.html |archive-date=2004-09-02 |url-status=dead }} {{webarchive|format=addlpages|url1=https://web.archive.org/web/20040722032824/http://www.nationalgeographic.com:80/adventure/0404/adventure_books_1-19.html |date1=2004-07-22|url2=https://web.archive.org/web/20040831001431/http://www.nationalgeographic.com:80/adventure/0404/adventure_books_20-39.html|date2=2004-08-31|url3=https://web.archive.org/web/20040831001359/http://www.nationalgeographic.com:80/adventure/0404/adventure_books_40-59.html|date3=2004-08-31|url4=https://web.archive.org/web/20040830100950/http://www.nationalgeographic.com:80/adventure/0404/adventure_books_60-79.html|date4=2004-08-31|url5=https://web.archive.org/web/20040831001341/http://www.nationalgeographic.com:80/adventure/0404/adventure_books_80-100.html|date5=2004-08-31}}</ref> of American [[exploration literature]]. It is about the [[Powell Geographic Expedition of 1869]] which was the first trip down the Colorado River by boat, including the first trip through the [[Grand Canyon]]. 

Powell's first written accounts of his exploration appeared in the January, February and March 1875 editions of ''[[Scribnerâ€™s Monthly]]'' as "The Canons of the Colorado".<ref>{{cite journal |title=The Canons of the Colorado |journal=[[Scribner's Monthly]] |author1=[[John Wesley Powell]] |date=1875 |volume=9 |url=https://babel.hathitrust.org/cgi/pt?id=coo.31924079633172;view=1up;seq=3 }} Pages 293, 394 and 523.</ref> The [[Smithsonian]] published it in book form in 1875 under title ''Report of the Exploration of the Colorado River of the West and Its Tributaries. Explored in 1869, 1870, 1871, and 1872, under the direction of the Secretary of the Smithsonian Institution''. It was revised and published in 1895 as ''The Exploration of the Colorado River and Its Canyons''.<ref name=penguinintro/>

It includes hundreds of wood engravings based on photographs by [[E.O. Beaman]], [[James Fennemore]] and [[John Karl Hillers]], and drawings by [[Thomas Moran]].<ref name=penguinintro>Penguin edition, [[Wallace Stegner]], "Introduction", 2003, {{ISBN|0-14-243752-2}}</ref>
