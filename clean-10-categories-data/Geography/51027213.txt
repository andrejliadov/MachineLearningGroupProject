'''[[Aokigahara]]''' is frequently featured in [[popular culture]] due to its historic association with "[[yūrei]]" or ghosts of the dead in [[Japanese mythology]] and is a notoriously common [[List of suicide sites|suicide site]] (wherein 54 took place in 2010).<ref name="japantimes2011">{{cite news | last=Gilhooly | first=Rob | url= http://search.japantimes.co.jp/cgi-bin/fl20110626x1.html |title= Inside Japan's 'Suicide Forest' | newspaper =[[Japan Times]] |date=26 June 2011| page= 7}}</ref> The forest is said to be the world’s second most popular suicide spot, the first being the [[Suicides at the Golden Gate Bridge|Golden Gate Bridge]].<ref name="ERIC">{{Cite web|url=http://eric.ed.gov/ERICWebPortal/custom/portlets/recordDetails/detailmini.jsp?_nfpb=true&_&ERICExtSearch_SearchValue_0=EJ383602&ERICExtSearch_SearchType_0=no&accno=EJ383602|title=EJ383602 - Aokigahara-jukai: Suicide and Amnesia in Mt. Fuji's Black Forest|last=Takahashi|first=Yoshitomo|year=1988|publisher=[[Education Resources Information Center|Education Resources Information Center (ERIC)]]|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref>

The rate of suicide has led officials to place a sign at the forest's entry, written in Japanese, urging suicidal visitors to seek help and not take their own lives. Annual body searches have been conducted by police, volunteers, and attendant journalists since 1970.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/text/nn20120509f3.html |title=Kyodo News: 'Suicide forest' helps skew Yamanashi's statistics|work=[[Japan Times]]|date=  May 9, 2012|page= 3}}</ref>

This page lists Aokigahara in popular culture from both within Japan and abroad. It is not an exhaustive list of the many games, films, manga and other cultural products that mention Aokigahara.

== Anime and manga ==
* In the anime series ''[[Tokyo Ghoul]]'', the ghoul-only Cafe Anteiku uses bodies from Aokigahara to feed ghouls to avoid actively killing humans for nourishment.<ref>{{cite web|title=A Review of Tokyo Ghoul|url=https://ahotaku39.wordpress.com/2014/11/09/a-review-of-tokyo-ghoul/|website=Ahotaku39|accessdate=2 January 2018}}</ref>

== Films ==

==Other media==
*[[YouTube]]r [[Logan Paul]] and his companions [[Logan Paul#Suicide video controversy|vlogged an excursion into the forest with the intention to camp there overnight on December 31, 2017]]. While in the forest, the group encountered a dead body hanging from a tree, the film of which was included in their video with the body's face blurred out. The video was met with considerable backlash from individuals outside Paul's fan base, stating that the video showed poor taste in regards to the deceased and was disrespectful to Japanese culture. Paul later took down the video and issued an apology.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-42538495|title=Logan Paul: Outrage over YouTuber's dead body video|publisher=[[BBC News]]|date=2018-01-01|accessdate=2018-01-01}}</ref>
