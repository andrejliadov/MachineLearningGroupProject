'''Degenerative disease''' is the result of a continuous process based on degenerative cell changes, affecting [[biological tissue|tissues]] or [[Organ (anatomy)|organs]], which will increasingly deteriorate over time.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.docdoc.com.sg/info/condition/degenerative-disease/|title=What is Degenerative Disease|website=docdoc.com.sg|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180917213617/https://www.docdoc.com.sg/info/condition/degenerative-disease/|archive-date=2018-09-17|url-status=live|access-date=2018-09-17}}</ref>

In '''neurodegenerative diseases''' cells of the central nervous system stop working or die via [[neurodegeneration]]. An example of this is [[Alzheimer's disease]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cancer.gov/publications/dictionaries/cancer-terms/def/neurodegenerative-disorder|title=neurodegenerative disorder|date=2011-02-02|website=National Cancer Institute|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180423115832/https://www.cancer.gov/publications/dictionaries/cancer-terms/def/neurodegenerative-disorder|archive-date=2018-04-23|url-status=live|access-date=2018-09-17}}</ref> The other two common groups of degenerative diseases are those that affect [[circulatory system]] (e.g. [[coronary artery disease]]) and [[neoplastic]] diseases (e.g. [[cancers]]).<ref name=":3" />

Many degenerative diseases exist and some are related to [[aging]]. Normal bodily wear or lifestyle choices (such as [[exercise]] or eating habits) may worsen degenerative diseases, but this depends on the disease.<ref name=":3" /> Sometimes the main or partial cause behind such diseases is [[gene]]tic.<ref name=":4">{{Cite news|url=https://medlineplus.gov/degenerativenervediseases.html|title=Neurodegenerative Diseases|access-date=2018-09-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180728020542/https://medlineplus.gov/degenerativenervediseases.html|archive-date=2018-07-28|url-status=live}}</ref> Thus some are clearly [[Genetic disorder|hereditary]] like [[Huntington's disease]].<ref name=":5">{{Cite journal|last=Nopoulos|first=PC|date=2016|title=Huntington disease: a single-gene degenerative disorder of the striatum|journal=Dialogues in Clinical Neuroscience|volume=18|issue=1|pages=91–98|issn=1294-8322|pmc=4826775|pmid=27069383}}</ref> Sometimes the cause is [[viruses]], poisons or other chemicals. The cause may also be unknown.<ref name=":4" />

Some degenerative diseases can be cured, but not always. It might still be possible to [[Palliative care|alleviate the symptoms]].<ref name=":3" />
