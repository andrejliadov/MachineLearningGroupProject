{{short description|Effects of COVID-19 viral outbreak on public transport}}
[[File:Разметка для соблюдения дистанции в метро 04.jpg|thumb|Red stickers placed every other seat in a [[Moscow Metro]] train encourage [[social distancing]]]]

The [[COVID-19 pandemic]] has had a significant impact on [[public transport]].

There have been claims that the use of public transport has also led to the spread of COVID-19<ref name="Chen2020"/> but there has been little evidence that public transit is a substantial disease vector.<ref name="JoselowSciAm2020">{{cite web|last1=Joselow|first1=Maxine|title=There Is Little Evidence That Mass Transit Poses a Risk of Coronavirus Outbreaks|url=https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/there-is-little-evidence-that-mass-transit-poses-a-risk-of-coronavirus-outbreaks/|publisher=[[Scientific American]]|access-date=2020-10-10|language=en|date=2020-07-28}}</ref>

=== China ===
On 23 January 2020, the entire [[Wuhan Metro]] network was shut down, along with all other public transport in the city, including national railway and air travel, to halt the spread of the virus.<ref name=":12">{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/goatsandsoda/2020/01/22/798602296/public-transport-in-wuhan-suspended-due-to-coronavirus-concerns|title=Public Transport In Wuhan Suspended Due To Coronavirus Concerns|publisher=[[NPR]]|language=en|access-date=2020-01-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-china-51215348|title=Virus-hit Chinese city shuts public transport|date=23 January 2020|work=[[BBC News]]|access-date=2020-01-23|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-china-51215348|title=Coronavirus: Wuhan shuts public transport over outbreak|date=January 23, 2020|work=[[BBC News]]|access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Lovelace Jr.|first=Berkeley|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2020/01/22/wuhan-suspends-public-transportation-to-combat-coronavirus-outbreak-state-media-says.html|title=Public transportation suspended in China city to combat coronavirus outbreak|date=January 22, 2020|publisher=[[CNBC]]|access-date=April 14, 2020|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200313052050/https://www.cnbc.com/2020/01/22/wuhan-suspends-public-transportation-to-combat-coronavirus-outbreak-state-media-says.html|archive-date=March 13, 2020}}</ref>

On January 24, 2020, the day after [[2020 Hubei lockdowns|the lockdown was declared in the city of Wuhan]], the [[Beijing Subway]] began testing body temperature of passengers at the entry points of 55 subway stations including the three main railway stations and the capital airport.<ref>[http://bj.people.com.cn/n2/2020/0126/c82840-33744920.html "北京道路省际客运今起全部停运" ''北京青年报''] 26 January 2020.</ref> Temperature checks were expanded to all subway stations by January 27.<ref>(Chinese) [http://bj.people.com.cn/n2/2020/0127/c14540-33746575.html "北京地铁将全路网推行测温 体温超37.3℃就需隔离" ''人民网''] 27 January 2020.</ref> To further control the spread of the virus, certain [[Line 6 (Beijing Subway)|Line 6]] trains were outfitted with smart surveillance cameras that can detect passengers who are not wearing masks.<ref>[http://www.ecns.cn/news/2020-04-09/detail-ifzviiqq6923848.shtml Beijing's 'intelligent' metro line able to identify unmasked passengers]. [[Xinhua News Agency]] via [[China News Service]], 9 April 2020.</ref>

On 28 March 2020, six lines (Line 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7) resumed operation, after a two-month lockdown. Line 8, Line 11, and the Yangluo Line remain out of service.<ref name="resume">{{Cite web|url=http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2020-03/25/c_138914547.htm|title=Wuhan buses hit the road after two-month lockdown|date=25 March 2020|publisher=[[Xinhua News Agency]]|language=en|access-date=2020-03-25}}</ref>

On {{date|2020-4-8}}, Phase 1 of Line 8 resumed operation. Services on Phase 3 of Line 8, as well as Line 11 and the Yangluo Line, remain suspended.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chinanews.com/gn/2020/04-07/9150068.shtml|title=武汉：4月8日起恢复出租车运营 适时恢复网约车运营|author=Huang Lei；Wang Yang|date=7 April 2020|website=Hubei Daily}}</ref>

=== United Kingdom ===
Bus, plane and train services were reduced in the United Kingdom.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/health-51736185|title=Coronavirus: What's the risk of taking buses or trains?|date=March 26, 2020|work=BBC News|access-date=April 14, 2020|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200405204018/https://www.bbc.com/news/health-51736185|archive-date=April 5, 2020}}</ref>  [[Public transport]] use has declined by around 90% in [[London]] since the national [[coronavirus lockdown]] was implemented. London's mayor [[Sadiq Khan]] has made all bus travel free from 20 April and told passengers to only board by the middle doors in a bid to protect bus drivers, after 20 of them died from Coronavirus.<ref name="The World Economic Forum"/> Bus travel fares began to be reinstated from 23 May, after a conditional bailout of Transport for London by the Department for Transport.<ref>{{cite web |title=Contactless and Oyster cards reinstated on more than 1,000 buses |url=https://tfl.gov.uk/info-for/media/press-releases/2020/may/contactless-and-oyster-cards-reinstated-on-more-than-1-000-buses |website=tfl.gov.uk |publisher=Transport for London |access-date=23 July 2020}}</ref>  From 15 June it became compulsory to wear a face covering on public transport in England.<ref>https://www.gov.uk/government/news/face-coverings-to-become-mandatory-on-public-transport</ref>

Throughout the pandemic, people had been told not to use public transport for non-essential travel, to help stop the spread of COVID-19 and allow for social distancing in carriages, for those who were unable to work from home. This advice was rescinded on 17 July, in advance of further easing of lockdown measures, including the removal of work from home advice.<ref>{{cite web |title=Prime Minister's statement on coronavirus (COVID-19): 17 July 2020 |url=https://www.gov.uk/government/speeches/pm-statement-on-coronavirus-17-july-2020 |website=Gov.uk |publisher=Prime Minister's Office, 10 Downing Street |access-date=23 July 2020}}</ref>

Based on data released by [[Moovit]], the United Kingdom saw a significant decrease in use of public transport during April 2020. This included an 80% percent decrease in London and South East, 79% in Yorkshire, 71% in West Midlands, 80% in the South West, 76% in the North West and 78% in Scotland.<ref>{{cite web |title=Impact of Coronavirus (COVID-19) on Public Transit usage|url=https://moovitapp.com/insights/en/Moovit_Insights_Public_Transit_Index-countries |website=Moovit Insights |access-date=3 December 2020}}</ref>

====Oregon====

Oregon's largest transit agency, [[TriMet]], started to require facial coverings for both passengers and operators. Oregon has gone so far as to allow people to order facial coverings. Oregon's governor Kate Brown issued an executive order 20–12, ruling on public transit and requirements for safe travel in Oregon.<ref name="oregon.gov">https://www.oregon.gov/odot/RPTD/Pages/Coronavirus.aspx</ref><ref>https://govstatus.egov.com/or-covid-19</ref> With these requirements, TriMet is required to clean vehicles every four hours, and recommended to sanitize high-touch surfaces, provide facial coverings and sanitizer, if possible. There are many exceptions to the rule that include people with disabilities and other health issues, and those concerned about profiling because of wearing a mask.<ref name="oregon.gov"/> Other public transit operators in Oregon, such as [[Amtrak]], have reduced services as well.

== Hazard controls ==
{{Further|Workplace hazard controls for COVID-19}}
The U.S. [[Centers for Disease Control and Prevention]] has issued guidance documents on COVID-19 prevention measures for public transportation.<ref>{{Cite web|last=CDC|date=2020-02-11|title=Protect Yourself When Using Transportation|url=https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/daily-life-coping/using-transportation.html|access-date=2020-08-10|website=Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=CDC|date=2020-04-30|title=What Rideshare, Taxi, Limo, and other Passenger Drivers-for-Hire Need to Know about COVID-19|url=https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/community/organizations/rideshare-drivers-for-hire.html|access-date=2020-08-10|website=Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=CDC|date=2020-04-30|title=COVID-19 Employer Information for Bus Transit Operators|url=https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/community/organizations/bus-transit-operator.html|access-date=2020-08-10|website=Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|language=en-us}}</ref> Drivers and passengers should wear face coverings, avoid frequently touched surfaces, and sit at least six feet apart if possible. To protect bus transit operators, passengers can enter and exit through the rear door. Frequently touched surfaces should be routinely cleaned. Signage and other visual cues such as decals and tape can alert passengers on appropriate COVID-19 precautions and seat designations. Travelers are encouraged to carry hand sanitizer and disinfectant wipes with them. It is best to travel during non-peak hours when it is easier to be physically distanced from other passengers.
