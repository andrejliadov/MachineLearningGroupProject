The '''Medi Teddy''' is a [[teddy bear]] sleeve that hides an [[IV bag]] from the patient.<ref>[https://boston.cbslocal.com/2019/06/14/medi-teddy-iv-bag-cover-ella-casano/|title=Young Girl Afraid Of IVs Invents 'Medi Teddy' To Disguise Them], published 2019-06-14, at CBS Boston;  retrieved 2020-01-14]</ref> It was developed in 2019 by 12 year old [[Idiopathic thrombocytopenia purpura|Idiopathic Thrombocytopenia Purpura]] (ITP) patient Ella Casano<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/medi-teddy-iv-bear-ella-casano-hospital-idiopathic-thrombocytopenia-purpura/|title=Once afraid of IVs, girl invents teddy bear pouches to hide them|website=www.cbsnews.com|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-14}}</ref> whose treatment included an IV transfusion every six to eight weeks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mymodernmet.com/medi-teddy-ella-casano/|title=12-Year-Old Girl with Rare Chronic Illness Invents Cuddly Teddy Bear to Hide IV Bag|date=2019-06-19|website=My Modern Met|language=en|access-date=2020-01-14}}</ref> With the product, the child only sees a teddy bear; on the other side (made of mesh),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodmorningamerica.com/family/story/girl-rare-disease-shatters-goal-medi-teddy-iv-63783788|title=Little girl with rare disease shatters funding goal for Medi Teddy IV bag cover|last=America|first=Good Morning|website=Good Morning America|language=en|access-date=2020-01-14}}</ref> medical staff get to monitor blood IV fluid movements.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://goodmorningamerica.com/family/story/girl-rare-disease-shatters-goal-medi-teddy-iv-63783788|title=Little girl with rare disease shatters goal for Medi Teddy IV bag cover|website=ABC News|language=en|access-date=2020-01-14}}</ref> [[Today (American TV program)|TODAY]] honored her as a Groundbreaker for International Day of the Girl.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.today.com/style/day-of-the-girl-2019|title=Groundbreakers: Celebrating 18 girls under 18 who are changing the world|website=TODAY.com|language=en|access-date=2020-01-14}}</ref> The Medi Teddy set out to arrange $5,000 on a [[GoFundMe]] page but within less than a week raised over $20,000.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newsweek.com/iv-cover-medi-teddy-bear-1444632|title=A 12-year-old girl with a rare disease has invented 'Medi Teddy' IV covers that look like teddy bears|last=EDT|first=Daniel Avery On 6/18/19 at 2:04 PM|date=2019-06-18|website=Newsweek|language=en|access-date=2020-01-14}}</ref> The bears are given to children in need at no cost.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/06/13/health/iv-teddy-bear-medi-teddy-trnd/index.html|title=A young girl was afraid of IVs. So she invented a teddy bear to disguise them|author1=Amanda Jackson |author2=Gianluca Mezzofiore|website=CNN|access-date=2020-01-14}}</ref> Ella described to her mother Meg Casano what the design should look like and her mother drew it for her.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.boredpanda.com/teddy-bear-hides-iv-bags-ella-casano/|title=12-Year-Old Girl With Rare Disorder Creates Teddy Bears That Hide IV Bags For Other Young Patients|website=Bored Panda|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-14}}</ref>
