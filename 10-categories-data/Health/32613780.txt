{{short description|Disease with two distinct phases}}
A '''biphasic disease''' is a [[disease]] which has two distinct phases or components. In clinically biphasic diseases, the phases are generally [[Chronology|chronologically]] separated. In [[Histopathology|histopathologically]] biphasic tumors (also called '''biplastic tumors'''), there is [[neoplasia|neoplastic]] tissue which contains two different cellular elements.<ref name="Santosh2014">{{cite journal|last1=Santosh|first1=Arvind Babu Rajendra|title=Histogenetic Concepts, Terminology and Categorization of Biphasic Tumours of the Oral and Maxillofacial Region|journal=Journal of Clinical and Diagnostic Research|volume=8|issue=2|pages=266–70|year=2014|issn=2249782X|doi=10.7860/JCDR/2014/7506.4078|pmid=24701553|pmc=3972583}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://patholines.org/Fibroadenoma|title=Fibroadenoma|website=Patholines.org}} This page was last edited on 4 November 2019</ref>
