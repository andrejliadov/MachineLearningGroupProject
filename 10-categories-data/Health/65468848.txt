'''J. Gayle Beck''' is a licensed [[Clinical psychology|clinical psychologist]] who specializes in trauma stress disorders<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/745766005|title=The Oxford handbook of traumatic stress disorders|date=2012|publisher=Oxford University Press|others=Beck, J. Gayle., Sloan, Denise M.|isbn=978-0-19-539906-6|location=Oxford|oclc=745766005}}</ref> and [[Anxiety disorder|anxiety disorders]].<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/612728659|title=Interpersonal processes in the anxiety disorders : implications for understanding psychopathology and treatment|date=2010|publisher=American Psychological Association|others=Beck, J. Gayle.|isbn=978-1-4338-0746-6|edition=1st|location=Washington, D.C.|oclc=612728659}}</ref> She is the Lillian and Morrie Moss Chair of Excellence in the Department of Psychology at the [[University of Memphis]].

Beck was President of the Society of Clinical Psychology, [[American Psychological Association]] (APA) Division 12, in 2012<ref>{{Cite web|title=Past Presidents of Society of Clinical Psychology {{!}} Society of Clinical Psychology|url=https://div12.org/past-presidents-of-society-of-clinical-psychology/|access-date=2020-11-30|website=div12.org}}</ref> and President of the Association for Behavioral and Cognitive Therapies in 2004-2005.<ref>{{Cite web|title=ABCT {{!}} Association for Behavioral and Cognitive Therapies {{!}} Cognitive Behavioral Therapy|url=http://www.abct.org/About/?m=mAbout&fa=PastPresidents|access-date=2020-11-30|website=www.abct.org}}</ref> She served as a member of the Board of the [[American Psychological Foundation]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Psychology Giving {{!}} June 2019 New APF board members|url=https://www.apa.org/apf/newsletter/2019/06/board-members|access-date=2020-11-30|website=www.apa.org}}</ref> and as Editor in Chief of [[Clinical Psychology: Science and Practice]].<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Gayle Beck|first=J.|date=2014|title=From the Editor: Continuity and Change|url=http://doi.wiley.com/10.1111/cpsp.12061|journal=Clinical Psychology: Science and Practice|language=en|volume=21|issue=1|pages=1–2|doi=10.1111/cpsp.12061}}</ref>

== Awards ==
Beck was elected a Fellow of the APA Division 12 (Society of Clinical Psychology), APA Division 38 (Society for Health Psychology), and APA Division 56 (Trauma Psychology).<ref>{{Cite web|title=Awards & Honors {{!}} Division of Trauma Psychology|url=https://www.apatraumadivision.org/85/awards-honors.html|access-date=2020-11-30|website=www.apatraumadivision.org}}</ref>  She received the APA Florence Halpern Award for Distinguished Professional Contributions to Clinical Psychology from the Society of Clinical Psychology in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=SCP 2016 Award Winners {{!}} Society of Clinical Psychology|url=https://div12.org/scp-2016-award-winners/|access-date=2020-10-13|website=div12.org}}</ref> 

== Biography ==
Beck received an A.B. in Psychology and Education from [[Brown University]] in 1979. She subsequently completed a Ph.D. in Clinical Psychology [[University at Albany, SUNY|State University of New York at Albany]] in 1984,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=J. Gayle Beck, Ph,D.|url=https://www.memphis.edu/psychology/people/faculty/beck.php|access-date=2020-10-13|website=www.memphis.edu|language=en}}</ref> with supported from a Presidential fellowship from the State University of New York (1979-1982).{{cn|date=December 2020}} Her titled "The effect of performance demand and attentional focus on sexual responding in functional and dysfunctional men,"<ref>{{Cite book|title=The effect of performance demand and attentional focus on sexual responding in functional and dysfunctional men|location=Doctoral dissertation, State University of New York at Albany, Department of Psychology.}}</ref> was supervised by [[David H. Barlow|David H. Barlow.]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=PsychTree - J. Gayle Beck Family Tree|url=https://academictree.org/psych/tree.php?pid=64560&fontsize=1&pnodecount=4&cnodecount=2|access-date=2020-11-30|website=academictree.org}}</ref>

Beck held faculty positions at the [[University of Houston]] (1984-1993) and the [[University at Buffalo|State University of New York, Buffalo]] (1993-2007) before moving to the University of Memphis in 2008.<ref>{{Cite web|title=J. Gayle Beck CV (December 2014)|url=https://www.memphis.edu/psychology/people/cvs/beck_cv_2014_dec.pdf}}</ref> Beck's clinical research on [[post-traumatic stress disorder]] (PTSD) has been funded by grants from the National Institute for Mental Health.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Group Therapy Helps Car-Accident Survivors Deal with Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder|url=http://ed.buffalo.edu/information/about/university/buffalo.host.html/content/shared/university/news/news-center-releases/2005/04/7220.detail.html|access-date=2020-11-30|website=ed.buffalo.edu|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Clapp|first1=Joshua D.|last2=Gayle Beck|first2=J.|date=2009|title=Understanding the relationship between PTSD and social support: The role of negative network orientation|url=https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S000579670800274X|journal=Behaviour Research and Therapy|language=en|volume=47|issue=3|pages=237–244|doi=10.1016/j.brat.2008.12.006|pmc=2656396|pmid=19162260}}</ref> She led a a multi-site [[Randomized control trial|randomized controlled trial]] evaluating the effectiveness of group [[Cognitive behavioral therapy|Cognitive Behavioral Therapy]] in the treatment of PTSD among veterans.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Sloan|first1=Denise M.|last2=Unger|first2=William|last3=Lee|first3=Daniel J.|last4=Beck|first4=J. Gayle|date=2018|title=A Randomized Controlled Trial of Group Cognitive Behavioral Treatment for Veterans Diagnosed With Chronic Posttraumatic Stress Disorder|url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/jts.22338|journal=Journal of Traumatic Stress|language=en|volume=31|issue=6|pages=886–898|doi=10.1002/jts.22338|issn=0894-9867|pmc=6295345|pmid=30499227}}</ref> Beck is Director of the Athena Project at the University of Memphis, which provides free services, resources, and support for victims of domestic abuse.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Media and News|url=https://www.memphis.edu/athena/media/index.php|access-date=2020-12-15|website=www.memphis.edu|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-08-29|title=I'm J. Gayle Beck, Professor of Psychology, and This is How I Work|url=https://tihiw.wordpress.com/2016/08/29/gayle-beck-how-i-work/|access-date=2020-11-30|website=This is How I Work - in Science|language=en}}</ref>
