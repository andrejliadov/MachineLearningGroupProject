'''Billy's Bootcamp''' is an exercise program developed by [[Billy Blanks]]. It created a pop culture phenomenon in Japan in 2007, much like [[Tae Bo]] did in the U.S. earlier, selling more than 200,000 copies in May of the same year alone.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chunichi.co.jp/s/chuspo/article/2007061801000393.html |title=ビリーの減量ＤＶＤ大人気　メタボ対策の通販商品 |accessdate=2007-06-18 |work=[[Kyodo News]] |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070620112040/http://www.chunichi.co.jp/s/chuspo/article/2007061801000393.html |archivedate=2007-06-20 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Billy Blanks visited Japan on June 21, 2007 to promote Bootcamp.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nikkansports.com/entertainment/f-et-tp0-20070620-215707.html |title=ビリーズブートキャンプがやってきた！ |accessdate=2007-06-20 |work=[[Nikkan Sports]] |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070626060707/http://www.nikkansports.com/entertainment/f-et-tp0-20070620-215707.html |archivedate=2007-06-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Upon arrival Blanks was greeted by 200 Japanese fans who affectionately call him "Taicho" ({{lang-ja|隊長}}, chief) at [[Narita International Airport]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sponichi.co.jp/osaka/ente/200706/21/ente207143.html |title=ビリー隊長来日、成田空港でファン200人歓迎 |accessdate=2007-06-21 |work=[[:ja:スポーツニッポン|Sports Nippon]] |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070625184854/http://www.sponichi.co.jp/osaka/ente/200706/21/ente207143.html |archivedate=2007-06-25 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Blanks appeared on many Japanese TV shows during his ten-day stay in Japan such as [[SMAP|SMAP×SMAP]] where [[Shingo Katori]], a member of popular Japanese boy band [[SMAP]], impersonated Blanks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hochi.yomiuri.co.jp/entertainment/news/20070618-OHT1T00050.htm |title=ビリー、２０日来日…ダイエットＤＶＤ「ブートキャンプ」ブレーク |accessdate=2007-06-18 |work=[[Sports Hochi]] |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070620124456/http://hochi.yomiuri.co.jp/entertainment/news/20070618-OHT1T00050.htm |archivedate=2007-06-20 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
