The [[COVID-19 pandemic in the Republic of Ireland]] has had a significant impact on the conduct of sports affecting both competitive sports leagues and tournaments and recreational sports.

==Background==
The [[Gaelic games]] of [[Gaelic football|football]], [[hurling]], [[camogie]], and [[ladies' Gaelic football|ladies' football]]—mostly played in [[Ireland]]—saw all competitions suspended from 12 March 2020. The [[2020 National Hurling League|National Hurling League]], [[2020 National Football League (Ireland)|National Football League]], [[2020 National Camogie League|National Camogie League]] and [[2020 Ladies' National Football League|Ladies' National Football League]] were suspended, with competitions not intended to resume until 29 March at the earliest.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.gaa.ie/news-archive/news/gaa-camogie-association-and-the-lgfa-suspend-all-activity/ |title=GAA, Camogie Association, and the LGFA suspend all activity |website=www.gaa.ie}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.balls.ie/gaa/gaa-camogie-association-gaa-suspend-activities-march-29th-427885 |title=GAA, Camogie Association And LGFA Suspend All Activity Until March 29th|first=P. J. |last=Browne |website=Balls.ie}}</ref> On 17 March, the [[Gaelic Athletic Association]] (GAA) confirmed that [[2020 Connacht Senior Football Championship|the opening fixture]] of the [[2020 All-Ireland Senior Football Championship]]—due to have taken place at [[Gaelic Park]] in [[Riverdale, Bronx|The Bronx]] on 3 May—had been postponed.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.irishexaminer.com/breakingnews/sport/gaa/galway-and-new-york-clash-first-major-casualty-of-pandemic-988773.html |title=Galway and New York clash first major casualty of pandemic |work=[[Irish Examiner]] |first=Eoghan |last=Cormican |date=18 March 2020 |accessdate=22 March 2020}}</ref>

On 26 June, the [[Gaelic Athletic Association]] (GAA) announced that all GAA competitions would commence in October 2020, with the hurling and football competitions to be an all-knockout competition. As from 12 September, [[London]] withdrew from the championship and from 3 November, [[Sligo]] withdrew from the [[Connacht]] football championship.

==Impact by sport==
Countless sporting events were affected by the arrival of the [[COVID-19|virus]] on 29 February 2020. Following the [[Taoiseach]]'s [[Leo Varadkar#2020|12 March announcement from Washington]], the [[Gaelic Athletic Association]] (GAA), [[Irish Rugby Football Union]] (IRFU) and [[Football Association of Ireland]] (FAI) all immediately announced the two-week suspension of games.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rte.ie/sport/gaa/2020/0312/1121866-gaa-cancel-all-games-euro-2020-under-review/|title=GAA, FAI & IRFU suspend all games, Euro 2020 up in air|publisher=[[RTÉ Sport]]|language=en|date=12 March 2020|access-date=12 March 2020}}</ref>
