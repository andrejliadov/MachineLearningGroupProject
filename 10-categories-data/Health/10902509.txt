'''''Ability''''' is an American bimonthly magazine founded by [[Chet Cooper]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abilitymagazine.com/ChetBio.htm |title=Biography For Chet Cooper |publisher=Ability magazine|accessdate=August 2, 2015}}</ref> in 1990 and launched as the first newsstand magazine{{citation needed|date=August 2019}} focused on issues of [[health]] and [[disability]]. ''Ability'' is ranked in the Top 50 Magazines in the World<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.onlinenewspapers.com/Top50/Top50-Current-Magazines.htm |title=Top 50 Magazines Viewed on OnlineNewspapers Last Month |publisher=Onlinenewspapers|accessdate=August 2, 2015}}</ref> — and is the magazine covering Health, Disability and Human Potential. It is distributed by [[Time Warner]] and has offices in [[Santa Ana, California|Santa Ana]] and [[Costa Mesa, California]].

== Content ==
''Ability'' covers the latest on health, environmental protection, assistive technology, employment, sports, travel, universal design, mental wellness. Magazine covers issues include the [[Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990|Americans with Disabilities Act]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abilitymagazine.com/20th-ADA.html |title=20th Anniversary of the Americans with Disabilities Act |publisher=Abilitymagazine.com |date=July 26, 1990|accessdate=August 2, 2015}}</ref> [[civil rights]] advancement, employment opportunities for people with disabilities, and [[human interest]] stories. Cover interviews consist of movie and TV celebrities, business leaders, sports figures, presidents, first ladies and more. Each cover story of ''Ability'' showcases a prominent public figure who either has a disability or who has a connection to a disability-related cause.

''Ability'' is dedicated to promoting accessibility in both ''content'' and ''form'' and is the first to embed VOICEYE (a high density matrix barcode system) on its editorial pages to hear print through smartphones and tablets—giving good access to people with low vision, blindness or reading challenges in 58 languages.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/84739025 |title=1st Magazine to VOICEYE Code Editorial on Vimeo |publisher=Vimeo.com |date=January 21, 2014|accessdate=August 2, 2015}}</ref>

== Representation ==
''Ability'' frequently participates in international conferences, public awareness events and opportunities to affect policy surrounding issues of disability; and has partnered with the [[United Nations]] in efforts to raise awareness.<ref>https://www.un.org/disabilities/documents/COP/COP7/cosp7_side_events.doc</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abilitymagazine.com/UN-ABILITY.html |title=United Nations Partners with Ability to Raise Awareness |publisher=Abilitymagazine.com |date= |accessdate=August 2, 2015}}</ref>

== Affiliations ==
''Ability'' is part of a diverse network of organizations geared toward advancement and inclusion of people with disabilities. These projects include:
* Ability Jobs: The first web-based [[job board]] and résumé bank for individuals with disabilities.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abilityjobs.com/ |title=Job Access - Ability Jobs - Affirmative Disability Action - Employment for People with Disabilities |publisher=Ability Jobs |date=July 16, 2015|accessdate=August 2, 2015}}</ref>
* Ability Awareness: A [[non-profit organization]] with a focus on elevating attitudes towards people with disabilities.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://abilitycorps.com/ |title=Engaging Volunteers with Disabilities in Community Service |publisher=Ability Corps |date= |accessdate=August 2, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150801120243/http://abilitycorps.com/ |archive-date=August 1, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
* Ability Corps: A major initiative fostering integration of [[Volunteering|volunteer]]s with disabilities into [[community service]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://abilitycorps.com/ |title=Engaging Volunteers with Disabilities in Community Service |publisher=Ability Corps |accessdate=August 2, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150801120243/http://abilitycorps.com/ |archive-date=August 1, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

''Ability'' created an alliance with the China Press for People with Disabilities — providing the rights to publish each other's selected stories, artworks and articles, in an effort to bring both countries to experience human interest stories that otherwise would never be known.{{citation needed|date=August 2015}}

''Ability'' has partnered with the [[Arc of the United States]] "to raise awareness about disability issues and the resources available to people with disabilities and their families."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thearc.org/get-involved/membership/individual/ability |title=Ability Magazine |publisher=The Arc |date= |accessdate=2015-08-02}}</ref>

''Ability'' is listed as an awareness resource by the [[United Cerebral Palsy]] organization<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ucp.org/resources/resource-guide/ |title=Resource Guide |publisher=UCP|accessdate=August 2, 2015}}</ref> and has provided media for the Disability Rights Legal Center.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.disabilityrightslegalcenter.org/ability-magazine |title=Ability Magazine |publisher=Disability Rights Legal Center |date=July 24, 2012 |accessdate=August 2, 2015 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20150221171555/http://www.disabilityrightslegalcenter.org/ability-magazine |archive-date=February 21, 2015 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>
