{{short description|Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on the military}}
{{Globalize|1=article|2=United States|date=April 2020}}
{{COVID-19 pandemic sidebar|expanded=issues}}
The [[COVID-19 pandemic]] has had a significant impact on the military. Many military training and exercises have been postponed or cancelled.<ref name="icds">{{cite web|url=https://icds.ee/the-impact-of-covid-19-on-military-exercises-in-europe/|title=The impact of COVID-19 on military exercises in Europe|first=Martin|last=Hurt|website=icds.ee|date=2020-03-17}}</ref>

==Responses==
On 27 February, South Korea and the United States (US) cancelled joint military exercises scheduled for March 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usnews.com/news/world-report/articles/2020-02-27/us-south-korea-cancel-military-exercises-amid-coronavirus-spike|title=U.S., South Korea Cancel Military Exercises Amid Coronavirus Spike|first=Paul D.|last=Shinkman|website=[[U.S. News & World Report]]|date=2020-02-27}}</ref>

On 11 March, the [[Norwegian Armed Forces]] cancelled the Cold Response 20 exercise planned to involve NATO and allied personnel.<ref name="icds"/>

On 16 March, the [[National Defense Industrial Association]] in the US cancelled the 2020 Special Operations Forces Industry Conference scheduled for May 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.militarytimes.com/news/your-army/2020/03/16/cancellation-updates-multinational-african-lion-exercise-canceled-amid-coronavirus-outbreak/|title=Cancellation updates: SOFIC cancelled|first=|last=|website=[[Military Times]]|date=2020-03-16}}</ref>

On 27 March, the United States cancelled large-scale exercises involving thousands of troops in the Philippines that had been scheduled for May 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/world/united-states/us-cancels-military-drills-with-philippines-over-coronavirus|title=US cancels military drills with Philippines over coronavirus|first=|last=|website=[[The Straits Times]]|date=2020-03-27}}</ref>

On 6 April, the [[United States Forces Japan]] declared a Public Health Emergency on the Kanto Plain installations.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=USForcesJapan|number=1247025278875889666|date=2020-04-06|title=USFJ commander declares Public Health Emergency for Kanto Plains installations.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-japan-usa/u-s-forces-japan-declares-health-emergency-for-bases-in-kanto-region-idUSKBN21O0F8|title=U.S. Forces Japan declares health emergency for bases in Kanto region|first=Chris|last=Gallagher|website=[[Reuters]]|date=2020-04-06}}</ref>

In  June 2020, the United States Navy comes up with guidance to combat the coronavirus and deploy safely using the smallest effort possible.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Information|first=From the Office of the Navy Chief of|title=Navy issues COVID-19 standardized operational guidance|url=https://www.dcmilitary.com/tester/news/local/navy-issues-covid-19-standardized-operational-guidance/article_5191ec29-50bd-5f57-bd1b-f00324aae165.html|access-date=2020-06-04|website=DC Military|language=en}}</ref>

===Withdrawal of US troops from Iraq===
{{Main|Withdrawal of U.S. troops from Iraq (2020)}}
On March 20, 2020, [[Combined Joint Task Force – Operation Inherent Resolve|CJTF-OIR]] confirmed that certain troops would be withdrawing from Iraq due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Iraq|coronavirus pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thehill.com/policy/defense/488618-us-led-coalition-in-iraq-drawing-down-over-coronavirus-concerns|title=US-led coalition in Iraq drawing down over coronavirus concerns|website=[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]|date=March 20, 2020|author=Rebecca Kheel|accessdate=April 6, 2020}}</ref>

==Infection==
===Military bases===

==== India ====
;INS Angre
On 2020.04.18, it was announced that 21 sailors staying at [[INS Angre]], a naval base in [[Mumbai]], had tested positive.<ref name="angre.ndtv">https://www.ndtv.com/india-news/15-to-20-indian-navy-sailors-in-mumbai-test-positive-for-coronavirus-quarantined-at-naval-hospital-2213852</ref>  Most of the cases were asymptomatic, and all of the cases had been traced to a sailor who tested positive on 2020.04.07.<ref name="angre.ndtv" />  The [[Indian Navy|Navy]] emphasized that no sailors serving on a ship or submarine had been infected.<ref name="angre.ndtv" />

====United Kingdom====
;Akrotiri and Dhekelia
{{Main|COVID-19 pandemic in Akrotiri and Dhekelia}}
On 15 March, the first two cases in Akrotiri and Dhekelia were confirmed.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://in-cyprus.com/coronavirus-british-bases-say-two-raf-personnel-test-positive/|title=Coronavirus: British Bases announce two confirmed cases|website=in-cyprus|access-date=19 September 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200318185500/https://in-cyprus.com/coronavirus-british-bases-say-two-raf-personnel-test-positive/|archive-date=18 March 2020|url-status=dead}}</ref>

====United States====
{{Main|COVID-19 pandemic in the United States#Military}}
[[File:2020 USAF Academy Cadet Graduation social distancing.jpg|thumb|[[United States Air Force Academy]] cadets partake in [[social distancing]] during their graduation ceremony, April 18, 2020]]
;Guantanamo Bay Naval Base
{{Main|COVID-19 pandemic in the Guantanamo Bay Naval Base}}
On 24 March, the first case in Guantanamo Bay Naval Base was confirmed.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.navy.mil/submit/display.asp?story_id=112454|title=Naval Station Guantanamo Bay Announces Positive COVID-19 Case|publisher=Naval Station Guantanamo Bay, Cuba Public Affairs|website=www.navy.mil|language=en|access-date=2020-04-03}}</ref>

;United States Forces Korea
On 26 February, the first case was confirmed to have spread to the [[Camp Humphreys]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.joins.com/article/23716158|title=부대 내 접촉 감염자에 비상걸린 軍…주한미군 1명 추가 확진|first=Geun-pyung|last=Lee|website=[[JoongAng Ilbo]]|date=2020-02-26}}</ref>

As of 22 April, a total of 22 SARS-CoV-2 cases were laboratory confirmed at [[United States Forces Korea]] bases: 10 at Camp Humpreys, 8 at Daegu and Gyeongsangbuk Province bases ([[Camp Carroll, South Korea|Camp Carroll]], [[Camp Henry]] and [[Camp Walker]]), 3 at the [[Osan Air Base]], and 1 at the [[Camp Casey, South Korea|Camp Casey]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.sbs.co.kr/news/endPage.do?news_id=N1005743501&plink=ORI&cooper=NAVER|title=평택 미군기지서 코로나19 확진…주한미군 22번째|first=Hyun-jong|last=Ha|website=[[Seoul Broadcasting System]]|date=2020-04-12}}</ref>

===Naval ships===
{{Main|COVID-19 pandemic on naval ships}}
The COVID-19 pandemic spread to a number of naval ships, with the nature of such ships, including working with others in small enclosed areas and the lack of private quarters for the vast majority of crew, contributing to the rapid spread of the disease, even more so than on [[COVID-19 pandemic on cruise ships|cruise ships]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.propublica.org/article/after-discovering-a-sailor-with-coronavirus-the-us-navy-crowded-dozens-into-one-room |title=After Discovering a Sailor With Coronavirus, the U.S. Navy Crowded Dozens Into One Room |work=Propublica.org |last=Faturechi |first=Robert |last2=Rose |first2=Megan |last3=Miller |first3=T. Christan |name-list-style=amp |date=16 March 2020 |access-date=8 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sfchronicle.com/bayarea/article/Exclusive-Captain-of-aircraft-carrier-with-15167883.php |title=Exclusive: Captain of aircraft carrier with growing coronavirus outbreak pleads for help from Navy |last=Gafni |first=Matthias |last2=Garofoli |first2=Joe |name-list-style=amp |work=San Francisco Chronicle |date=31 March 2020 |access-date=8 April 2020}}</ref>
