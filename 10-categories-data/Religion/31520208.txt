'''Aiy''' ([[Sakha language|Sakha]]: ''Айыы'', [[Russian language|Russian]]: ''Айыы'') are benevolent spirits in [[Yakuts|Yakut]] [[mythology]]. They are creators of the world and of the fabulous land of the Yakut [[Olonkho]]s. In Sakha belief Urung Ai Toyon, creator of the world, is the greatest of them.<ref>Türk Mitolojisi Ansiklopedik Sözlük, Celal Beydili, Yurt Yayınevi {{in lang|tr}}</ref> According to mythology, the Aiy live in the Upper World. It is believed that the Aiy will not accept blood [[sacrifice]] and therefore they are presented "victims" of [[vegetable]] and [[dairy product]]s.

Aiy symbols are required for the ''Ysyakh'' (spring feast) and regulated by the government of the [[Sakha Republic]].<ref>ПРАВИТЕЛЬСТВО РЕСПУБЛИКИ САХА (ЯКУТИЯ).
