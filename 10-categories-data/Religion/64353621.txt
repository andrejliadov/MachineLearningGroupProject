The [[COVID-19 pandemic]] affected the 2020 [[Hajj]] (pilgrimage), which is the fifth pillar of the [[Five Pillars of Islam]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pillars of Islam - Oxford Islamic Studies Online|url=http://www.oxfordislamicstudies.com/article/opr/t125/e1859|access-date=2020-06-22|website=www.oxfordislamicstudies.com}}</ref> where millions of Muslims from around the world visit [[Mecca]] and [[Medina]] every year during Hajj season for a week. Over 2,400,000 pilgrims attended Hajj in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Abstract of Hajj Statistics 1440 H|url=https://www.stats.gov.sa/sites/default/files/haj_40_en.pdf}}</ref> In March 2020, the minister of Hajj and Umrah encouraged those planning to visit the holy sites of Mecca and Medina later in the year to wait before making any bookings.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-04|title=If Saudi Arabia is forced to put the Hajj on hold, it will not be without precedent|url=https://arab.news/4xx98|access-date=2020-06-23|website=Arab News|language=en}}</ref> Due to the highly contagious nature of COVID-19 in crowded places, flight restrictions in many countries, and the unavailability of a vaccine against the virus the ministry of Hajj and Umrah took such serious measures.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-04-01|title=Saudi Arabia asks Muslims to delay Hajj bookings|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-52118803|access-date=2020-06-23}}</ref> However, in June 2020, the ministry of Hajj and Umrah announced that people from all nationalities who reside in [[Saudi Arabia]] are allowed to perform Hajj with a limited number of pilgrims, to ensure the safety and to prevent the transmission of COVID-19.<ref>{{Cite web|author1=Hamdi Alkhshali |author2=Larry Register|title=Saudi Arabia says Hajj pilgrimage is on -- with limited numbers|url=https://www.cnn.com/2020/06/22/middleeast/hajj-pilgrimage-saudi-arabia-coronavirus-intl/index.html|access-date=2020-06-23|website=CNN}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Ministry of Hajj and Umrah: Hajj 1441H Is Decided to Take Place This Year with Limited Number of Pilgrims from All Nationalities Residing in Saudi Arabia The official Saudi Press Agency|url=https://www.spa.gov.sa/viewfullstory.php?lang=en&newsid=2100951|access-date=2020-06-23|website=www.spa.gov.sa}}</ref>

== History ==

REPORT|url=https://www.who.int/docs/default-source/coronaviruse/situation-reports/20200121-sitrep-1-2019-ncov.pdf?sfvrsn=20a99c10_4}}</ref> The outbreak of the virus was first noted in [[Wuhan]], [[China]] in early December 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=WHO urges countries to ensure the continuity of malaria services in the context of the COVID-19 Due to COVID-19, Hajj in 2020 was performed by relatively few people. Only those who live in Saudi Arabia, were between the ages of 20 and 50, and who were not displaying symptoms of COVID-19 could participate. Participants had to first apply online prior to being selected to participate, and preference was given to those who have not participated in the past.<ref>{{cite news|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|title=Watch again: Pilgrims arrive in Saudi Arabia for first day of Hajj, as COVID-19 reshapes experience|url=https://www.euronews.com/2020/07/29/pilgrims-arrive-in-saudi-arabia-for-the-first-day-of-hajj-as-covid-19-reshapes-experience|work=euronews|location=Saudi Arabia|date= 2020-07-29|access-date=2020-09-19}}</ref>

== Historical cancellations of Hajj ==
Hajj (pilgrimage) has been cancelled 40 times through the history of Islam due to plague, political disagreements, and battles.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-14|title=Hajj has been cancelled 40 times in history|url=https://www.siasat.com/hajj-has-been-cancelled-40-times-history-1874010/|access-date=2020-06-28|website=The Siasat Daily|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Chitwood|first=Ken|title=Hajj cancellation due to coronavirus is not the first time plague has disrupted this Muslim pilgrimage|url=http://theconversation.com/hajj-cancellation-due-to-coronavirus-is-not-the-first-time-plague-has-disrupted-this-muslim-pilgrimage-135900|access-date=2020-06-28|website=The Conversation|language=en}}</ref> In 1523-1533 (930-940 AD), Hajj was canceled due to the [[Qarmatians|Qarmatian]] attack which led to the murder of 30,000 pilgrims and the looting of the Black Stone.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-04|title=The Hajj Has Been Canceled 40 Times In The History|url=https://theislamicinformation.com/hajj-has-been-canceled-40-times-history/|access-date=2020-06-28|website=The Islamic Information|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Team|first=T. M. V.|date=2020-04-02|title=A Brief Look At When Hajj Was Cancelled In History - TMV|url=https://themuslimvibe.com/muslim-current-affairs-news/a-brief-look-at-when-hajj-was-cancelled-in-history|access-date=2020-06-28|language=en-GB}}</ref> In 1831, Hajj was suspended after a plague that was brought by the pilgrims coming from India, which is claimed to have ended the lives of more than half of the pilgrims in Mecca.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Team|first=T. M. V.|date=2020-04-02|title=A Brief Look At When Hajj Was Cancelled In History - TMV|url=https://themuslimvibe.com/muslim-current-affairs-news/a-brief-look-at-when-hajj-was-cancelled-in-history|access-date=2020-06-28|language=en-GB}}</ref> The first outbreak of [[Cholera]] spread in Mecca happened in 1846, killing more than 15,000 and causing a plague lasting until 1850 in the city. The second outbreak of Cholera happened in 1858, leading to pilgrims being quarantined inside Hajj camps in [[Egypt]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chitwood|first=Ken|title=Hajj cancellation due to coronavirus is not the first time plague has disrupted this Muslim pilgrimage|url=http://theconversation.com/hajj-cancellation-due-to-coronavirus-is-not-the-first-time-plague-has-disrupted-this-muslim-pilgrimage-135900|access-date=2020-06-28|website=The Conversation|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Hajj It has stopped in history more than 40 times .. Will it stop again because of Corona virus?|url=https://www.arabiaweather.com/content/الحج-توقف-عبر-التاريخ-أكثر-من-40-مرة-فهل-يتوقف-مرة-أخرى-بسبب-فايروس-كورونا؟|access-date=2020-06-28|website=www.arabiaweather.com|language=en}}</ref>

== International responses to Hajj 2020 ==

On 15 May 2020, the [[Islam in Singapore|Singapore]] Muslims Council (MUIS) advised to skip Hajj in this year due to COVID-19 pandemic.

On 2 June 2020, the [[Islam in Indonesia|Indonesian]] Minister of Religious Affairs declared cancelling the Hajj of 2020 before any groups of Indonesian pilgrims head to Mecca.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Indonesia Cancels Hajj Pilgrimage, Citing Risks Of Travel During Pandemic|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/06/02/867976382/indonesia-cancels-hajj-pilgrimage-citing-risks-of-travel-during-pandemic|access-date=2020-06-28|website=NPR.org|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Post|first=The Jakarta|title='It's impossible': Indonesia holds firm on haj cancellation|url=https://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2020/06/09/its-impossible-indonesia-holds-firm-on-haj-cancellation.html|access-date=2020-06-28|website=The Jakarta Post|language=en}}</ref>

On 6 June 2020, the [[Islam in India|Indian]] Hajj Committee announced it would provide refunds to all the pilgrims due to the inability of attending Hajj in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hajj cancelled for Indian pilgrims for this year; to receive full refund through direct transfer: Govt|url=https://www.timesnownews.com/india/article/hajj-cancelled-for-indian-pilgrims-for-this-year-to-receive-full-refund-through-direct-transfer-govt/610569|access-date=2020-06-28|website=www.timesnownews.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|others=PTI|date=2020-06-06|title=Coronavirus {{!}} Haj unlikely this year for Indian Muslims, say sources|language=en-IN|work=The Hindu|url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/coronavirus-haj-unlikely-this-year-for-indian-muslims-say-sources/article31767668.ece|access-date=2020-06-28|issn=0971-751X}}</ref>

On 11 June 2020, [[Islam in Malaysia|Malaysia]] announced it was barring pilgrims from attending Hajj in 2020 out of concerns over the danger of COVID-19, and the high spreadability of the virus in crowded places.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-06-11|title=Malaysia bars citizens from haj pilgrimage on coronavirus fears|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-malaysia-haj-idUSKBN23I1KG|access-date=2020-06-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Malaysia Joins Nations Pulling Out of Hajj Pilgrimage Over COVID-19 Concerns|url=https://www.benarnews.org/english/news/malaysian/hajj-off-06112020155802.html|access-date=2020-06-28|website=BenarNews|language=en}}</ref>

=== Precautions ===
The Saudi [[Ministry of Health (Saudi Arabia)|Ministry of Health]] reported taking serious measures during the Hajj 2020, pilgrims who were not effected by the [[Coronavirus disease 2019|virus]] were required to quarantine themselves at hotel rooms or at their homes if they live close enough to Mecca for a week prior to the arrival to perform Hajj.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-25|title=Coronavirus: Saudi Arabia welcomes Hajj pilgrims under strict COVID-19 measures|url=https://english.alarabiya.net/en/coronavirus/2020/07/25/Coronavirus-Saudi-Arabia-welcomes-Hajj-pilgrims-under-strict-COVID-19-measures.html|access-date=2020-08-09|website=Al Arabiya English|language=en}}</ref> Pilgrims were also asked to maintain a safe social distance of two meters inside the holy mosque, pilgrims were separated into groups of 20 individuals per group who are accompanied by a guide through their whole Hajj performance.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-31|title=Coronavirus: WHO chief hails Saudi Arabia’s COVID-19 measures during Hajj|url=https://english.alarabiya.net/en/coronavirus/2020/07/31/Coronavirus-WHO-chief-hails-Saudi-Arabia-s-COVID-19-measures-during-Hajj.html|access-date=2020-08-09|website=Al Arabiya English|language=en}}</ref>
