'''''God zij met ons''''' ({{lang-en|God be with us}}) is a proverb phrase written on [[Kingdom of the Netherlands|Dutch]] [[coin]]s. This caption was formerly written on the edge of the [[Dutch guilder|guilder]], [[rijksdaalder]] (two and a half guilder), five guilders, ten guilders and twenty-five guilders and today on [[Dutch euro coins|2-euro Dutch coins]].<ref>[http://eur-lex.europa.eu/LexUriServ/LexUriServ.do?uri=OJ:C:2004:051E:0238:0238:EN:PDF Written question P-2322/03 by VÃ©ronique De Keyser (PSE) to the Council], ''[[Official Journal of the European Union]]'', 2004. Retrieved 26 September 2012.</ref>

This shortened text expands into its Latin origin "[[Si Deus nobiscum quis contra nos]]" (If God is with us, who shall be against us?). This biblical motto was used in the [[Eighty Years' War]] and taken over by the [[Dutch Republic]] for use on its coins.{{cn|date=April 2020}}
