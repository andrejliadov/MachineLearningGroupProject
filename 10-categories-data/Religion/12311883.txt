[[File:BerkleyCenter Logo.jpg|right|thumb|300px|Logo of the Berkley Center for Religion, Peace, and World Affairs at Georgetown University]]
The '''Berkley Center for Religion, Peace, and World Affairs''' is an academic research center at [[Georgetown University]] in [[Washington, DC]] dedicated to the interdisciplinary study of [[religion]], [[ethics]], and [[politics]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://berkleycenter.georgetown.edu/about |title=About Us &#124; Berkley Center for Religion, Peace, and World Affairs &#124; Georgetown University |publisher=Berkleycenter.georgetown.edu |access-date=2012-02-04}}</ref> The center was founded in 2006<ref>Carol Zimmerman for ''The Boston Pilot''. April 11, 2016. [http://www.thebostonpilot.com/article.asp?ID=176212 Religion must be key part of foreign policy, says Madeleine Albright]</ref> under a gift from [[W. R. Berkley|William R. Berkley]], a member of Georgetown's Board of Directors.<ref>International Insurance Society [http://www.internationalinsurance.org/events/global-insurance-forum/presenters/william-berkley/ William Berkley biographical sketch] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924035526/http://www.internationalinsurance.org/events/global-insurance-forum/presenters/william-berkley/ |date=2015-09-24 }} Page accessed August 12, 2015</ref><ref name=IGGprofile>[https://globalengage.org/about/partners Berkley Center profile at IGG] Page accessed August 12, 2015</ref> The center's founding director was  Thomas Banchoff.<ref name=IGGprofile/>
