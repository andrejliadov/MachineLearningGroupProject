=== Atheists who became an unspecified/uncertain form of theism or deism ===
* [[Eben Alexander (author)|Eben Alexander]] - [[neurosurgeon]], [[author]], teaching physician. Author of ''Proof of Heaven: A Neurosurgeon's Journey into the Afterlife''.{{Citation needed|date=September 2018}}
*[[Plutarco Elías Calles]] former president of Mexico who was an atheist but became a [[spiritism|spiritist]] later in life.{{Citation needed|date=September 2018}}
*[[Gabrielle Carey]] - Australian co-author of ''[[Puberty Blues (novel)|Puberty Blues]]'' who was raised atheist, but converted to Catholicism and then explored other theologies.<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/austory/transcripts/s685468.htm ABC Australia].</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.theaustralian.news.com.au/story/0,20867,20717523-5003900,00.html |title=The Australian |access-date=2007-10-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090919171625/http://www.theaustralian.news.com.au/story/0,20867,20717523-5003900,00.html |archive-date=2009-09-19 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
*[[Antony Flew]] - philosopher and respected atheist thinker of the 20th century, became a [[deism|deist]].<ref>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/religion/programmes/belief/scripts/antony_flew.html BBC Interview] Quote: "What I was converted to was the existence of an Aristotelian God, and Aristotle's God had no interest in human affairs at all."</ref>
*[[Moses Hess]] - [[Socialism|Socialist]] philosopher and [[Young Hegelians|Left Hegelian]] who first influenced [[Karl Marx]] in his criticism of religion, but who later tried to combine the [[pantheism]] of [[Baruch Spinoza]] with [[Hegelianism]].<ref>[http://webcache.googleusercontent.com/search?q=cache:Nw2ymWM1ZxkJ:assets.cambridge.org/97805213/87569/frontmatter/9780521387569_frontmatter.pdf+%22moses+hess%22&hl=en&ct=clnk&cd=7&gl=us Moses Hess: The Holy History of Mankind and other writings]</ref>
*[[William Luther Pierce]] - American [[White supremacy|white supremacist]] and [[Activism|political activist]] who created [[Cosmotheism]].<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=g-LXDQAAQBAJ&q=william+pierce+cosmotheism&pg=PA35|title=Dark Ideas: How Neo-Nazi and Violent Jihadi Ideologues Shaped Modern Terrorism|last=Morris|first=Travis|date=2016-12-14|publisher=Lexington Books|isbn=9780739191057|language=en}}</ref>
*[[Anne Rice]] - best-selling [[United States|American]] author of Gothic and religious-themed books.<ref>[http://www.christianitytoday.com/ct/2005/december/11.50.html Christianity Today]</ref> She returned to Catholicism, and remained as such for many years, but has since announced that although she still believes in God and in Christ she no longer considers herself a Christian.<ref>[http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/news/la-et-quick-20100730,0,7660849.story LA Times]</ref><ref>[http://weblogs.baltimoresun.com/news/faith/2010/07/anne_rice_christian.html Baltimore Sun]: My faith in Christ is central to my life. My conversion from a pessimistic atheist lost in a world I didn't understand, to an optimistic believer in a universe created and sustained by a loving God is crucial to me. But following Christ does not mean following His followers. Christ is infinitely more important than Christianity and always will be, no matter what Christianity is, has been or might become.</ref>
*[[J. Neil Schulman]] - [[Libertarian science fiction]] writer who states he met, or experienced, God and that this ended his atheism. The first such experience would have occurred when he was 35. That stated he remains skeptical of "the church" and does not belong to any religion.<ref>[http://www.pulpless.com/jneil/libertarianprophet.html Interview with Schulman]: "After a thorough analysis of my previous life's experiences, and later experiences that lent validation, I concluded that the reality was that what had happened to me were really encounters with God -- therefore proving God's existence to me."</ref>
*[[Dave Sim]] - comics writer and [[Antifeminism|anti-feminist]]. He converted to, or created, his own mixture of [[Abrahamic religion]]s.<ref>[http://www.villagevoice.com/books/0413,hendrix,52244,10.html Village Voice] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080613213102/http://www.villagevoice.com/books/0413%2Chendrix%2C52244%2C10.html |date=2008-06-13 }}</ref><ref>[http://www.suncomics.com/cerebus%20fans/cerebite.htm Sun Comics]</ref>
*[[Ted Turner]] - media mogul who stated that he is no longer an atheist or an agnostic. He has not embraced any specific religion.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/news/health/2008-04-01-turner-churches_N.htm|title=Ted Turner apologizes, joins churches' $200M malaria fight|date=2008-04-01|work=USA Today|publisher=Associated Press|accessdate=2009-10-27}}</ref>
*[[Mark Zuckerberg]] - [[Facebook]] founder and philanthropist.<ref name="wapo-12302016">{{cite news|author1=Julie Zauzmer|title=Mark Zuckerberg says he's no longer an atheist, believes 'religion is very important'|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/acts-of-faith/wp/2016/12/30/mark-zuckerberg-says-hes-no-longer-an-atheist-believes-religion-is-very-important/|access-date=December 30, 2016 | work=The Washington Post|date=December 30, 2016}}</ref>
