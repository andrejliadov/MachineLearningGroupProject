'''Muong ethnic religion'''  is an [[ethnic religion]], among the [[Muong people|Muong]], in [[Vietnam]]. It is [[polytheism|polytheistic]], sharing many supernatural beings with [[Vietnamese folk religion]].<ref>https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=LnIEBfOGWjsC&pg=PA547&dq=muong+religion&hl=de&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiJuIyluqjtAhUJuRoKHX4oAVkQ6AEwAHoECAAQAg#v=onepage&q=muong%20religion&f=false</ref> This ethnic religion has the lunar new year as main religious festival, including 
[[ancestor veneration]]. 
<ref>
https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=ZRZwetkStM4C&pg=PA315&dq=muong+religion&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiJuIyluqjtAhUJuRoKHX4oAVkQ6AEwAXoECAIQAg#v=onepage&q=muong%20religion&f=false</ref>

Every living person is thought to have many souls. <ref>
https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=ZRZwetkStM4C&pg=PA315&dq=muong+religion&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiJuIyluqjtAhUJuRoKHX4oAVkQ6AEwAXoECAIQAg#v=onepage&q=muong%20religion&f=false</ref>
It sees the passage from life to death in stages. <ref>https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=LnIEBfOGWjsC&pg=PA547&dq=muong+religion&hl=de&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiJuIyluqjtAhUJuRoKHX4oAVkQ6AEwAHoECAAQAg#v=onepage&q=muong%20religion&f=false</ref>
It sees the soul as being divided in 90 parts. <ref>https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=LnIEBfOGWjsC&pg=PA547&dq=muong+religion&hl=de&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiJuIyluqjtAhUJuRoKHX4oAVkQ6AEwAHoECAAQAg#v=onepage&q=muong%20religion&f=false</ref>
