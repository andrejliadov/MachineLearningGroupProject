'''Administrative history''' is a [[historiography|historiographic field]] which looks at the history of state administrations and [[bureaucracy|bureaucracies]]. Originally considered a sub-field of [[Public administration|Administrative Science]]s that was intended to improve contemporary governance, administrative history has become an increasingly separate field.{{sfn|Raadschelders|1994|p=117}} Administrative historians study the changes in administrative ideologies and [[administrative law]] while also looking at civil servants and the relationship between government and society.{{sfn|Raadschelders|1994|pp=119-20}} It is related to [[political history|political]] and [[constitutional history]]. The discipline is most common in [[France]], [[Germany]], the [[United Kingdom]], and [[Italy]].{{sfn|Raadschelders|1994|p=118}}
