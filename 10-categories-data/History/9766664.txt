The year '''1925''' saw a number of significant events in '''[[radio broadcasting]]''' history.
__TOC__

==Events==
*1 January &ndash; In [[Sweden]], AB Radiotjänst (forerunner of [[Sveriges Radio]]) broadcasts its first programme.
*27 January &ndash; [[Australia]]'s second oldest surviving radio station, [[2HD]], goes on air for the first time in [[Newcastle, New South Wales]].
*1 February &ndash; The Polish Radiotechnical Society (Polskie Towarzystwo Radiotechniczne, PTR) makes its first official broadcast from [[Warsaw]].
*22 February &ndash; First radio transmission of a religious service in [[Denmark]], from the [[Garrison Church, Copenhagen]].
*4 March &ndash; [[Second inauguration of Calvin Coolidge]] as President of the United States, the first inauguration to be broadcast.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.inaugural.senate.gov/about/facts-and-firsts|title=Facts, Firsts and Precedents|website=Fifty-Seventh Presidential Inauguration|publisher=[[United States Senate]]|accessdate=2015-01-03|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150118104115/http://www.inaugural.senate.gov/about/facts-and-firsts|archivedate=January 18, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>
*8 March &ndash; [[Westinghouse Electric]], owner of [[KDKA (AM)|KDKA]] among other stations, announces from its [[Pittsburgh]] headquarters a proposal to form "radio networks" via shortwave technology.
*22 March &ndash; JOAK, [[NHK|NHK Radio One]] of Tokyo, an official inauguration service start, and a first license radio station in Japan.{{citation needed|date=May 2020}} 
*1 April &ndash; In Denmark, Radioordningen (Statsradiofonien from 1926, [[DR (broadcaster)|Danmarks Radio]] from 1959) is established.
*23 April &ndash; KRO (the [[Katholieke Radio Omroep]]) is established in the [[Netherlands]].
*1 June &ndash; JOBK, NHK Radio One of Osaka, an official inauguration service start in Japan.{{citation needed|date=May 2020}}
*17 June &ndash; In [[Spain]], Unión Radio opens station EAJ-7 Radio Madrid.
*7 July &ndash; Inauguration in [[France]] of state radio station Marseille PTT.
*15 July &ndash; JOCK, NHK Radio One of [[Nagoya]], an official inauguration service start in Japan.{{citation needed|date=May 2020}}
*27 July &ndash; The [[British Broadcasting Company]]'s [[Daventry transmitting station]] on [[Borough Hill]], [[Daventry]] in central England opens as the world's first [[longwave]] broadcast radio transmitter, taking over from its [[Chelmsford]] facility.<ref>{{cite book|first=Norman|last=Tomalin|title=Daventry Calling the World|location=Whitby|publisher=Caedmon|year=1998|isbn=0-905355-46-6|url=http://www.bbceng.info/Books/dx-world/dx-calling-the-world-2008a.pdf|accessdate=2015-05-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131224203242/http://www.bbceng.info/Books/dx-world/dx-calling-the-world-2008a.pdf|archive-date=24 December 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>
*25 September &ndash; The [[Funkturm Berlin|Berliner Funkturm]] (Berlin Radio Tower) begins transmissions.
*1 November &ndash; [[Omroepvereniging VARA|VARA]] (the Vereeniging van Arbeiders Radio Amateurs) is established in the [[Netherlands]].
*16 December &ndash; [[Colombo Radio]] launches in Ceylon, origin of [[Radio Ceylon]].

==Debuts==
*14 January &ndash; First broadcast on [[Sveriges Radio|Swedish national radio]] (AB Radiotjänst) of one of the world's longest-running radio programmes, ''Barnens brevlåda'' ("Children's letterbox"), which will run for 1,785 editions – all presented by "Uncle Sven" (the radio [[sports commentator]] Sven Jerring) – until 1972.
*21 March &ndash; [[Lowell Thomas]] is first heard on the radio on [[Pittsburgh]] station [[KDKA (AM)|KDKA]].
*31 March &ndash; Radio station [[WOWO (AM)|WOWO]] in [[Fort Wayne, Indiana]] begins broadcasting.
*8 April &ndash; Station WADC commences regular programming in [[Akron, Ohio]]. It had debuted earlier (in February 1925) as a temporary station during a car show held at the Central Garage, the call letters standing for the station's sponsor, the Automotive Dealers Company. Known from June 2, 2005 as [[WARF]], it becomes Akron's oldest surviving radio station.
*4 October &ndash; ''[[The Atwater Kent Hour]]'' debuts on [[WNBC (AM)|WEAF]] and 10 other connected stations.<ref name=dunningota/> 
*5 October &ndash; [[WSM (AM)|WSM]] signs on in Nashville, Tennessee.
*15 November &ndash; First transmission from Radio RV-10 in the [[Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic]] (modern-day [[Belarus]]).
*28 November &ndash; The weekly [[country music]]-variety program ''[[Grand Ole Opry]]'' is first broadcast on [[WSM (AM)|WSM radio]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]],<ref name="dunningota">Dunning, John. (1998). ''On the Air: The Encyclopedia of Old-Time Radio''. Oxford University Press. {{ISBN|978-0-19-507678-3}}.</ref> as the "WSM Barn Dance".
*24 December &ndash; [[KMOX]] begins broadcasting in St. Louis, Missouri.

==Closings==
*April &ndash; [[WGI (radio station)|WGI]]-[[Medford, MA#Neighborhoods|Medford Hillside]], [[Massachusetts]] declares bankruptcy and shuts down for good; this leaves [[WBZ (AM)|WBZ]]-Springfield as the oldest surviving station in [[New England]].
*Undated &ndash; WAAB 1150 AM ceases broadcasting. 1150 AM will return the next year as [[WJBO]].
