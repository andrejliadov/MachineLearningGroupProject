'''Peace Data''' or '''PeaceData''' is a [[fake news website]] run by the [[Internet Research Agency]], a Russian outlet connected to the country's government, which publishes in both English and Arabic.<ref name="BBC20200902">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-53980979|title=Facebook and Twitter 'dismantle Russian network'|publisher=[[BBC News]]|date=2 September 2020|access-date=3 September 2020}}</ref>

Part of its object is to [[Russian interference in the 2020 United States elections|interfere in the 2020 United States elections]] with the intention of helping [[Donald Trump]] to gain re-election by defeating the Democrat's candidate [[Joe Biden]] and his running mate [[Kamala Harris]].<ref name=NYT20200901>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/09/01/technology/facebook-russia-disinformation-election.html|title=Russians Again Targeting Americans With Disinformation, Facebook and Twitter Say|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=1 September 2020|last1=Frenkel|first1=Sheera|last2=Barnes|first2=Julian E.|access-date=3 September 2020}}</ref> The aim was to reduce support for the Biden-Harris ticket among liberal voters.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2020/09/01/facebook-disinformation-takedown/|title=Facebook takes down Russian operation that recruited U.S. journalists, amid rising concerns about election misinformation|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|date=1 September 2020|last1=Dwoskin |first1=Elizabeth|last2=Timberg|first2=Craig|access-date=3 September 2020}}</ref> According to Graphika, a social media analytics firm, only about 5 percent of the website's English-language content concerned the American elections, but "this facet of the operation suggests an attempt to build a left-wing audience and steer it away from Biden's campaign". It also targeted left-wing voters in the UK,<ref>{{cite news|last=Martin|first=Alexander|url=https://news.sky.com/story/fake-left-wing-news-organisation-targeting-uk-and-us-from-russia-banned-by-facebook-12061662?dcmp=snt-sf-twitter|title=Fake left-wing news organisation targeting UK and US from Russia banned by Facebook|work=Sky News|date=2 September 2020|access-date=3 September 2020}}</ref> and featured articles about Algeria, Egypt and Turkey.<ref name="RTR20200901">{{cite news|last=Stubbs|first=Jack|url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-usa-election-facebook-russia/facebook-says-russian-influence-campaign-targeted-left-wing-voters-in-u-s-uk-idUKKBN25S5UB|title=Facebook says Russian influence campaign targeted left-wing voters in U.S., UK|work=Reuters|date=1 September 2020|access-date=3 September 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://leftfootforward.org/2020/09/a-note-on-peace-data/ |title=A note on ‘Peace Data’ |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=4 September 2020 |website=[[Left Foot Forward]] |access-date=5 September 2020}}</ref> The website hired unsuspecting journalists in the United States as well as inventing false personas with computer created avatars.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Collier|first1=Kevin|last2=Dilanian|first2=Ken|url=https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/russian-internet-trolls-hired-u-s-journalists-push-their-news-n1239000|title=Russian internet trolls hired U.S. journalists to push their news website, Facebook says|work=NBC News|date=1 September 2020|access-date=3 September 2020}}</ref> These fake identities included the three permanent staff members listed on the website, according to Graphika.<ref name="RTR20200901" />

On September 1, 2020, [[Facebook]] and [[Twitter]] announced they had been warned by the [[Federal Bureau of Investigation]] about the [[disinformation]] effort and said they had removed or suspended accounts associated with it.<ref name="BBC20200902" /> ''[[The Daily Beast]]'' website reported that the operation tried and failed to place content with [[Jacobin (magazine)|''Jacobin'']], ''[[In These Times]]'' and [[Truthout]].<ref>{{Cite news|last1=Rawnsley|first1=Adam|last2=Tani|first2=Maxwell|date=11 September 2020|title=Leaked Documents Show Russian Trolls Tried to Infiltrate Left-Wing Media|language=en|work=[[The Daily Beast]]|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/leaked-documents-show-russian-trolls-tried-to-infiltrate-left-wing-media|url-status=live|access-date=11 September 2020}}</ref>
