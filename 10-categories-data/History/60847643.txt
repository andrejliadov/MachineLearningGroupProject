The '''Gomphotherium land bridge''' was a [[land bridge]] that connected [[Eurasia]] to the [[Arabian Plate]] approximately 19 Mya (million years ago).<ref name="Harzhauser">{{cite journal|doi=10.1016/j.jcz.2007.05.001|title=Biogeographic responses to geodynamics: A key study all around the Oligo–Miocene Tethyan Seaway|journal=Zoologischer Anzeiger - A Journal of Comparative Zoology|volume=246|issue=4|pages=241–256|year=2007|last1=Harzhauser|first1=Mathias|last2=Kroh|first2=Andreas|last3=Mandic|first3=Oleg|last4=Piller|first4=Werner E.|last5=Göhlich|first5=Ursula|last6=Reuter|first6=Markus|last7=Berning|first7=Björn|url=https://nhm-wien.ac.at/jart/prj3/nhm/data/uploads/mitarbeiter_dokumente/kroh/2007/Harzhauser_et_al_2007_ZoolAnz.pdf}}</ref>

==Significance==
Passage of fauna between [[Eurasia]] and the [[Arabian Plate]] was largely hindered before the [[Early Miocene]], as animals could not cross the open [[Tethyan seaway]].
However, during the mid-[[Burdigalian]], the [[tectonic plate]]s of Afro-Arabia and Eurasia collided, creating a terrestrial [[isthmus]] connecting the two landmasses.
This faunal exchange that resulted is known as the Proboscidean Datum Event.<ref name="Harzhauser"/>
The land bridge allowed the elephantine [[Gomphothere]]s to leave Africa.<ref>{{cite journal|last=Rögl| first= F.| year=1999| title= Mediterranean and Paratethys. Facts and hypotheses of an Oligocene to Miocene paleogeography (short overview)| journal= Geologica Carpathica| volume= 50| issue=4| pages= 339–349| url=https://www.nhm-wien.ac.at/jart/prj3/nhm/data/uploads/mitarbeiter_dokumente/roegl/1999_Roegl_Palgeo_GeolCarp.pdf}}</ref>
