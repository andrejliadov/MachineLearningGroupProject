{{short description|Israeli archaeologist and historian (1904-1974)}}
Dr. '''Michael Avi-Yonah''' (September 26, 1904 â€“ March 26, 1974) was an [[Israelis|Israeli]] [[archaeologist]] and historian. During his career he was a Professor of Archaeology at the [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]],<ref name=ProCon.org>{{cite web|title=Michael Avi-Yonah, PhD|url=http://israelipalestinian.procon.org/view.source.php?sourceID=4849|work=ProCon.org}}</ref> and served as secretary of the [[Department of Antiquities (Mandatory Palestine)|Department of Antiquities]] as well.

==Biography==
Born in [[Lemberg]], [[Austria-Hungary]] (today Lviv, [[Ukraine]]), Avi-Yonah moved to the [[Land of Israel]] with his parents in 1919 during the [[Third Aliyah]]. He first studied at [[Gymnasia Rehavia]] in [[Jerusalem]], then he went to England and studied history and archeology at the [[University of London]]. Upon his return to the Land of Israel, he studied at the [[British School of Archaeology in Jerusalem]]. His first archaeological excavations were at [[Tel el-Ajjul]], near [[Gaza City|Gaza]], and [[The Ophel in Jerusalem|Jerusalem Ophel]].<ref name="Avi-YonahStern1978">{{cite book|last1=Avi-Yonah|first1=Michael|last2=Stern|first2=Ephraim|title=Encyclopedia of Archaeological Excavations in the Holy Land|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=orbpAAAAMAAJ|accessdate=11 April 2013|year=1978|publisher=Prentice-Hall|isbn=978-0-13-275115-5|page=58}}</ref> At the end of his studies, he joined the Department of Antiquities of the [[Mandatory Palestine|British government of Palestine]]. He worked as a librarian and archivist. After the independence of the state of [[Israel]], he became secretary of the Department of Antiquities.

In 1949, he conducted excavations at [[Givat Ram]] in Jerusalem during the construction of the [[International Convention Center (Jerusalem)|International Convention Center]], where he was the first to discover a brick factory of the [[Legio X Fretensis]]. He participated in the first survey that preceded the [[Masada]] excavations, and conducted a limited excavation north of [[Caesarea Maritima]] where he discovered an ancient [[synagogue]].

He was awarded the [[Bialik Prize]] in 1955 for his book ''Antiquities of our land''. Avi-Yonah died in Jerusalem in 1974.
