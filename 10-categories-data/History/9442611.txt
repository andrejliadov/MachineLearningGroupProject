'''1923 in radio''' details the internationally significant events in '''[[radio broadcasting]]''' for the year 1923.

==Events==
*1 January &ndash; In the [[United States]] the well-known [[American Football]] [[Rose Bowl Game]] is broadcast for the first time, on [[Los Angeles]] station [[KHJ (AM)|KHJ]].
*4 January &ndash; [[WNBC (AM)|WEAF]] in [[New York City]] and [[WRKO|WNAC]] in [[Boston]] simultaneously broadcast a saxophone solo—the first network broadcast.<ref name="coxotd">Cox, Jim (2008). ''This Day in Network Radio: A Daily Calendar of Births, Debuts, Cancellations and Other Events in Broadcasting History''. McFarland & Company, Inc. {{ISBN|978-0-7864-3848-8}}.</ref>
*8 January &ndash; First outside broadcast by the [[British Broadcasting Company]]: a [[British National Opera Company]] production of ''[[The Magic Flute]]'' from the [[Royal Opera House]], [[Covent Garden]].
*18 January &ndash; The [[United Kingdom Postmaster General]] grants the BBC a licence to broadcast.
*20 January &ndash; Inauguration of Paris PTT, a station organized by and broadcasting from the [[Télécom ParisTech|École supérieure des postes et télégraphes]] (the French post office's higher educational institute of engineering). 
*8 February &ndash; [[Norman Albert]] calls the first live broadcast of an ice hockey game, the third period of an [[Ontario Hockey League]] Intermediate playoff game, on [[Toronto]] radio station [[CFCA (AM)|CFCA]].<ref>{{cite news |work=Toronto Star |date=February 9, 1923 |page=12 |author=Albert, Norman |title=Conacher Scored Six for North Toronto}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|author=Kitchen, Paul|title=Win, Lose or Wrangle: The Inside Story of the Old Ottawa Senators - 1883–1935|publisher=Penumbra Press|location=Manotick, Ontario|page=246|year=2008}}</ref>
*13 February &ndash; First BBC broadcast from [[Cardiff]], [[Wales]] (station 5WA).
*6 March &ndash; First BBC broadcast from [[Glasgow]], [[Scotland]] (station 5SC).
*13 March &ndash; Production of the first radio set incorporating a loudspeaker. All previously produced sets have required the use of headphones.
*14 March &ndash; [[Pete Parker]] calls the play-by-play of the first ice hockey game ever broadcast on the radio in its entirety, between the Regina Capitals and the [[Edmonton Eskimos (ice hockey)|Edmonton Eskimos]] of the [[Western Canada Hockey League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.broadcasting-history.ca/index3.html?url=http%3A//www.broadcasting-history.ca/personalities/personalities.php%3Fid%3D173 |title=Hooper, Albert W. "Bert" |website=The History of Canadian Broadcasting |accessdate=January 28, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304030630/http://www.broadcasting-history.ca/index3.html?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.broadcasting-history.ca%2Fpersonalities%2Fpersonalities.php%3Fid%3D173 |archive-date=March 4, 2016 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref> 
*1 April &ndash; In [[Vienna]] the Czeija & Nissl electrical company begins test transmissions from its premises in co-operation with a technical high school, the Technisches Gewerbemusem. This marks the start of radio broadcasting in [[Austria]].
*2 May &ndash; [[WPGP (AM)|WCAE]] signs on as Pittsburgh's third radio station.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://user.pa.net/~ejjeff/taehist.html|title=Jeff Roteman's WTAE Radio Page - WTAE History Page|first=Jeff|last=Roteman|website=user.pa.net}}</ref>
*14 May &ndash; [[RCA]] purchases [[WABC (AM)|WJZ]] from the [[Westinghouse Electric Corporation]]; it would also have its [[city of license]] transferred from [[Newark, New Jersey]] to [[New York City|New York]].
*18 May &ndash; The first regular radio broadcasts begin in [[Czechoslovakia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.radio.cz/en/static/history-of-radio-prague/czech-radio-history|title=Czech Radio history - Radio Prague|publisher=}}</ref>
*1 June &ndash; The publicly owned [[Canadian National Railways]] establishes the [[CNR Radio]] network to supply programming on its fleet of passenger cars; it is the first national network in [[North America]] and precursor to the [[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation]].
*3 July &ndash; [[WSAR]] in Fall River, Massachusetts, makes its first broadcast, having been licensed in the previous month.
*21 July &ndash; The [[Netherlands|Dutch]] radio manufacturing company Nederlandsche Seintoestellen Fabriek begins regular radio broadcasting in the Netherlands.
*26 September &ndash; In [[Cleveland, Ohio]], S. E. Lawrence and Theodore Willard launch [[WTAM]] in the name of the [[Exide|Willard Storage Battery Company]].
*28 September &ndash; First publication of the [[BBC]] listings magazine, ''[[Radio Times]]'', in Britain.<ref name=PPoTD>{{cite book|title=Penguin Pocket On This Day|publisher=Penguin Reference Library|isbn=0-14-102715-0|year=2006}}</ref>
*10 October &ndash; First BBC broadcast from [[Aberdeen]] in the north of Scotland (station 2BD).
*17 October &ndash; first BBC broadcast from [[Bournemouth]] in the south of England (station 6BM).
*29 October &ndash; Regular radio broadcasting in [[Germany]] officially begins with the first evening transmission from the Sendestelle Berlin installed at the [[Potsdamer Platz#Vox-Haus|Vox-Haus]] in [[Potsdamer Platz]].<ref>''Radio'' magazine, Berlin, 1923, vol. 13, page 512</ref><ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6zMsZ8vG9JQ Sound recording of the opening announcement] (YouTube) {{in lang|de}}</ref>
*13 November &ndash; [[Australia]]'s first licensed radio station, [[702 ABC Sydney|2SB]], begins transmission in [[Sydney, Australia|Sydney]].
*23 November &ndash; In [[Belgium]], French-speaking station Radio Bruxelles begins broadcasting (it will change its name to Radio Belgique on 1 January 1924).
*31 December
** [[KDKA (AM)|KDKA]] in [[Pittsburgh]] conducts the first transcontinental voice broadcast with a station in [[Manchester, England]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Today in Pittsburgh History|url=http://www.historyorb.com/countries/usa/pennsylvania/pittsburgh|work=HistoryOrb|accessdate=2012-03-21}}</ref>
** The BBC broadcasts the chimes of [[Big Ben]] from London for the first time.<ref name=PPoTD/>

==Debuts==
*22 March &ndash; ''[[Hockey Night in Canada]]'' is first broadcast on the [[Toronto Star]]'s private station [[CFCA (AM)|CFCA]].

==Births==
*26 January &ndash; [[Patricia Hughes (radio presenter)|Patricia Hughes]], English continuity announcer (died [[2013 in radio|2013]])
*2 March &ndash; [[Jean Metcalfe]], English radio broadcaster (died [[2000 in radio|2000]])
*9 May &ndash; [[Johnny Grant (radio personality)|Johnny Grant]], American radio host and producer (died [[2008 in radio|2008]])
*10 October &ndash; [[Nicholas Parsons]], British entertainer (died [[2020 in radio|2020]])
*21 November &ndash; [[Margaret Lyons]], born Keiko Margaret Inouye, Canadian broadcast executive (died [[2019 in radio|2019]])
*22 December &ndash; [[John Ebdon]], British radio broadcaster, Graecophile, author and director of the London Planetarium (died [[2005 in radio|2005]])
*25 December &ndash; [[Gordon Baxter]], American radio personality, author and columnist (died [[2005 in radio|2005]])
