==Reception==
''[[New York Times]]'' columnist [[Charles Poore]] placed ''Lands Beyond'' on his annual list of books recommended for Christmas giving.<ref>"Books of The Times", ''The New York Times'', December 20, 1952</ref> [[Kirkus Reviews]] recommended it as "a zestful geographical round-up which combines fact, legend and literature in equally interested parts".<ref>[https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/willy-de-camp-l-sprague-ley/lands-beyond/ Kirkus review]</ref>

[[Anthony Boucher|Boucher]] and [[J. Francis McComas|McComas]] praised the book, saying it was "written with scholarly authority, literary grace, and an amusedly tolerant exposition of error, to make one of the season's most enjoyable items."<ref>"Recommended Reading," ''[[F&SF]]'', October 1952, pp. 98=99</ref> ''[[New Worlds (magazine)|New Worlds]]'' reviewer [[Leslie Flood]] described it as "fascinating".<ref>"Book Reviews", ''New Worlds'', April 1954, p.126</ref> ''[[Weird Tales]]'' commended ''Lands Beyond'' to its audience, saying de Camp and Ley "ably treated" their subjects "for reader enjoyment".<ref>''The Eyrie'', ''Weird Tales'', January 1953, p.76</ref> [[George O. Smith]] wrote that it was "a book good for the younger and more impressionable to read, because it reduces to the realm of practicality many of the fabulous mysteries of the past, thus stripping the glamorous Long-Ago of its false superiority".<ref>"Science: Fact and Fiction", ''[[Space Science Fiction]]'', November 1952, p.104</ref>
