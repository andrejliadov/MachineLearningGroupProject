'''Irasema Alcántara-Ayala''' (born 1970) is a Professor of [[Natural hazard|Natural hazards]] and Risk at the [[National Autonomous University of Mexico]].<ref name=gs>{{Google scholar id}}</ref> She combines [[natural science]] with [[social science]]s, and in particular studies the occurrences of [[landslide]]s, [[natural hazard]]s and vulnerability. She was awarded the 2016 [[European Geosciences Union]] Sergey Soloviev Medal.

== Early life and education ==
Alcántara-Ayala was born in [[Mexico City]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://twas.org/directory/alcantara-ayala-irasema|title=Alcántara-Ayala, Irasema|website=TWAS|language=en|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> She studied [[geography]] at [[National Autonomous University of Mexico]] and earned her bachelor's degree in 1993.<ref name=":0" /> Based on her performance during her undergraduate studies, she was awarded the Gabino Barreda medal. She was a graduate student at [[King's College London]], where she worked on [[geomorphology]] and was awarded a [[PhD]] in 1997.<ref name=PhD-267249>{{cite thesis|degree=PhD|publisher=King's College London (University of London)|oclc=1027287560|url=https://copac.jisc.ac.uk/id/23220392?style=html|title=Modelling mass movement risk under semi-arid mountainous conditions : the Alpujarride complex, Spain|first= Irasema|last=Alcantara-Ayala|date=1997|id={{EThOS|uk.bl.ethos.267249}}|website=jisc.ac.uk}}</ref><ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://katerva.net/awards/expert-panels/validation-panel/176-irasema-alcantara-ayala|title=Irasema Alcántara-Ayala {{!}} Katerva|website=katerva.net|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> 

== Research and career ==
In 1998 Alcántara-Ayala joined [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] as a [[postdoctoral]] [[research fellow]], where she worked in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.coniunctus.amc.edu.mx/cvpremios/IrasemaAlcantara.pdf|title=Ciencias sociales|website=Coniunctus|access-date=2019-06-30}}</ref>  In 2000 Alcántara-Ayala joined [[National Autonomous University of Mexico]] as an Assistant Professor. She was promoted to Professor and the youngest woman to be made Director of the Institute of Geography in 2008.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/profile/person/17486859|title=Irasema Alcantara-Ayala, International Geographical Union: Profile and Biography|website=Bloomberg.com|language=en|access-date=2019-06-30}}</ref> Her work focuses on [[Latin America]] and the [[Caribbean]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.irdrinternational.org/2016/04/20/exciting-news-the-2016-sergey-soloviev-medal-is-awarded-to-irdr-science-committee-vice-chair-irasema-alcantara-ayala/|title=Exciting News: The 2016 Sergey Soloviev Medal is awarded to IRDR Science Committee Vice-Chair Irasema ALCÁNTARA-AYALA|date=2016-04-20|website=IRDR|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-30}}</ref> She works with the [[Mexico]] National Centre for Disasters Prevention and the Natural Hazards Centre.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hazards.colorado.edu/biography/irasema-alcantara-ayala|title=Natural Hazards Center {{!}}{{!}} Irasema Alcántara-Ayala|website=hazards.colorado.edu|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> Alcántara-Ayala is a member of the Network of Social Studies on the Prevention of Disasters in Latin America.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Alcántara‐Ayala|first=Irasema|date=2019|title=Time in a bottle: challenges to disaster studies in Latin America and the Caribbean|journal=Disasters|language=en|volume=43|issue=S1|pages=S18–S27|doi=10.1111/disa.12325|issn=1467-7717}}</ref> She has assessed the hazard presented by [[rainfall]]-induced landslides, using aerial photographs, field observations and slope instability analysis.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Alcantara-Ayala|first=I|date=2004|title=Hazard assessment of rainfall-induced landsliding in mexico|journal=Geomorphology|series=Hazards of Mass Movements|volume=61|issue=1|pages=19–40|doi=10.1016/j.geomorph.2003.11.004|issn=0169-555X}}</ref> She combines this field work with [[social science]] studies, including [[social vulnerability]] analysis.<ref name=":1" /> She has emphasised the need for [[Geomorphology|geomorphologists]] to be involved with the assessment of risk and management of [[Natural disaster|disasters]] in the developing world.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Alcántara-Ayala|first=Irasema|date=2002|title=Geomorphology, natural hazards, vulnerability and prevention of natural disasters in developing countries|journal=Geomorphology|series=Geomorphology in the Public Eye: Political Issues, Education, and the Public|volume=47|issue=2|pages=107–124|doi=10.1016/S0169-555X(02)00083-1|issn=0169-555X}}</ref> In general, Alcántara-Ayala believes that [[Natural disaster|disasters]] are socially constructed, that there is a nexus between risk, the environment and development and that risks can be managed at a local level.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Alcántara‐Ayala|first=Irasema|date=2019|title=Time in a bottle: challenges to disaster studies in Latin America and the Caribbean|journal=Disasters|language=en|volume=43|issue=S1|pages=S18–S27|doi=10.1111/disa.12325|issn=1467-7717}}</ref> She collaborates with other colleagues in the Forensic Investigations of Disaster project, which investigates the causes and drivers of risk creation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.preventionweb.net/publications/view/53119|title=Forensic Investigations of Disaster (FORIN): towards the understanding of root causes of disasters {{!}} PreventionWeb.net|website=www.preventionweb.net|access-date=2019-06-30}}</ref> Alcántara-Ayala has also proposed transforming the National Civil Protection System in Mexico into a more oriented policy of Integrated Disaster Risk Management.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lopezdoriga.com/nacional/el-tema-que-pese-a-su-importancia-no-ha-sido-tocado-por-los-candidatos/|title=El tema que pese a su importancia no ha sido tocado por los candidatos|date=2018-06-12|website=López-Dóriga Digital|language=es-MX|access-date=2019-06-30}}</ref> 

=== Academic service ===
Alcántara-Ayala is former Vice President of the International Association of Geomorphologists.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.geomorph.org/irasema-alcantara-ayala/|title=Irasema ALCÁNTARA-AYALA|website=IAG - International Association of Geomorphologists|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> She has previously served as Vice President of the [[International Geographical Union]]<ref name=":0" /> and of the Integrated Research on Disaster Risk Programme (ISC). She serves on the leadership council of the Mountain Research Initiative (MRI).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mountainresearchinitiative.org/index.php/who-we-are/team/science-leadership-council/2124-irasema-alcantara-ayala|title=Irasema Alcántara-Ayala|last=artd|first=Super User|website=MRI|language=en-gb|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> She is a member of the [[Organization for Women in Science for the Developing World]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://owsd.net/member/alc%C3%A1ntara-ayala-irasema|title=Alcántara-Ayala Irasema {{!}} OWSD|website=owsd.net|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> and of the Mexican Academy of Sciences. Her first book, Geomorphological Hazards and Disaster Prevention, was published by [[Cambridge University Press]] in 2010.<ref>{{Cite book|ISBN=9781107414785|url=https://www.cambridge.org/gb/academic/subjects/earth-and-environmental-science/geomorphology-and-physical-geography/geomorphological-hazards-and-disaster-prevention|title=Geomorphological hazards and disaster prevention {{!}} Geomorphology and physical geography|doi=10.1017/CBO9780511807527.020|website=cambridge.org|publisher=Cambridge University Press|language=en|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> She is a Young Affiliate of [[The World Academy of Sciences]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://jms.imde.ac.cn/web/21915/Irasema%20Alc%C3%A1ntara-Ayala|title=Journal of Mountain Science(JMS) - IMHE|website=jms.imde.ac.cn|access-date=2019-06-30}}</ref>
