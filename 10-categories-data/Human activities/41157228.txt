'''Noor Hossain Day''' ({{lang-bn|নূর হোসেন দিবস}}) is a national day in [[Bangladesh]] observed every 10 November in order to commemorate the martyrdom of [[Nur Hossain|Noor Hossain]] while protesting for the establishment of [[democracy]] in the country against the autocratic rule of [[Hussain Muhammad Ershad]] in 1987.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.theindependentbd.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=192714:noor-hossain-day-today&catid=187:online-edition&Itemid=223 |title=Noor Hossain Day today |work=The Independent |location=Dhaka |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203002909/http://www.theindependentbd.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=192714:noor-hossain-day-today&catid=187:online-edition&Itemid=223 |archive-date=2013-12-03}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Babul |first=Parvez |date=10 November 2013 |url=http://www.thedailystar.net/beta2/news/democracy-and-the-role-of-media/ |title=Tribute to Shaheed Nur Hossain - Democracy and the role of media |work=The Daily Star}}</ref> Noor Hossain who was a motor driver by profession was killed by the police force.  At the time he was shot, his body carried several slogans in white paint. He wore the slogan "''Down with autocracy''" (''Sairachar nipat jak'') on his chest, and on his back, he had written the slogan "''Let Democracy Be Free''" (''Ganatantra mukti pak''). The day is honored each year as cultural and political organizations sponsor special programs for observance the day.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://newagebd.com/detail.php?date=2012-11-11&nid=29746 |url-status=dead |title=Noor Hossain Day observed |work=New Age |date=11 November 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131202235646/http://newagebd.com/detail.php?date=2012-11-11&nid=29746 |archive-date=2013-12-02 |access-date=2013-06-10}}</ref>
It was first called "''Historic November 10 observance''" but the [[Awami League]] supported the phrase "''Noor Hossain Day''", by which it is known today.<ref>Qadir (11 November 1991). "Autocracy dethroned, but Bangladesh ponders future of democracy". Agence France Presse.</ref>
