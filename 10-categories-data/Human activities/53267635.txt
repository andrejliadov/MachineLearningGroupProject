'''Traffic obstruction''' is a common tactic used during public protests and demonstrations.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Badger|first1=Emily|title=Why highways have become the center of civil rights protest|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/wonk/wp/2016/07/13/why-highways-have-become-the-center-of-civil-rights-protest/|accessdate=7 September 2017|work=The Washington post|date=13 July 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Yongshun|first1=Cai|title=Collective Resistance in China: Why Popular Protests Succeed Or Fail|date=2010|publisher=Stanford University Press|isbn=978-0804763394|page=110-111}}</ref> The transport users affected by such disruptions are often unsympathetic to the cause.<ref>{{cite news|title=Proposed laws would crack down on protesters who block roadways|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2017/01/25/us/nd-protest-driver-bill-trnd/index.html|accessdate=7 September 2017|work=CNN|date=26 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Stopping traffic: Minnesota lawmakers try increase penalties for common protest tactic|url=http://www.salon.com/2017/01/14/stopping-traffic-minnesota-lawmakers-try-increase-penalties-for-common-protest-tactic/|website=Salon|accessdate=7 September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=In Protests, Who Owns the Highways?|website=citylab|url=https://www.citylab.com/equity/2014/10/in-protests-who-owns-the-highways/381963/|accessdate=7 September 2017}}</ref>{{Failed verification|date=February 2020}}<ref>{{cite book|last1=Chen|first1=Xi|title=Social Protest and Contentious Authoritarianism in China|date=2011|publisher=Cambridge University Press|page=176}}</ref>

== Legality ==
Most jurisdictions{{which?|date=September 2020}} consider the obstruction of traffic an illegal activity and have developed rules to prosecute those who ''block, obstruct, impede, or otherwise interfere with the normal flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic upon a public street or highway''.<ref> https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/protesters-cant-block-roads-or-public-spaces-rules-sc/articleshow/78544230.cms </ref> Some jurisdictions also penalize slow moving vehicle traffic.{{citation needed|date=September 2020}} The unimpeded flow of traffic in the public road-space is often considered a common right. {{citation needed|date=September 2020}}

== Raasta roko==
'''Raasta roko''' ({{lang-hi|"obstruct the road"}}) is a form of protest commonly practised in [[India]].<ref name="BhushanKatyal2003">{{cite book|author1=K. Bhushan|author2=G. Katyal|title=Attack on Akshardham: temple terror|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-YPaAAAAMAAJ|accessdate=28 September 2012|date=1 January 2003|publisher=A.P.H. Pub. Corp.|isbn=978-81-7648-446-6}}</ref> It usually involves large number of people preventing vehicular traffic from using a busy thoroughfare. Pedestrian traffic is not targeted.

'''Rail roko''' is similar blocking of a railway.<ref name="AzzelliniNess2011">{{cite book|author1=Immanuel Ness|author2=Dario Azzellini|title=Ours to Master and to Own: Workers' Control from the Commune to the Present|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=JxsXaC8ajvcC&pg=PA360|accessdate=28 September 2012|date=5 July 2011|publisher=Haymarket Books|isbn=978-1-60846-119-6|pages=360â€“}}</ref>
