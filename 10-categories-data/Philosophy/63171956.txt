'''Philosophical Society of South Africa''' (PSSA) is a professional association of philosophers which aims to represent "the interests of the academic philosophical community in Southern Africa."<ref>{{cite web |title=Annual Conference of the Philosophical Society of Southern Africa (PSSA) Home |url=https://www.ufs.ac.za/conferences/conference/annual-conference-of-the-philosophical-society-of-southern-africa-(pssa)-home |website=www.ufs.ac.za}}</ref> It publishes ''[[South African Journal of Philosophy]]''.

==Controversy==
The society became controversial in 2017 due to allegations of [[racism]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Weinberg |first1=Justin |title="On The Brink of Collapse": The Philosophical Society of South Africa |url=http://dailynous.com/2017/02/09/on-the-brink-of-collapse-the-philosophical-society-of-south-africa/ |website=[[Daily Nous]] |date=9 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Demise of philosophical society not black and white |url=https://mg.co.za/article/2017-02-07-no-one-philosophical-about-demise-of-philosophical-society/ |work=The Mail & Guardian |date=7 February 2017 |language=en-ZA}}</ref>
