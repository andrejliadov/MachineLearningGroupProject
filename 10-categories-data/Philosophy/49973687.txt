'''Archaic mother''' (primal mother or ''Ur-mutter'') is the mother of earliest infancy, whose continuing influence is traced in [[psychoanalysis]], and whose (repressed) presence is considered to underlie the [[horror film]].

==In psychoanalysis==
[[Sigmund Freud]] recognised in his writings the power of the archaic mother as "first nourisher and first seducer",<ref>{{cite book |editor-first1=Gail S. |editor-last1=Reed |editor-first2=Howard B. |editor-last2=Devine |title=On Freud's "Screen Memories" |date=2015 |publisher=Karnac |location=London |page=98 |isbn=978-1-78220-055-0 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1mS2BAAAQBAJ&pg=PA98}}</ref> and the image of the archaic mother as seductress became widespread in psychoanalysis in his wake.<ref>{{cite book |editor-first=Leticia G. |editor-last1=Fiorini |editor-first2=Graciela Abelin-Sas |editor-last2=Rose |title=On Freud's "femininity" |publisher=Karnac |location=London |date=2010 |page=75 |isbn=978-1-85575-701-1 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hC5bzLqw02cC&pg=PA75}}</ref> Jung too was absorbed in his writings by the concept of the archaic mother,<ref>{{cite book |editor-first1=Coline |editor-last1=Covington |editor-first2=Barbara |editor-last2=Wharton |title=Sabina Spielrein: Forgotten Pioneer of Psychoanalysis |date=2015 |orig-year=2003 |edition=2nd |page=175 |publisher=Routledge |isbn=978-0-415-81748-6 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HOYjCQAAQBAJ&pg=PA175}}</ref> and his followers have warned of the danger of that [[imago]] being re-activated in the [[transference]] by the female therapist.<ref>{{cite book |editor-first1=Polly |editor-last1=Young-Eisendrath |editor-first2=Terence |editor-last2=Dawson |title=The Cambridge Companion to Jung |publisher=Cambridge University Press |isbn=0-521-47309-8 |date=1997 |page=236 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=F2t1TWvRYeAC&pg=PA236}}</ref>  For [[Jacques Lacan]], the primitive, untrammelled power of the archaic mother could only be contained by the emergence and consolidation of the [[Oedipus complex|paternal metaphor]].<ref>{{cite book |first=Charles |last=Shepherdson |title=Vital Signs: Nature, Culture, Psychoanalysis |publisher=Routledge |date=2000 |page=72 |isbn=0-415-90879-5 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=c1iSAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA72}}</ref>

Feminist analysts like [[Luce Irigaray]] have subsequently attempted to reclaim the archaic mother as an empowering force for female identity. Sceptics, however, have accepted [[Julia Kristeva]]'s warning about the Utopian, indeed [[Narcissism|narcissistic]] perils of attempting to circumvent society, and the cultural sphere, by regressing to a phantasisised merger with the archaic, undifferentiated mother.<ref>{{cite book |first=Margaret |last=Whitfield |title=Luce Irigaray: Philosophy in the Feminine |publisher=Routledge |date=2014 |isbn=978-0-415-05968-8 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Fx3HBQAAQBAJ}}</ref> Kristeva also considered the Jungian approach as a "dead end with its archetypal configurations of libidinal substance taken out of the realm of sexuality and placed in bondage to the archaic mother".<ref>{{cite book |first=Julia |last=Kristeva |title=Desire in Language: A Semiotic Approach to Literature and Art |publisher=Columbia University Press |date=1980 |page=276 |isbn=0-231-04806-8 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=d2BaPShWHR8C&pg=PA276}}</ref> Artist and psychoanalyst-theorist [[Bracha L. Ettinger]] disagrees with Kriseteva's views that the archaic mother, starting with the pregnant mother, represents psychotic undifferentiation. In Ettinger's view the rejection of the archaic mother is culturally instituted by a patriarchal and phallic society.<ref>{{cite book |first=Bracha L. |last=Ettinger |title=The Matrixial Borderspace |publisher=University of Minnesota Press |date=2006 |isbn=978-0816635870 }}</ref> She addresses the archaic mother as the major subjectivising agency for the infant in a relations that she defines as matrixial, where psychic differentiation coincides with co-emergence.

==In the arts==
*[[Film theory]] has emphasised the role of the archaic mother as monstrous figure in the horror film,<ref>{{cite book |first=Barbara |last=Creed |author-link=Barbara Creed |title=The Monstrous-Feminine: Film, Feminism, Psychoanalysis |date=2012 |orig-year=1993 |publisher=Routledge |isbn=978-0-415-05258-0 |page=20 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hpHkO4S5tFsC&pg=PA20}}</ref> more terrifying and less uncontained than the [[Phallic woman|phallic mother]] in her undifferentiated grotesqueness.<ref>{{cite book |editor-first1=Lisa S. |editor-last1=Starks |editor-first2=Courtney |editor-last2=Lehmann |title=The Reel Shakespeare: Alternative Cinema and Theory |date=2002 |publisher=Associated University Press |location=London |page=139 |isbn=0-8386-3939-9 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Hg-3hBD8cMEC&pg=PA139}}</ref>
*A similar figure appears as the 'black queen' in romances such as ''[[The Fairie Queene]]'', and as the witch of folklore.<ref>{{cite book |first=Northrop |last=Frye |title=Anatomy of Criticism: Four Essays |date=1971 |orig-year=1957 |publisher=Princeton University Press |page=196 |isbn=0-691-01298-9 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=jmkZBgAAQBAJ&pg=PA196}}</ref>
*[[Sylvia Plath]], under the influence of Jung, wrote what she called a "diatribe against the Dark Mother. The Mummy. Mother of Shadows...".<ref>{{cite book |first=Christina |last=Britzolakis |title=Sylvia Plath and the Theatre of Mourning |date=1999 |publisher=Clarendon |page=113 |isbn=978-0-1981-8373-0}}</ref>
