'''''The Afterlife Experiments''''' (full title: '''''The Afterlife Experiments: Breakthrough Scientific Evidence of Life After Death''''') is a book written by [[Gary Schwartz]] and William L. Simon, with a foreword by [[Deepak Chopra]].<ref>{{cite book |last=Schwartz |first=Gary |authorlink=Gary Schwartz |title=The Afterlife Experiments |publisher=[[Simon & Schuster|Atria Books]] |date=March 2003 |isbn=978-0-7434-3659-5}}</ref> The book contains four reports detailing a series of experiments that utilized [[mediumship|mediums]] and sitters to investigate whether or not there is [[afterlife|life after death]].

The psychologist [[Ray Hyman]] published a detailed criticism of Schwartz's techniques, titled "How Not to Test Mediums".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.csicop.org/si/show/how_not_to_test_mediums_critiquing_the_afterlife_experiments |title=How ''Not'' to Test Mediums |last=Hyman |first=Ray |authorlink=Ray Hyman |work=[[Skeptical Inquirer]] |publisher=[[Committee for Skeptical Inquiry]] |date=Januaryâ€“February 2003 |accessdate=2006-12-04}}</ref> Schwartz responded with a detailed rebuttel titled "How Not to Review Mediumship Research",<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.csicop.org/si/show/how_not_to_review_mediumship_research/ |title=How ''Not'' to Review Mediumship Research |last=Schwartz |first=Gary |authorlink=Gary Schwartz |year=2003 |accessdate=2006-12-04 }}</ref><ref>Schwartz, Gary (2003) [https://web.archive.org/web/20030420070550/http://www.openmindsciences.com/ How NOT to Review Mediumship Research (Extensive Commentary)] (archived) . From Schwartz's Skeptical Inquirer article : "Space precludes my providing a detailed and thorough commentary here illustrating how pervasively Hyman ignores and omits important information. (An extensive commentary has been published on various Web sites, including www.openmindsciences.com.)" .
