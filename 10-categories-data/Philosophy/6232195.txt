==Overview==
In [[Denmark]],<ref>{{cite web|last1=Solvang|first1=Lin|title=Theoretical Philosophy|url=http://mcc.ku.dk/research/focus-areas/theoreticalphilosophy/|website=mcc.ku.dk|accessdate=31 January 2017|language=en|date=3 June 2015}}</ref> [[Finland]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Theoretical Philosophy - University of Helsinki|url=http://www.helsinki.fi/theoreticalphilosophy/|website=www.helsinki.fi|accessdate=30 January 2017}}</ref> [[Germany]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Theoretical Philosophy {{!}} Philosophisches Seminar - English|url=http://www.blogs.uni-mainz.de/fb05philosophieengl/institutes/theoretical-philosophy/|website=www.blogs.uni-mainz.de|accessdate=31 January 2017|language=de}}</ref> [[Netherlands]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Theoretical Philosophy|url=https://www.uu.nl/en/research/philosophy/theoretical-philosophy|website=Universiteit Utrecht|accessdate=31 January 2017|language=en|date=5 December 2014}}</ref> [[Sweden]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Theoretical Philosophy {{!}} Department of Philosophy, Lund University|url=http://www.fil.lu.se/en/research/teoretisk-filosofi/|website=www.fil.lu.se|accessdate=30 January 2017|language=en}}</ref> and [[United States]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Theoretical Philosophy {{!}} Philosophy|url=http://uwm.edu/philosophy/theoretical-philosophy/|website=uwm.edu|accessdate=31 January 2017}}</ref> courses in theoretical and practical philosophy are taught separately, and are separate degrees. Other countries may use a similar scheme—some [[Scotland|Scottish]] universities, for example, divide philosophy into [[logic]], [[metaphysics]], and [[ethics]]—but in most universities around the world philosophy is taught as a single subject. There is also a unified philosophy subject in some Swedish universities, such as [[Södertörn University College|Södertörns Högskola]].

Theoretical philosophy is sometimes confused with [[analytic philosophy]], but the latter is a philosophical movement, embracing certain ideas and methods but dealing with all philosophical subject matters, while the former is a way of sorting philosophical questions into two different categories in the context of a [[curriculum]].
