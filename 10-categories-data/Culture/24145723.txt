The '''2010 Arab Capital of Culture''' was chosen to be [[Doha]], [[Qatar]].<ref>{{cite news | first=Felix | last= Misha | title=Doha makes the running | url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/travel/destinations/middle_east/article6908095.ece | publisher= The Sunday Times | date=7 November 2009}}</ref> The [[Arab Capital of Culture]] is an initiative undertaken by [[UNESCO]],<ref name="herbert">{{cite book
  | last =Herbert
  | first =Ian
  | authorlink =
  |author2=Nicole Leclercq|author3=International Theatre Institute 
  | title =The World of Theatre: An Account of the World's Theatre Seasons 1999-2000, 2000-2001 and 2001-2002 
  | publisher =Routledge
  | date =2003
  | location =
  | url =https://archive.org/details/worldtheatreedit00herb
  | url-access =limited
  | doi =
  | isbn =  0-415-30621-3
  | page =[https://archive.org/details/worldtheatreedit00herb/page/n235 225]}}
</ref> under the ''Cultural Capitals Program'' to promote and celebrate [[Arab culture]] and encourage cooperation in the [[Arab world|Arab region]]. The Qatari government has begun preparations in many different fields including a US $82 million Cultural Village.<ref>{{cite news | title=QATAR: Doha gladly takes on responsibility as 2010 Arab Capital of Culture | url=http://www.itnsource.com/shotlist/RTV/2010/02/08/RTV323910/?v=1&a=0 | publisher= ITN | date=8 February 2010}}</ref><ref>{{citation | first=Louise | last= Oakley | url=http://www.arabianbusiness.com/565405-qatars-riches?start=1 | publisher=arabianbusiness.com | title=Qatar's riches | date=26 August 2009}}</ref>
