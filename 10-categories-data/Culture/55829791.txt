{{short description|Overview of the events of 2018 in art}}
{{Year nav topic5|2018|art}}

The year '''2018 in [[art]]''' involves various significant events.

==Events==
* [[February 16]] - [[Edgar Degas]]'s 1877 pastel ''[[Les Choristes]]'', stolen from the [[Musée Cantini]] in [[Marseille]] at the end of [[2009 in art|2009]], is found in the luggage compartment of an intercity bus outside Paris during a search by customs officials.<ref>{{cite news|last=Breeden|first=Aurelien|title=Degas Painting, Stolen in 2009, Is Found on Bus Near Paris|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/23/world/europe/degas-painting-bus.html|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=February 23, 2018|accessdate=January 24, 2019}}</ref>
* April - The Terrus museum at [[Elne]] in the south of France, dedicated to paintings by [[Étienne Terrus]], discovers that half its collection, 82 works, is counterfeit.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-43933530|title=Étienne Terrus museum in Elne uncovers fake art in collection|website=BBC News|date=2018-04-28|accessdate=2018-04-28}}</ref>
* [[April 21]] - The [[Institute for Contemporary Art, Richmond|Institute for Contemporary Art]]  at [[VCU]] in [[Richmond, Virginia]] designed by [[Steven Holl]] opens to the public.<ref>https://www.wtvr.com/2018/04/13/vcus-landmark-ica-opens-with-declaration-to-power-of-art-and-diversity/amp/</ref><ref>https://www.archdaily.com/893277/institute-for-contemporary-art-at-vcu-steven-holl-architects</ref>
* May - The Collection of Peggy Rockefeller and [[David Rockefeller]] is auctioned off in three sales at [[Christie's]], [[Rockefeller Center]], [[New York City]]. On the evening of May 8 the first night of the sale ''Young Girl with a Flower Basket'' (oil on canvas, 1905) by [[Pablo Picasso]] is sold for $US115 million.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-05-06/rockefeller-trove-ignites-bidding-frenzy-with-26-000-money-clip|title=Picasso's Nude Fetches $115 Million at Rockefeller Auction|date=8 May 2018}}</ref> along with world record prices for works by [[Claude Monet]] (''Nymphéas en fleur'' 1914-1917 $84.7 million) and [[Henri Matisse]] (''Odalisque couchée aux magnolias'' 1923 $80.7 million), all contributing to a new world record for a single collection at auction of $832.6 million, the record previously having been held by the $484 million total achieved for the art collection of [[Pierre Berge]] and [[Yves Saint Laurent (designer)|Yves Saint Laurent]].<ref>https://www.cnn.com/style/article/rockefeller-auction-christies-picasso/index.html</ref><ref>http://www.blouinartinfo.com/news/story/3039026/rockefeller-art-collection-breaks-world-record-for-single</ref>
* [[May 25]] - Second attack on [[Ilya Repin]]'s painting ''[[Ivan the Terrible and His Son Ivan]]'' in the [[Tretyakov Gallery]], [[Moscow]], by an inebriated visitor.
* [[June 15]] - Second major fire at [[Glasgow School of Art]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carrell|first1=Severin|last2=Brooks|first2=Libby|last3=Rawlinson|first3=Kevin|title='Heartbreaking': fire guts Glasgow School of Art for the second time|url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2018/jun/16/firefighters-tackle-blaze-at-glasgow-school-of-art|accessdate=2018-06-16|work=[[The Guardian]]|location=London|date=2018-06-16}}</ref>
*July - A 1967 painting, "Untitled" by [[Robert Motherwell]] missing for more than forty years is returned after apparently having been stolen by a ring of art thieves.<ref>https://www.washingtonpost.com/amphtml/national/robert-motherwell-painting-stolen-in-1978-in-new-york-is-returned/2018/07/13/dd98eeb4-818e-11e8-b658-4f4d2a1aeef1_story.html</ref>
* [[August 13]] - Despite warning signs, a man in his sixties is hospitalized after falling into a cavity created in a gallery floor at  the [[Serralves Museum#Museum|Serralves Museum]] in [[Porto]], [[Portugal]], for the 1992 Anish Kapoor work "Descent into Limbo" (titled after the [[Descent into Limbo (Mantegna)|1492 painting]] by [[Andrea Mantegna]]).<ref>https://www.dezeen.com/2018/08/20/anish-kapoor-art-accident-descent-into-limbo-serralves-museum/amp/</ref>
* [[October 5]] - A version of [[Banksy]]'s artwork ''[[Balloon Girl]]'' is auctioned at [[Sotheby's]] in London for £1,042,000. Moments after the closing bid, the artwork partially shreds itself by means of a [[paper shredder]] hidden in the frame<ref name="Banksy shreds">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-england-bristol-45770028|title=Banksy artwork shreds itself after £1m sale at Sotheby's|date=2018-10-06|work=BBC News|accessdate=2018-10-06}}</ref> to become ''[[Love Is in the Bin]]''.
* [[November 14]] - At a sale of the art collection of [[Barney A. Ebsworth]] at Christie's New York, [[Edward Hopper]]'s ''[[Chop Suey (painting)|Chop Suey]]'' (1929) sells for US$92m and [[Willem de Kooning]]'s ''Woman as Landscape'' (1955) for US$68.9m.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hopper's Chop Suey in record-breaking $92m sale|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-46205604|work=BBC News|date=2018-11-14|accessdate=2018-11-14}}</ref>
* [[November 15]] - A canvas by [[David Hockney]], ''[[Portrait of an Artist (Pool with Two Figures)]]'' (1972) sells at Christies in New York City for $90.3 million US dollars with fees, thus shattering the previous record for the most expensive work of art by a living artist ever sold at auction. The record price was previously held by a "Balloon Dog (orange)" sculpture by [[Jeff Koons]] for $58.4 million also at Christies in New York City in 2013.<ref>https://www.nytimes.com/2018/11/15/arts/design/david-hockney-christies-portrait-of-an-artist-jeff-koons.html</ref><ref>https://www.cbsnews.com/news/david-hockney-painting-poised-to-shatter-auction-records-fetch-80-million/</ref>
* [[December 1]] - The [[Galerie nationale du Jeu de Paume]] museum in [[Paris]] is torched in politically related mob violence as the [[Tuileries Gardens]] are stormed by protestors.<ref>https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/world-news/paris-violence-jeu-de-paume-13672795</ref>
* [[December 7]] - In the United States of America, the [[National Rifle Association]] (NRA), the defendant in a lawsuit brought by artist [[Anish Kapoor]], settles with the plaintiff in the case. Therein the gun lobby group had without the sculptor's consent used a filmed image of his work in an approximately one minute long promotional video called "The Violence of Lies". Kapoor says of the victory which is inclusive of having the image of his work removed from the NRA's film that "They have now complied with our demand to remove the unauthorized image of my sculpture ''Cloud Gate'' from their abhorrent video, which seeks to promote fear, hostility, and division in American society.”<ref>https://www.artforum.com/news/anish-kapoor-settles-lawsuit-with-nra-over-toxic-video-featuring-his-art-77942</ref><ref>https://news.artnet.com/art-world/anish-kapoor-nra-settlement-1412923</ref>
