'''''Abbia: Cameroon Cultural Review''''' ([[French language|fr]]: ''Abbia: Revue Culturelle Camerounaise'') was an [[academic journal]] covering the [[culture of Cameroon]]. It was established by [[Bernard Fonlon]] in 1962 and ran until 1982. Its influence was discussed by [[Milton Krieger]] in 1996<ref>{{Cite journal |last=Krieger |first=Milton |date=1996 |title=Building the Republic through letters: "Abbia: Cameroon Cultural Review," 1963–82, and its legacy |journal=[[Research in African Literatures]] |volume=27 |issue=2 |pages=155–177 |jstor=3820169}}</ref> and 2014<ref>{{Cite book |title=Cameroon's contemporary culture and politics prospects and problems |last=Krieger |first=Milton |date=2014 |publisher=[[Project MUSE]] |isbn=9789956790272 |oclc=934794271}}</ref> as well as the contributors to Fonlon's Festschrift 1989.<ref>{{Cite book |title=Socrates in Cameroon: the life and works of Bernard Nsokika Fonlon |last=Lyonga |first=Nalova |date=1989|publisher=[[University of Yaoundé]] |oclc=24347680}}</ref> The journal exemplified Fonlon's beliefs about the importance of French-English [[bilingualism]] (see his article in Abbia V 4 1963 'A case for early bilingualism').
