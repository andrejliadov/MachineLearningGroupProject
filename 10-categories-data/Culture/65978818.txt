[[File:Huanca_en_quebrada_Uquián.jpg|thumb|300x300px|Huanca in the Uquián ravag, on the way from Olleros to Chavín de Huántar, in the [[Huascarán National Park]] in Peru.]]
A '''huanca''' (in [[Ancash Quechua|Quechua ancashino]]: ''wanka'')<ref>{{Cite book|last=Julca Guerrero|first=Félix|title=Quechua Ancashino - Una mirada actual|publisher=Fondo Editorial del Pedagógico San Marcos|year=2009|isbn=978-612-45425-4-1|location=Lima|pages=397|language=es-es}}</ref> or '''chichic''' (''tsitsiq'')<ref>{{Cite book|last=Zuidema|first=Reiner Tom|title=El sistema de ceques del Cuzco : la organización social de la capital de los Incas: con un ensayo preliminar (1st ed)|publisher=Fondo Editorial de la Pontificia Universidad Católica del Perú |year=1995 |isbn=8483909995 |location=Lima |pages=188|language=es-es}}</ref> is an elongated vertical stone considered sacred, with multiple symbolisms, in the Andean worldview. Huancas were worshipped and given rituals and offerings.<ref>Museo Nacional de la Cultura Peruana presenta conferencia ‘El mojón de piedra o huanca prehispánico y su pervivencia en la etnografía del Callejón de Huaylas». Ministerio de Cultura del Perú.18 June 2015.</ref> The stone placed at the top is called ''chacrayoc''; (in Quechua of Ancash, ''chakrayuq'' or "lord of the chakra").

Huanca, while monoliths, are similar to ''totems'' (from the [[Ojibwa]] word ''ototeman'', meaning 'he is my kinship') of the Native American peoples of North America, as they are considered sacred and can symbolize the ancestor of a community, and thus fulfil a tutelar function. They also resemble ''[[menhir]]'', megalithic monuments built in the late [[Neolithic]] and [[Bronze Age]] in the western Mediterranean and Atlantic Europe.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Fullola|first=Josep Mª; Nadal|title=Introducción a la prehistoria. La evolución de la cultura humana|publisher=UOC|year=2005|isbn=84-9788-153-2|location=Barcelona|pages=165–166|language=Spanish}}</ref>

== Geographical Distribution ==
Huancas are present in several places in the [[Ancash]] Andes. Many have been found in the [[Callejón de Huaylas]] in the provinces of [[Recuay Province|Recuay]], [[Huaraz]] (Huancajirca, Pesebre, [[Markahirka]]), [[Carhuaz Province|Carhuaz]] ([[Piruro]] II), [[Yungay Province|Yungay]] (Cotu, Marcayoc, Keushu), [[Caraz District|Caraz]] (Pueblo Viejo) and [[Sihuas Province|Sihuas]] (Huayubamba). Also, huancas have been discovered in [[Huamanga Province]] in [[Ayacucho]], in the northern basin of [[Lake Titicaca]] in [[Peru]]'s [[Puno Province|Puno]] region, and in Argentina's [[Salta Province|Salta]] and [[Tucumán Province|Tucumán]] provinces.<ref>{{Cite book|last=García Azcárate|first=Jorgelina|title=Monolitos-Huancas: Un intento de explicación de las piedras de Tafí (Rep. Argentina)|publisher=Chungara Revista de Antropología Chilena (Arica: Departamento de Antropología de la Universidad de Tarapacá)|year=1996|isbn=0717-7356|location=|pages=159–174|language=Spanish}}</ref>

== Age ==
According to Francisco Bazán del Campo, the oldest huancas are approximately 5000 years ago (from 4000 to 2000 BC), in the Late Precephalic, in places such as [[Caral]] and [[Bandurria, Peru|Bandurria]].<ref>Bazán del Campo 2007: 7.</ref>

== Meanings ==
[[File:El_Lanzón.jpg|thumb|The huanca in the Ancient Temple of Chavín de Huántar, still positioned in a place that would adapt to the concept of axis mundi referred to by Mircea Eliade.]]
Colonial chronicles and idolatry removal documents give us an idea of the meanings of huancas in the original Andean world. Rodrigo Hernández Príncipe, an idolatry visitor who lived between 1578 and 1638, told of the existence of a huanca in early Colony in Peru in the Callejón de Huaylas area:

{{Quote|text="... a un cuarto de legua desta población muy antigua donde en un adoratorio rodeado de cantería y en medio hecho un caracol estaba su respetada y principal Huanca llamada Llamoq que era una piedra al modo de una calavera tan pesada como fiera que mirarla ponia horror estaba rodeada de muchos sacrificios adoráanla con airjuas y trompetas estos llactas y era huaca e la madre del cacique y los deste ayllo dijeron proceder de esta huaca..."|author=Hernandez Principe 1622 <ref>{{Cite book|title= Espacio y Cronología en la sierra de Norte del Perú: Balance de las Investigaciones Arqueológicas en la Provincia de Huari|last= Ibarra Asencios|first= Bebel|publisher= Instituto de Estudios Huarinos|year= 2013|isbn=|location=|pages= 42}}</ref>|title=|source=}}

The term huaca today is popularly used to designate an [[archaeological site]]. However, according to ethnohistomical documents this concept referred to the sacrality of different things: stones, trees, places of nature, temples, people, animals and human experiences such as crying. Therefore, huancas are huacas: stones considered sacred to which [[rite]]s and offerings were dedicated, had their own name and even indicated the place of origin of a community.

Likewise, according to a quote from another idolatry remover, the Spanish [[Jesuit]] Pablo José de Arriaga (who claimed to have destroyed 189 Huancas monoliths), who lived between 1564 and 1622, it follows that the stone represents the ancestors, who act as intermediaries between the terrestrial world and the divine world, and assist and complement the agricultural work by 'fertilising' the land.

{{Quote|text="Chíchic o Huanca llaman una piedra larga, que suelen poner empinada en sus Chácaras, y la llaman también Chacrayoc, que es el Señor de la Chácara, porque piensan que aquella Chácra fué de aquella Huaca, y que tiene a cargo su augmento, y como tal la reverencian, y especialmente en tiempo de las sementeras le ofrecen sus sacrificios".|author=Arriaga|title=|source=1621<ref>Arriaga 1920 [1621]: 21.</ref>}}

The cult of huancas was an Andean manifestation of the cult of the ancestors, which are a set of universal religious practices centered on the ancestors under the belief that they continue to care for their descendants. In the Andean world, each collective or ayllu had its own ancestors, and in the greater rank of these the founding ancestor was positioned, often depicted in stone as a huanca. Since the Andean worldview is [[Animism|animistic]], the hills, lagoons and stones have life and are therefore subjects of worship. Centuries later, according to current studies, other practices such as the cult of the Mallqui (momias) are incorporated in chullpas, such as in the necropolis of Marcajirca in the province of Huari in Peru.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Ibarra Asencios|first=Bebel|title="Marcajirca: Cronología, Función y Muerte". En Ibarra Asencios, Bebel, ed. Historia Prehispánica de Huari: Desde Chavín hasta los Inkas 3000 años de Historia (Huari (Áncash, Perú)|publisher=Instituto de Estudios Huarinos)|year=2009|isbn=978-603-45405-0-7|location=|pages=63–76|language=Spanish}}</ref>

== Types and Functions ==
Huancas are typed accordingly:

by morphology:

* By their individual shape: [[prismatic]], [[rectangular]], [[lanceolate]], [[Table (landform)|tabular]], [[cube|cubic]], [[cylindrical]] and irregular.<ref name=":0">Bazán del Campo 2007: 8-9.</ref><ref>Sánchez García 2015: 9.</ref>
* By their finish: smooth or tilled.<ref name=":1">Sánchez García 2015: 4.</ref>
* By their arrangement: in parallel rows, circular wall with rubbers, platform with rubber and rubber with pedestal.<ref name=":0" />
* Natural huancas: rocks and hills, and boulders (natural but moved).<ref name=":0" />

By their function:<ref>Bazán del Campo 2007: 9-12.</ref>

* Huancas of the peoples: the huancas called Marcayoc that symbolized the founding ancestor thus occupying a tutelar function.<ref name=":2">Duviols 1979: 9.</ref>
* Huancas de las sementeras: Chacrayoc huancas, symbolizing fertility, increasing and protecting crops in the chakra.<ref name=":2" />
* Huancas of the waters or heights: they are located near the lagoons and rivers, fulfilling a conducive role for abundance.<ref name=":1" />
* Huancas of the roads: they are located at points on the roads that demarcate a beginning, an end or a high point, fulfilling a function similar to the Apaches and then to the Inca [[ushnu]]s of the Late Horizon.<ref>Albornoz en Duviols 1967: 24.</ref>
* Huancas of the temples: they are located in isolated temples or near the villages, in some cases the temple develops according to the cult of the huanca, as in [[Chavín de Huántar]] and in some buildings of sites such as Caral.<ref name=":3">{{Cite book|last=Shady Solís|first=Ruth|title=Caral. La civilización más antigua de las Américas: 15 años develando su historia,|last2=Cáceda Guillén|first2=Daniel|last3=Crispín Balta|first3=Aldemar|last4=Machacuay Romero|first4=Marco|last5=Novoa Bellota|first5=Pedro|last6=Quispe Loayza|first6=Edna|publisher=Ministerio de Cultura del Perú|year=2012|isbn=978-612-45179-0-7|location=Lima: Zona Arqueológica Caral-Supe|pages=34|language=Spanish}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|last=Middendorf|first=Ernst W|title=Perú: observaciones y estudios del país y sus habitantes durante una permanencia de 25 años.|publisher=Universidad Nacional Mayor de San Marcos.|year=1900|isbn=5222026|location=|pages=77|language=Spanish}}</ref>

== Emblematic Huancas ==
[[File:PeruCaral14.jpg|thumb|300x300px|Huanca Stone in [[Caral]].]]

'''La Huanca de [[Caral]]'''

Another important huanca is located in front of one of the pyramidal buildings of Caral, an archaeological site on the central north coast of Peru, in the district of Supe, [[Barranca Province]]. In the Plazuela de La Huanca, at the front of the facade of one of the pyramids (Pyramidal Building La Huanca, Sector I in Caral Alto) is precisely a swell on the ground and is 2.15 m high.<ref name=":3" />

'''La Huanca de [[Chavín de Huantar|Chavín]]'''

The most emblematic huanca in the Andes of Peru is popularly known as the monolithic Lanzón, belonging to the archaeological [[Chavín culture]] that developed during the Early Horizon. It was the German explorer {{ILL|Ernst Wilhelm Middendorf|de}} who picked up the name ''huanca'' for perhaps the most important element of the [[Chavín de Huántar]] ceremonial center when he visited the village of the same name in the late nineteenth century.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Middendorf|first=Ernst Wilhelm|title=PERU; Beobachtungen und Studien über das Land und seine Bewohner während eines 25 Jährigen Aufenthalts [PERÚ: Observaciones y estudios del pais y sus habitantes durante una permanencia de 25 años] (en alemán), Berlín: Meisenbach Riffarth & Co|publisher=|year=1895|isbn=978-1138898998|location=|pages=77|language=German}}</ref>
