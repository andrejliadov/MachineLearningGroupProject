The '''Max Born Medal and Prize''' is a scientific prize awarded yearly by the [[Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft|German Physical Society]] (DPG) and the British [[Institute of Physics]] (IOP) in memory of the German physicist [[Max Born]],<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WG8-z6arzyAC&pg=PA97 |title=In the Wings of Physics |first=Maurice|last=Jacob |date=January 25, 1995 |publisher=World Scientific|via=Google Books|page=97|isbn=9789810221782 }}</ref> who was a German-Jewish physicist, instrumental in the development of [[quantum mechanics]]. It was established in 1972 after the death of Born in 1970,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newsheads.in/science/news/max-born-google-doodle-all-you-need-to-know-about-the-german-physicist-article-10286 |title=Google Doodle celebrates Max Born's 135th birthday: All you need to know |date=11 December 2017 |publisher=News Heads}}</ref> and first awarded in 1973.

The terms of the award are that it is "to be presented for outstanding contributions to [[physics]]". The award goes to physicists based in Germany and in the UK or Ireland in alternate years.<ref name="iop medal">{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/bilateral/born/page_38469.html |title=The Born medal and prize |publisher=[[Institute of Physics]] |access-date=25 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="dpg medal">{{cite web |title=Max-Born-Preis und -Medaille |trans-title=Max Born Prize and Medal |publisher=[[Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft|German Physical Society]] |url=https://www.dpg-physik.de/auszeichnungen/dpg-preise-mit-anderen-organisationen/max-born-preis-und-medaille/max-born?set_language=en |access-date=25 January 2020}}</ref> The prize is accompanied by a silver medal "about 6 cm in diameter and 0.5 cm thick. One face carries a profile of Max Born and his name and dates. The other face carries the equation pq - qp = h/2πi and the full names of IOP and DPG. The recipient's full name and year of award is engraved around the rim." The medal is accompanied by €3000.<ref name="dpg medal" />
