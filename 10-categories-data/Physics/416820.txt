The '''Albert Einstein World Award for Science''' is an annual award given by the [[World Cultural Council]] "as a means of recognition and encouragement for scientific and technological research and development", with special consideration for researches which "have brought true benefit and well being to mankind".<ref name= official>{{cite web |title= Albert Einstein World Award of Science |url= http://www.consejoculturalmundial.org/awards/world-award-of-science/ | website= consejoculturalmundial.org | publisher= World Cultural Council | date= 27 July 2015 | accessdate=14 August 2013}}</ref> Named for [[physics|physicist]] and theoretician [[Albert Einstein]] (1879–1955); the award includes a diploma, a commemorative medal, and [[United States dollar|$]]10,000.<ref name=UGA>{{cite web | url= https://research.uga.edu/research-announcements/2016/09/27/wcc-awards/ | title= Awards & Honors: 2017 World Cultural Council Arts and Science Awards | date= 27 September 2016 | website= UGA.edu | publisher= Office of Research, [[University of Georgia]] | access-date= 7 November 2016 | archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20161108084140/https://research.uga.edu/research-announcements/2016/09/27/wcc-awards/ | archive-date= 8 November 2016 | url-status= dead | df= dmy-all }}</ref>

The recipient of the award is evaluated and elected by an Interdisciplinary Committee, which is composed of world-renowned scientists, among them 25 [[Nobel Prize|Nobel]] laureates.<ref name= official />
