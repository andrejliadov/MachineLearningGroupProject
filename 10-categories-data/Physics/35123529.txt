The following [[Outline (list)|outline]] is provided as an overview of, and topical guide to, applied physics:

'''[[Applied physics]]''' &ndash; [[physics]] intended for a particular technological or practical use.<ref>{{cite web|title=Applied Physics|url=http://www.articleworld.org/index.php/Applied_physics|work=ArticleWorld|accessdate=10 September 2011}}</ref> 
It is usually considered as a bridge or a connection between "pure" physics and [[engineering]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Applied Physics at Caltech - Overview|url=http://www.aph.caltech.edu/overview.html|publisher=Caltech|accessdate=10 September 2011}}</ref>

'''[[Applied Physics]]''' &ndash; is the proper name of a journal founded and edited by Helmut K.V. Lotsch in 1972 and published by Springer-Verlag Berlin Heidelberg New York from 1973 on<ref>Heinz Götze: Der Springer-Verlag - Stationen seiner Geschichte, Teil 2: 1945 - 1992, Springer-Verlag Berlin Heidelberg 1994, S. 332 and 331 mit Foto</ref>

'''[[Topics in Applied Physics]]''' &ndash; is the proper name of a series of quasi-monographs founded by Helmut K.V. Lotsch and published by Springer-Verlag Berlin Heidelberg New York<ref>Heinz Götze: Der Springer-Verlag - Stationen seiner Geschichte, Teil 2: 1945 - 1992, Springer-Verlag Berlin Heidelberg 1994, S. 334</ref>
