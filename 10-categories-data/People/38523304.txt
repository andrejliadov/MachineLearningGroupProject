'''General Souleymane Kelefa Diallo''' (December 2, 1959 - February 11, 2013) was chief of staff of the [[Guinean Army]]. Born to [[Elhadj]] Kelefa Diallo and Fatoumata Diakité. He was a graduate of the [[University of Conakry]] and an army school in [[Thies, Senegal.]]  He and several military officials were killed on February 11, 2013, when their [[CASA/IPTN CN-235|CASA 235]] crashed near the town of Charlesville, near [[Harbel]], [[Liberia]], about 8 kilometers away from [[Roberts International Airport]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/02/201321110422385837.html |title=Guinea army chief dies in Liberia air crash |author= |accessdate=2013-02-14 |date=2013-02-11}}</ref> At his funeral, which took place at the [[Palais du Peuple (Guinea)|Palais du Peuple]] he was decorated by the President [[Alpha Condé]].  Diallo was one of the leaders who had seized power in [[Guinea]] in 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://theinquirer.com.lr/content1.php?main=news&news_id=1664 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130413143725/http://theinquirer.com.lr/content1.php?main=news&news_id=1664 |url-status=dead |archive-date=2013-04-13 |title=Guinean Army Chief, Several Killed...Pres. Sirleaf Declares Day Of Mourning; Defense Cancels Recepti |accessdate=2013-02-14 |date=2013-02-12 }}</ref>
