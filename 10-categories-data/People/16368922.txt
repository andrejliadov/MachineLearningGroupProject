'''John Lowe''' (6 August 1912 – 16 August 1995) was a [[Association football|footballer]] and [[List of Hamilton Academical F.C. managers|manager]] for [[Hamilton Academical F.C.|Hamilton Academical]].<ref name=litsterprewar>{{cite journal|title=A Record of pre-war Scottish League Players|author=John Litster|publisher=Scottish Football Historian magazine|date=October 2012}}</ref><ref>[https://sites.google.com/site/hamiltonacademicalmemorybank/players/Lowe-john-1935 Lowe, John (1935)], Hamilton Academical Memory Bank</ref>

==Biography==
Lowe, a [[Defender (association football)|centre-half]], signed for [[Hamilton Academical F.C.|Hamilton Academical]] in August 1935 from [[Royal Albert F.C.|Royal Albert]]. He also played for [[Galston F.C.|Galston]], and [[Clyde F.C.|Clyde]]. He scored on his debut for ''Accies'' in January 1936 against [[Airdrieonians F.C. (1878)|Airdrieonians]]. Hamilton finished sixth in the [[Scottish Football League First Division|First Division]] in [[1935–36 in Scottish football|1935–36]], eighth in [[1936–37 in Scottish football|1936–37]], 13th in [[1937–38 in Scottish football|1937–38]], and seventh in [[1938–39 in Scottish football|1938–39]].

Lowe served with the [[Royal Air Force]] during the [[World War II|Second World War]]. He was also a member of the Tommy Walker XI Touring Team which toured [[India]] and [[Sri Lanka|Ceylon]] in 1945. Hamilton allowed Lowe to sign for [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]] in 1940 as he was stationed there. In April 1945 he also guested for [[Port Vale F.C.|Port Vale]], where he played two games.<ref name="kent">{{cite book|last=Kent|first=Jeff|title=Port Vale Personalities|publisher=Witan Books|page=178|year=1996|isbn=0-9529152-0-0|url=https://www.amazon.ca/Port-Vale-Personalities-Jeff-Kent/dp/0952915200}}</ref> He also guested for [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] and [[Aberdeen F.C.|Aberdeen]].

Lowe became Hamilton's Club Secretary in 1951 and was appointed as assistant manager at [[Douglas Park]] in 1953. He became manager in 1956 after replacing former teammate [[Jackie Cox (footballer)|Jacky Cox]]. He led the [[Scottish Football League Second Division|Second Division]] club to 11th and 10th place in [[1956–57 in Scottish football|1956–57]] and [[1957–58 in Scottish football|1957–58]]. However, he resigned from the position in 1958, and was replaced by [[Andy Paton]].

After retiring from football, Lowe ran a fruit merchants business in [[Hamilton, South Lanarkshire|Hamilton]]. At the time of his death, on 16 August 1995, he was residing in [[Lochranza]] on the [[Isle of Arran]].
