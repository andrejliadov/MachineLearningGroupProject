'''Benoni Hill''' (c. 1787 - 31 October 1841) was a [[Publican (United KIngdom)|publican]] and [[List of freemen of the City of Gloucester|freeman]] of the [[City of Gloucester]].

==Early life and family==
Benoni Hill was born in Gloucestershire<ref>[https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:M7M6-5V9 Benoni Hill England and Wales Census, 1841.] Family Search. Retrieved 25 June 2019. {{subscription required}}</ref> around 1787, the son of Elijah Hill, a [[victualler]],<ref name=rip/> and his wife Eleanor. He was christened at [[St Mary de Lode Church, Gloucester|St Mary-de-Lode Church]], Gloucester, in 1790.<ref>[https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:NVLB-JYB Benoni Hill England Births and Christenings, 1538-1975.] Family Search. Retrieved 25 June 2019. {{subscription required}}</ref>

He married Charlotte Spencer in Bristol in 1814.<ref>[https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:NFRK-SZW Benoni Hill England Marriages, 1538â€“1973.] Family Search. Retrieved 25 June 2019. {{subscription required}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Gloucester, Bull Lane - geograph.org.uk - 1091382.jpg|thumb|Modern view of Bull Lane]]
Hill was the landlord of the Bull Inn public house in Bull Lane.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=XA8HAAAAQAAJ&q=Benoni+Hill+Gloucester&pg=PA107 |title = The Gloucestershire directory, by R. Gell & T. Bradshaw|last1 = Gell|first1 = R.|last2 = )|first2 = Thomas Bradshaw (of Belfast|year = 1820}}</ref> He was renowned as the "greatest wit" in the City of Gloucester, and was said to consume 28 pints of his home-brewed beer each day.<ref name=water>"Many watering holes are sadly now gone", ''[[The Citizen (Gloucester)|The Citizen]]'', 2 May 2019, p. 29.</ref>

He was one of the witnesses that exposed the perjury of the Italian valet [[Teodoro Maiocchi]] during the trial of [[Caroline of Brunswick|Queen Caroline]] in claiming that he had never visited England before.<ref>https://archive.org/details/gloucestershireb00straiala/page/186</ref><ref>https://archive.org/stream/cu31924030494953#page/n233/mode/2up/search/Maiocchi</ref>

On 8 July 1813, he was appointed a freeman of the City of Gloucester during the mayorship of [[Edward Weaver]].<ref name=rip>Ripley, Peter, & John Jurica (Ed.) (1991) ''A Calendar of the Registers of the Freemen of the City of Gloucester 1641-1838''. [[Bristol and Gloucestershire Archaeological Society]]. p. 208. {{ISBN|0900197323}}</ref>

==Death==
Hill died in Gloucester on 31 October 1841 at the age of 54.<ref name=water/><ref>https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:2NX1-1T4</ref>
