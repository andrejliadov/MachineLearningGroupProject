'''Antoine Franklin McColister''' (born May 11, 1988),<ref>https://www.complex.com/music/2018/05/ace-hood-celebrates-30th-birthday-trust-the-process-2-undefeated</ref> better known by his [[stage name]] '''Ace Hood''', is an American rapper. He was born in [[Port St. Lucie, Florida]] and raised in [[Deerfield Beach, Florida]]. He was initially signed to [[DJ Khaled]]'s label [[We the Best Music Group]] in 2008.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite news|last=Concepcion|first=Mariel|title=Ace Hood|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1045337/ace-hood|work=Billboard|date=2008-05-20}}</ref> He has released four studio albums with his most successful of which being ''[[Blood, Sweat & Tears (Ace Hood album)|Blood, Sweat & Tears]]'' (2011) and ''[[Trials & Tribulations (Ace Hood album)|Trials & Tribulations]]'' (2013). The albums contained his highest charting singles, "[[Hustle Hard]]" and "[[Bugatti (song)|Bugatti]]", respectively. In 2016, McColister announced his departure from We the Best Music and is currently working as an independent artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://respect-mag.com/2017/08/ace-hood-interview/|publisher=[[Respect. (magazine)|Respect]]|author=White, Omari|title=Ace Hood Talks New Music, Evolution as an Artist & Life After 'We The Best'|date=August 24, 2017|access-date=July 2, 2018}}</ref>

==Early life==
Ace Hood was born in Port St. Lucie, Florida, and was raised by his mother with his younger cousin Ty Barton Jr. in Deerfield Beach, part of Broward County. He graduated from [[Deerfield Beach High School]]. Following a football injury in the 10th grade, and after realizing he wouldn't be able to go pro, the Broward County native began to seriously consider rapping as a career. Hood teamed up with a local group called Dollaz & Dealz and released a single titled "M.O.E." in 2006. He also began promoting himself via open mic events and talent shows around town.<ref name="Billboard" /><ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p1056083/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Ace Hood > Biography|last=Cordor|first=Cyril|year=2008|website=allmusic|access-date=2009-03-10}}</ref>

==Musical career==

===2006–08: Career beginnings and ''Gutta''===
[[File:Ace Hood at Howard University.jpg|thumb|left|Ace Hood at Howard University in 2008]]
In 2007, he met [[DJ Khaled]] outside the office of the [[WEDR|WEDR 99 Jamz]] radio station. After Hood gave Khaled an autobiography and his demo tape, Khaled asked Hood to do a freestyle over the instrumental of his song "[[I'm So Hood]]" from his second album, ''[[We the Best]]'', and later signed Hood to his [[We the Best Music Group]] [[record label|label]].<ref name="Reid 2008"/> Hood was named, along with several others, on the ''Freshmen of '09'' by [[XXL (magazine)|''XXL'' magazine]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Check out the (3) Cover for XXL's Dec. Issue (#109) |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=27195 |work=XXL |date=2008-10-20 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081105144134/http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=27195 |archive-date=2008-11-05 }}</ref>

His first album, ''Gutta'', was released in 2008. Singles included "[[Cash Flow (song)|Cash Flow]]" featuring [[T-Pain]] and [[Rick Ross]], and "[[Ride (Ace Hood song)|Ride]]" featuring [[Trey Songz]]. Hood also released mixtapes called ''Ace Won't Fold'' and ''All Bets On Ace''. He made a guest performance among several rappers on DJ Khaled's single "[[Out Here Grindin']]", also featuring [[Akon]], Rick Ross, [[Plies (rapper)|Plies]], [[Lil Boosie]] and [[Trick Daddy]], which peaked at number 38 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="Reid 2008">{{cite news|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|title=Shawty Lo Wants To Confiscate T.I.'s 'Hood Pass; DJ Khaled Banks On His New Artist Ace|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1587247/20080509/id_0.jhtml|work=Mixtape Monday|publisher=MTV News|date=2008-05-09}}</ref> It is from Khaled's third studio album ''[[We Global]]'', and was eventually certified gold by the [[RIAA]]. Ace Hood appeared on Wildstyle Radio on [[WUAG]] 103.1FM in [[Greensboro, North Carolina]], on September 7, 2008, to promote his album, ''[[Gutta (album)|Gutta]]'', along with DJ Khaled.<ref>[http://photos-g.ak.fbcdn.net/photos-ak-sf2p/v323/79/46/1393560204/n1393560204_30411518_520.jpg ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110726152111/http://photos-g.ak.fbcdn.net/photos-ak-sf2p/v323/79/46/1393560204/n1393560204_30411518_520.jpg |date=July 26, 2011 }}</ref>  The album debuted at number 36 on the ''Billboard'' 200, selling 25,000 copies its first week.

===2009–12: ''Ruthless'' and ''Blood, Sweat, & Tears''===
On June 30, 2009, seven months later, Hood released his second album, titled [[Ruthless (Ace Hood album)|''Ruthless'']], once again on Def Jam. The album's first single was [[Overtime (Ace Hood song)|"Overtime"]], which was produced by The Runners and featured Akon and T-Pain. The album's second single is "Loco Wit The Cake", which was produced by Schife, of the Palm Beach County Karbeen Mafia. The album's guest list includes Rick Ross, [[Ludacris]], [[Jazmine Sullivan]], [[The-Dream]], [[Birdman (entertainer)|Birdman]], and [[Lloyd (singer)|Lloyd]].  The album debuted at number 23 on the ''Billboard'' 200, selling 20,000 copies its first week.

Hood confirmed that his third album will be called, ''[[Blood, Sweat & Tears (Ace Hood album)|Blood, Sweat & Tears]]''. It was released on August 9, 2011. The first official single, "[[Hustle Hard]]", became Ace's highest charting solo single, peaking at number 60 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. The second official single is "[[Go 'N' Get It]]". Both tracks are produced by [[Lex Luger (producer)|Lex Luger]]. The third single is "[[Body 2 Body (song)|Body 2 Body]]" featuring [[Chris Brown (American entertainer)|Chris Brown]] and the song is produced by [[J.U.S.T.I.C.E. League]]. It peaked at number 65 on the Hot 100. The album features artists like [[T-Pain]], [[Yo Gotti]], [[Kevin Cossom]], Rick Ross & [[Lil Wayne]]. The album debuted at number 8 on the ''Billboard'' 200, selling 26,000 copies, becoming the rapper's most successful album so far. He promoted the album with his first concert tour, the Hustle Hard Tour.<ref>{{cite web|last=Paine |first=Jake |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.16772/title.ace-hood-plans-hustle-hard-tour-dates-revealed |title=Ace Hood Plans Hustle Hard Tour, Dates Revealed &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2011-09-07 |access-date=2012-03-30}}</ref> He also appeared on three tracks from DJ Khaled's fifth studio album, ''[[We the Best Forever]]'': "I'm Thuggin'", "Future", and the "[[Welcome to My Hood|Welcome to My Hood (Remix)]]".

Hood started 2012 by collaborating with [[Cash Money Records]] artist [[Bow Wow (rapper)|Bow Wow]] on a song called "We Going Hard". He will be appearing on [[Maybach Music Group|Maybach Music]]'s ''[[Self Made Vol. 2]]'' album. Birdman and [[DJ Khaled]] signed Hood and [[We The Best Music Group]] to Cash Money Records. Hood will be recording his fourth studio album under [[We The Best Music Group|We The Best]] and Cash Money Records. He has made guest appearances on tracks by other recording artists such s [[Talib Kweli]], [[Jadakiss]], [[Brisco (rapper)|Brisco]], and "The Drill" by [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]], also featuring [[Meek Mill]], which appears on his ''[[California Republic (mixtape)|California Republic]]''.

===2013–present: ''Trials & Tribulations''===
For his debut single on the We The Best & Cash Money label, he reunited with [[Trey Songz]] after 4 years, to make the early-2000s-styled love single known as "I Need Your Love". Hood released his mixtape ''Starvation 2'' on January 10, 2013. The mixtape features guest appearances from [[Meek Mill]], [[French Montana]] and [[Plies (rapper)|Plies]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Horowitz |first=Steven J. |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22481/title.ace-hood-starvation-2-mixtape-tracklist-cover-art |title=Ace Hood "Starvation 2" Mixtape Tracklist & Cover Art &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-01-09 |access-date=2014-08-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130527205341/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22481/title.ace-hood-starvation-2-mixtape-tracklist-cover-art |archive-date=2013-05-27 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On January 16, 2013, Hood announced his fourth studio album titled ''[[Trials & Tribulations (Ace Hood album)|Trials & Tribulations]]'' and released the first single from the album titled "[[Bugatti (song)|Bugatti]]" featuring [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and Rick Ross. The song is produced by [[Mike WiLL Made It]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/singles/id.23507/title.ace-hood-f-rick-ross-future-bugatti- |title=Ace Hood f. Rick Ross & Future - Bugatti &#124; New Hip Hop Music & All The New Rap Songs 2011 |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-01-17 |access-date=2014-08-09}}</ref> On February 6, 2013, the music video was released for "Bugatti" featuring Future and Rick Ross.<ref>{{cite web|title=Video: Ace Hood f/ Future & Rick Ross – 'Bugatti'|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/02/07/video-ace-hood-f-future-rick-ross-bugatti/|work=[[Rap-Up]]|publisher=Devin Lazerine|access-date=February 7, 2013|date=February 7, 2013}}</ref> The song made a hot-shot debut on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart at number 42. "Bugatti" has since peaked at number 33 on the Hot 100, becoming Hood's most successful single to date as a lead artist, and his first top 40 hit since "Out Here Grindin'". On February 19, 2013, it was announced that ''Trials & Tribulations'' would be released on July 16, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapradar.com/2013/03/08/ace-hood-trials-tribulations-trailer/ |title=Ace Hood Trials & Tribulations Trailer |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2013-03-08 |access-date=2014-08-09}}</ref>

On April 7, 2013, in an interview on Power 99 Philly with [[Mina SayWhat]] about his "Trials and Tribulations" album, Hood opens up about his family and deciding to go a different route with this album, working with [[Anthony Hamilton (musician)|Anthony Hamilton]] and [[John Legend]] and plans to get [[Lil Wayne]] and [[Kendrick Lamar]] to feature on the album too.<ref>{{cite web|last=Meara |first=Paul |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23494/title.ace-hood-explains-trials-tribulations-title-gives-update-on-we-the-best- |title=Ace Hood "Trials & Tribulations" Release Date, Cover Art & Tracklist &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-06-26 |access-date=2014-08-09}}</ref> On May 6, 2013, Hood released the remix to "Bugatti" featuring guests [[DJ Khaled]], Future, Meek Mill, [[T.I.]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Birdman (rapper)|Birdman]], [[French Montana]] and [[2 Chainz]]. On June 5, 2013, Hood released the second  single called "[[We Outchea]]" featuring his Cash Money label-mate Lil Wayne. On June 26, 2013, the final track listing was revealed featuring appearances from Meek Mill, Anthony Hamilton, [[Chris Brown (American entertainer)|Chris Brown]] and [[Betty Wright]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/06/heres-the-tracklist-to-ace-hoods-trials-tribulations/ |title=Here's The Tracklist To Ace Hood's 'Trials & Tribulations' - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2013-06-26 |access-date=2013-07-16}}</ref>

Hood released his ''Starvation 3'' mixtape on January 17, 2014. The free project included features from We The Best label-mates [[Vado (rapper)|Vado]] and [[Mavado (singer)|Mavado]], as well as [[Betty Wright]] and [[Kevin Cossom]]. Production for the mixtape came from [[Cool & Dre]], The Renegades, StreetRunner, and The Beat Bully, among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ace-hood-starvation-3-new-mixtape.105691.html |title=Ace Hood - Starvation 3 &#124; Download & Listen [New Mixtape&#93; |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=2014-01-17 |access-date=2014-08-09}}</ref> ''Body Bag 3'' was released on August 29.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ace-hood-body-bag-3-new-mixtape.115468.html |title=Ace Hood - Body Bag 3 &#124; Download & Listen [New Mixtape&#93; |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=2014-08-29 |access-date=2014-11-19}}</ref> Two months after that, Hood would team up with [[Rich Homie Quan]] for a return single titled "We Don't".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ace-hood-we-don-t-feat-rich-homie-quan-new-song.1958648.html |title=Ace Hood - We Don't Feat. Rich Homie Quan &#124; Download & Listen [New Song&#93; |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=2014-10-24 |access-date=2014-11-19}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Ace Hood is a [[Christians|Christian]] and states that he "highly believe[s] in God".<ref name="Smith2011">{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Jay |title=A Few Minutes With Ace Hood |url=https://www.pollstar.com/article/a-few-minutes-with-ace-hood-21372 |publisher=[[Pollstar]] |access-date=7 January 2020 |language=en |date=7 January 2011}}</ref>

Hood and his previous girlfriend, Shanice Tyria Sarratt, had twin girls, Lyric and Sailor Blu McColister, on May 25, 2011. Lyric died shortly after birth due to health complications. He also has a son.<ref>{{cite web|last=SOHH.com |title=Ace Hood Discusses Daughter's Death, 'I Know God Does Everything For A Reason' |url=http://www.sohh.com/2012/03/ace_hood_discusses_daughters_death_i_kno.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130315063118/http://www.sohh.com/2012/03/ace_hood_discusses_daughters_death_i_kno.html |archive-date=2013-03-15 }}</ref>
He proposed to longtime girlfriend Shelah Marie in April 2019; they got married on February 7, 2020.
