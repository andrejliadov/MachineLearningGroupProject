This is a list of oldest and youngest [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] winners and nominees in the award categories "acting" and "directing".

This list is based on "statistics valid through the nomination announcement for the [[88th Academy Awards|2015 (88th Academy Awards)]], announced on January 14, 2016", as documented in ''The Official Academy Awards Database''.<ref name=AAD>{{cite web|author=[[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] |title=''The Official Academy Awards Database'' |url=http://awardsdatabase.oscars.org/ampas_awards/ |archive-url=https://archive.is/20120701144137/http://awardsdatabase.oscars.org/ampas_awards/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-07-01 |publisher=oscars.org ([[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences|AMPAS]]) |date=2008-02-24 |accessdate=2008-07-27 |quote=The [searchable] Academy Awards Database contains the record of past Academy Award winners and nominees. The data is complete through the [[81st Academy Awards|2008 (81st) Academy Awards]]. ... The ["Academy Award Statistics" in the "Directing" and "Acting" sections of the ''Awards Database''] are based on the most reliable birthdate information available to us at the time of publishing. We welcome any information from primary sources that might indicate a different birthdate for an individual. Such sources might be birth or death certificates or databases based on such records, published interviews with the individual or published documents that cite their sources. This would ''not'' include the [[Internet Movie Database]] or other [[World Wide Web|web]] sites that do not cite primary sources for their information. }} [[Nota bene|N.B.]]: Ages (including for those persons receiving nominations and/or awards posthumously) are computed by the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] (AMPAS); for nominations, all ages are computed "from date of birth to date of nominations announcement"; for awards won, all ages are computed "from date of birth to date of awards ceremony."</ref>

At the [[90th Academy Awards]], [[James Ivory]] became the oldest ever Oscar winner in any category at the age of 89 after receiving the award for [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay|Best Adapted Screenplay]] for his work on ''[[Call Me by Your Name (film)|Call Me by Your Name]]''.<ref name="Guardian">{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2018/mar/05/james-ivory-is-oldest-oscar-winner-ever-with-adapted-screenplay-award-for-call-me-by-your-name |title=James Ivory is oldest Oscar winner ever with screenplay award for Call Me by Your Name |newspaper=The Guardian |accessdate= March 5, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goldderby.com/article/2018/2018-baftas-james-ivory-interview-best-adapted-screenplay-winner/ |work=Goldderby |title=2018 BAFTA Awards backstage: James Ivory ('Call Me By Your Name') on his way to making Oscar history |date=2018-02-18 }}</ref> There are only two people in Oscar history to have won two Oscars before the age of 30; [[Luise Rainer]] for [[The Great Ziegfeld|''The Great Ziegfeld'']] and [[The Good Earth (film)|''The Good Earth'']] and [[Jodie Foster]] for ''[[The Accused (1988 film)|The Accused]]'' and [[The Silence of the Lambs (film)|''The Silence of the Lambs'']] [[Academy Award for Best Actress|Best Actress in a Leading Role]].
