'''Aristonicus''' (Greek Ἀριστόνικος Aristonikos) of [[Carystus]] was a  ball player (sphairistes) in [[Alexander the Great]]'s entourage.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=NR4Wn9VU8vkC&pg=PT64#v=onepage&q&f=false|title=Who's Who in the Age of Alexander the Great: Prosopography of Alexander's Empire|page=64}}</ref> He was granted [[Athens|Athenian]] citizenship as well as a statue due to his athletic prowess.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nES4Cr-nK7IC&pg=PA296#v=onepage&q&f=false|page=296|title=A Smaller Dictionary of Greek and Roman Antiquities|author=Wiliam Smith}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=MuXOAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA40#v=onepage&q&f=false|page=40|author=Benjamin Ide Wheeler|title=Alexander the Great}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/treatisesonprov00whitgoog|page=[https://archive.org/details/treatisesonprov00whitgoog/page/n278 270]|title=Treatises On Providence|publisher=American Book Company|year=1877|author=Lucius Annaeus Seneca|author-link=Lucius Annaeus Seneca}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Brushing Up Sports|author=Laufer|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/2906161/the_anniston_star/|page=8|date=December 9, 1929|accessdate=July 29, 2015|via=[[Newspapers.com]]|work=The Anniston Star}} {{Open access}}</ref>
