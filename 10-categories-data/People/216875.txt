'''Anwar ul-Haq Ahady''' (August 12, 1951) is a [[politics of Afghanistan|politician]] in [[Afghanistan]], formerly serving as [[Council of Ministers (Afghanistan)|Afghan Minister of  Commerce and Industry]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pajhwok.com/en/2011/02/12/landmark-afghan-pak-trade-pact-goes-effect |title=Landmark Afghan-Pak trade pact goes into effect |editor=Pashtun Shinwari |publisher=[[Pajhwok Afghan News]] |date=February 12, 2011 |accessdate=2011-02-13}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tolonews.com/en/election-2014/12278-presidential-candidate-ahadi-against-karzais-loya-jirga |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2014-11-12 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141112154842/http://www.tolonews.com/en/election-2014/12278-presidential-candidate-ahadi-against-karzais-loya-jirga |archivedate=2014-11-12 }}</ref> He had served as the nation's [[Ministry of Finance (Afghanistan)|Finance Minister]] from December 2004 to February 5, 2009. Prior to that he held the position as head of [[Da Afghanistan Bank]], the [[central bank]] of Afghanistan, from 2002 to 2004. He is also prominent [[academic]] and writer.<ref name="MOF">{{cite web|url=http://www.mof.gov.af/the-minister.htm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2010-07-01 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101103183456/http://www.mof.gov.af/the-minister.htm |archivedate=2010-11-03 }}</ref>

== Early years ==
Ahady was born on 12 August 1951 in [[Surobi, Kabul|Sarobi]], [[Kabul Province|Kabul province]], Afghanistan. He is the son of ''Abdul Haqhas'', a [[Pashtun people|Pashtun]] and native of the [[Surobi District (Kabul)|Sarobi district]] in Kabul province. Ahady holds a [[Master of Business Administration]] (MBA) and a [[Ph.D]] in [[political science]]<ref name="MOF"/> from the post-graduate faculty of [[Northwestern University]],<ref>http://www.northwestern.edu/magazine/northwestern/fall2003/features/ahady/index.htm</ref> in [[Evanston, Illinois]], north of [[Chicago]]. He earned a [[Bachelor's degree]] as well as a [[Master's degree]] in [[economics]] and [[political science]] from the [[American University of Beirut]] in [[Lebanon]].<ref name="MOF"/>

== Careers ==
Ahady served as an assistant professor of political science at [[Carleton College]], in [[Northfield, Minnesota]], in the [[United States]], and as the banking director of Continental Elona of Chicago from 1985 to 1987. He was a professor of political science at [[Providence College]], a [[Catholic Church|Catholic]] [[university]] in [[Providence, Rhode Island]], from 1987 to 2002. 

After the removal of the [[Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan|Taliban government]] and the formation of the [[Karzai administration]] in late 2001, Ahady was chosen as the head of Afghanistan's central bank, Da Afghanistan Bank. He has many writings in academic journals, books, and popular dailies of the United States.

== Political affiliation ==
Ahady is currently the elected leader of the [[Afghan Millat Party]].
