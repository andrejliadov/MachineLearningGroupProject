'''Sam Boardman-Jacobs''' (born 1942) is a Yorkshire born-Midlands & London raised  playwright,director, scenographer who now lives in France & Spain (after 25 years based in Wales UK) He is a director scenographer and recently choreographer, since receiving  a master's degree from Trinity/ Laban.  He now commutes between France, Spain and the UK.

==Biography==
Boardman-Jacobs was Reader in Theatre & Media Drama at the [[University of Glamorgan]]. His research interests include [[The Holocaust in popular culture#Theater|Holocaust drama]], [[Yiddish theatre]], [[:Category:LGBT theatre|gay and lesbian theatre]], Spanish playwright [[Federico García Lorca]], and the [[Spanish Civil War]].<ref>[http://www.glam.ac.uk/hass/1186/376  Staff Details - Sam Boardman-Jacobs] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20070702083158/http://www.glam.ac.uk/hass/1186/376 |date=2007-07-02 }} [http://www.glam.ac.uk University of Glamorgan] {{webarchive|url=https://www.webcitation.org/5QiptXTeQ?url=http://www.glam.ac.uk/ |date=2007-07-30 }}. Retrieved on April 18, 2007</ref> These interests are reflected in his plays.

He won acclaim<ref>[http://www.theatre-wales.co.uk/individuals/individuals_details.asp?individualID=100 Sam Boardman-Jacobs], [http://www.theatre-wales.co.uk/index.asp Theatre in Wales.] Retrieved on April 18, 2007</ref> for his work on Holocaust and Yiddish drama with the [[Manchester Youth Theatre]] and received a grant from the European Association of Jewish Culture in 2002 for his play ''Trying To Be'', an exploration of Jewish identity set in contemporary Britain.<ref>[http://www.jewishcultureineurope.org/bio.htm 2002 Grant Recipients], [http://www.jewishcultureineurope.org European Association for Jewish Culture]. Retrieved on April 18, 2007</ref> 
Sam recently took an MA in Choreography at Laban, London, and now makes choreographic dance theatre with  Found Reality Dance Theatre, Cardiff, of which he is artistic director.

''Play Federico For Me'' is the fictional story of Catalan actress [[Margarita Xirgu]], who, during her exile after the Spanish Civil War, depends upon the ghost of Federico García Lorca, in her political-artistic battle with [[Eva Perón]] over the first performance of Lorca's "The House of Bernarda Alba". His translation and adaptation of Lorca's ''[[El público]]'' was produced by the Found Reality Theatre Company in 2005. His 2007 radio play, ''The Sixth Column Has Better Legs'', describes the experiences of four chorus girls in [[Siege of Madrid|Madrid]] while the city is under siege.

''Passion for the Impossible'' tells the story of [[Violette Leduc]] and [[Jean Genet]] in wartime [[Paris]] and ''Red Hot and Blue'' is the story of singer [[Libby Holman]], on the night before her suicide, as she looks back over a life that included a murder trial, an affair with [[Montgomery Clift]] and early [[Civil rights movement|Civil Rights]] campaigning during the [[Second World War]].

In 2003 he taught for the Lemonia Disabled Writers' Residential Course, a project organised by [[Graeae Theatre Company]], Writernet and [[Tŷ Newydd]].<ref>[http://www.writernet.co.uk/projects__partners/recent_projects/lemonia_2003/lemonia_full_report.phtml  Lemonia - Full Report] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061001021153/http://www.writernet.co.uk/projects__partners/recent_projects/lemonia_2003/lemonia_full_report.phtml |date=2006-10-01 }}, [http://www.writernet.co.uk/php/map.php?id=294  Writernet] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070428090957/http://www.writernet.co.uk/php/map.php?id=294 |date=2007-04-28 }}. Retrieved on April 21, 2007</ref> The production of his 2004 play, ''Embracing Barbarians'', based on the political and sexual fantasies of dying Greek poet [[Constantine Cavafy]], Sam attempted to make the piece accessible to both deaf and hearing performers and audiences, while casting a deaf performer in the role of a hearing character.<ref>[http://www.theatre-wales.co.uk/plays/author_playlist.asp?author=Sam+Boardman-Jacobs|Sam Boardman-Jacobs - ''Embracing Barbarians'' synopsis] [http://www.theatre-wales.co.uk/index.asp Theatre in Wales.] Retrieved on April 21, 2007</ref>

He has taught on several  Writing Menoring and Dramaturgy courses at venues ranging from The Soho Theatre and  The Actor's centre London to the Arvon Foundation and Ty Newydd in North Wales and on  MA in Scriptwriting courses in Cardiff and Exeter. He now teaches Master classes in Scriptwriting and Dramaturgy in France and the UK.

He was also a scriptwriter for 12 years on [[BBC Radio 4]]'s ''[[The Archers]]'' and one of the  writers for [[Channel 4]]'s ''[[Brookside]]''. He translates from Spanish to English.
