This is a list of tables of the '''oldest people''' in the world in ordinal ranks. To avoid including [[Longevity claims|false or unconfirmed claims of old age]], names here are restricted to those people whose ages have been validated by an international body dealing in longevity research, such as the [[Gerontology Research Group]] (GRG) or ''[[Guinness World Records]]'' (GWR), and others who have otherwise been [[WP:RS|reliably sourced]].

According to this criterion, the longest human lifespan is that of [[Jeanne Calment]] of [[France]] (1875–1997), who lived to age 122 years and 164 days. She supposedly met [[Vincent van Gogh]] when she was 12 or 13.<ref name="Calment">{{cite news|last=Whitney|first=Craig R.|title=Jeanne Calment, World's Elder, Dies at 122|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9C01E7D7113DF936A3575BC0A961958260|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=5 August 1997|accessdate=2 March 2008}}</ref> She received news media attention in 1985, after turning 110. Subsequent investigation found documentation for Calment's age, beyond any reasonable question, in the records of her native city, [[Arles]], France.<ref>{{Cite book|authors=[[Jean-Marie Robine|Robine, Jean-Marie]]; Allard, Michel|year=1999|title=Jeanne Calment: Validation of the Duration of Her Life|url=http://www.demogr.mpg.de/books/odense/6/09.htm|location=Odense |publisher=[[University Press of Southern Denmark]]}}</ref> More evidence of Calment's lifespan has been produced than for any other [[supercentenarian]], such that her case serves as an [[prototype|archetype]] in the methodology for verifying the ages of the world's known oldest people.<ref name="Calmentthoroughly">{{cite web|last=Shaw|first=Michael D.|date=1 December 2008|title=You Say You Want to Live for a Long Time?|url=http://www.gasdetection.com/news2/health_news_digest184.html|publisher=Health News Digest}}</ref>{{Ref label|Calment|a}}

As [[Life expectancy#Sex differences|women live longer than men on average]], combined records for both sexes are predominated by women. The longest lifespan for a man is that of [[Jiroemon Kimura]] of [[Japan]] (1897–2013), who lived to age 116 years and 54 days.

Since the death of 117-year-old Chiyo Miyako of Japan on 22 July 2018, {{age|1903|1|2}}-year-old [[Kane Tanaka]], also of Japan, born 2 January 1903, is the oldest living person in the world whose age has been validated.  Since the death of 111-year-old Dumitru Comănescu of [[Romania]] on 27 June 2020, fellow {{age|1909|2|8}}-year-old Saturnino de la Fuente of [[Spain]], born 8 February 1909, is the world's oldest known living man.

This is a list of tables of the '''oldest people''' in the world in ordinal ranks. To avoid including [[Longevity claims|false or unconfirmed claims of old age]], names here are restricted to those people whose ages have been validated by an international body dealing in longevity research, such as the [[Gerontology Research Group]] (GRG) or ''[[Guinness World Records]]'' (GWR), and others who have otherwise been [[WP:RS|reliably sourced]].

According to this criterion, the longest human lifespan is that of [[Jeanne Calment]] of [[France]] (1875–1997), who lived to age 122 years and 164 days. She supposedly met [[Vincent van Gogh]] when she was 12 or 13.<ref name="Calment">{{cite news|last=Whitney|first=Craig R.|title=Jeanne Calment, World's Elder, Dies at 122|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9C01E7D7113DF936A3575BC0A961958260|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=5 August 1997|accessdate=2 March 2008}}</ref> She received news media attention in 1985, after turning 110. Subsequent investigation found documentation for Calment's age, beyond any reasonable question, in the records of her native city, [[Arles]], France.<ref>{{Cite book|authors=[[Jean-Marie Robine|Robine, Jean-Marie]]; Allard, Michel|year=1999|title=Jeanne Calment: Validation of the Duration of Her Life|url=http://www.demogr.mpg.de/books/odense/6/09.htm|location=Odense |publisher=[[University Press of Southern Denmark]]}}</ref> More evidence of Calment's lifespan has been produced than for any other [[supercentenarian]], such that her case serves as an [[prototype|archetype]] in the methodology for verifying the ages of the world's known oldest people.<ref name="Calmentthoroughly">{{cite web|last=Shaw|first=Michael D.|date=1 December 2008|title=You Say You Want to Live for a Long Time?|url=http://www.gasdetection.com/news2/health_news_digest184.html|publisher=Health News Digest}}</ref>{{Ref label|Calment|a}}

As [[Life expectancy#Sex differences|women live longer than men on average]], combined records for both sexes are predominated by women. The longest lifespan for a man is that of [[Jiroemon Kimura]] of [[Japan]] (1897–2013), who lived to age 116 years and 54 days.

Since the death of 117-year-old Chiyo Miyako of Japan on 22 July 2018, {{age|1903|1|2}}-year-old [[Kane Tanaka]], also of Japan, born 2 January 1903, is the oldest living person in the world whose age has been validated.  Since the death of 111-year-old Dumitru Comănescu of [[Romania]] on 27 June 2020, fellow {{age|1909|2|8}}-year-old Saturnino de la Fuente of [[Spain]], born 8 February 1909, is the world's oldest known living man.
