'''[[Swami Vivekananda]]''' (1863-1902)<ref name="Chaube p52">{{Harvnb|Chaube|2005|p=52}}</ref> was an Indian Hindu [[kayasth]] monk and a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of [[Vedanta]] and [[Yoga]] to the western world.<ref name="Vivekananda 2006 p11">{{Harvnb|Vivekananda|2006|p=11}}</ref> He was one of the most influential philosophers and social reformers in his contemporary India and the most successful and influential missionaries of Vedanta to the Western world.<ref name="Mohapatra p14">{{Harvnb|Mohapatra|2009|p=14}}</ref><ref name="Piazza p59">{{Harvnb|Piazza|1978|p=59}}</ref> Indian Nobel laureate poet [[Rabindranath Tagore]]'s suggested to study the works of Vivekananda to understand India. He also told, in Vivekananda there was nothing negative, but everything positive.<ref name="Chattopadhyaya p314" />

In last one century, hundreds of scholarly books have been written on Vivekananda, his works and his philosophy in different languages. [[Sister Nivedita]], who was a disciple and a friend of Vivekananda, wrote two books ''[[The Master as I Saw Him]]'' and ''[[Notes of some wanderings with the Swami Vivekananda]]''. The first one was published in 1910 and the second one was published in 1913.<ref name="Chattopadhyaya p299">{{Harvnb|Chattopadhyaya|1999|p=299}}</ref> [[Sister Gargi]]'s lifelong research work, a series of six volumes of books, ''[[Swami Vivekananda in the West: New Discoveries]]'' was first published in two volumes in 1957. In 1983-87 these series was republished in six volumes.<ref name="Chattopadhyaya p326">{{Harvnb|Chattopadhyaya|1999|p=326}}</ref> Bengali scholar and critic Sankari Prasad Basu, who was a director of Swami Vivekananda Archives, [[Ramakrishna Mission Institute of Culture]] wrote several books on Vivekananda such as ''Vivekananda o Samakalin Bharatbarsha'' ({{in lang|bn}} 7 volumes), ''Sahasya Vivekananda'' {{in lang|bn}}, ''Bandhu Vivekananda'' {{in lang|bn}} etc.<ref name="Dutt p121">{{Harvnb|Dutt|1999|p=121}}</ref>

Monks of Ramakrishna Math and Mission too have written several notable books on the life and works of Vivekananda. ''Swami Vivekananda and Modern India'' written by Swami Jagadiswarananda was first published in 1941. In this book the author covered the biography of Vivekananda in brief.<ref name="Chattopadhyaya p314">{{Harvnb|Chattopadhyaya|1999|p=314}}</ref> [[Swami Nikhilananda]] wrote ''Vivekananda: A Biography'' which was first published in 1943 from [[Advaita Ashrama]].<ref name="Chattopadhyaya p316">{{Harvnb|Chattopadhyaya|1999|p=316}}</ref> ''Yuganayak Vivekananda'' {{in lang|bn}}, written by [[Swami Gambhirananda]] was first published in 1966–1967.<ref name="Chattopadhyaya p326" />
