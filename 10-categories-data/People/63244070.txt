'''Usumain Tukuny Baraka''' is a Sudanese activist and asylum seeker living in [[Israel]]. He is a leader of Israel's asylum-seeking community and the first Darfuri refugee to graduate from a Hebrew-language program in an Israeli university.

== Biography==
Usumain Baraka was born in [[Darfur, Sudan]].<ref name="auto3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.haaretz.com/israel-news/.premium-these-africans-came-to-israel-as-kids-now-they-could-be-deported-1.5790621|title=These African asylum seekers came to Israel alone as kids. Now they could face deportation: 'Israel is part of who I am'|website=Haaretz.com}}</ref><ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tVo1McsvLug</ref> in the small village of Dirata,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jiss.org.il/en/navon-africans-seeking-refuge-in-the-promised-land/|title=Africans Seeking Refuge in the Promised Land|date=March 27, 2018|website=JISS}}</ref> close to the city of [[Geneina]]. He is a member of the [[Masalit people]]. At age 9, the [[Darfur genocide]] came to his village, and [[Janjaweed]] militants killed his father—the leader of his village—and brother.<ref name="auto3"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ynetnews.com/article/HkGBCKruP|title=Sudanese nationals in Israel fear deportation after peace deal|date=October 27, 2020|website=ynetnews}}</ref> Baraka fled on foot through the jungle and found shelter in a refugee camp in [[Chad]] with his mother and sisters.<ref name="auto3"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://themedialine.org/top-stories/sudanese-nationals-in-israel-fear-deportation-after-peace-deal/|title=Sudanese Nationals in Israel Fear Deportation after Peace Deal|date=October 26, 2020|website=The Media Line}}</ref>

After three years in the refugee camp, Baraka left in search of a normal life and education,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://au.finance.yahoo.com/news/sudanese-israel-fear-being-returned-020212490.html|title=Sudanese in Israel fear being returned after normalisation|website=au.finance.yahoo.com}}</ref> He traveled   through [[Libya]] and [[Egypt]].<ref name="auto3"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jewishjournal.com/israel/323747/sudanese-nationals-in-israel-fear-deportation-after-peace-deal/|title=Sudanese Nationals in Israel Fear Deportation after Peace Deal|date=October 27, 2020|website=Jewish Journal}}</ref> In Egypt, he saw a television program on the history of the Jewish people and the [[Holocaust]].<ref name="auto3"/> In 2008, he paid Bedouin smugglers to take him across the desert into Israel.<ref>https://momentmag.com/why-im-inviting-an-asylum-seeker-to-my-seder/,</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://desooz.wordpress.com/2016/11/26/real-steel-usumain-baraka/|title=Real Steel: Usumain Baraka|date=November 26, 2016}}</ref> Upon reaching Israel, he had no shoes. The first Israeli soldier he encountered took off his own shoes and socks and gave them to Baraka.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://momentmag.com/why-im-inviting-an-asylum-seeker-to-my-seder/|title=Why I'm Inviting an Asylum Seeker to My Seder|date=March 22, 2018}}</ref>

Baraka submitted an asylum application in 2013,<ref>{{cite web |title=Sudan deal plunges migrants in Israel into new uncertainty |url=https://www.startribune.com/sudan-deal-plunges-migrants-in-israel-into-new-uncertainty/572933662/ |website=Star Tribune}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Sudan deal plunges migrants in Israel into new uncertainty |url=https://apnews.com/article/virus-outbreak-israel-sudan-middle-east-tel-aviv-e991b4abaf0595b6139d158c9c5c7c96 |website=AP News}}</ref> and received humanitarian temporary residency status.<ref name="auto4">{{cite web |title=Darfuri refugee dreams of building bridges between the states of Israel and Sudan |url=https://www.unhcr.org/il/en/23347-darfuri-refugee-dreams-of-building-bridges-between-israel-and-sudan.html |website=UNHCR}}</ref>

== Education and national service==
Baraka received his high school diploma from [[Yemin Orde]],<ref name="auto3"/> a Jewish<ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/world/wp/2018/03/05/feature/african-migrants-facing-deportation-in-israel/|title=Raised and schooled in Israel, these young Africans could be sent back to a country they do not know|website=Washington Post}}</ref> boarding school for at-risk and immigrant youth near [[Haifa]], Israel. After completing high school, Baraka hoped to enlist in the [[Israel Defense Forces]], but was prevented due to his lack of Israeli citizenship.<ref name="auto2"/> Instead, he completed a year and a half of volunteer service.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/international/archive/2018/01/african-migrants-israel/551747/|title=African Deportations Are Creating a Religious Controversy in Israel|first=Emma|last=Green|date=January 30, 2018|website=The Atlantic}}</ref> He frequently returns to Yemin Orde to give inspirational talks to current students.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.impact-israel.org/2020/01/graduate-day/|title=Graduate Day Offers Opportunities to Receive and to Give Back|date=January 22, 2020|website=ImpactIsrael}}</ref>

Baraka graduated from the [[Interdisciplinary Center Herzliya]] in 2019 with a bachelor's degree in Government and Diplomacy Relations.<ref name="auto2"/> After completing his bachelor's degree, he enrolled in a Master's program<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.hias.org/blog/darfuris-israel-are-grateful-challenges-continue|title=Darfuris in Israel Are Grateful, But Challenges Continue|website=HIAS}}</ref> in Public Policy, also at the IDC<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jewishweek.timesofisrael.com/despite-tough-treatment-african-migrants-praise-israel/|title=Despite Tough Treatment, African Migrants Praise Israel|first=Gary|last=Rosenblatt|website=jewishweek.timesofisrael.com}}</ref> and graduated in summer 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.timesofisrael.com/sudanese-in-israel-fear-deportation-after-normalization-say-they-face-danger/|title=Sudanese in Israel fear deportation after normalization, say they face danger|first=Guillaume|last=LAVALLÉE|website=www.timesofisrael.com}}</ref> During his first degree he was a volunteer member of the IDC student union.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/idc.aguda/posts/10155706915249300|title=אגודת הסטודנטים הבינתחומי הרצליה - IDC Student Union|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref> Baraka completed his studies in Hebrew, one of five languages he speaks, and is the first refugee in Israel to gain a master's degree in Hebrew.<ref name="auto4"/>

== Political activism ==
Baraka is a Co-Founder of the [https://asoisrael.org/ African Students Organization in Israel]. From 2016 to 2019 he served as the organisation's Education Director, and took over as the CEO in January 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://asoisrael.org/our-team/|title=Our Team xxx – African Students Organization in Israel}}</ref> In 2019 he gave a keynote address at the organization's inaugural annual conference.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=etIvJxVqutM&t=130s</ref>

Baraka makes frequent appearances on Israeli television, radio, and print media, where he represents the asylum-seeking community. He appeared on Kan 11 February 2020<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IdiLuCYNgf0</ref> following [[Benjamin Netanyahu]]'s meeting in Uganda with [[Abdel Fattah al-Burhan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.haaretz.com/israel-news/.premium-alongside-netanyahu-ugandan-president-says-he-s-considering-embassy-in-jerusalem-1.8492154|title=Netanyahu, Sudanese leader meet in Uganda, agree to start normalizing ties|website=Haaretz.com}}</ref> He appeared on 103fm and in [[Haaretz]] in August 2020<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://103fm.maariv.co.il/programs/media.aspx?ZrqvnVq=IDHHDI&c41t4nzVQ=GJL|title=103FM - גולן יוכפז וענת דוידוב - דרך השלום?|website=103FM - האזנה לרדיו און ליין}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.haaretz.co.il/news/politics/.premium-1.9083559|title=גם אם יושג הסכם שלום, הדרך להשבת מבקשי המקלט לסודאן ארוכה ורצופה מכשולים|website=Haaretz הארץ}}</ref> following an announcement that Israel and Sudan were undergoing peace talks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.timesofisrael.com/sudan-and-israel-say-talks-underway-for-deal-to-normalize-ties/|title=Sudan and Israel say talks underway for deal to normalize ties|first1=Aaron|last1=Boxerman|first2=Marissa|last2=Newman|website=www.timesofisrael.com}}</ref> He was also featured on an episode of the  Israeli television series 'Slicha al hashe'ela' or Excuse the Question, which discussed African asylum seekers in Israel.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d6EXh-Hs3ts</ref> In April 2020, he was the subject of a Kan 11 mini-documentary, which described his activism to help fellow asylum seekers in Israel during the Coronavirus.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/kanipbc/videos/%D7%93%D7%95%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%90%D7%95%D7%A1%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A2%D7%99%D7%9F/158591915457949/|title=דוקותיים: אוסומעין|via=www.facebook.com}}</ref>

Baraka also gives tours and lectures to groups in English and Hebrew on the topic of asylum seekers in Israel, the Darfur genocide, and South Tel Aviv.<ref name="auto1"/> He also runs a Hebrew language school for fellow asylum seekers in Israel, through which he teaches Hebrew to dozens of asylum seekers from Darfur and elsewhere.<ref name="auto4"/>

On June 28, 2017 Baraka spoke to the Israeli [[Knesset]] on behalf of asylum seekers in Israel<ref>{{cite web |title=Protocol No. 250  From a meeting of the State Audit Committee  Wednesday, Wednesday, June 28, 2017, 9:00 AM |url=https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law103/20_ptv_390399.htm |website=Nevo}}</ref>
