'''Ralph Andrew Adams''' (born July 9, 1907, date of death 1976) was a [[Canada|Canadian]] [[Athletics (sport)|athlete]] who competed in the [[1928 Summer Olympics]].

He was born in [[Hamilton, Ontario]].

In 1928 he was eliminated in the quarter-finals of the [[Athletics at the 1928 Summer Olympics - Men's 100 metres|100 metres event]] as well as of the [[Athletics at the 1928 Summer Olympics - Men's 200 metres|200 metres competition]]. He was also a member of the Canadian relay team which was disqualified in the final of the [[Athletics at the 1928 Summer Olympics – Men's 4 × 100 metres relay|4×100 metres contest]].

At the 1930 Empire Games he won the gold medal with the Canadian relay team in the 4×110 yards event. In the 220 yards competition he was eliminated in the heats.
