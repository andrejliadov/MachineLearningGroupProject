The following is a list of actors, comedians and celebrities who auditioned for a part in the [[NBC]] sketch comedy series ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' but did not get cast in the show.<ref name=biography>{{cite web|last=Chang|first=Rachel|title=10 Famous Comedians Who Were Rejected by 'Saturday Night Live'|date=5 May 2020|publisher=[[Biography.com]]|url=https://www.biography.com/news/famous-comedians-rejected-from-saturday-night-live|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref><ref name=screenrant>{{cite web|last=McCormick|first=Colin|title=Saturday Night Live: 14 Famous Actors Who Auditioned... And Didn't Get Cast|date=12 February 2019|publisher=[[Screen Rant]]|url=https://screenrant.com/saturday-night-live-famous-actors-turned-down/|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref><ref name=mentalfloss>{{cite web|last=Wood|first=Jennifer M.|title=31 Famous People Rejected by Saturday Night Live|date=4 December 2015|publisher=[[Mental Floss]]|url=https://www.mentalfloss.com/article/55008/32-famous-people-rejected-saturday-night-live|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref>  Many of these celebrities managed to achieve successful careers in the entertainment industry, with some of them eventually appearing on the show as a guest host or in some other capacity.<ref name=grunge>{{cite web|last=Meisfjord|first=Tom|title=ACTORS TURNED DOWN BY SNL WHO ARE NOW HUGE STARS|date=23 May 2018|publisher=[[Grunge.com]]|url=https://www.grunge.com/123741/actors-turned-snl-now-huge-stars/|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref><ref name=ew>{{cite web|last=DeSantis|first=Rachel|title=Saturday Night Live: See the auditions that didn't make the cut|date=3 October 2016|publisher=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|url=https://ew.com/article/2016/10/03/saturday-night-live-failed-auditions/|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref><ref name=insider>{{cite web|last=Renae|first=Kirstie|title=14 celebrities who got rejected by 'Saturday Night Live' and went on to become famous in their own right|date=22 July 2020|publisher=[[Insider.com]]|url=https://www.insider.com/stars-who-got-rejected-by-saturday-night-live#legendary-comedy-actor-jim-carrey-didnt-make-it-onto-snl-1|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref>  This list is in alphabetical order by last name.

==A==
* [[Dave Attell]] auditioned in 1993.  Instead, he was hired as a writer for the show during its [[Saturday Night Live (season 19)|19th season]].<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=vulture/>

==B==
* [[Charlie Barnett (comedian)|Charlie Barnett]] auditioned in 1980, but passed it up because he was self-conscious about his inability to read.<ref name=vulture/>
* [[Richard Belzer]] claimed that series creator [[Lorne Michaels]] promised to cast him in the show but later changed his mind.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=grunge/><ref name=vulture/>
* [[Rachel Bloom]] auditioned in 2012.<ref name=ew/>
* Although he was a writer for the show's [[Saturday Night Live (season 25)|25th season]], [[Kevin Brennan (comedian)|Kevin Brennan]] auditioned for a part in the ''[[Weekend Update]]'' after [[Colin Quinn]] left the show.<ref name=parttwo/>
* [[Nicole Byer]] auditioned in 2013 when the show was looking to add an African-American woman to the cast but lost to [[Sasheer Zamata]].<ref name=twitter>{{Cite tweet |title=With my girls @Breshawebb @simoneshepherd @TiffanyHaddish & other funny ladies I got to meet at our #SNL showcase :) |user=gabrielledennis |number=407591261462212608 |date=2 December 2013 |access-date=2020-09-28|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> 

==C==
* [[Jim Carrey]] auditioned in 1980.<ref name=grunge/><ref name=ew/><ref name=insider/><ref name=business>{{cite web|last=Renae|first=Kirstie|title= 14 celebrities who got rejected by 'Saturday Night Live' and went on to become famous in their own right|date=8 October 2019|publisher=[[Business Insider]]|url=http://assets.businessinsider.com/stars-who-got-rejected-by-saturday-night-live/#legendary-comedy-actor-jim-carrey-didnt-make-it-onto-snl-1|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref><ref name=youtube>{{cite web|title=Auditions - SNL 40th Anniversary Special|date=16 February 2015|publisher=[[YouTube]]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Dzae7_80fg|access-date=16 September 2020}}</ref> He went on to host the show three times in 1996, 2011 and 2014.<ref name=biography/><ref name=mentalfloss/>  Lorne Michaels regrets not hiring Carrey.<ref name=screenrant/>
* [[Louis C.K.]] auditioned in 1993.<ref name=vulture/>  He went on to host the show four times.<ref name=grunge/>
* [[Jennifer Coolidge]] auditioned in 1995.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=insider/><ref name=business/><ref name=parttwo/>
* [[Stephen Colbert]] auditioned in 1992.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=grunge/><ref name=ew/><ref name=insider/><ref name=youtube/>
* [[David Cross]] auditioned in 1992.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=grunge/><ref name=vulture/>

==D==
* [[Geena Davis]] auditioned in 1984 but lost the part to [[Pamela Stephenson]].<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=vulture/>
* [[Gabrielle Dennis]] auditioned in 2013 when the show was looking to add an African-American woman to the cast but lost to [[Sasheer Zamata]].<ref name=twitter/><ref name=jasmine>{{cite web|url=http://thejasminebrand.com/2013/12/11/exclusive-actress-bresha-webb-talks-about-snls-black-women-showcase-audition-we-were-all-honored/|title=[EXCLUSIVE] Actress Bresha Webb Talks About SNL's Black Women Showcase Audition: We were all honored.|last=thejasminebrand|date=11 December 2013}}</ref>

==F==
* [[Dave Foley]] auditioned in 1985.<ref name=mentalfloss/>

==G==
* [[Zach Galifianakis]] auditioned in 1999.<ref name=ew/><ref name=insider/><ref name=business/><ref name=youtube/>  He was eventually hired to write for two episodes of the show's [[Saturday Night Live (season 25)|25th season]].<ref name=screenrant/>  He also hosted the show three times in 2010, 2011 and 2013.<ref name=biography/><ref name=mentalfloss/>
* [[Jon Glaser]] auditioned in 1995.<ref name=parttwo/>
* [[Donald Glover]] auditioned twice for the show only to lose the part to [[Fred Armisen]].<ref name=mentalfloss/>  He eventually hosted the show in 2018.<ref name=biography/><ref name=grunge/><ref name=insider/>
* [[John Goodman]]  auditioned in 1980.<ref name=insider/><ref name=vulture/><ref name=business/>  He later went on to host the show thirteen times.<ref name=biography/><ref name=screenrant/><ref name=mentalfloss/>
* [[Kathy Griffin]] auditioned in 1990 but lost the part to [[Julia Sweeney]].<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=insider/><ref name=business/>

==H==
* [[Tiffany Haddish]] auditioned in 2013 when the show was looking to add an African-American woman to the cast but lost to [[Sasheer Zamata]].<ref name=twitter/><ref name=jasmine/> She later hosted the show during the [[Saturday Night Live (season 43)|43rd]] season.<ref name="Forbes-SNL-2017">{{cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/adriennegibbs/2017/11/08/girls-trip-breakout-star-tiffany-haddish-makes-history-hosting-snl-taylor-swift-performs/#bbaa2547841d|title='Girls Trip' Breakout Star Tiffany Haddish Making History Hosting 'SNL'; Taylor Swift Performing|last1=Gibbs|first1=Adrienne|date=November 11, 2017|work=[[Forbes (magazine)|Forbes]]}}</ref>
* [[Kevin Hart]] auditioned for a part in the show.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=parttwo/><ref name=business/><ref name=youtube/>  He went on to host the show several times since his failed audition.<ref name=biography/><ref name=screenrant/><ref name=ew/>
* [[Rob Huebel]] auditioned more than once during the mid-2000s.<ref name=mentalfloss/>

==K==
* [[Mindy Kaling]] failed her audition to join ''Saturday Night Live'' but was offered to write for the show, which she turned down.<ref name=biography/><ref name=screenrant/><ref name=insider/>
* [[Andy Kaufman]] auditioned for a spot on the original cast.<ref name=youtube/>
* [[Mimi Kennedy]] was offered a part in the show in 1975, but [[Gilda Radner]] was worried that they were both too similar to each other.<ref name=vulture>{{cite web|last=Evans|first=Bradford|title=The Lost ‘SNL’ Cast Members: Part 1 (1975-1995)|date=18 April 2013|publisher=[[Vulture.com]]|url=https://www.vulture.com/2013/04/the-lost-snl-cast-members-part-1-1975-1995.html|access-date=1 August 2020}}</ref>
* [[Kerri Kenney-Silver]] auditioned in 1996.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=insider/><ref name=business/>
* [[Nick Kroll]] auditioned in 2008.<ref name=parttwo/>
* [[Lisa Kudrow]] auditioned in 1990.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=vulture/>  She later hosted the show in 1996.<ref name=biography/><ref name=insider/><ref name=business/>

==M==
* [[Marc Maron]] auditioned in 1995 in attempt to replace [[Norm Macdonald]]'s spot in the ''Weekend Update.''<ref name=screenrant/><ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=grunge/>
* [[Andrea Martin]] auditioned in 1984 but lost the part to [[Pamela Stephenson]].<ref name=vulture/>
* [[Jack McBrayer]] auditioned in 2001.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=grunge/>
* [[Bruce McCulloch]] auditioned in 1985.<ref name=mentalfloss/>
* [[Kevin McDonald]] auditioned in 1985.<ref name=mentalfloss/>  He was offered a part in 1995 but he turned it down.<ref name=parttwo/>
* [[Adam McKay]] auditioned in 1995.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=parttwo/>
* [[T.J. Miller]] auditioned in 2008.<ref name=screenrant/><ref name=parttwo/>
* [[Kel Mitchell]] auditioned in 2003 alongside former ''[[Kenan & Kel]]'' cast mate [[Kenan Thompson]], who was hired.<ref name=mentalfloss/>
* [[John Mulaney]] auditioned in 2008. Instead, he was hired as a writer for the show during its [[Saturday Night Live (season 34)|34th season]], and later went on to host during seasons [[Saturday Night Live (season 43)|43]], [[Saturday Night Live (season 44)|44]], [[Saturday Night Live (season 45)|45]] and [[Saturday Night Live (season 46)|46]].<ref name=parttwo/>

==N==
* [[Kumail Nanjiani]] auditioned in 2012.<ref name=parttwo/> He would later host during the show's [[Saturday Night Live (season 43)|forty-third]] season.

==P==
* [[Jordan Peele]] auditioned to play the role of [[Barack Obama]] on the show only to lose it to Fred Armisen.<ref name=screenrant/><ref name=parttwo/> 
* [[Aubrey Plaza]] auditioned in 2008.<ref name=business/><ref name=parttwo/>  Plaza also worked as an intern of the show three years prior.<ref name=biography/><ref name=screenrant/><ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=insider/>

==R==
* [[Paul Reubens]] auditioned in 1980 only to lose the part to [[Gilbert Gottfried]].<ref name=biography/><ref name=screenrant/><ref name=insider/>  Reubens claims Gottfried was favored over him because he was friends with one of the producers of the show.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=vulture/>
* [[Mercedes Ruehl]] auditioned in 1980, but was passed over in favor of [[Denny Dillon]].<ref>[https://www.vulture.com/article/denny-dillon-snl-interview.html/ "Andy Hoglund-Vulture interview - August 2020"], Vulture.com, accessed August 13, 2020</ref>
* [[John Roberts (actor)|John Roberts]] auditioned in 2008.<ref name=parttwo/>
* [[Jeff Ross]] auditioned in 2000 for a part in the ''Weekend Update'' after Colin Quinn left the show.<ref name=mentalfloss/><ref name=parttwo/>
* [[Amber Ruffin]]  auditioned in 2013 when the show was looking to add an African-American woman to the cast but lost to [[Sasheer Zamata]].<ref name=twitter/> She would go on to be a writer for ''[[Late Night with Seth Meyers]]'' since the show's beginning in 2014.
