'''Dren Abazi''' (born 13 February 1985 in [[Pristina]], [[Socialist Autonomous Province of Kosovo|Kosovo]]), is a singer-songwriter and a producer of Albanian origin.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dren Abazi & Elina Duni|url=http://koha.net/?id=4&l=21657|website=Koha Ditore ( Albanian Daily Newspaper )|accessdate=17 November 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Balkan Bras World|url=http://www.balkanbrassworld.com/bands/zigzagorchestra.htm|website=Balkan Brass World|accessdate=12 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140605201512/http://www.balkanbrassworld.com/bands/zigzagorchestra.htm|archive-date=5 June 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> He is the founder and leader of Zig Zag Orchestra, a popular band in [[Kosovo]],{{efn|name=status}} [[Albania]], and other Balkan countries. His music is a fusion of [[Balkan music]],  [[jazz]], [[klezmer]], [[ska]] and rock.{{citation needed|date=September 2017}}

==Biography==
Although Dren Abazi made his debut at a very young age, as a guitar player for different local rock bands, he gained public and media attention and made his breakthrough after creating his own band Zig Zag Orchestra in 2008. He rose to fame quickly in 2011 after composing and performing "Ti dhe Une"<ref>{{cite web|title=Ti Dhe Une|url=http://top-channel.tv/lajme/artikull.php?id=219217|website=Albeu ( in Albanian )|accessdate=5 December 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170913135923/http://top-channel.tv/lajme/artikull.php?id=219217|archive-date=13 September 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Ti Dhe Une|url=http://top-channel.tv/lajme/artikull.php?id=219217|website=Top Channel ( in Albanian )|accessdate=17 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170913135923/http://top-channel.tv/lajme/artikull.php?id=219217|archive-date=13 September 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> a duet with Albanian female singer [[Anjeza Shahini]], the song quickly became a hit in [[Albania]] and was chosen as the song of the year by national Albanian TV [[Top Channel]].

In 2014 his song "Naten" became a part of the soundtrack for ''[[Let's Be Cops]]'', which starred [[Andy Garcia]] and [[Nina Dobrev]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Koha Ditore|url=http://koha.net/?id=4&l=4587|website=Koha Ditore ( Albanian Daily Newspaper )|accessdate=5 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Telegrafi – Dren abazi|url=http://www.telegrafi.com/magazina/dren-abazi-intim-me-mbreti-nates-6-22988.html|website=Telegrafi.com ( Kosovo Albanian Media Portal )|accessdate=17 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160201004001/http://www.telegrafi.com/magazina/dren-abazi-intim-me-mbreti-nates-6-22988.html|archive-date=1 February 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> In addition, the song is part of the album ''Afterparty'', released in 2012 won the National Prize KULT<ref>{{cite web|title=Albanian Kult Price Winner|url=http://www.gazetaexpress.com/roze/dren-abazi-publikon-projektin-duvideo-138673/|website=Gazeta Express (Kosovo Albanian Daily)|accessdate=17 November 2015}}</ref> in [[Tirana]], [[Albania]], for the best Albanian album of the year.<ref>{{cite web|title=Anabel – Albanian Kult Price Winner|url=http://www.anabel.al/artikull/2281/ok/|website=Anabel (in Albanian)|accessdate=13 November 2015}}</ref>
