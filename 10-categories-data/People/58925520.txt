'''Ian Alexander''' (born April 20, 2001) is an American actor known for his roles as Buck Vu on ''[[The OA]]'' and as Lev in ''[[The Last of Us Part II]]''.

== Early and personal life ==
Alexander was born in Salt Lake City, Utah,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Azzopardi |first=Chris |date=February 15, 2018 |title=16-Year-Old Trans Actor Ian Alexander Is Bringing Queer Power to the Big Screen |url=https://www.intomore.com/culture/16yearold-trans-actor-ian-alexander-is-bringing-queer-power-to-the-big-screen|access-date=October 31, 2018 |work=Into}}</ref> to a white father and a mother of Vietnamese descent who migrated to the United States from Vietnam.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Ramos |first=Dino-Ray |date=April 2, 2019 |title=''The OA''{{'}}s Ian Alexander Is The Future Of Trans Visibility In Hollywood|url=https://deadline.com/2019/04/the-oa-ian-alexander-netflix-lgbtq-trans-diversity-reprsentation-inclusion-1202585939/|access-date=April 2, 2019|website=[[Deadline Hollywood|Deadline]]}}</ref> Due to his father's work with the [[United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]], his family often moved and has lived in places such as Hawaii, Japan and Washington, DC.<ref name=":22">{{Cite web |date=December 21, 2016 |title=Q & A with ''The OA''{{'}}s Ian Alexander|url=http://blog.angryasianman.com/2016/12/q-with-oas-ian-alexander.html|access-date=October 31, 2018|website=[[Angry Asian Man]]}}</ref> During elementary school, he would participate in community theater and chorus.<ref name=":22" /> He received [[viral video|viral]] attention online through his photo response to a [[transphobia|transphobic]] incident perpetrated by four UCLA college students.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Karlan |first=Sarah |date=May 19, 2016 |title=People Can't Handle This Trans Teen's Response To This Viral Anti-Trans Photo |work=[[BuzzFeed]] |url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/skarlan/trans-teen-response-viral|access-date=November 6, 2018}}</ref>

Though he was raised in a [[Mormon]] family, he is not a participating member.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Alexander |first=Ian |date=January 27, 2017 |title=I'm Ian Alexander/Buck! AMA : TheOA |url=https://www.reddit.com/r/TheOA/comments/5qkcpt/im_ian_alexanderbuck_ama/dczz62n|access-date=October 31, 2018 |website=[[Reddit]]}}</ref> He [[came out]] as [[transgender]] in 2014,<ref name="NBC News">{{Cite news |last=Weik |first=Taylor |date=February 2, 2017 |title=Meet ''The OA'' actor who wants to help pave the way for trans representation |work=NBC News |url=https://www.nbcnews.com/news/asian-america/meet-oa-teen-actor-who-wants-help-pave-way-trans-n714406|access-date=October 31, 2018}}</ref> uses he/him and [[singular they|they/them]] pronouns, and is [[non-binary]] and [[pansexual]].<ref name="forbes-5nov2020">{{cite news |last1=Ennis |first1=Dawn |date=November 5, 2020 |title=Transgender Actor Ian Alexander, The Newest ''Star Trek'' Star, Didn't Get The Part He Auditioned For |work=[[Forbes]] |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/dawnstaceyennis/2020/11/05/transgender-actor-ian-alexander-the-newest-star-trek-star-didnt-get-the-part-he-auditioned-for/ |accessdate=November 14, 2020}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite news |last=Walker |first=Harron |date=December 22, 2016 |title=The OA's Ian Alexander on His Big Acting Debut and Trans Representation |work=[[Vulture (magazine)|Vulture]] |url=https://www.vulture.com/2016/12/the-oa-netflix-ian-alexander-buck.html|access-date=October 31, 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
Alexander's debut acting role was on [[Netflix]]'s ''[[The OA]]'', where he plays Buck Vu. Buck, like Alexander, is also a transgender youth and was partially based on Alexander's real experiences.<ref name="NBC News" /> He was cast from an open casting call online that spread through [[Tumblr]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=August 25, 2015 |title=Casting Call: Netflix series seeks Asian Transgender Teen |url=http://blog.angryasianman.com/2015/08/casting-call-netflix-series-seeks-asian.html|access-date=October 31, 2018 |website=[[Angry Asian Man]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Houlihan |first=Ryan |date=December 28, 2016 |title=How Netflix's Newest Teen Actor Landed His Job on Tumblr |work=[[Teen Vogue]] |url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/ian-alexander-netflix-series-the-oa-tumblr-interview|access-date=October 31, 2018}}</ref> Later he was cast in the 2018 feature film ''[[Every Day (2018 film)|Every Day]]'', based on the [[Every Day (novel)|book]] by [[David Levithan]], playing Vic, a trans teen whom the spirit "A" inhabits for a day.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Dresden |first=Hilton |date=February 15, 2018 |title=Watch This Exclusive Clip From ''Every Day'' Featuring Trans Actor Ian Alexander |work=[[Out (magazine)|Out]] |url=https://www.out.com/movies/2018/2/15/watch-exclusive-clip-every-day-featuring-trans-actor-ian-alexander|access-date=October 31, 2018}}</ref> In October 2017, [[Naughty Dog]] announced Alexander had joined the cast for ''[[The Last of Us Part II]]'', the sequel to their [[The Last of Us|popular video game]].<ref>{{cite tweet |user=Naughty_Dog |number=925057785645056000 |date=October 30, 2017 |title=Ian Alexander is Lev. #TheLastofUsPartII |author=Naughty Dog |author-link=Naughty Dog |access-date=October 31, 2018}}</ref> He played Lev, a transgender character in the game.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rosenblatt |first1=Kalhan |date=July 8, 2020 |title=''The Last of Us Part II'' brings queer stories to a pandemic-ravaged dystopia |url=https://www.nbcnews.com/feature/nbc-out/last-us-part-ii-brings-queer-stories-pandemic-ravaged-dystopia-n1233073|access-date=July 21, 2020 |website=[[NBC News]]}}</ref>

In June 2019, to mark the 50th anniversary of the [[Stonewall riots|Stonewall Riots]], sparking the start of the modern [[LGBT social movements|LGBTQ rights movement]], ''[[Queerty]]'' named him one of their Pride50: "trailblazing individuals who actively ensure society remains moving towards [[LGBT equality|equality]], acceptance and dignity for all [[queer]] people".<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.queerty.com/oas-ian-alexander-making-safer-trans-teens-come-parents-accept-20190524 |title=''The OA''{{'}}s Ian Alexander is making it safer for trans teens to come out & parents to accept them |last=Reddish |first=David |date=May 24, 2019 |website=[[Queerty]] |access-date=June 8, 2019 }}</ref> Similarly, Alexander was one of the cover stars for ''[[them.]]''{{'}}s 2019 Pride issue, "spotlighting three rising queer artists whose work and lives are breaking new ground for LGBTQ+ visibility".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.them.us/story/june-2019-pride-issue-ian-alexander-king-princess-chika |title=Welcome to Our Pride Issue, Featuring Ian Alexander, King Princess, and Chika |work=[[them.]] |date=June 21, 2019 |access-date=June 21, 2019}}</ref> In March 2020, it was announced Alexander will star in the independent film ''Daughter''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Ramos |first=Dino-Ray |date=March 10, 2020 |title=Casper Van Dien, Ian Alexander, Vivien Ngô, Elyse Dinh And Megan Le Join Corey Deshon's Thriller ''Daughter'' |url=https://deadline.com/2020/03/ian-alexander-casper-van-dien-vivien-ngo-elyse-dinh-corey-deshon-thriller-daughter-1202878468/|access-date=March 29, 2020 |website=[[Deadline Hollywood|Deadline]]}}</ref>

In September 2020, it was announced Alexander had joined the cast of ''[[Star Trek: Discovery]]''; he plays the first transgender character in the Star Trek canon.<ref name="forbes-5nov2020" /><ref>{{Cite web |last=Ramos |first=Dino-Ray |date=September 2, 2020 |title=''Star Trek: Discovery'' Introduces First-Ever Non-Binary And Trans Characters With Blu Del Barrio And Ian Alexander |url=https://deadline.com/2020/09/star-trek-discovery-non-binary-transgender-characters-blu-del-barrio-ian-alexander-lgbtq-diversity-inclusion-representation-1234568890/|access-date=October 15, 2020 |website=[[Deadline Hollywood|Deadline]]}}</ref>
