'''''The Goddamn George Liquor Program''''' is a 1997<ref name ="wired">{{cite news | title = In His Way, John K. Will Challenge the World | work = [[Wired (magazine)|Wired]] | url = http://archive.wired.com/culture/lifestyle/news/1997/10/7566 |accessdate = 2014-04-29 | date=1997-10-08}}</ref><ref name ="ads">{{cite web | title = John K's Guide to Surviving the End of Television | publisher = Cold Hard Flash| url = http://www.coldhardflash.com/2007/04/john-ks-guide-to-surviving-end-of.html |date = April 23, 2007 |accessdate = 2008-01-01}}</ref><ref name="johnkbio">{{cite web| title = Director: John Kricfalusi| publisher = Hoytyboy Pictures| url = http://www.hoytyboy.com/directors/Director_John_K/JOhn_K_Bio.html| accessdate = 2008-02-09| url-status = dead| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080215112316/http://www.hoytyboy.com/directors/Director_John_K/JOhn_K_Bio.html| archivedate = 2008-02-15}}</ref> [[Adobe Flash]] cartoon series created by [[John Kricfalusi]] and starring the animated character [[George Liquor]]. It was originally developed as a web-based cartoon by [[Microsoft]]'s then-new [[MSN]], in partnership with [[Spümcø]] studio. After failing to receive approval for publishing from [[Microsoft]], all properties were released to Spümcø, and the web-based cartoon was [[Spin-off (media)|spun out]] directly by the Spümcø studio. [[Michael Pataki]] voiced Liquor in the series. Spümcø produced eight one-minute shorts.<ref name ="flashimation">{{cite web | title = Flashimation: The Context and Culture of Web Animation| publisher = | url = http://old.siggraph.org/artdesign/gallery/S06/paper1.pdf |accessdate = 2008-02-06}}</ref>

Premiering on October 15, 1997,<ref name="Washington"/><ref name ="wired"/><ref name ="ads"/> ''The Goddamn George Liquor Program'' was the first cartoon series to be produced exclusively for the internet.<ref name="awardinfo">{{cite web | title = 27th Annual Annie Award Nominee Showcase: Goddamn George Liquor Program | publisher = AWM.com | url = http://www.awn.com/annieawards/george.php3 | accessdate = 2007-12-14 | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20071009221906/http://www.awn.com/annieawards/george.php3 | archivedate = 2007-10-09 }}</ref> George appeared on the series with his nephew, [[Jimmy The Idiot Boy]]; Jimmy's cousins, Slab and Ernie; Jimmy's [[Romantic interest|love interest]], Sody Pop; and George and Jimmy's pet dog, Dirty Dog.  Without the control of a [[Television network|network]], the series could be made as Kricfalusi wanted.<ref name ="awardinfo"/><ref name="Washington">{{cite web|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/tech/ffwd/1197/georgeliquor.htm|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20000817233250/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/tech/ffwd/1197/georgeliquor.htm|work=[[The Washington Post]]|archivedate=2000-08-17|date=1997-11-07|accessdate=2019-07-04|title=Web-toons: Hot Flash?|first=Dan|last=Pacheco}}</ref> ''The Goddamn George Liquor Program'' won the 1999 [[Annie Award]] for "Outstanding Achievement in an Animated Interactive Production".<ref name="annie27">{{cite web | title = ASIFA-Hollywood's 27th Annual Annie Awards | publisher = ASIFA Hollywood | url = http://www.asifa-hollywood.org/annies99.html | accessdate = 2007-12-14 | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20070202142621/http://www.asifa-hollywood.org/annies99.html | archivedate = 2007-02-02 }}</ref> Later the show was being developed as a series of cartoon commercials for [[Pontiac Vibe]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://johnkstuff.blogspot.com/2008/03/george-liquor-pilot-sketches.html |title=George Liquor Pilot Sketches |author=John Kricfalusi |date=March 20, 2008 |accessdate=2009-12-27}}</ref> The new series remained unreleased after [[General Motors]] discontinued the Pontiac Vibe auto line in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|first=Ray|last=Wert|date=April 27, 2009|title=Pontiac Vibe, Solstice Are Dead, Won't Live On As Chevys|url=http://jalopnik.com/5229396/pontiac-vibe-solstice-are-dead-wont-live-on-as-chevys|publisher=[[Jalopnik.com]]|accessdate=2009-04-27}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Frank|last=Williams|date=June 18, 2009|title=Bye-Bye, Vibe|url=http://www.thetruthaboutcars.com/bye-bye-vibe/|publisher=[[The Truth About Cars]]|accessdate=2009-06-18}}</ref>

==Characters==
The following characters appear in ''The Goddamn George Liquor Program''.<ref name="Chars1">"[http://johnkstuff.blogspot.com/2007/04/george-liquor-storybible.html George Liquor Storybible]", ''All Kinds of Stuff''</ref><ref>"[http://johnkstuff.blogspot.com/2007/04/george-liquor-bible-2-more-characters.html George Liquor StoryBible 2 – more characters]", ''All Kinds of Stuff''</ref>
* '''[[George Liquor|George Liquor, American]]''' – Liquor is an ultra-patriotic American. 
* '''[[Jimmy The Idiot Boy]]''' – Jimmy is an incredibly stupid teenage boy. 
* '''Sody Pop'''<ref name ="wired"/> – Sody is a 15-year-old [[Canadians|Canadian]] girl who is Jimmy's love interest. But George Liquor tells her to stay away from Jimmy.
* '''"Slab 'n' Ernie"''' – Slab and Ernie, two bullies, are George's nephews and Jimmy's cousins.
* '''"Dirty Dog"''' – "Dirty Dog", the "neighborhood watchdog", does not like cats. In the first episode of ''The Goddamn George Liquor Program'' "Dirty Dog" [[defecation|defecates]] on a lawn, upsetting the [[sun]].
Other characters who appears in comic:
* '''Mable''' – Mable was George Liquor's wife. After she died, George Liquor preserved her torso and legs and nailed the parts to a trophy stand with the buttocks facing the room.
* '''Merle''' – 30-year old Merle, a "health nut", is Sody's older sister. Merle tries to seduce George Liquor.
* "'''The Bad Catholic Girls'''" – Three [[Catholic school]] teenage girls who [[smoking|smoke]], trying to "corrupt" Jimmy.
* '''Mildrew''' – Mildrew, Slab and Ernie's mother, is married to Frank, George's brother. George Liquor sometimes flirts with her.
* '''Cigarettes the Cat''' – [[Cigarette]]s is Jimmy's pet [[cat]].
* '''Victor Lugnuts''' – Lugnuts operates "Victor Lugnuts' House of [[Meat]]s".
* '''Victor Produce''' – Lugnuts's rival, Victor Produce, operates a [[vegetable]] store.
* '''Eddie the Town Atheist''' – Eddie, an [[atheism|atheist]], is Lugnuts's son. George tries to convince him to adopt Christianity.
* '''General "Buck" Neckfold''' – Neckfold, a general at [[The Pentagon]], is one of George Liquor's friends.
* '''Nutty the Friendly Dump''' – Jimmy [[defecation|defecated]] Nutty. Nutty has [[Maize|corn]] kernels for eyes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/icon/content/971101/cover.html|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/19990117004913/http://www.smh.com.au/icon/content/971101/cover.html|archivedate=1999-01-17|date=1997-11-01|accessdate=2019-07-04|publisher=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|title='Toons too hot for TV}}</ref>
* '''Dr. Stump''' – Stump, George Liquor's family doctor, prescribes [[amputation]] as treatment for various ills.
* '''Harvey Whiteman, the Barber''' – Whiteman is George Liquor's [[barber]].
* "'''The Lord'''" – [[God]] is one of George Liquor's "[[fishing]] buddies".
