'''Arweet'''/'''Ngarweet''' is an important tribal position in the [[Boonwurrung]] and [[Wathaurong]] peoples of the [[Indigenous Australian]] [[Kulin nation|Kulin]] alliance who live from [[Western Port]], [[Port Phillip]], [[Geelong]] to [[Ballarat]].<ref name="briggs">Carolyn Briggs, ''[http://www.fatsil.org.au/Volume-38/Boon-wurrung-Arweets-Carolyn-Briggs.html Boon wurrung Arweets Carolyn Briggs]{{dead link|date=October 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}'', Federation of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Languages, Accessed 9 November 2008</ref><ref name="stockyardhill">Tardis Enterprises Pty Ltd, cultural heritage advisors, ''[http://www.land.vic.gov.au/CA256F310024B628/0/0F0ED1D88FC0F116CA257480000AF553/$File/2008-07+Annex+E+-++Detailed+Cultural+Heritage+Assessment+Part+2.pdf Stockyard Hill Wind Farm – Desktop Cultural Heritage Assessment]  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110706122941/http://www.land.vic.gov.au/CA256F310024B628/0/0F0ED1D88FC0F116CA257480000AF553/%24File/2008-07%2BAnnex%2BE%2B-%2B%2BDetailed%2BCultural%2BHeritage%2BAssessment%2BPart%2B2.pdf |date=6 July 2011 }}'', Accessed 9 November 2008</ref> An Arweet is a leader or headman and holds a similar tribal standing as a [[ngurungaeta]] of the [[Wurundjeri]] people.

Notable Arweet include:

* [[Derrimut (Indigenous Australian)|Derrimut]] (1810c - 1864), arweet of the Yalukit-willam clan of the [[Boonwurrung]] people<ref name="rahs">Ian D. Clark, ''[http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-12173454_ITM "You have all this place, no good have children ..." Derrimut: traitor, saviour, or a man of his people?]'', in the ''Journal of the Royal Australian Historical Society'', 1 December 2005. Accessed 8 November 2008</ref>
* [[Ningerranarro]] ( Died 1847) also known as Old Benbow of the Boonwurrung<ref name="briggs" />
* Noonallaboon (1842–1844), Burrumbeet balug of the Wathaurong<ref name="stockyardhill" />
* Balybalip also called Bullurp Bullurp, Bil-le-bil-lup, and King Billy of Ballarat (c.1823-1881), Burrumbeet balug of the Wathaurong<ref name="stockyardhill" />
