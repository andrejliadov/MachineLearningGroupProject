'''Little Compton Street''' was a street in [[Soho]], [[London]] WC2, [[England]]. It connected the east end of [[Old Compton Street]] at its junction with [[Charing Cross Road]] to [[New Compton Street]] at [[Stacey Street, London|Stacey Street]]. Until 1896, the current eastern section of Old Compton Street (the part east of [[Greek Street]]) was known as Little Compton Street.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.londonremembers.com/memorials/little-compton-street | title=Memorial: Little Compton Street | publisher=London Remembers | work=Memorials | accessdate=3 October 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.british-history.ac.uk/report.aspx?compid=41080 | work=Survey of London: volumes 33 and 34: St Anne Soho | title=Portland Estate: Old Compton Street | editor-first=F. H. W. | editor-last=Sheppard | publisher=[[English Heritage]]/British History Online | date=1966 | accessdate=3 October 2014 }}</ref> The section of Little Compton Street between Charing Cross Road and New Compton Street is now blocked by an office block.

==Remaining traces==
A street sign for Little Compton Street remains visible on a wall of a utility tunnel beneath a street grate on a traffic island in the middle of the junction of Old Compton Street and Charing Cross Road. Contrary to popular belief, this was a tunnel that previously ran under Little Compton Street, rather than the latter having been "buried."<ref>{{cite web| url=http://greatwen.com/2013/11/07/secret-london-streets-beneath-streets-of-london/ | work=The Great Wen | first=Peter | last=Watts | title=Secret London: streets beneath streets of London | date=7 November 2013 | accessdate=3 October 2014 }}</ref> It now forms part of the Cambridge Circus Utility Tunnels.<ref>{{cite web | title=GES215 â€“ Cambridge Circus Utility Tunnels, London | work=Guerrilla Exploring  | url=http://www.guerrillaexploring.com/gesite/public_html/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=343:ges215-cambridge-circus-utility-tunnels-l0ndon&catid=5:subterranean&Itemid=10 | accessdate=30 July 2013 }}</ref>
