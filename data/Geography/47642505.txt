The '''Livingstone Medal''' is awarded by the [[Royal Scottish Geographical Society]] in recognition of outstanding service of a humanitarian nature with a clear geographical dimension. This was awarded first in 1901.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://rsgs.org/inspiring-people/medals-awards/livingstone-medal/|title= Livingstone Medal|publisher= Royal Scottish geographical Society|access-date= 27 August 2015|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20150913084110/http://rsgs.org/inspiring-people/medals-awards/livingstone-medal/|archive-date= 13 September 2015|url-status= dead}}</ref>

Named after the African explorer [[David Livingstone]] it was endowed in 1901 by his daughter, Agnes Livingstone Bruce. Designed by the sculptor [[James Pittendrigh MacGillivray]], it has a portrait of Livingstone on the front and a depiction of the Spirit of Civilisation on the reverse. <ref name = RSGS> {{cite web|url=http://rsgsexplorers.com/tag/livingstone-medal/|title= Explorers of the RSGS|publisher= Royal Scottish Geographical Society|access-date = 27 August 2015}} </ref>
