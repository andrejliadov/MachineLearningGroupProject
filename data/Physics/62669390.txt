The '''James Joule Medal and Prize''' is awarded by the [[Institute of Physics]]. It was established in 2008,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/joule/page_38559.html |title=James Joule Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics |accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref> and was named in honour of [[James Prescott Joule]], British physicist and brewer. The award is made for distinguished contributions to [[applied physics]]. The medal is silver and is accompanied by a prize of £1000.

[[Image:Joule's Apparatus (Harper's Scan).png|thumb|150px|right|Joule's apparatus for measuring the mechanical equivalent of heat]]

The medal gained international recogition in 2018 when it was awarded to Sri Lankan scientist [[Ravi Silva]]<ref name="sundaytimes2019" /> of [[University of Surrey]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.surrey.ac.uk/news/professor-ravi-silva-awarded-prestigious-institute-physics-prize |title=Professor Ravi Silva awarded prestigious Institute of Physics prize |publisher=University of Surrey |date=25 December 2018 |accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref> whose work in part led to the establishment of the [[Sri Lanka Institute of Nanotechnology]] (SLINTec).<ref>{{Cite news |title=Sri Lankan scientist win awards and international recognition |url=http://www.island.lk/index.php?page_cat=article-details&page=article-details&code_title=197603 |date=12 January 2019 |work=The Island |publisher=Upali Newspapers}}</ref>
