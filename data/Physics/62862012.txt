{{short description|List of IOP medals and prizes}}
{{third-party|date=January 2020}}
The [[Institute of Physics]] awards numerous prizes to acknowledge contributions to physics research, education and applications.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/index.html|title=IOP awards |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=20 December 2019}}</ref> It also offers smaller specific subject-group prizes, such as for [[PhD thesis]] submissions.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/activity/groups/group-prizes/page_67648.html |title=Group Prizes |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Bilateral Awards==
* The [[Max Born Medal and Prize]] is awarded yearly by the [[Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft|German Physical Society]] and the Institute of Physics in memory of the German physicist [[Max Born]]. The prize recognizes "outstanding contributions to physics" and is awarded to physicists based in Germany and in the UK or Ireland in alternate years.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/bilateral/born/page_38469.html|title=Max Born Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=30 August 2011}}</ref><ref name="DPG_Born_Prize">{{cite web |url=http://www.dpg-physik.de/preise/preistraeger_mb.html |title=Max-Born-Preis |language=German |trans-title=Max Born Prize |publisher=[[Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft|German Physical Society]] |accessdate=August 30, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110813233420/http://www.dpg-physik.de/preise/preistraeger_mb.html |archive-date=August 13, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
* The [[Fernand Holweck Medal and Prize]] is awarded jointly by the French and British Physical Societies for distinguished work in any aspect of physics that is ongoing or has been carried out within the 10 years preceding the award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/bilateral/holweck/page_38477.html|title=Fernand Holweck Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=19 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Harrie Massey]] Medal and Prize is awarded biennially jointly by the Institute of Physics and by The [[Australian Institute of Physics]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/bilateral/massey/page_38485.html|title=Harrie Massey Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=19 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Giuseppe Occhialini]] Medal and Prize is awarded to physicists in alternating years who work in Italy (even dated years) or the UK or Ireland (odd dated years).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/bilateral/occhialini/page_38492.html|title=Giuseppe Occhialini Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=19 December 2019}}</ref>

==Business Awards==
* The [[Katharine Burr Blodgett Medal and Prize]] is a gold medal awarded annually for outstanding contributions to the organisation or applications of physics to a physicist in an industrial or commercial context in any sector.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/gold/burr-blodgett/page_38458.html|title=Katharine Burr Blodgett Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|access-date=15 October 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Dennis Gabor Medal and Prize]] is a prize awarded for distinguished contributions to the application of physics in an industrial, commercial or business context.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/gabor/page_72430.html |title=Dennis Gabor Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live}}</ref>
* The [[Clifford Paterson Medal and Prize]] is awarded for exceptional early career contributions to the application of physics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/early-career/paterson/page_38665.html |title=Clifford Paterson Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|access-date=22 December 2019}}</ref>
* The Lee Lucas Award<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/activity/business/innovation/page_72672.html#leelucasaward|title=Lee Lucas Award – medical and healthcare sector|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|access-date=20 December 2019}}</ref>
* The Business Innovation Award
* The Business Start-Up Award
* The Apprentice Award
* The Apprenticeship Employer Award

==Education Awards==
* The [[Lawrence Bragg]] Medal and Prize, first awarded in 1967, is a gold medal for outstanding and sustained contributions to physics education<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/gold/bragg/page_38627.html |title=Lawrence Bragg Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=9 January 2020}}</ref>
* The [[Marie Curie-Sklodowska]] Medal and Prize, established in 2016, is awarded for "distinguished contributions to physics education and to widening participation within it."<ref name=":0" />
* The [[Daphne Jackson]] Medal and Prize, established in 2016, is awarded "for exceptional early career contributions to physics education and to widening participation within it."<ref name="DJMP">{{cite web|url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/bronze/daphne-jackson/page_67980.html|title=Daphne Jackson Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=11 July 2018}}</ref>
* The Teacher of Physics Award celebrates the success of secondary school physics teachers who have raised the profile of physics and science in schools.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/teachers-of-physics/page_38686.html|title=Teacher of Physics Award|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=20 December 2019}}</ref>
* The Technician Award, to recognise the experience of technicians and their contribution to physics<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/technician-award/page_72771.html |title=Technician Award |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=9 January 2020}}</ref>

==Outreach Awards==
* The [[Kelvin Medal and Prize]] is a gold medal instigated in October 1994 in recognition of the importance of promoting public awareness of the place of physics in the world, of its contributions to the quality of life and its advancement of an understanding of the physical world and the place of humanity within it.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/gold/kelvin/page_38636.html| title=William Thomson, Lord Kelvin Medal and Prize | publisher=Institute of Physics| access-date=26 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Lise Meitner]] Medal and Prize, established in 2016, is awarded for "distinguished contributions to public engagement within physics."<ref name=":0">{{cite web | url =http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/widening-participation-public-engagement/page_68106.html| title=Physics education and widening participation within it and public engagement within physics| publisher=Institute of Physics| access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref>
* The [[Mary Somerville]] Medal and Prize<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/early-career/somerville/page_72457.html| title=Mary Somerville Medal and Prize | publisher=Institute of Physics| access-date=26 December 2019}}</ref>

==Research Awards==
* The [[Institute of Physics Isaac Newton Medal|Isaac Newton Medal and Prize]] is a gold medal awarded annually to any physicist, regardless of subject area, background or nationality, for outstanding contributions to physics. It is accompanied by a prize of £1000, and the recipient is invited to give the Newton lecture.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/newton/page_38399.html|title=Isaac Newton Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=5 November 2014}}</ref>
* The [[Dirac Medal#Dirac Medal of the IOP|Paul Dirac Medal and Prize]] is a gold medal awarded for outstanding and sustained contributions to theoretical physics.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/gold/dirac/page_38427.html|title=Paul Dirac Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=19 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Institute of Physics Michael Faraday Medal and Prize|Michael Faraday Medal and Prize]] is a gold medal awarded annually for outstanding contributions to experimental physics to a physicist of international reputation in any sector.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/gold/faraday/page_38445.html|title=Michael Faraday Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=24 August 2011}}</ref>
* The [[Richard Glazebrook Medal and Prize]], established in 1965, is a gold medal awarded for "outstanding and sustained contributions to leadership in a physics context."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/gold/glazebrook/page_38436.html|title=Richard Glazebrook Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|access-date=15 October 2019}}</ref>
* The [[John William Strutt, Lord Rayleigh]] Medal, established in 2008, is awarded biennially in odd-numbered years, for distinguished research in theoretical, mathematical or computational physics.<ref>{{cite web|url =http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/strutt/page_72441.html |title=John William Strutt Medal and Prize |publisher= Institute of Physics}}</ref>
* The [[Sam Edwards (physicist)|Sam Edwards]] Medal and Prize is awarded for distinguished contributions in soft matter physics<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/edwards/page_72444.html |title=Sam Edwards Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Rosalind Franklin]] Medal and Prize is awarded for distinguished contributions to physics applied to the life sciences<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/franklin/page_72410.html |title=Rosalind Franklin Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Nevill Mott Medal and Prize]] is awarded for distinguished contributions to condensed matter physics<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/mott/page_72447.html |title=Nevill Mott Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[David Tabor]] Medal and Prize is awarded for distinguished contributions to surface or nanoscale physics.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/tabor/page_72411.html|title=David Tabor Medal and Prize|publisher= Institute of Physics|accessdate= 20 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Cecilia Payne-Gaposchkin]] Medal and Prize is awarded for plasma or space physics<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/payne_gaposchkin-/page_72438.html |title=Cecilia Payne-Gaposchkin Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Institute of Physics Edward Appleton Medal and Prize|Edward Appleton Medal and Prize]] is awarded for distinguished research in [[environmental physics|environmental]], [[earth physics|earth]] or [[atmospheric physics]]. Originally named after [[Charles Chree]], it was established in 1941 and is currently awarded in even-dated years.<ref name="Appleton medal and prize">{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/appleton/page_72412.html |title=Edward Appleton Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Thomas Young (scientist)|Thomas Young]] Medal and Prize is awarded biennially in odd-numbered years, for distinguished research in the field of optics, including physics outside the visible region.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/young/page_72423.html |title=Thomas Young Medal and Prize |publisher= Institute of Physics|accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Institute of Physics Joseph Thomson Medal and Prize|Joseph Thomson Medal and Prize]], established in 2008, is awarded biennially, in even-numbered years, for distinguished research in [[atomic physics]] (including [[quantum optics]]) or [[molecular physics]].<ref>{{cite web|url =http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/thomson/page_72421.html |title=Joseph Thomson Medal and Prize |publisher= Institute of Physics|accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Ernest Rutherford]] Medal and Prize, awarded biennially in even-numbered years, was instituted in 1966, replacing the Rutherford Memorial Lecture. The award recognises distinguished research in [[nuclear physics]] or [[nuclear technology]] and is named in honour of [[Ernest Rutherford|Lord Rutherford of Nelson]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/rutherford/page_72417.html|title=Ernest Rutherford Medal and Prize|publisher= Institute of Physics|accessdate = 20 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[James Chadwick]] Medal and Prize is awarded "for distinguished contributions to particle physics."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/chadwick/page_72415.html|title=James Chadwick Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=19 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Fred Hoyle Medal and Prize]] is awarded for distinguished contributions to astrophysics or cosmology<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/hoyle/page_72451.html |title=Fred Hoyle Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Peter Mansfield]] Medal and Prize is awarded for [[medical physics]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/mansfield-prize/page_72481.html |title=Peter Mansfield Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[James Joule Medal and Prize]] is awarded for applied physics<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/subject/joule/page_38559.html |title=James Joule Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=26 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[James Clerk Maxwell]] Medal and Prize has been awarded annually since 1962 to recognize outstanding early-career contributions to theoretical physics.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iop.org/about/awards/early-career/maxwell/page_38656.html|title=James Clerk Maxwell Medal and Prize|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=18 January 2020}}</ref>
* The [[Henry Moseley]] Medal and Prize is awarded for exceptional early career contributions to experimental physics<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/early-career/moseley/page_38648.html |title=Henry Moseley Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live}}</ref>
* The [[Jocelyn Bell Burnell]] Medal and Prize was originally known as the 'Very Early Career Female Physicist Award'<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/career/bell-burnell/page_67977.html |title=Jocelyn Bell Burnell Medal and Prize |publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=23 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Simon Memorial Prize]]

==Service to the IOP Awards==
* The [[President's Medal of the IOP|President's Medal]] can be given to both physicists and non-physicists who have provided meritorious services in various fields of endeavour which were of benefit to physics in general and the Institute in particular.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/president/page_38412.html|title=The President's medal|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=19 December 2019}}</ref>
* The [[Phillips Award]] is awarded for distinguished service to the Institute of Physics.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/phillips/page_53486.html|title=Phillips Award|publisher=Institute of Physics|url-status=live|accessdate=20 December 2019}}</ref>
