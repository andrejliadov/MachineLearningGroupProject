'''Bennett's laws''' of [[quantum information]] are:
# 1 [[qubit]] <math>\geqslant</math> 1 [[bit]] ([[classical information|classical]]),
# 1 qubit <math>\geqslant</math> 1 [[Bell state|ebit]] ([[quantum entanglement|entanglement]] bit),
# 1 ebit + 1 qubit <math>\geqslant</math> 2 bits (i.e. [[superdense coding]]),
# 1 ebit + 2 bits <math>\geqslant</math> 1 qubit (i.e. [[quantum teleportation]]),

where <math>\geqslant</math> indicates "can do the job of".

These principles were formulated around 1993 by [[Charles H. Bennett (computer scientist)|Charles H. Bennett]].
