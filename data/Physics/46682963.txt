'''Marcia Anna Keith''' (1859–1950)<ref name="Women in Science: Antiquity Through the Nineteenth Century : a Biographical Dictionary with Annotated Bibliography">{{cite book|last1=Ogilvie|first1=Marilyn Bailey|title=Women in Science: Antiquity Through the Nineteenth Century : a Biographical Dictionary with Annotated Bibliography|date=1990|publisher=The MIT Press|page=107}}</ref> was a [[physicist]], teacher of physics to women, and a charter member of the [[American Physical Society]] since its founding in 1899.

==Early life and education==

Marcia Keith was born in [[Brockton, Massachusetts|Brockton]], Massachusetts on 10 September 1859<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/agenealogydesce00keitgoog|title=A genealogy of the descendants of Benjamin Keith through Timothy, son of Rev. James Keith, together with an historical sketch of the early family and personal reminiscences of recent generations|last=Keith|first=Ziba C. (Ziba Cary)|date=1889|publisher=Brockton, G. A. Goodall, printer|others=University of Wisconsin - Madison}}</ref> to parents Mary Ann (nee Gary) and Arza Keith (married 25 April 1854). She attended [[Mount Holyoke College]], earning her BS in 1892. She was a “special student” at [[Worcester Polytechnic Institute]] in 1887 and 1889, and went to the [[University of Berlin]] from 1897 to 1898. In the summer of 1901 Keith attended the [[University of Chicago]].<ref name="Women in Science: Antiquity Through the Nineteenth Century : a Biographical Dictionary with Annotated Bibliography" />

==Teaching career==

From 1876 until 1879 Keith taught in the public school system of Massachusetts. Four years later, in 1883, she began to work as a science instructor at the Michigan Seminary, in [[Kalamazoo, Michigan|Kalamazoo]]<ref name="Alumnae Association of Mount Holyoke College, South Hadley, Massachusetts">{{cite web|url=https://www.mtholyoke.edu/~dalbino/letters/women/mkeith.html|title=Marcia Anna Keith 1883|last=|first=|date=|website=Mount Holyoke College|publisher=Alumnae Association of Mount Holyoke College, South Hadley, Massachusetts|location=Bulletin Series 30, no. 5|archive-url=|archive-date=|accessdate=12 May 2015}}</ref>  until 1885. Beginning in 1885 Keith taught mathematics at Mount Holyoke, and then became the school’s first full-time teacher in the physics department there. From 1889 until 1903 Keith was the head of the department.<ref name="Women in Science: Antiquity Through the Nineteenth Century : a Biographical Dictionary with Annotated Bibliography" />   In 1904 Keith taught at [[Norton, Massachusetts|Norton]].<ref name="Alumnae Association of Mount Holyoke College, South Hadley, Massachusetts" />  She taught at [[Lake Erie College]] in [[Painesville]], Ohio,<ref name="Alumnae Association of Mount Holyoke College, South Hadley, Massachusetts" />  from 1905 until 1906.<ref name="CWP at UCLA">{{cite web|title=Keith, Marcia Anna|url=http://cwp.library.ucla.edu/Phase2/Keith,_Marcia_Anna@941234567.html|website=CWP at UCLA|publisher=Contributions of Twentieth Century Women to Physics at UCLA|accessdate=12 May 2015|archivedate=}}</ref>
 
Keith was a pioneer in the education of women in the science of physics. It is believed that she is the first to introduce individual laboratory work to students.<ref name="CWP at UCLA" />  She also launched the physics colloquium at Mt. Holyoke in order to increase awareness among students of the growing field of physics.<ref name="CWP at UCLA" />

==Research==

Keith investigated the physics of heat transmission in gases at low temperatures.<ref name="CWP at UCLA" />

==Non-academic career==

From 1906 until 1908<ref name="CWP at UCLA" /> Keith worked for the firm of Herbert C. Keith in New York City as a consulting engineer.<ref name="Women in Science: Antiquity Through the Nineteenth Century : a Biographical Dictionary with Annotated Bibliography" /><ref name="CWP at UCLA" />

==American Physical Society==

Keith was a charter member of the American Physical Society, helping to establish the group in 1899.<ref name="Women in Science: Antiquity Through the Nineteenth Century : a Biographical Dictionary with Annotated Bibliography" />  Keith and [[Isabelle Stone]] of Vasser were the only two women who were in attendance at the founding of the group.<ref name="CWP at UCLA" />

==Family==

Marcia Anna had four sisters, Lucy Keith, Mary Helen Keith, Sarah Emma Keith, and Cora Frances Keith, and a niece, Mary Keith Warren.<ref name="Alumnae Association of Mount Holyoke College, South Hadley, Massachusetts" />
