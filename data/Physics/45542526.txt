{{short description|Center for Theoretical Studies, University of Miami,}}
The '''J. Robert Oppenheimer Memorial Prize''' and Medal was awarded by the [[Center for Theoretical Studies, University of Miami]], from 1969, until 1984. Established in memory of US physicist [[J. Robert Oppenheimer]], the award consisted of a medal, certificate and a $1000 honorarium. It was awarded for "outstanding contributions to the theoretical natural sciences [...] during the preceding decade".{{sfn|Walter|1982|p=438}}

The acceptance speech for the inaugural award to Dirac was published as ''The Development of Quantum Theory'' (1971).<ref>{{cite book|last=Dirac|first=P.A.M.|title=The Development of Quantum Theory|publisher=Gordon and Breach|location=New York|year=1971}}</ref>
